**Nithon** is the informal name for **TNO 2301-DR-44**, a trans-
Neptunian object catalogued in 2301. The IAU reference is the
catalog number; "Nithon" is used internally by M-K and (through
obscure provenance) the Chamber.

## Public orbital data

- **Classification:** Trans-Neptunian object, scattered disk.
- **Perihelion:** ~84 AU.
- **Mean diameter:** ~1,100 km.
- **Surface:** metallic-silicate regolith.
- **Exosphere:** thin, nitrogen and neon traces.
- **Surface temperature:** ~–230 °C.
- **Surface gravity:** ~0.04 g.
- **Illumination:** the sun reads as a bright star.

## Mission scope (public)

The *Halyard*'s charter is a nine-to-twelve-month survey of the
body: surface operations from the Kestrel landers, spectroscopy
and radiological survey, sample return. The charter does not
name the body in its public text.

## Prior missions

Two prior M-K vessels surveyed the area:

- **MKS *Meridian***, 2344. Short survey; returned.
- **MKS *Ostland***, 2346. Short survey; returned.

Both are on the public M-K mission record. The *Halyard* is
scheduled as the first **extended-duration** mission to the body.

## Arrival

Expected orbital insertion: early November 2348, pending burn
profile adjustments. First Kestrel surface deployment typically
follows 3–5 days after parking.
