# Iapetus

(ARTEMIS visibility set to ``unknown`` — the body's
Elder-Thing-adjacent dark-hemisphere material is
classified above ARTEMIS's allocation. SIGMA-4 has
public-record knowledge.)

Iapetus is Saturn's third-largest moon, the most
visually striking of the major Saturnian bodies, and —
in ways that public records do not fully describe — the
*second* major Elder Thing site in the human-accessible
solar system after the Antarctic complexes on Earth.
The 40,000 Iapetians live in the moon's brighter trailing
hemisphere; the famously dark *leading hemisphere*
remains under joint UN-OPA classified-survey
restrictions parallel to the Pallas regime.

For most off-world residents, Iapetus is the *odd
moon* — the one with the visible color difference,
the one with the equatorial ridge that no twentieth-or-
twenty-first-century geologist could account for, the
one that the major outer-system observatory operates
from. The deeper structure of the body's anomalies is
Keeper-only.

## The body

- Diameter: 1,470 km
- Surface gravity: 0.022 g
- Surface temperature: -150°C (mean)
- Composition: water-ice with substantial dark-
  organic-coating on the leading hemisphere
- Distance from Sun: 9.5 AU (Saturn system)

Iapetus has two visually distinct hemispheres:

- The **trailing hemisphere** — bright water-ice
  surface, similar in appearance to other Saturnian
  moons. Most human settlement is here.
- The **leading hemisphere** — coated with a thick
  layer of dark organic material; reflectivity
  approximately 4% versus 60% on the trailing side.
  The contrast is the most extreme on any solar-system
  body. Settlement here is restricted.

The body also has the *Iapetian Equatorial Ridge* — a
12-km-tall structure running along the moon's equator,
spanning roughly 1,300 km of the leading hemisphere. The
ridge's geological origin was unexplained for centuries.
The classified record is that the ridge is *partly
artificial* — Elder Thing infrastructure overlaid on a
natural feature, with sub-surface installations beneath
it.

## The classified component

(*Keeper-only; ARTEMIS visibility set to "unknown".*)

The Iapetian leading hemisphere contains a substantial
Elder Thing installation, larger than Pallas but smaller
than the Antarctic complexes. The installation includes:

- The Equatorial Ridge sub-surface structures —
  roughly 800 km of accessible installation along the
  ridge's length
- Several outlying sub-surface sites scattered across
  the leading hemisphere
- A small population of dormant Elder-Thing-engineered
  organisms (not shoggoths in the standard sense; a
  related but distinct lineage that the SPB has
  classified separately)

The site has been classified since 2287, with formal
joint UN-OPA reserve protocols established 2294 (six
years before the parallel Pallas protocol). The
Iapetian protocol is *more restrictive* than the
Pallas one — even SPB has minimal direct access; most
research is done through carefully-monitored
sample-return protocols rather than on-site
investigation.

The Caretakers are aware of the Iapetian site and
consider it *a relatively minor outer-system
installation* compared to what they describe as the
larger Elder Thing operational legacy elsewhere. The
Iapetian site's significance is more *Solar-System-
internal* than the gate at Nithon.

## Settlement structure

Iapetus's 40,000 residents are concentrated:

- **Cassini Station** (24K) — the principal
  settlement on the trailing hemisphere. The
  Saturnian system's largest astronomical observatory
  is here. Includes the Cassini Astronomical Archive
  (one of the most extensive astronomical data
  collections in human space).
- **Trailing Hemisphere Outposts** (12K) — smaller
  residential and supply communities.
- **Leading Hemisphere Reserve Stations** (4K) —
  the security-and-monitoring installations on the
  reserve perimeter. Personnel are vetted through
  joint UN-OPA protocols; the population rotates
  through security-cleared posts.

## Politics

Iapetus's local politics are *small-community-
technocratic*. Cassini Station functions effectively as
a research-institutional community with a small
attached service population. Politically the body
defers to the broader Saturnian system politics
(coordinated through Titan and the SNCA).

The Cassini Station Director is Dr. Aurelio Mendiola-
Choi, an astronomer of substantial reputation. The
Director's role is more institutional than political;
the body's larger political interests are handled
through Titan-based representation.

## Economy

Iapetus's economy is small ($24B UN-equivalent),
specialized, and substantially government-funded.
Sectors:

- **Astronomical research** — the dominant sector;
  the Cassini Observatory's work is one of the
  major astronomical research programs in human
  space
- **Reserve security and monitoring** — the
  classified-site operations
- **Specialized supply and logistics** — small but
  high-margin

## Cultural texture

Iapetus's cultural register is *astronomical-quiet*:

- **The dark-hemisphere awareness.** Iapetians live
  with the visual reality of their two-toned moon
  every day. The cultural production engages with
  the contrast — visual arts, literature, music
  all reference the boundary, the asymmetry, the
  *darkness on the other side*.
- **The astronomical register.** The Cassini
  Observatory's presence shapes much of the
  intellectual life. Astronomical lectures,
  scientific publications, observation-data are
  part of daily cultural awareness.
- **The reserve-adjacent register.** Like Pallas,
  many Iapetians know that *something is on the
  leading hemisphere* without knowing what. The
  cultural production engages with this in a
  particular way — Iapetian fiction has a
  recognizable *uncanny* register that other
  outer-system cultural traditions lack.
- **The Saturnian gaze.** Saturn dominates the sky
  more dramatically from Iapetus than from most
  Saturnian bodies (the orbital position keeps
  Saturn substantially face-on for much of the
  Iapetian year). The cultural relationship with
  Saturn is correspondingly intense.

## Iapetus and the *Halyard*

The *Halyard* does not visit Iapetus. However:

- **Halverson** has academic relationships at the
  Cassini Observatory; some of her published work
  uses Cassini data. Her connections here are
  legitimate and substantial.
- **The classified Iapetian site** is, like the
  classified Pallas site, a parallel campaign
  resource. If the *Halyard*'s findings produce
  political pressure for Iapetian disclosure, the
  politics get complicated.
- The *equatorial ridge anomaly* is in the public
  record as a *geological mystery*. A *Halyard*
  PC who has read widely in planetary science may
  be aware of it. Whether they connect it to
  what they find at Nithon is up to play.

## Notes for play

- An Iapetian PC is rare. The astronomical-research
  background is the natural option.
- The dark-hemisphere awareness is a useful
  character texture for cosmic-horror fiction.
- The Iapetian site connection is a Keeper resource
  for late-act political-disclosure plot lines.
