# Hyperion

Hyperion is one of Saturn's irregular outer moons, the
smallest of the bodies with a permanent human
presence in the Saturnian system, and the only major
moon in the solar system with *chaotic rotation* —
its tumbling rotation makes Hyperion's surface days
unpredictable in length, orientation, and duration.

The 30,000 Hyperion residents work small-scale
specialty mining operations on a body whose chaotic
geography and irregular shape make standard
extraction techniques impossible. The work is
specialized, the conditions are unusual, and the
cultural register is correspondingly distinctive.

## The body

- Average diameter: 270 km (highly irregular shape;
  not spherical)
- Surface gravity: extremely variable due to
  irregular shape; effective range 0.005-0.012 g
- Surface temperature: -180°C (mean)
- Composition: water-ice with organic-compound
  surface coloration; substantial sub-surface
  porosity
- Distance from Sun: 9.5 AU (Saturn system)
- Rotation: chaotic; the body tumbles rather than
  rotates predictably

The chaotic rotation is unique among major moons.
The body's tumbling makes:
- Solar exposure cycles unpredictable
- Surface-feature day-length varying with position
- Orbital approach navigation more complex than
  for any other major moon

## Operations

Hyperion's specialty mining focuses on:

- **Organic compounds** in the body's surface
  staining — used for specialty manufacturing
- **Specific mineral compositions** in the sub-
  surface porous structure — accessible because of
  the body's low density
- **Astronomical observation** — Hyperion's
  irregular position relative to Saturn provides
  observational angles unavailable from other
  Saturnian bodies

Settlement is sub-surface throughout (the chaotic
rotation makes surface installations untenable). The
30,000 residents live in tunnel-and-chamber complexes
within the body's porous structure.

## Politics

Hyperion's local politics is informal; the body's
small population doesn't justify major institutional
infrastructure. Coordination with the broader
Saturnian system runs through Titan-based OPA
representation.

## Cultural texture

Hyperion's culture is *unusual* in distinct ways:

- **The chaotic-rotation register.** Hyperion
  residents live with day-length variability that
  no other human population experiences. The
  cultural production engages with this — the
  *unpredictable day* is a recurring metaphor
  in Hyperion writing and music.
- **The irregular-body register.** Hyperion's
  shape is not spherical; the body's geography is
  irregular in ways that other moons aren't. The
  cultural production engages with the
  *unconventional geometry* of the body.
- **The specialty-mining register.** Hyperion
  workers take pride in the body's specialized
  operations; the work is technically demanding
  in particular ways.
- **The remote-Saturnian register.** Hyperion is
  remote even by Saturnian standards; the
  cultural register is correspondingly *quiet,
  isolated, specialized*.

## Notes for play

- A Hyperion PC is *very rare*. The chaotic-
  rotation experience is unusual enough to be a
  distinctive character texture.
- The specialty-mining background is natural for
  Hyperion PCs.
