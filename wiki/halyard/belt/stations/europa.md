# Europa

Europa is the smallest of the four major Jovian moons by
human population, the most jurisdictionally constrained
body in the human solar system, and — relevantly for the
campaign's underlying themes — the only place humanity
has confirmed *non-human life* exists, even if the life
in question is microbial.

The 4 million Europans live in tightly-regulated surface
and near-surface installations under the *Europa Treaty
Authority*'s strict environmental protocols. The body's
sub-surface ocean — known to harbor a complex chemo-
synthetic microbial ecosystem since 2298 — is the
largest *no-contact reserve* in human space.

The Europa precedent is the campaign-relevant background.
*Humanity has already encountered non-human life and
established protocols for managing the encounter.* The
protocols have, mostly, worked. The campaign will test
whether they extend.

## The body

- Diameter: 3,122 km
- Surface gravity: 0.134 g
- Surface temperature: -171°C (mean)
- Composition: rock-and-ice; substantial sub-surface
  liquid-water ocean (the *Europa Ocean*)
- Atmosphere: thin oxygen
- Distance from Sun: 5.2 AU (Jupiter system)

The Europa Ocean is the body's defining feature. Beneath
approximately 10-30 km of surface ice (variable by
location), a global liquid-water ocean covers the
sub-surface to a depth of 80-100 km. The ocean is
warmed by tidal heating from Jupiter's gravitational
interaction.

## The Europan biosphere

The Europa Ocean contains an established chemosynthetic
microbial ecosystem. The ecosystem was first detected by
indirect evidence in the 2150s; confirmed by direct
sampling in 2236; formally characterized in 2298 (the
*Europa Findings* of that year are one of the most
significant scientific publications in human history).

The biosphere's characteristics:

- Chemosynthetic; energy from chemical gradients at
  the ocean-rock interface and from radiolytic
  reactions at the ice-ocean interface
- Microbial; no multicellular life detected
- Ecologically diverse; multiple distinct microbial
  lineages occupy different niches in the
  sub-surface environment
- Genetically *not* compatible with Earth biology;
  the Europan organisms use a different genetic
  encoding (DNA-analog molecules with different base
  chemistry; the technical details are on file in
  the *Europa Findings* and have been the subject of
  enormous biological-research effort)
- Probably ancient; the ecosystem's diversity
  suggests a multi-billion-year evolutionary history

The Europan organisms are the *only* confirmed
non-human life in the human solar system. (The Mars
microbial findings of the early twenty-second century
turned out to be Earth-contamination; later Mars-side
work has not produced any unambiguous Mars-native
biology.)

## The Europa Treaty Authority

The body is governed by the **Europa Treaty Authority**
(ETA), a multilateral body established by the 2298
Europa Treaty. Signatories include the UN, the MCR,
the OPA, and the Lunar Republic. The Authority's
mandate:

- Strict no-contamination protocols protecting the
  Europan biosphere from Earth-biological
  contamination
- Strict no-extraction protocols preventing
  industrial exploitation of Europan resources
- Regulated scientific access; all sub-surface
  research must be ETA-authorized
- Enforcement authority; the Authority operates a
  small but well-equipped enforcement service that
  can interdict unauthorized landings

The Treaty's protocols are *strict* by inner-system
standards. All Europan settlements operate in
biological isolation; all surface vehicles use
sterilized fuels and propulsion; all personnel
undergo decontamination cycles before entering the
sub-surface research zones.

## Settlement structure

Europa's 4 million residents live primarily in:

- **Conamara** (1.4M) — the planetary capital and
  the ETA headquarters. Surface installation; one of
  the most environmentally controlled cities in
  human space.
- **Pwyll** (0.9M) — the principal scientific-research
  city. The Europan Biosphere Institute is here.
- **Tara** (0.6M) — administrative and supply.
- **Argadnel** (0.4M) — research and supply.
- **Smaller settlements and orbital installations** —
  about 0.7M total.

Most Europans are scientific-research or administrative
personnel. The body has no significant industrial sector
by design.

## Politics

Europa's politics are *technocratic-conservation*. The
body's OPA representation has been *centrist-protective*:
strongly committed to the Treaty protocols, suspicious
of any pressure for relaxed access, willing to
cooperate with Inner-System authorities on enforcement.

The current Provincial Governor is Marisol Yamashita-
Owolabi, a former biospheric researcher. Yamashita-
Owolabi has been the most prominent Europan voice
in the broader OPA's debates over biosphere-protection
policies.

## Economy

Europa's economy is small ($120B UN-equivalent) but
specialized. Sectors:

- **Scientific research** — overwhelmingly dominant
- **Government and ETA services** — substantial
- **Specialized supply and logistics** — small but
  high-value (the strict protocols require specialized
  equipment and consumables)
- **Tourism** — limited but exists; Europa receives
  approximately 80,000 visitors per year, mostly
  scientific and academic

## Cultural texture

Europa's culture has features no other Belt body has:

- **The protocol register.** Europans live in an
  environment where *strict procedures* are not
  negotiable. The cultural register is correspondingly
  *deliberate, careful, attention-to-detail-oriented*.
- **The biosphere-aware register.** The Europan
  microbial ecosystem is a constant cultural
  reference. Europan literature, music, and visual
  arts engage with the biosphere as a primary
  subject.
- **The Treaty-conservation register.** Europan
  cultural production has a strong *conservation
  ethos* that shapes much of the body's intellectual
  life.
- **The scientific-monastic register.** The
  combination of strict protocols, biosphere-
  awareness, and remote location has produced a
  particular cultural mode that some observers
  describe as *scientific-monastic* — patient,
  attentive, oriented toward long-term study.

## Europa and the *Halyard* campaign

The Europa precedent is *substantively important* for
the campaign's underlying themes:

- The Treaty has functioned as humanity's *first
  protocol* for managing non-human life. The
  protocols have been imperfect but have largely
  worked.
- The biological discovery did *not* destabilize human
  civilization. The cosmological-religious
  consequences were absorbed by the major
  traditions; the political consequences were
  manageable.
- This precedent is *the most relevant analogy* for
  what would happen if the *Halyard* contract
  produces non-human-life findings of any kind.

If the campaign engages with this analogy, Europa's
example suggests:

- *Strict precautionary protocols can work*
- *Cosmological-religious adjustment is achievable*
- *Political stability can hold across non-human-life
  discoveries*
- But — and this is the campaign's deeper question —
  the Europan biosphere is microbial. The Caretakers
  are not. Whether the Europa precedent extends to
  intelligent life is unsettled.

## Notes for play

- A Europan PC has the most *deliberate, scientifically-
  protocol-oriented* register of any Belt-PC option.
- The biospheric-research background is natural for
  Europan PCs.
- The Europa precedent is a useful narrative resource
  for the campaign's thematic conversations. Players
  who know the Europa story will reach for it
  naturally as a reference point when the *Halyard*'s
  findings come into question.
