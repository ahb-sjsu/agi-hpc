# Io

Io is the most volcanically active body in the solar
system, the most radiation-exposed major Jovian moon,
and the smallest of the four Galilean moons by human
population. Its 2 million residents work specialized
volcanic-cycle industries that no other Belt body can
support — extraction of sulfur and silicate compounds
in unusual quantities, exploitation of the moon's
ongoing geothermal activity, and the operation of the
Jovian system's specialized high-radiation industrial
sector.

Io's character is *extreme*. The body's surface
conditions are the most hostile of any settled human
location; the population is small by design; the work
is specialized and dangerous; the cultural register is
correspondingly distinctive.

## The body

- Diameter: 3,643 km
- Surface gravity: 0.183 g
- Surface temperature: highly variable; -130°C base
  with localized volcanic features reaching +1,500°C
- Composition: silicate rock with substantial sulfur-
  compound surface deposits
- Distance from Sun: 5.2 AU (Jupiter system)
- Geological activity: extreme; over 400 active
  volcanoes, continuous surface resurfacing on
  geological timescales of centuries to millennia
- Radiation environment: the most intense of any
  major moon; deep within Jupiter's magnetosphere

Surface settlement is *minimal*. All habitation is
sub-surface; surface operations require the most
aggressive shielding-and-rotation protocols of any
human location. Surface workers operate in 6-hour
shifts with strict cumulative exposure limits.

## Settlement structure

Io's 2 million residents are concentrated:

- **Pele Station** (1.2M) — the planetary capital;
  sub-surface tunnel city in the Pele region. Named
  for the volcanic feature above it.
- **Loki Industrial Zone** (0.5M) — the principal
  sulfur-and-silicate extraction operation.
- **Smaller volcanic-region settlements** (0.3M) —
  specialized operations near specific volcanic
  features.

## Politics

Io's politics are *labor-OPA-extreme*. The Asteroid
Miners' Federation's Io chapter has historically been
the most confrontational of the Federation's
constituencies; Io's working conditions and political
sensibilities are intense.

The current Provincial Governor is Hara Brennt-Akande,
a former volcanic-extraction engineer. Brennt-Akande's
political register is *unflinchingly pro-labor,
firmly OPA-Frontier-aligned, suspicious of any
Inner-System or even Inner-Belt intervention*.

## Economy

Io's economy is roughly $0.18T UN-equivalent.
Sectors:

- **Sulfur extraction and processing** — Io supplies
  substantial fractions of human space's sulfur
  for industrial chemistry
- **Silicate-compound extraction** — specialty
  silicate compounds with applications in fusion
  and high-temperature manufacturing
- **Geothermal energy harvesting** — limited but
  growing; Io's geological activity is being
  exploited for specialized power generation
- **Specialty manufacturing** — small-volume,
  high-margin; products that benefit from extreme
  manufacturing conditions

## Cultural texture

Io's cultural register is *intense*:

- **The volcanic-aware register.** Ionians live above
  active volcanism. The cultural production engages
  with this — fire, eruption, transformation are
  recurring themes in Ionian art and literature.
- **The radiation-managed register.** Time-outside
  is a measured commodity. The cultural production
  reflects the relationship with limit, with
  exposure, with the body's fundamental hostility.
- **The labor-political register.** Most Ionians
  are politically engaged; the cultural production
  reflects active labor politics.
- **The extreme-environment register.** Ionian
  culture has a pride about working *the worst
  conditions in human space* that other Belt cultures
  recognize and respect.

## Notes for play

- An Ionian PC is *uncommon* but distinctive. The
  extreme-environment register and OPA-Frontier
  politics are character textures unlike any other.
- The radiation-management background is useful for
  outer-system PCs.
- Ionian PCs default to a particular kind of pride
  — *we work the worst conditions* — that other PCs
  may find slightly intimidating.
