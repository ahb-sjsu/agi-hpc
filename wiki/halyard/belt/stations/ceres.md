# Ceres Station

![Cunha Drum spin habitat interior, Ceres](https://halyard.atlas-sjsu.duckdns.org/art/belt-ceres-cunha-drum.png)

Ceres is the largest single human settlement in the asteroid
Belt, the operational and cultural capital of the Belt's
inner-system reach, and — relevantly for this campaign —
the *Halyard*'s departure port. The 28 million Ceresians,
mostly distributed across the dwarf planet's surface and
sub-surface infrastructure, comprise the largest Belt
population by a substantial margin.

## The body

Ceres itself is the smallest of the dwarf planets — 940 km
mean diameter, 0.029 g surface gravity, primarily silicate-
ice composition with a substantial water-ice component near
the surface. The natural environment is hostile: vacuum,
~-100°C surface temperature, no atmosphere, and a gravity so
low that human residents must be physiologically managed
against bone loss.

The original 2087 settlement worked the surface directly. By
the 2150s the population had begun moving sub-surface, where
the rock provided radiation shielding and thermal stability.
By 2348 the surface settlements are minimal — landing pads,
solar farms, comm arrays — and the population lives in the
sub-surface tunnels and spin habitats.

## The Spin

Ceres's defining feature is the **Cunha Drum** — a 22-km-
diameter rotating cylindrical habitat that takes up the
inner volume of the principal sub-surface chamber, spinning
to provide 0.3 g of artificial gravity for the bulk of the
Ceresian population.

The Drum was constructed 2244–2287 in three phases. It is
the largest single artificial-gravity environment in human
space (Tycho's spin-habs are larger in cumulative volume but
smaller individually). The Drum spins at 6 rotations per
hour; the gravity is comfortable (0.3 g, a Belter-friendly
level); the inner surface is ~1,500 km² of habitat zone —
neighborhoods, parks, schools, the Cunha Public Library, the
Ceres Concert Hall, and the substantial portion of the
city's residential housing.

About 80% of Ceres's population lives in the Drum. The
remaining 20% live in:

- The **Outer Port**, the docking and transit infrastructure
  in the lower-gravity sub-surface tunnels around the Drum's
  axes.
- The **Industrial Sub-Surface**, the heavy-industry zones
  too noisy or hazardous for the Drum.
- The **Surface Stations**, the small surface installations
  for landing operations, solar collection, and the like.

## The Outer Port

This is where the *Halyard* will depart from and where
Session 0's contract scene is set. The Outer Port is a
labyrinth of low-gravity tunnels, docking bays, and
warehouse spaces. It is the busiest port in Belt space —
roughly 1,400 ship movements per day — and the principal
node for inter-system traffic.

The Outer Port has three primary zones:

- **The Concourse** — the public-facing area. Restaurants,
  bars, hotels, the offices of the major shipping
  companies, and the small private-office rentals (one of
  which Halverson has rented for the contract scene).
- **The Bays** — the docking infrastructure. 480 active
  docking bays of varying sizes; the *Halyard*'s bay (B-29)
  is in the medium-vessel cluster.
- **The Industrial Wing** — bulk-cargo handling, fuel
  depots, the major workshops.

Walking the Concourse, a visitor passes through every
flavor of Belt life: spinner crews on shore leave,
merchant captains negotiating freight, OPA delegations,
UN customs officers (a small detachment, present by
treaty), Mars-Belt traders, and the constant background
of Lang Belta conversation that sometimes feels like a
fourth ambient noise alongside the air handlers, the
cargo movers, and the distant rumble of the Drum.

## Politics

Ceres is governed by the **Ceres Provincial Council**, a
35-member body elected from districts. The Council sits in
the **Provincial Hall** — a refurbished 2147 administrative
building in the Drum's central plaza. The current Provincial
Governor is **Adisa Onoa**, a third-generation Belter
politician.

Ceres's politics are a microcosm of OPA politics. The major
factions:

- **The Cunha Caucus** — pro-trade, pro-Earth-relations,
  the establishment. Currently in power.
- **The Frontier League** — pro-OPA-strengthening, more
  skeptical of Earth, with a strong base among the
  spinner unions.
- **The Independents' Coalition** — locally focused,
  technocratic, populist.

Ceres's relationship with the larger OPA is *first among
peers* — Ceres is the largest single OPA member, but it
does not dominate. The Plenum has a strong tradition of
preventing any single province from exercising effective
control.

## Culture

Ceresian culture is recognizably Belt but with its own
character:

- **Cosmopolitanism.** Ceres has the most diverse Belt
  population. Lang Belta is dominant but English, Mandarin,
  Yoruba, Russian, and Hindi are heard everywhere. Most
  Ceresians are functionally bilingual or trilingual.
- **The Drum life.** Most Ceresians grew up in the Drum
  and have a particular relationship to artificial gravity
  — a comfort with the slight wobble at 0.3 g, an
  ear-trained-by-spin sensibility, an instinct for the
  geometry of cylindrical environments.
- **The Outer Port culture.** A subset of Ceresians live
  closer to the Port than the Drum and have a more
  rough-and-ready style. Port culture is faster-moving,
  more transactional, and more directly engaged with the
  rest of human space.
- **The Cunha Public Library.** The largest library in the
  Belt. Operates an unusual *open archive* policy — anyone
  can deposit any document and request access to anything
  on file. The result is one of the most varied
  collections in human space, including a substantial
  oral-history archive of Belt working-class life.

## The contract scene context

Halverson has rented a small private office in the Concourse
for the *Halyard* contract signing. The office is in the
**Belt Trade Annex**, a building on the Concourse's
secondary level whose tenants are mostly small consulting
firms, freight brokers, and (ironically) two intelligence
liaison offices for the UNN and the MCR.

Halverson's office is unremarkable — a small reception
area, a single windowed room with a view of the Concourse
below, a printed contract on the desk, a coffee service
that no one is drinking. The setup is deliberate. Halverson
wanted a space that signals *seriousness* without signaling
*power*; the Belt Trade Annex office accomplishes both.

The walk from the *Halyard*'s docking bay (B-29) to the
office takes about 25 minutes — through the Bays' transit
corridor, up two levels in the gravity gradient, into the
Concourse, and to the Annex. PCs may walk this together
(in which case bonds form along the way) or separately (in
which case they meet for the first time at the office).

After the contract is signed, the next time the crew
gathers will be aboard the *Halyard*, two weeks later, for
the outbound burn.

## Notes for play

- The Concourse is a setting the campaign may return to
  if the crew passes back through Ceres on the return
  leg. Keep the geography in mind.
- A non-Belter PC visiting Ceres has small adjustments to
  make — gravity, language, atmospheric standards.
  Ceresian customs officials are friendly but precise; the
  station's medical inspections are thorough.
- The Cunha Public Library is a useful narrative resource
  if a PC needs to research something during the layover.
  The Library's collection is broad and accessible.
- The Outer Port has bars where any Belter PC has a
  history. The campaign may use one of them as a flashback
  setting.
