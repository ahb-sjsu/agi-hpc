# Ganymede

Ganymede is the largest moon in the solar system, the
Jovian system's principal population center, and one of
the most jurisdictionally complex bodies in human space.
The 18 million Ganymedeans live under joint OPA-MCR-UN
administration — a unique tripartite governance regime
that has held since the 2298 Ganymede Compact and that
operates better in practice than its byzantine structure
would suggest.

The body's political-cultural identity is *Belt-by-
inclination, Jovian-by-residence, scientifically-engaged-
by-vocation*. Ganymede's residents identify primarily
as Belters; the OPA's Plenum representation reflects
this. The MCR and UN administrative roles are accepted
as the price of the body's complex history.

## The body

Ganymede is large:

- Diameter: 5,268 km (larger than Mercury)
- Surface gravity: 0.146 g
- Surface temperature: -163°C (mean)
- Composition: silicate-and-water-ice; substantial
  sub-surface ocean
- Atmosphere: very thin (oxygen, primarily)
- Distance from Sun: 5.2 AU (Jupiter system)
- Magnetosphere: yes; Ganymede has its own (the only
  moon in the solar system that does)

Ganymede's magnetosphere has been a substantial
operational asset — the magnetic field protects surface
operations from Jupiter's intense radiation belts.

## Settlement structure

Ganymede's population is concentrated in:

- **Galileo City** (8M) — the planetary capital. A
  largely-sub-surface city under the Galileo Regio
  ice surface. Includes the joint-administration
  governmental complex, the University of Ganymede,
  and the major industrial-and-research facilities.
- **Marius Bay** (4M) — the second-largest
  settlement. Industrial focus.
- **Sippar** (2M) — agricultural and water-extraction
  focus. The largest single deuterium-extraction
  facility in the Jovian system is here.
- **Phaeton** (2M) — research-oriented; the Jovian
  Astronomical Observatory's principal facility.
- **Smaller settlements** — about 2M total across
  many smaller communities.

## The Ganymede Compact

The body's tripartite governance derives from the 2298
Ganymede Compact, an OPA-MCR-UN agreement that:

- Recognized Ganymede as Belt-flagged territory under
  OPA jurisdiction
- Granted MCR concurrent administrative authority
  over Mars-related operations (the MCRN's Third
  Fleet maintains a major Ganymede base)
- Granted UN concurrent administrative authority over
  UN-funded scientific operations (Galileo City's
  research-institution complex)
- Established a joint administrative council with
  representation from all three powers
- Created a unique citizenship regime: Ganymedeans
  hold OPA recognition by default plus may hold
  UN or MCR citizenship as elected

The Compact has worked. The tripartite structure looks
unwieldy but operates with a practical division of
authority — OPA for civic matters, MCR for naval-
strategic matters, UN for scientific-and-research
matters.

## Politics

Ganymede's political register is *Belt-cosmopolitan*. The
body's OPA representation has been historically
*Centrist-to-pro-Earth-cooperation* (not Frontier-League);
the cultural traffic with the inner system is heavy
enough that Ganymedeans have less inclination toward
confrontational OPA postures.

The current Provincial Governor is Hara Mendoza-
Volkonsky. Mendoza-Volkonsky has been a credible voice
for the *Outer Belt* faction within the broader OPA —
the politicians who advocate for the Jovian system's
specific interests within the larger Belt politics.

## Economy

Ganymede's economy is roughly $0.9T UN-equivalent.
Sectors:

- **Scientific research** — the largest single sector;
  Galileo City's research-institution complex is
  one of the most productive in human space
- **Water-ice extraction** — substantial; Ganymede
  supplies much of the inner Jovian system's water
- **Deuterium extraction** — Sippar's facility is
  one of the largest in the Belt
- **Manufacturing** — specialized; precision instruments,
  scientific equipment, fusion-component
  manufacturing
- **Logistics** — Ganymede is the Jovian system's
  principal trade-and-transit hub
- **Higher education** — the University of Ganymede
  is one of the Belt's principal educational
  institutions

## Cultural texture

Ganymede's cultural register is distinct from inner
Belt:

- **The scientific-research register.** The
  research-institution complex shapes the city's
  intellectual culture. Academic events, scientific
  publications, research-popularization are major
  cultural activities. The Galileo Lectures (an
  annual series) draw substantial off-world
  attention.
- **The Jovian-aware register.** Jupiter dominates
  the sky from Ganymede. The body's cultural
  production engages with the planet constantly —
  visual arts, music, literature all reference
  the Jovian presence.
- **The cosmopolitan register.** Ganymede's
  international population (Belt, Mars, Earth, all
  represented in significant fractions) makes the
  body more cosmopolitan than most Belt locations.
- **The radiation-aware register.** Outdoor
  activity at Ganymede is risky; the magnetosphere
  helps but does not fully protect. Ganymedean
  cultural production has a *protected interior*
  aesthetic, similar to but distinct from
  Lunar-Earth-comparison.
- **The ice-and-water register.** Ganymede's
  sub-surface ocean and surface ice landscape have
  produced a distinctive Jovian aesthetic that
  influences the rest of the outer system.

## The sub-surface ocean

Ganymede's sub-surface ocean is one of the largest
liquid-water bodies in the solar system. The ocean
sits beneath approximately 100-150 km of ice; it
contains more water than Earth's oceans combined.

The ocean's *biological status* has been contested. Early
2150s research suggested no native biology; later
studies (2236, 2287, 2331) found ambiguous chemical
signatures that some interpreted as biological. The
current scientific consensus is *probably no native
biology, but the question is not closed*. Drilling has
been deliberately limited under the *Ganymede Sub-
Surface Reserve* protocols.

The reserve protocols have been politically contentious
— some industrial interests have argued for more
aggressive sub-surface exploitation. The OPA's position
has been firm: the reserve protocols hold until the
biological question is definitively settled.

## Notes for play

- A Ganymedean PC has access to a unique tri-citizenship
  texture: Belt-OPA by default, possibly UN or MCR by
  election. This is unusual and useful narratively.
- The scientific-research background is natural for
  Ganymedean PCs; the Galileo City research community
  produces a substantial fraction of Belt
  technical contractors.
- The radiation-aware register is a useful texture
  for outer-system PCs; Ganymedeans default to
  comfort with high-radiation environments.
