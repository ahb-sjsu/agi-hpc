# The Trojan Habitats

The Trojan Habitats are the cluster of 80-odd
permanent space habitats anchored at the Sun-Jupiter
L4 and L5 Lagrangian points. Their combined 14
million residents constitute one of the most
distinctive human populations in the solar system: a
*purely-orbital* civilization with no planetary
attachment, organized around the gravitational
stability of the Trojan points and the long-cycle
trade flows between Jupiter and the inner system.

The Trojans are *Belt-by-affiliation, orbital-by-
existence*. The cluster has its own subculture
distinct from any planetary or major-body Belt
community — older, in some ways more conservative, in
other ways more genuinely cosmopolitan than any single
Belt body.

## The Lagrangian points

The Sun-Jupiter L4 and L5 points are gravitationally
stable; objects placed there can remain indefinitely
without active station-keeping. The natural
Jupiter-Trojan asteroids have populated these points
for billions of years; human habitats joined them
beginning in the 2147-2168 *Trojan Initiative*.

The first habitats were small (1,000-5,000 residents
each); subsequent construction across two centuries
has produced a graduated mix of habitat sizes:

- **Capital habitats** (4 in total) — the largest,
  with populations of 800K to 1.4M each. Each is a
  city-scale spinning cylinder. The largest is
  *Adeola Habitat* at L4, with 1.4M residents.
- **Major habitats** (24 total) — medium-scale
  habitats with populations of 100-400K each.
- **Specialty habitats** (~50 total) — smaller
  habitats with specific functions (industrial,
  research, residential, agricultural).

The cluster is divided roughly equally between L4
(*the Greeks*, in the historical convention) and L5
(*the Trojans* proper). The naming has been
preserved despite occasional efforts to update it.

## Population

The cluster's 14 million residents are distributed:

- **L4 cluster** (the Greeks) — 7.4M across 40-odd
  habitats. Larger habitats; longer-established;
  more politically established.
- **L5 cluster** (the Trojans) — 6.6M across 40-odd
  habitats. Slightly newer construction average;
  more industrial focus.

The cluster's population is unusually multi-
generational. Trojan-born is a recognized identity;
many Trojans have ancestry on the cluster going back
five or six generations.

## Politics

The cluster's politics are coordinated through the
**Trojan Council** — a federation of habitat
governments meeting quarterly at rotating sites. The
Council represents the cluster in the broader OPA
Plenum and handles cluster-internal coordination.

Trojan politics is *cosmopolitan-establishment* in
its register. The cluster has been one of the OPA's
more centrist-aligned constituencies; the Cunha
Caucus has substantial Trojan representation. The
Trojans have generally supported negotiated Inner-
System cooperation rather than confrontational
postures.

The current Trojan Council Chair is Eleanora
Mendoza-Volkonsky of Adeola Habitat. Mendoza-
Volkonsky has been the cluster's senior political
figure for two decades.

## Economy

The Trojans' economy is roughly $1.2T UN-equivalent.
Sectors:

- **Trade and logistics** — dominant. The Trojan
  position makes it the natural trans-shipment
  point between Jupiter-system and inner-system
  traffic. Substantial fractions of Belt-Earth-Mars
  trade flows through Trojan brokerage.
- **Manufacturing** — specialty; the cluster's
  zero-g and stable-gravity-environment production
  is suited to specific industries (high-precision
  instruments, large-volume specialty chemicals,
  fusion components)
- **Agriculture** — the cluster includes substantial
  agricultural habitats; the Trojan agricultural
  economy supplies most of the inner Jovian system's
  fresh produce
- **Higher education** — the *Trojan Academy*, a
  federated multi-habitat university, is one of the
  Belt's principal educational institutions

## Cultural texture

The Trojan cultural register has features distinct
from any other Belt community:

- **The orbital-civilization register.** Trojans have
  no planetary attachment. Their cultural
  identification is to *the cluster*, *the L-point
  position*, *the orbital civilization*. Earth, Mars,
  Belt-bodies are *places to visit*; the cluster is
  *home*.
- **The multi-generational register.** Trojan
  families are typically multi-generational on the
  cluster. Cultural transmission works through
  cluster-internal institutions; the cluster's
  literary, musical, and artistic traditions have
  recognizable continuity.
- **The trade-cosmopolitan register.** Trojans
  encounter Earth, Mars, Belt-body, Lunar visitors
  constantly. The cluster's cultural production is
  cosmopolitan in a specifically Trojan way —
  *traders' cosmopolitanism*, oriented toward
  understanding others well enough to trade with
  them.
- **The conservative-establishment register.** The
  cluster has been politically and culturally
  *establishment* within the OPA. The cultural
  production reflects this — patient, careful,
  attentive to institutions.

## The *Halyard* and the Trojans

No member of the *Halyard*'s standing crew is
Trojan-born. The cluster is, however, on the
*Halyard*'s outbound trajectory; the ship will pass
relatively near L4 during its outbound burn (within
about 0.4 AU of the cluster's outer edge). The
cluster's sensor systems will track the ship's
passage; the OPA's relevant authorities will be
informed of the *Halyard*'s passage as a routine
matter.

If the campaign requires a Belt-internal political
beat, the Trojan Council is one of the OPA bodies
most likely to take an early position on the
*Halyard*'s eventual findings.

## Notes for play

- A Trojan PC has access to a *purely orbital,
  cosmopolitan-trade* register that no other Belt
  PC option provides.
- The trade-and-logistics background is natural
  for Trojan PCs.
- The multi-generational orbital identity is a
  useful character texture; Trojan PCs default to
  *we are the cluster* in a way that distinguishes
  them from station Belters.
