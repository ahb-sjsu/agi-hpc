# Vesta

Vesta is the second-largest population center in the Belt
after Ceres, the *mining capital* of human space, and the
operational center of the asteroid Belt's heavy-extraction
economy. The dwarf planet's 11 million residents work the
metallic-rich body itself and its surrounding fleet of
mining operations across the inner Belt.

Vesta is *industrial Belt* in concentrated form — less
cosmopolitan than Ceres, more working-class, more
politically pro-OPA-labor in its character. The Vesta
Provincial Council has historically been a stronghold of
the *Frontier League* faction within the OPA Plenum.

## The body

Vesta is the second-largest body in the asteroid Belt:

- Diameter: 525 km
- Surface gravity: 0.022 g
- Composition: differentiated; iron-nickel core,
  basaltic mantle, regolith surface
- Distance from Sun: 2.36 AU (varies)

Vesta's metallic richness is the reason for its
industrial dominance. The body's pre-extraction iron-
nickel reserves were estimated at quantities sufficient
to supply human industrial demand for centuries. The
extraction economy began in 2102 and has been continuous
since.

## Settlement structure

Vesta's settlement is heavily sub-surface. The body's
low gravity makes spinning-habitat construction
attractive, and Vesta has multiple major spin habitats
in addition to its sub-surface tunnel cities:

- **Cunha-Vesta** (the largest spin habitat) — 4M
  residents in a 14-km cylinder. Comparable in scale
  to Ceres's Cunha Drum but in a different design.
- **Sub-surface tunnel cities** — 5M total across
  several major facilities. The largest is the
  Vestan capital, **Kornelius**.
- **Surface installations** — minimal residential;
  mostly mining-operations infrastructure
- **Orbital satellites** — about 2M in orbital
  habitats around Vesta and at the Vesta-Jupiter L4
  point

## Politics

Vesta's politics are *labor-OPA*. The Vesta Provincial
Council is one of the OPA Plenum's most reliably
Frontier-League-aligned constituencies. The
Asteroid Miners' Federation has its largest single
membership concentration on Vesta; the Federation's
politics have historically been more confrontational
toward Earth and Mars than the Cunha-line politics
that dominate Ceres.

The current Provincial Governor is Abebe Volkov-Uka, an
Asteroid Miners' Federation veteran. Volkov-Uka's
political register is *unflinchingly pro-labor, pro-OPA,
suspicious of Inner-System cooperation*.

## Economy

Vesta's economy is roughly $1.6T UN-equivalent. Sectors:

- **Metallic ore extraction** — dominant; Vesta
  produces approximately 38% of the Belt's iron-
  nickel output and substantial fractions of the
  rare-earth, copper, and titanium production
- **Refining and metallurgy** — significant; Vesta's
  refineries handle both Vestan ore and substantial
  imported ore from smaller asteroid operations
- **Manufacturing** — mostly heavy-industrial:
  spacecraft components, structural materials,
  bulk machinery
- **Logistics** — Vesta is the Belt's principal
  metallic-shipment hub
- **Services** — smaller than Ceres but substantial

## Cultural texture

Vesta's cultural register is distinctive within the Belt:

- **The mining-and-labor register.** Vesta culture
  centers on the work — physical extraction, the
  rhythms of mining operations, the dangers of
  industrial accidents, the camaraderie of crew-and-
  team work. Vesta's literary tradition is heavy on
  workers' fiction and labor-historical writing.
- **The political-confrontational register.** Vesta
  has been the site of several major Belt labor
  actions across its history. The 2278 *Tycho-Ceres
  Strike* was substantially organized from Vesta;
  the 2336 Belt crisis had its political origin
  here. Vesta cultural production engages with
  political struggle as a major theme.
- **The spin-cultural register.** The Cunha-Vesta
  habitat is a major center of spin-habitat culture,
  with its own particular character: more
  industrial-aesthetic than Ceres's Cunha Drum,
  more concerned with collective work, less
  cosmopolitan.
- **The harsh sensibility.** Vesta is, even by Belt
  standards, a rough place. The cultural register
  is *unsentimental, working-class, suspicious of
  pretension*.

## Notes for play

- A Vesta PC will be more *labor-political, working-
  class, OPA-Frontier-League* than other Belt PC
  options.
- The mining background is natural; many Vesta PCs
  have mining-engineering or extraction-industry
  experience.
- The political register makes Vesta PCs good fits
  for plot lines involving Belt-internal political
  tensions.
