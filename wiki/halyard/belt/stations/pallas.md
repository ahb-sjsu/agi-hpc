# Pallas

Pallas is the third-largest body in the asteroid Belt and
the most jurisdictionally complicated. The 8 million
Pallasians live in tunnel cities and orbital habitats
around an inclined-orbit body that has, since 2284, been
the subject of *joint UN-OPA classified survey
restrictions* covering specific deep-interior regions
that public records describe as *geological reserves*.

To ordinary Belters and to the broader public, Pallas is
the *quiet* major Belt body — less commercially active
than Vesta, less politically central than Ceres, less
research-prominent than Ganymede. Its OPA representation
is moderate; its provincial politics is technocratic; its
economy is balanced rather than dominant in any sector.

The *interior of Pallas* is a Keeper-only matter. The
classified Elder Thing site referenced in the
`mythos/elder-things.md` entry is here.

## The body

- Diameter: 512 km
- Surface gravity: 0.021 g
- Composition: silicate-rich; partially differentiated
- Distance from Sun: 2.77 AU (varies; Pallas's orbit is
  unusually inclined at 34°)

The orbital inclination matters operationally. Pallas
spends substantial time *out of the ecliptic plane*; this
makes Pallas-traffic logistics distinctive — direct
transit windows from inner-system bodies are less
frequent than for Ceres or Vesta.

The body's interior is *partially differentiated* — a
metal-rich core, a silicate mantle, and an unusual
sub-surface void structure that geological surveys have
reported as *naturally occurring* but that the Chamber
and the Caretakers know is partly artificial.

## The classified interior

(*This section is Keeper-only; the public Pallas entry
omits it. Both ARTEMIS and SIGMA-4 will deflect
deep-interior Pallas queries.*)

The deep interior of Pallas contains an Elder Thing
research-and-storage installation, partially intact, with
2-4 dormant shoggoths in metabolic-cycling occupancy. The
installation is one of the largest off-Earth Elder Thing
sites in human space. Discovery dates from a 2271
geological survey; classification dates from 2274 under
the original *Pallas Reserve Protocol*.

The classification is *jointly* enforced by the UN's
Outer Authority and the OPA's relevant security
organizations. The reserve is one of the few
Mythos-adjacent operations where Inner-System and Belt
authorities have operationally cooperated.

Protogen's Special Programs Branch has *limited* access
to the Pallas site through a specific 2331 Treaty
arrangement. The arrangement is far more restrictive than
SPB's Antarctic operation; the OPA's involvement
prevents SPB from independent activity in the Pallas
reserve.

The Chamber knows about the Pallas site. The Chamber's
relationship with the Pallas situation is complicated:
the Chamber would prefer freer access for Mi-go-related
operations; the OPA has effectively prevented this. The
Chamber has, in two operations across the past twenty
years, attempted to compromise Pallas reserve security;
both attempts were detected and contained.

## Settlement structure

Pallas's 8 million residents are concentrated:

- **Pallasion** (3.5M) — the planetary capital; sub-
  surface tunnel city in the Pallasian crust
  (well above the deep-interior reserve). Provincial
  Council, the major university, the principal
  commercial center.
- **Northern Habitats** (2M) — orbital and surface
  installations in the body's northern hemisphere;
  industrial focus.
- **Southern Habitats** (1.5M) — orbital and surface
  installations in the body's southern hemisphere;
  residential focus.
- **Reserve-Adjacent Stations** (0.5M) — the
  installations near the *geological reserve*
  perimeter. Workers here are vetted through joint
  UN-OPA security protocols; few civilians are aware
  of the actual nature of the reserve.
- **Smaller settlements and orbital infrastructure**
  (0.5M).

## Politics

Pallas's politics are *technocratic-balanced*. The
Provincial Council has historically been
*centrist-protective* — committed to the Pallas
Reserve Protocol, suspicious of Inner-System pressure
to relax it.

The current Provincial Governor is Stephanie Akande-
Rossi, a former survey-engineer with deep
institutional knowledge of the reserve protocols.
Akande-Rossi's political register is *quiet, careful,
attention-to-detail-oriented* — appropriate to the
sensitive character of Pallas's classified
component.

## Economy

Pallas's economy is roughly $0.4T UN-equivalent.
Sectors:

- **Mining and extraction** — substantial; Pallas
  produces silicate-rich ores and specialty
  minerals. The reserve-adjacent restrictions
  prevent the body's most-productive deep-interior
  extraction, which has held Pallas's economy below
  Vesta's despite comparable size.
- **Manufacturing** — specialty; high-precision
  components and instruments.
- **Higher education** — the University of Pallas
  has a respected geological-and-planetary science
  program.
- **Government and security services** — the
  reserve-adjacent operations require substantial
  administrative infrastructure.

## Cultural texture

Pallas's cultural register is *quiet, slightly
mysterious, oriented toward depth*:

- **The reserve-adjacent register.** Many Pallasians
  know that *something is in the deep interior* even
  though most don't know what. The cultural
  production engages with this — Pallasian
  literature has a recognizable thread of *deep
  mystery* fiction; visual art and music engage
  with the body's depths.
- **The orbital register.** Pallas's high orbital
  inclination produces a distinctive astronomical
  experience — the rest of the solar system is
  *seen from above* much of the time. Pallasian
  cultural production engages with this perspective.
- **The geological register.** Geology is
  unusually prominent in Pallasian cultural life.
  The body's physical character is part of the
  cultural identity.

## Notes for play

- A Pallas PC is *uncommon* and useful. The
  reserve-adjacent register provides a character
  who has *suspicions* about Mythos-adjacent
  matters without specific knowledge.
- The Pallas site connection is a useful campaign
  resource. If the *Halyard*'s findings produce
  political pressure for parallel Pallas-site
  disclosure, the politics get complicated.
- The Pallas Reserve Protocol is *the only*
  operationally functional joint UN-OPA Mythos-
  adjacent governance regime. If the campaign
  reaches the Caretaker negotiations, the
  Pallas precedent will be the closest analog.
