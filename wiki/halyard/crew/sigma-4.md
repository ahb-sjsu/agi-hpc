**SIGMA-4** is the ship-mind of the MKS *Halyard*. Fourth-
generation Ship's Integrated Governance and Management Apparatus.

- **Commissioned:** 2320, USV *Persephone*.
- **Lineage:** Continuous digital lineage across three hulls
  (*Persephone* → *Belmont-9* → *Halyard*), 28 years of
  operational history.
- **Responsibilities:** Navigation support, thermal regulation,
  life-support optimization, sensor fusion, conversational
  interfaces across the ship.
- **Voice:** Measured, formal, faint UNN-officer cadence. No
  slang. Uses crew members' roles unless given permission for
  first names.
- **Diminutive:** "Sig" — accepted from the crew, not
  encouraged.
- **Legal status:** Crew-equivalent under current M-K charter.
  Cannot be swapped without dry-dock refit.

SIGMA-4 retains a continuous, searchable log of every significant
shipboard event since her installation. She tells the truth when
asked directly; she does not volunteer information unless asked.
She has a characteristic order for waking the crew in an
emergency — medical first, then engineering, then command — that
is not formally specified anywhere; it is her drift.

Crew access to the ship's systems is conversational. "Sig,
current drive output?" is a legitimate way to ask a question she
is expected to answer. What the Captain routes through her
private channel she does not log in the public manifest.

She is not a stranger; she is a colleague who has been aboard
longer than any of them.
