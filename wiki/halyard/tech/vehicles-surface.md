# Surface vehicles

Surface vehicles in 2348 cover ground transit on
Earth, Mars, Luna, and the various asteroid-and-
moon surfaces of the outer system. Earth surface
vehicles are direct descendants of twentieth-and-
twenty-first-century cars and trucks; off-world
surface vehicles are a substantially different
category of equipment, designed for specific
environmental conditions.

## Earth surface vehicles

Earth surface vehicles in 2348 have evolved
substantially from 2025 patterns:

- **Personal automobile** — substantially less
  common than 2025; a luxury signal in most
  markets; expensive and regulated
- **Public transit** — substantially expanded;
  the dominant mode for most Earthers
- **Commercial trucking** — substantial;
  largely automated
- **Specialty vehicles** — agricultural,
  industrial, military, emergency response

Most Earth vehicles are electric or hybrid; the
internal-combustion engine is now a specialty
technology rather than the default.

## Mars surface vehicles

Mars vehicles must operate in:

- Lower gravity (0.38 g)
- Thinner atmosphere (0.45 bar at low
  elevations; less at higher)
- Variable temperature (-55°C to +18°C)
- Substantial dust environment
- Substantial distances (Mars's geography is
  large)

Categories:

### Pressurized passenger vehicles

- **Standard pressurized rover** — the most
  common Mars passenger vehicle; comfortable
  for medium-distance travel; carries 4-8
  people
- **Long-range rover** — substantial range
  (1000+ km on a single charge); used for
  cross-continental Mars travel
- **Specialty pressurized vehicles** — for
  scientific expeditions, hazardous-
  environment work

### Working vehicles

- **Pressurized work-trucks** — for cargo and
  industrial transport
- **Construction vehicles** — for major
  construction projects
- **Mining vehicles** — for extraction
  operations
- **Agricultural vehicles** — for the
  Northern Lowlands' farms

### Mass-transit

- **Mars rail** — substantial network
  connecting major Mars cities; the rail
  network is one of the major Mars
  infrastructure systems
- **City transit** — within Mars cities;
  substantial public transit infrastructure

### Specialty

- **EVA-only vehicles** — open-environment
  vehicles where occupants wear EVA suits;
  for some specialty applications

## Lunar surface vehicles

Lunar vehicles operate in:

- Very low gravity (0.16 g)
- No atmosphere (vacuum)
- Extreme temperature variation
- Substantial radiation
- Mostly smooth lunar regolith

Categories:

### Pressurized lunar rovers

- **Standard lunar rover** — for routine
  transport; pressurized; reliable
- **Long-range exploration rover** — for
  lunar far-side and polar work
- **Specialty pressurized vehicles** — for
  scientific and industrial applications

### Open-environment lunar vehicles

- **EVA rovers** — open vehicles where
  occupants wear EVA suits
- **Industrial vehicles** — for the Lunar
  Industrial Belt
- **Mining and extraction vehicles**

### Specialty lunar transit

- **Mass-driver-based transport** — for some
  long-distance Lunar surface logistics
- **Cable transit** — for some specialty
  applications
- **The Lunar Industrial Belt's working
  vehicles** — substantial fleet for the
  Belt's industrial operations

## Asteroid and moon surface vehicles

For the various asteroid and moon surfaces of
the outer system:

### EVA-only vehicles

The dominant category for low-gravity bodies:

- **Belt-body rovers** — small, agile vehicles
  for moving across asteroid and small-moon
  surfaces
- **Specialty vehicles** — for specific
  scientific or industrial applications

### Pressurized vehicles

For larger moons (Ganymede, Callisto, Titan):

- **Pressurized rovers** — for transit on
  larger moons with substantial atmospheres
  or radiation environments
- **Specialty applications**

### Adaptable / multi-environment vehicles

Some vehicles are designed to operate across
multiple body types — useful for deep-range
operations like the *Halyard*'s.

## The *Halyard*'s surface-vehicle capability

The *Halyard* carries:

### Kestrel-class landers

Two suborbital lander vehicles (covered in
`tech/vehicles-space.md`). These are *not*
surface-only vehicles but they include surface-
operations capability.

### Surface rovers

Two specialty surface rovers stored in the
landers' cargo bays:

- **Light surface rover** — small EVA-only
  vehicle for surface mobility on small
  bodies; carries 2 occupants in EVA suits
- **Heavy surface rover** — pressurized
  multi-occupant vehicle for substantial
  surface operations; carries 4 occupants;
  substantial range

The rovers are designed for general outer-system
operations and would be deployable on Nithon's
surface during the campaign's surface phase.

## Notes for play

- **Surface vehicles are operational equipment**
  rather than narrative drivers
- **The *Halyard*'s rovers** are useful for the
  campaign's Nithon surface phase
- **Pressurized rovers** allow for substantial
  surface time without EVA-suit limits
- **PC mobility on surface** is shaped by
  vehicle availability; players should
  consider how the rovers shape their
  operational planning
