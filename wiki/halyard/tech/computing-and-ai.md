# Computing and AI in 2348

Computing in 2348 has matured along the trajectory
2025 anticipated, with one major exception: no
artificial general intelligence has emerged. Computing
power, storage, and connectivity have all improved by
many orders of magnitude. Expert AI systems are
ubiquitous. But the *general intelligence
threshold* — synthetic intelligence equivalent to
human cognition — has not been crossed, and the
2300s consensus is that *it will not be crossed*
without breakthroughs that have not occurred and may
not occur.

## Computing capability

The standard handheld of 2348 is, by 2025 measures,
*absurdly capable*. Specific characteristics:

- **Processing**: roughly 10^12 FLOPS per gram of
  hardware; the standard handheld has 10^16 FLOPS
- **Storage**: petabyte-scale on consumer devices;
  exabyte-scale on professional equipment
- **Display**: holographic; 10K-resolution
  equivalent in 2D space, with substantial
  volumetric capacity
- **Battery**: weeks to months of operation on a
  single charge under typical use; rapid charging
  to full in seconds
- **Connectivity**: 10+ gigabit/sec wireless to
  ordinary infrastructure; 100+ gigabit/sec on
  station/ship networks

Industrial computing is correspondingly larger. A
modern fusion reactor's control system has compute
capacity that 2025's largest data centers would have
envied; spacecraft AI systems run continuously across
multi-month voyages.

## The handheld

The personal handheld is the universal computing
device of 2348. Form factor: slightly larger than a
2025 smartphone, with a folding-display capability.
Functions:

- Communications (voice, text, video, holographic
  presence)
- Personal data and identity management
- Local AI assistance (with regional regulation
  varying)
- Environmental monitoring (atmospheric, radiation,
  network conditions)
- Identity verification (biometric, cryptographic)
- Payment and banking
- Navigation and mapping
- Reading, learning, entertainment
- Specialized professional functions per
  occupation

Most adults carry a handheld continuously; many have
a secondary device (a wrist-computer or
spectacles-mounted display) integrated with the
handheld. Some industries (medical, military,
research) issue specialized handhelds with additional
capabilities.

The *Halyard*'s standard-issue crew handheld is the
**Pavonis Mark 7** (manufactured at the Pavonis
Industrial District; 2344 model year). Specifications
are middling-quality professional-grade; the device
has full deep-range certification, Lloyd's-approved
encryption, and the standard *Halyard*-network
interfaces.

ARTEMIS is, in fact, a *handheld AI system* — an
unusual high-end Protogen-issued model that
Halverson carries as her professional reference
device. The handheld's hardware is distinctive
(bigger than standard, with substantially more
processing capacity), the AI software is
substantially upgraded, and the specific training
data has been curated by Protogen researchers.

## Expert AI systems

Expert AI systems are ubiquitous in 2348. The
standard architecture:

- **Domain-specialized** — trained for a specific
  range of tasks rather than general intelligence
- **Large-language-model based** — building on the
  twenty-first-century transformer-architecture
  foundation, with two centuries of refinement
- **Tool-augmented** — capable of using
  domain-specific tools (database access,
  computational tools, sensor integration) to
  extend their capabilities
- **Validated** — most professional applications
  use expert AI systems with formal validation
  frameworks ensuring outputs meet domain standards

Examples of routine expert AI applications:

- **Medical diagnostic AI** — universal in clinical
  settings; substantially better than 2025 medical
  AI; still requires human physician oversight
  for treatment decisions
- **Engineering design AI** — used in most
  professional engineering work; validated through
  the Engineers' Syndicate's certification
  framework
- **Legal AI** — extensive in legal practice;
  regulatory frameworks vary by jurisdiction
- **Financial AI** — universal in trading and
  finance; closely regulated
- **Scientific research AI** — universal in
  research; the major scientific publications
  require disclosure of AI tool use
- **Spacecraft control AI** — universal; SIGMA-4
  is an example of this category at the
  ship-mind scale

Most expert AI is *trustworthy* in its domain and
*useless* outside it. A medical diagnostic AI
cannot meaningfully discuss philosophy; a financial
AI cannot meaningfully diagnose disease. Each
system is a domain expert.

## ARTEMIS and SIGMA-4 in this context

The *Halyard*'s two major AI systems sit at different
points in the contemporary AI landscape:

### SIGMA-4

A standard ship-mind. The Halyard-class ship-mind
architecture is mature technology — reliable,
well-validated, professionally deployed across the
deep-range fleet. SIGMA-4 specifically is a
2331-build (with subsequent updates) and is a
fairly typical deep-range ship-mind. Her capabilities
include:

- Spacecraft systems management
- Crew-services support
- Communications and external coordination
- Limited natural-language conversation
- Pattern recognition and anomaly detection
- Tool-using for specific operational purposes

SIGMA-4 is *competent* and *trustworthy* in her
domain. She is *not* general-intelligence; she does
not have philosophical conversations effectively;
she does not engage with questions outside her
operational scope.

### ARTEMIS

An unusual handheld AI. The standard Protogen-
research-grade handheld AI system, with several
custom modifications:

- Substantially larger context window than typical
  handheld AIs
- Substantial training on Halverson's specific
  research domains (xenoarchaeology, contact-
  protocol research, related fields)
- Tool-using extended to research and analytical
  applications
- Formal validation under the Outer Authority's AI
  Convention frameworks
- Multiple safety layers including the ErisML
  validator described in `docs/ARTEMIS.md`

ARTEMIS is *more capable* than the standard handheld
AI but is *not* general-intelligence. Like SIGMA-4,
ARTEMIS is domain-specialized — though her domain is
substantially broader than SIGMA-4's.

(Keeper-only context: ARTEMIS's training and validation
have been Protogen-curated. The system is, in
campaign-terms, a Protogen instrument as much as it
is Halverson's research tool.)

## AI regulation

Three major regulatory frameworks apply to AI in
2348:

### The 2304 Outer Authority AI Convention

The international framework establishing minimum
standards for AI systems:

- Validation requirements for systems above
  specified capability thresholds
- Disclosure requirements for AI use in
  consequential decisions
- Prohibitions on autonomous lethal AI
- Required human oversight for systems involved in
  high-stakes operations

The Convention is the *floor* for AI regulation;
individual jurisdictions impose stricter rules.

### The Engineers' Syndicate certification

For AI systems used in engineering applications, the
Syndicate certifies validation frameworks; many
applications require Syndicate-certified validation.

### Domain-specific regulation

Different industries have specific regulatory
frameworks (medical AI, financial AI, transportation
AI, etc.). Most are stricter than the Convention's
minimum.

## What AI cannot do

AI in 2348 has specific *limitations* that the
campaign should respect:

- **No general intelligence.** AI cannot reason
  outside its training domain effectively. ARTEMIS
  cannot, despite her sophisticated training,
  reason about a problem entirely outside her
  domain frameworks.
- **No human-equivalent creativity.** AI can
  generate within domains it knows but cannot
  produce *unprecedented* creative work the way
  humans can.
- **No moral reasoning.** AI systems can apply
  ethical frameworks they've been trained on but
  cannot perform original moral reasoning.
- **No genuine understanding.** AI processes
  information without understanding it in the
  way humans do. The pragmatic effects can be
  similar in many cases; the underlying
  mechanism is different.

These limitations are *philosophically* significant
and *practically* meaningful. A Player who tries to
ask ARTEMIS a question that requires general-
intelligence reasoning will get either a clear
*I cannot reason about this* response or, if pressed,
an admittedly-domain-specialized response that
doesn't quite address the question.

## Notes for play

- The AI tools are *useful* but *limited*. Players
  who use ARTEMIS or SIGMA-4 effectively will get
  substantial benefit; players who try to use
  them as general-intelligence oracles will be
  disappointed.
- The AI conversation interface is *natural-
  language*. Players type or speak to the AIs as
  to a person; the AIs respond in person-like
  language. The AIs are not, however, persons.
- The AI *limitations* are useful narrative
  resources. ARTEMIS will refuse to answer some
  questions; SIGMA-4 will say *I cannot
  comment on that* on others; both will be
  occasionally wrong.
- Computing-and-AI is *not* the campaign's
  primary plot driver. The technology serves the
  story; the story is about people facing
  cosmic encounters.
