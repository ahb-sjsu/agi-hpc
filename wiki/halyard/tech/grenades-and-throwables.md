# Grenades and throwables

Grenades and similar throwable devices in 2348 cover
a substantial range of applications: from frag
grenades for ground combat through specialty
breach charges, electronic-effects grenades,
emergency-flotation devices, and the various
specialty items modern operations require. Most
grenades are not used aboard pressurized spacecraft
under any circumstances; the few that *are*
shipboard-safe are specifically designed for that
use.

This entry catalogs the major categories.

## Combat grenades

### Fragmentation grenades

Standard military fragmentation grenades:

- **Standard frag** — pre-formed-fragment metal
  case; substantial lethal radius (10-20 m
  typical); 4-second fuse standard
- **Mini-frag** — smaller; 3-5 m lethal radius;
  shorter fuse
- **Specialty frag** — directional, fragmentation-
  optimized for specific applications

Fragmentation grenades are *not aboard-ship-safe*.
The fragments produce penetration risks against
hull and pressurized-volume integrity that prohibit
shipboard use.

### Concussion grenades

Blast-effect grenades without substantial
fragmentation:

- **Standard concussion** — produces shock-wave
  and overpressure; effective against personnel
  in confined spaces
- **Vacuum-rated concussion** — modified for
  vacuum operations (the *Halyard*'s arms
  locker contains some)

Vacuum-rated concussion grenades are *not* aboard-ship-
safe in pressurized environments — the over-
pressure risks against ship structures are real.
They *are* used in vacuum tactical operations.

## Less-lethal grenades

### Flashbang grenades

Standard distraction grenades:

- **Sound-and-light flashbang** — produces
  intense light flash (~1 million candela for
  ~1 second) and high-intensity sound (~170 dB)
- **Sustained-effect flashbang** — produces
  multi-pulse effects
- **Vacuum-rated flashbang** — adapted for
  vacuum operations; sound effects are limited
  in vacuum but light effects function

Flashbangs are *aboard-ship-safe* in most
configurations. The *Halyard* carries several.

### Gas grenades

- **Tear-gas grenades** — chemical irritant;
  hand-deployed
- **Sleep-gas grenades** — anesthetic; longer-
  duration deployment
- **Specialty gas grenades** — various

The *Halyard* carries tear-gas grenades. Sleep-gas
grenades are heavily restricted.

### Stun grenades

Electrical-shock grenades:

- **Area-effect stun** — produces electrical
  discharge across a designated area
- **Targeted stun** — releases conductive
  particles or projectiles toward target
- **Continuous-effect stun** — emits sustained
  pulses

Aboard-ship safety varies; most modern stun
grenades are designed with shipboard safety in
mind.

## Specialty grenades

### Smoke grenades

For obscuration:

- **Standard smoke** — colored or white smoke;
  hand-deployed
- **IR smoke** — opaque to thermal imaging;
  used in specialty military applications
- **Vacuum-rated smoke** — adapted for vacuum
  operations using specialty propellants

### Incendiary grenades

For destruction of materials:

- **Thermite grenades** — produce extremely high
  temperatures; capable of burning through
  most materials
- **White phosphorus-class** — various incendiary
  compounds

Incendiary grenades are *not* aboard-ship-safe. The
fire and toxic-fume risks prohibit shipboard use
except in extreme contingencies.

### Breach charges

For tactical breaching:

- **Breaching grenades** — directed-explosive
  charges for door/wall breach
- **Specialty breach** — for specific structural
  breach applications

Breach charges are typically used by trained
personnel; their applications are specialized.

## Electronic-effects grenades

### EMP grenades

Localized electromagnetic-pulse devices:

- **Standard EMP grenades** — produce
  electromagnetic pulse effective against
  unshielded electronics in 5-10 m radius
- **Targeted EMP** — produces narrower-effect
  pulse; useful against specific targets
  without disrupting friendly electronics

EMP grenades are operationally complex aboard ship —
many shipboard systems are EMP-hardened, but
collateral effects are substantial.

### Sensor-disruption grenades

For disabling surveillance and detection systems:

- **Optical disruption** — produces intense
  visible/IR emission to overwhelm imaging sensors
- **Acoustic disruption** — produces sounds
  designed to confuse acoustic sensors
- **RF disruption** — produces radio-frequency
  emission to disrupt RF-based sensors

These are increasingly important in specialty
operations as electronic surveillance becomes more
ubiquitous.

## Specialty throwables

### Restraint nets

(See also: less-lethal entry)

- **Hand-thrown nets** — for immediate-range
  restraint
- **Specialty net launchers** — for medium-range

### Tracking grenades

- **Sticky-tag grenades** — deploy adhesive
  tracking tags onto targets
- **Spray-tag grenades** — deploy tracking
  compounds across an area

### Decoy grenades

- **Heat-decoy** — produces thermal signature
  to confuse heat-seeking weapons
- **Electronic-decoy** — produces electromagnetic
  signature to confuse electronic seekers
- **Visual-decoy** — produces visual signature
  to draw attention

## Aboard the *Halyard*

The *Halyard*'s grenade-and-throwable inventory:

- **Flashbang grenades (4)** — for tactical use
  aboard ship
- **Tear-gas grenades (2)** — for crowd-control
  scenarios
- **Smoke grenades (2)** — for obscuration
- **Restraint nets (4)** — for restraint
- **Vacuum-rated concussion grenades (2)** —
  for EVA tactical use
- **Specialty breach charges (small inventory)** —
  for emergency hatch breach

All inventory is in the arms locker. Cross has
keys; Marsh has emergency override; deployment
requires Captain's authorization for most
applications.

## Notes for play

- **Aboard-ship grenade use** is *unusual*. A PC
  who deploys a grenade aboard ship has produced
  a significant operational situation.
- **The flashbang/tear-gas inventory** is
  appropriate for the campaign's likely scenarios.
  Most aboard-ship conflict can be resolved with
  less-lethal options.
- **Vacuum-rated concussion grenades** are
  potentially relevant for surface operations
  on Nithon. The Keeper should consider when
  these become tactically appropriate.
- **EMP grenades** are *not* in the *Halyard*'s
  inventory by default. The Keeper may add them
  to plot-relevant scenarios.
