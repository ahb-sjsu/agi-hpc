# Military armor in 2348

Military armor in 2348 has matured along the
trajectory 2025 anticipated, with substantial
improvements in weight-to-protection ratio, mobility,
sensor integration, and life support. The dominant
military armor categories are *heavy infantry armor*
(power-armored exoskeletons), *light infantry armor*
(unpowered or semi-powered ballistic protection),
and *specialty armor* (vacuum-rated boarding suits,
EOD armor, sniper ghillie systems, etc.).

The campaign rarely engages with military armor at
the *we are wearing it* level — the *Halyard*'s
crew are not military combatants. Military armor
appears in the campaign as *background tech* and as
*equipment owned by NPCs the players might
encounter*.

## Heavy infantry armor

The signature category of contemporary military
armor:

### UNN power armor (M-7 Marauder)

The standard UNN heavy infantry armor:

- **Mass**: 80 kg fully loaded (without operator)
- **Powered**: substantial battery; 8-12 hour
  operational time typical
- **Protection**: substantial against rifle rounds;
  protection against most personal weapons
- **Mobility**: power-assisted; substantially better
  than unpowered armor of equivalent weight
- **Integration**: substantial weapon and sensor
  integration; HUD; comms; environmental sensors
- **Vacuum-capable**: yes (most M-7 variants)

The Marauder is the standard UNN issue for the
Marine Expeditionary Forces. Substantial training
required (8 weeks minimum); substantial logistical
support required.

### MCRN heavy armor (G-9 Goliath)

The MCRN equivalent:

- **Mass**: 75 kg fully loaded
- **Powered**: similar capability
- **Protection**: comparable to Marauder
- **Mobility**: somewhat better than Marauder
- **Integration**: similar to Marauder; different
  manufacturer interfaces
- **Vacuum-capable**: yes

The Goliath is professionally regarded as marginally
better than the Marauder in most measures. UNN
combat troops have known this for years; their
training emphasizes tactics that compensate.

## Light infantry armor

For situations where heavy power armor is
inappropriate or unavailable:

### UNN light armor (L-2 Sentinel)

- **Mass**: 12-18 kg
- **Unpowered**: passive ballistic protection
- **Protection**: rifle-grade ballistic; substantial
  fragmentation protection
- **Mobility**: high; the Sentinel is designed for
  mobility-prioritized tactical operations
- **Integration**: limited compared to power armor;
  HUD-integration through the operator's
  individual handheld

### MCRN light armor (L-2 Equivalent)

Comparable capability under different manufacturer.

### Specialty light armor

- **Boarding suits** — vacuum-rated light armor for
  ship-boarding operations
- **EOD armor** — heavy ballistic-and-blast
  protection for explosive ordnance disposal
- **Sniper armor** — lightweight; ghillie-system
  integrated; substantial concealment

## Specialty military armor

### Naval boarding armor

Combat-rated EVA suits (covered in
`tech/civilian-armor.md`):

- Substantial ballistic protection
- Vacuum capability
- Mobility-optimized at the cost of some
  protection
- Used by MCRN and UNN boarding-action specialists

### Pilot armor

For combat-aircraft and small-craft pilots:

- Anti-G capabilities
- Vacuum capability for emergency egress
- Survival-systems integration
- Limited combat capability

### Specialty research armor

For UN Outer Authority operations:

- Substantial environmental protection
- Limited combat capability
- Specialty for hazardous-environment investigation

## Personal armor for security details

The *Halyard*'s security officer (Cross) has access
to:

- **Combat-rated EVA suit** (mentioned in civilian-
  armor entry) — for tactical operations
- **Standard light armor** — for shipboard use
- **Specialty equipment** — restraints, less-lethal
  integration, emergency-medicine support

This is *substantially better* than what most
civilian security personnel have access to but
*substantially less* than what military forces deploy.
Cross's military background gives him substantial
familiarity with the equipment.

## Aboard the *Halyard*

The *Halyard*'s military-grade armor inventory:

- **Cross's combat-rated EVA suit** (covered above)
- **Cross's light armor** for shipboard use
- **Specialty engineering armor** — for hazardous-
  environment work; designed for radiation,
  chemical, and thermal hazards rather than
  ballistic protection

The ship does *not* carry power-armor-class
equipment. Such equipment would be inappropriate for
a civilian science vessel.

## Notes for play

- **Military armor is *background* tech** for the
  campaign. Players will likely *encounter* it
  (UNN or MCRN forces, security details at major
  stations) but rarely *wear* it.
- **Power armor is operationally complex.** A PC
  who is wearing power armor without specific
  training is at substantial disadvantage; a PC
  with power-armor training has substantial
  combat capability.
- **Cross's combat-rated EVA suit** is the
  closest the *Halyard*'s crew has to military
  armor in regular access. It is a useful
  operational asset for tactical scenarios.
