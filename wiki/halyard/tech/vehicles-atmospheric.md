# Atmospheric vehicles

Atmospheric vehicles in 2348 are the descendants of
twentieth-and-twenty-first-century aircraft. The
fundamental physics has not changed; the technology
has matured substantially. Earth's atmosphere is
still the principal atmospheric-vehicle environment,
with Mars's growing atmosphere now supporting a
substantial atmospheric-flight industry.

## Earth atmospheric vehicles

### Passenger aircraft

- **Suborbital passenger** — substantial; major
  cities are connected by suborbital passenger
  service with 60-90 minute global travel times
- **Commercial airliners** — the standard
  long-distance travel option for most
  Earthers; substantially more efficient than
  2025 equivalents
- **Regional aircraft** — mid-distance travel
  within continents and across short ocean
  routes
- **Personal aircraft** — limited consumer
  market; substantial regulation; costly

### Cargo aircraft

- **Bulk cargo** — substantial; the dominant
  long-distance cargo mode for time-sensitive
  shipping
- **Specialty cargo** — heavy lift, hazardous
  materials, livestock, etc.

### Specialty applications

- **Atmospheric monitoring** — substantial
  fleet of specialized aircraft for
  atmospheric science
- **Surveillance and reconnaissance** —
  substantial; both civilian (weather,
  agriculture) and military
- **Disaster response** — substantial
  capability for emergency response
- **Specialty industrial** — agricultural,
  forestry, construction

### Propulsion

Most Earth atmospheric vehicles use:

- **Hybrid-electric** for most passenger and
  light cargo applications
- **Sustainable jet fuels** for high-performance
  applications
- **Electric** for shorter-range and personal
  applications
- **Specialty propulsion** for high-altitude
  and suborbital applications

## Mars atmospheric vehicles

The growing Martian atmosphere supports a
substantially developed atmospheric-flight
industry:

### Passenger aircraft

- **Inter-city passenger** — substantial;
  Mars's atmosphere is now thick enough that
  conventional aircraft work effectively
  (substantially adapted for the lower
  pressure and gravity)
- **Regional aircraft** — for shorter trips
- **Specialty long-range** — for crossing
  Mars's substantial distances

### Cargo and specialty

- **Cargo aircraft** — substantial
- **Atmospheric monitoring** — extensive;
  the MAA operates substantial aircraft fleets
- **Specialty applications** — including
  agricultural-and-forestry aircraft for
  the Lowlands biome

### Propulsion

Mars atmospheric vehicles typically use:

- **Hybrid-electric** for most applications
- **Specialty engines** designed for the
  thinner atmosphere
- **Electric** for shorter-range
- **Specialty propulsion** for specific
  high-performance applications

## Vacuum-and-atmosphere transitional vehicles

Some vehicles operate across both vacuum and
atmosphere, with appropriate modifications:

### Surface-to-orbit shuttles

- **Pressurized passenger shuttles** — for
  surface-to-orbit travel
- **Cargo shuttles** — for surface-to-orbit
  freight
- **Substantial Mars-specific designs** —
  given the Mars atmosphere's specific
  characteristics

### Atmospheric-to-vacuum specialty vehicles

- **Reentry vehicles** — for re-entering
  Earth-and-Mars atmospheres
- **Atmospheric-skip craft** — for
  high-altitude long-range travel using
  atmospheric skip

## Civilian access

Most civilians have access to:

- **Passenger air travel** — substantial; the
  major option for long-distance Earth and
  Mars travel
- **Personal vehicles** — limited and
  regulated; substantial cost
- **Charter services** — substantial
  availability for specialty applications

## Military atmospheric vehicles

Substantial:

- **Combat aircraft** — for both Earth and
  Mars; substantially advanced from 2025
  equivalents
- **Surveillance aircraft** — substantial
  deployment
- **Cargo and transport** — substantial
  fleet
- **Specialty applications** — including
  atmospheric-to-vacuum dual-use designs

## Aboard the *Halyard*

The *Halyard* does not carry atmospheric vehicles
of its own. The ship's two Kestrel-class landers
(covered in `tech/vehicles-space.md`) are
suborbital lander vehicles capable of operating
in vacuum and in thin atmospheres but are not
proper atmospheric aircraft.

If the campaign reaches a Mars-side or Earth-side
phase requiring atmospheric vehicles, the relevant
operations would be conducted by ground-based
agencies rather than from the *Halyard*.

## Notes for play

- **Atmospheric vehicles are routine** technology;
  PCs from Earth and Mars will be familiar with
  most categories
- **The *Halyard*'s Kestrel-class landers** are
  the closest the ship gets to atmospheric
  vehicles; their capabilities are described
  in the space-vehicles entry
- **Military atmospheric vehicles** are
  background presence; PCs may encounter them
  but rarely deploy from them
