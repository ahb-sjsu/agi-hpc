# Spy-tech and counter-surveillance

Spy-tech in 2348 has matured along the broad
trajectory 2025 anticipated: surveillance is *more
ubiquitous* than 2025 imagined, *more sophisticated*
in its information-extraction, and *more
constrained* by the parallel maturation of
counter-surveillance technology. The contemporary
intelligence environment is one in which most
operations involve substantial *measure-countermeasure*
balance — every form of surveillance has corresponding
defenses; every defense has corresponding penetration
techniques.

This entry covers the major categories of spy-tech
and counter-surveillance with attention to what's
operationally relevant for the campaign.

## Surveillance categories

### Audio surveillance

- **Conventional microphones** — inexpensive,
  widely deployed; substantially better than 2025
  in noise-cancellation and selective-pickup
- **Laser microphones** — read window-vibration to
  reconstruct room audio; substantial range
- **Acoustic-resonance pickups** — detect
  vibration patterns through structural elements
- **Through-wall microphones** — substantially
  capable; the higher-end versions can read
  conversations through walls and floors

Counter-surveillance: *acoustic shielding* (specialty
materials), *active noise generation* (white-noise
and pink-noise generators), *physical isolation*
(soundproof rooms; the *Halyard*'s captain's office
has substantial acoustic isolation built in).

### Visual surveillance

- **Conventional cameras** — ubiquitous;
  substantially miniaturized; effectively
  impossible to clear an environment of all
  cameras
- **Through-wall imaging** — limited; substantial
  in research; some operational deployment
- **Laser-based reconstruction** — read laser
  scattering off surfaces to reconstruct hidden
  details
- **Specialty imaging** — thermal, infrared,
  ultraviolet, multi-spectral

Counter-surveillance: *anti-imaging surfaces*
(specialty materials that disrupt imaging),
*active visual masking* (dazzle systems), *physical
shielding* (containment-class environments).

### Electronic surveillance

The largest single category in 2348:

- **Network monitoring** — substantial; most
  network traffic is routinely scanned by
  intelligence services
- **Device monitoring** — most personal handhelds
  have surveillance vectors; some are
  intentional, some are exploitable
- **Signal interception** — substantial RF
  monitoring across all populated regions
- **Side-channel analysis** — extracting
  information from electromagnetic, thermal, or
  other side-channels

Counter-surveillance: *encryption* (mature; covered
in cyber-security entry), *air-gapped systems*
(physically isolated from networks), *electromagnetic
shielding* (Faraday cage equivalents),
*device-hygiene practices* (regular security
audits, controlled supply chains).

### Behavioral surveillance

- **Pattern-of-life analysis** — substantial; AI
  systems track movement, communication, and
  behavioral patterns at scale
- **Predictive analytics** — increasingly
  sophisticated; can identify anomalous behavior
  patterns
- **Social-network analysis** — substantial
  capability across most jurisdictions

Counter-surveillance: *operational discipline*
(consistent patterns; avoiding anomalies),
*identity management* (specialty techniques;
substantial for intelligence operations).

## Counter-surveillance equipment

### Personal counter-surveillance

- **Bug detectors** — handheld; detect most
  audio and electronic surveillance devices
- **Camera detectors** — detect most camera
  installations
- **Network monitoring** — software for
  detecting unauthorized network access on
  personal devices
- **Signal jammers** — limited; legally
  restricted in most jurisdictions

### Professional counter-surveillance

- **Sweep teams** — trained personnel with
  specialty equipment for clearing
  environments
- **Protected facilities** — installations
  designed for surveillance resistance
- **Cryptographic infrastructure** — full
  cryptographic protection for sensitive
  operations

### Specialty counter-surveillance

- **Acoustic-isolation rooms** — physical
  shielding against audio surveillance
- **Faraday cages** — electromagnetic shielding
- **Anti-imaging surfaces** — for specific
  applications

## Tools used by intelligence services

The major intelligence services (FSec, MIB, OPA
security, Lloyd's intelligence) deploy:

### Collection

- **Human intelligence (HUMINT)** — agents and
  sources; substantial training and operational
  infrastructure
- **Signals intelligence (SIGINT)** — substantial;
  the largest single category by volume
- **Imagery intelligence (IMINT)** — substantial;
  including space-based imagery
- **Open-source intelligence (OSINT)** —
  substantially capable; the most-used
  category for routine analysis
- **Specialty collection** — including
  technical surveillance, financial intelligence,
  cyber-collection

### Operational

- **Surveillance teams** — for in-person
  surveillance operations
- **Specialty equipment** — for breaking-and-
  entering operations, technical surveillance
  installation, evidence-collection
- **Cyber operations capability** — for digital
  collection and operations
- **Cover support** — fictional identities,
  fictional employers, fictional histories

### Analytical

- **Multi-source analysis tools** — for
  integrating information across collection
  categories
- **Predictive modeling** — for forecasting
  operational developments
- **Threat assessment** — for operational
  prioritization

## Counter-surveillance for civilians

Civilian PCs in the *Halyard* expedition have
*limited* counter-surveillance training by
default. Specifically:

- **Cross** has substantial professional
  counter-surveillance training from his FSec
  and Hollow Hand careers. He can:
  - Identify substantial surveillance attempts
  - Conduct basic counter-surveillance sweeps
  - Advise on operational security
  - Run detection-and-evasion operations
- **Halverson** has substantial *practical*
  counter-surveillance experience from her
  Chamber-and-Protogen activities. Her techniques
  are different from Cross's but substantially
  capable.
- **Most other crew** have *limited* counter-
  surveillance capability; ordinary professional
  operational security but nothing specialized.

## Surveillance aboard the *Halyard*

The *Halyard* has substantial *internal* surveillance
infrastructure:

- **Network monitoring** — all internal
  communications and computer-system activity is
  logged
- **Camera coverage** — most public spaces have
  camera coverage; private spaces (cabins,
  captain's office) typically do not
- **Audio monitoring** — limited; specific public
  spaces; not in private quarters

This is *standard* for deep-range vessels. The
surveillance is for safety, accountability, and
audit purposes; it is *not* operationally
intelligence-class.

External surveillance of the *Halyard*:

- **Outer Authority beacon network** — passive
  monitoring of the ship's location and general
  operations
- **MCRN Third Fleet sensor coverage** — the
  *Halyard*'s outbound trajectory is monitored
- **Lloyd's underwriting intelligence** — passive;
  through publicly-available signals
- **FSec Outer Section** — passive; through OSA
  signal-corps infrastructure
- **MIB Earth Bureau** — limited active interest

The *Halyard*'s crew has *no expectation of privacy*
from external monitoring. The cryptographic systems
protect content; the metadata is visible to all
interested parties.

## Notes for play

- **Counter-surveillance is a Cross-and-Halverson
  capability** primarily. Other PCs without
  specialized training can do basic things but
  not professional-grade work.
- **Privacy expectations** matter narratively. PCs
  conducting sensitive conversations should
  reasonably assume the metadata is visible. The
  *content* is protected by encryption; *the fact
  of the conversation* is visible.
- **The *Halyard*'s logs are auditable**. PCs who
  do something operationally sensitive aboard
  ship are doing it on the record. This matters
  for late-act political-disclosure plot lines.
