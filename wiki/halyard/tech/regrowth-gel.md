# Regrowth-gel — mechanism and use

Regrowth-gel is the dominant regenerative-medicine
technology of 2348. It is a programmable biological
matrix that, applied to specific injury types under
specific conditions, accelerates and directs the
regrowth of damaged tissue. The gel is one of the
*signature* medical technologies of contemporary
human civilization, deployed across all scales from
emergency-trauma kits through hospital regenerative
medicine to specialty surgical applications.

This entry explains what regrowth-gel is, how it
works, what it can and cannot do, and how it is
deployed aboard the *Halyard* and in similar
contexts.

## What it is

Regrowth-gel is a biologically-active material
incorporating:

- **Living cell stocks** — engineered
  pluripotent stem cells with substantial
  guidance proteins
- **Extracellular matrix scaffolding** —
  biological material that provides structural
  support for cell growth
- **Programmable molecular signals** — the
  *direction* mechanism that tells the cells
  what tissue type to become
- **Vascularization-promoting factors** —
  for sustained tissue support
- **Anti-rejection factors** — to prevent
  immune-system attack on the regrowing tissue

The gel is supplied in single-use sterile
containers. Each container is *coded* for a specific
tissue type (skin, muscle, bone, nerve, organ-
specific variants). The gel must be applied
*matched* to its target tissue type.

## How it works

The regrowth-gel mechanism, simplified:

1. **Application** — the gel is applied to the
   wound site, either topically (for skin
   wounds), surgically (for deeper tissue), or
   through specialty delivery for internal
   applications.
2. **Initial cell deposition** — the gel's
   stem cells deposit themselves at the wound
   site.
3. **Direction signaling** — the programmable
   molecular signals begin directing cell
   differentiation.
4. **Cell differentiation** — over hours to
   days, the cells differentiate into the
   appropriate tissue type.
5. **Tissue integration** — the new tissue
   integrates with the surrounding healthy
   tissue.
6. **Vascularization** — the tissue develops
   blood-vessel supply through the
   vascularization-promoting factors.
7. **Maturation** — over days to weeks, the
   tissue matures to full functional state.

The total timeline depends on tissue type and
injury severity:

- **Skin regeneration** — 2-7 days for routine
  applications; immediate for minor wounds
- **Muscle regeneration** — 1-4 weeks
- **Bone regeneration** — 2-8 weeks
- **Nerve regeneration** — 2-12 weeks
  (substantially slower; some injuries take
  months)
- **Organ regeneration** — substantially
  variable; some applications are days,
  others months
- **Complex multi-tissue regeneration** —
  weeks to months

## What it can do

### Routine applications

- **Burn treatment** — substantial; major burns
  that would have been disabling in 2025 are
  now routinely treated to full recovery
- **Wound closure** — most penetrating wounds
  can be treated with regrowth-gel for
  full functional recovery
- **Limb-segment regeneration** — substantial;
  damaged or partially-amputated limb segments
  can often be regrown to full function
- **Specific organ regeneration** — for
  selected organs (kidney, liver, pancreas,
  some others)
- **Bone regeneration** — for most skeletal
  injuries
- **Nerve regeneration** — for many
  peripheral-nerve injuries
- **Reconstructive surgery** — substantial;
  many reconstructive procedures use
  regrowth-gel

### Emergency applications

- **Trauma stabilization** — emergency
  application can stabilize wounds rapidly
- **Mass-casualty response** — the gel is
  designed for use in field conditions
- **Aboard-spacecraft trauma** — the gel
  works in microgravity and vacuum-adjacent
  conditions

## What it cannot do

The gel has substantial limitations:

### Cannot regrow

- **The brain** — central nervous system tissue
  cannot be regrown with current technology.
  Significant brain injury results in permanent
  damage; the gel can stabilize but not regenerate
  brain tissue.
- **Spinal cord** (most segments) — the spinal
  cord can be partially regenerated in specific
  segments but full functional recovery is rare
- **Specific organ types** — including the
  thymus, parts of the immune system, certain
  endocrine functions; the gel works for many
  organs but not all
- **Sensory organs (some)** — the eye and the
  cochlea can be partially regenerated; full
  functional recovery is variable
- **Whole limbs from full amputation** — the
  gel can extend regrowth substantially but
  full whole-limb regeneration is not yet
  achievable; severe amputations are
  prosthetically managed

### Cannot resurrect

- **The dead** — the gel works on living
  tissue. A patient who has been dead long
  enough that cellular damage is irreversible
  cannot be saved by gel application. The
  approximate window is *minutes to a few
  hours* depending on conditions.
- **Whole-body trauma** — certain injuries
  (vacuum exposure for prolonged periods,
  catastrophic compression injuries) produce
  damage the gel cannot address.

### Has contraindications

- **Some genetic conditions** — patients with
  specific genetic markers cannot tolerate
  the gel
- **Some immunological conditions** — patients
  with specific immune dysfunctions
- **Some medications** — pharmaceutical
  contraindications exist
- **Specific tissue types** — the gel is
  *type-coded*; mismatched application can
  produce abnormal regrowth

### Has costs

- **The gel is expensive** — roughly $4K-40K
  per application depending on volume and
  type
- **The gel has a shelf life** — typically
  6-18 months under refrigeration
- **The application requires skill** — most
  applications require trained medical
  personnel

## Deployment aboard the *Halyard*

The *Halyard*'s regrowth-gel inventory:

- **Skin regeneration kit** (substantial
  inventory) — for routine wounds and burns
- **Muscle regeneration kit** (moderate
  inventory) — for traumatic muscle injuries
- **Bone regeneration kit** (moderate
  inventory) — for skeletal injuries
- **Nerve regeneration kit** (limited
  inventory) — for nerve injuries
- **Organ-specific kits** (limited inventory)
  — for emergency organ injuries
- **Emergency-trauma kit** (multiple) — for
  immediate field application

The Medical Officer manages the inventory; the
Captain and the Security Officer have access for
emergency use.

## Specific applications during the campaign

The campaign will likely produce regrowth-gel-
relevant scenarios:

- **EVA injuries** — punctures, decompression-
  related injuries, cold-injury; most are
  gel-treatable
- **Surface-operations injuries** — falls,
  equipment-related injuries; most are
  gel-treatable
- **Combat injuries** — ballistic wounds,
  burns, blast injuries; most are
  gel-treatable
- **Specialty injuries** — Mythos-related
  injuries (specifically the shoggoth-related
  injuries that are theoretically possible
  in late acts) — *outside* normal gel
  efficacy; standard gel-treatment may not
  suffice

## Notes for play

- **Regrowth-gel is *substantial*** but not
  *unlimited*. PCs should be able to recover
  from most injuries with sustained gel
  treatment, but death and permanent
  disability remain possible.
- **The 30-day cruise routine** during the
  long transit produces many minor injuries
  that are gel-treated. The crew gets used to
  treating each other's small wounds with
  gel.
- **Major trauma during the surface phase**
  at Nithon will require gel treatment that
  takes the affected PC out of action for
  days to weeks of recovery.
- **The shoggoth-injury limit** is a Keeper
  resource. If the campaign reaches that
  late-act beat, gel-treatment limitations
  produce specific dramatic stakes.
