# Spacecraft

Spacecraft in 2348 form a substantial taxonomy by
function, scale, and operating environment. The
Epstein drive (covered in `tech/epstein-drive.md`)
is the dominant propulsion technology; ship designs
are organized around its capabilities and
constraints.

This entry covers the major civilian spacecraft
categories. Military vessels are covered in
faction-specific entries (UNN, MCRN). The *Halyard*
itself is described in `ship/mks-halyard.md`.

## Ship classes by function

### Passenger ships

- **Liner-class passenger ships** — for major
  inter-system passenger routes (Earth-Mars,
  Earth-Belt, Mars-Belt, etc.); large; comfortable;
  expensive
- **Standard passenger transport** — for
  routine inter-station passenger service
- **Specialty passenger** — for short-distance
  or specialty applications

### Freighter classes

- **Bulk freighters** — large vessels carrying
  enormous cargo volumes; relatively slow
- **Fast freighters** — smaller, faster;
  for time-sensitive cargo
- **Specialty freighters** — for hazardous,
  perishable, or specialty cargo
- **Tug-class vessels** — for moving large
  unpowered cargo containers and stations

### Specialty civilian ships

- **Science vessels** — like the *Halyard*;
  designed for deep-range scientific operations
- **Survey vessels** — for surveying
  asteroids, planetary bodies, and orbital
  positions
- **Medical ships** — equipped as mobile
  hospitals
- **Mining ships** — designed for asteroid
  mining operations
- **Construction ships** — for orbital and
  space-based construction
- **Specialty operations ships** — including
  specific OPA, MCR, and UN-government
  applications

### Recreational ships

- **Yachts** — luxury private vessels
- **Specialty recreational** — including
  some specialty deep-range tourism

## Sister classes to the Halyard

The Lambert-class deep-range science vessel is
the *Halyard*'s class. Sister vessels currently in
operation:

- **MKS Halyard** — the campaign's vessel
- **MKS Astoria** — built 2287; currently
  operating in the trans-Neptunian region
  on a different consortium contract
- **MKS Brixham** — built 2294; currently
  in extended refit at Pavonis Orbital
- **MKS Cohasset** — built 2301; currently
  on a Belt-region contract
- **MKS Dunedin** — built 2334; the newest
  of the class; on a Saturn-system contract

Each vessel is operationally similar but has its
own crew, contract history, and current
deployment.

The Lambert class has been substantially
successful as a deep-range science vessel design;
the class's longevity (the original MKS Halyard
build dates from 2289) speaks to the soundness of
the basic configuration.

## Smaller specialty craft

### Landers

The *Halyard* carries two **Kestrel-class
landers**:

- **Kestrel-1** — typically operated by Lt. Rae
  Kosugi
- **Kestrel-2** — typically operated by Jonah
  Bailey

The Kestrel class is a small (12 m length) suborbital
vehicle capable of:

- Vacuum operations
- Light atmospheric operations (entry into
  atmospheres of approximately 0.4 bar or
  thinner)
- Substantial range (about 14,000 km from base
  in vacuum operations)
- Crew of 4 plus 2 cargo containers
- Surface landing-and-takeoff on most
  small-body terrain
- Limited cargo capability (~4 tons)

The Kestrels are essential for surface operations
on Nithon. Each carries a surface rover (covered
in `tech/vehicles-surface.md`) and substantial
EVA-and-surface-equipment.

### Shuttles

Small surface-to-orbit shuttles. The Mars Lift
and major Lunar shuttle services use this
category. The *Halyard* does not carry shuttles
in the standard sense; the Kestrels handle
landing-and-orbit operations.

### Cargo pods

Small unpowered cargo containers; carried on
larger vessels and detached for specific
operations.

### Specialty small craft

- **EVA work-pods** — small vehicles for EVA-
  specific work outside vessels
- **Specialty research craft** — for specific
  scientific applications
- **Specialty industrial craft** — for specific
  operational applications

## Naval vessels

Covered in faction-specific entries (UNN, MCRN).
Brief summary of categories:

### UNN

- **Capital ships** — Tsavo, Tassili, Habsburg
  classes (carriers/capital combatants)
- **Cruisers** — substantial fleet
- **Frigates and corvettes** — substantial
  fleet
- **Specialty vessels** — including OSA
  patrol craft

### MCRN

- **Capital ships** — Donnager class
- **Cruisers** — Pavonis, Tempest classes
- **Frigates and destroyers**
- **Corvettes and patrol craft**

### Belt and OPA

- **Various vessels** — substantial diversity;
  Belt-flagged vessels include both
  commercial and OPA-affiliated military craft

## Cost and access

Civilian spacecraft access:

- **Personal spacecraft** — extraordinarily
  expensive; only the wealthiest individuals
  own private craft
- **Commercial passenger transport** — accessible
  to most Earthers, Martians, and Belters at
  some cost
- **Specialty operations** — substantial cost;
  substantial regulatory oversight
- **Deep-range vessels** — like the *Halyard* —
  substantial capital cost; usually
  consortium-or-corporate-owned rather than
  individual

## Aboard the *Halyard*

The *Halyard* itself is described in detail in
`ship/mks-halyard.md`. The ship is:

- A Lambert-class deep-range science vessel
- 140 m length
- 0.4 g hab ring
- BSL-3 containment lab
- Two Kestrel-class landers
- Decommissioned aft PDC mount
- Crew of 11 (10 standard plus the Captain)
- Substantial deep-range life-support and
  equipment

## Notes for play

- **The *Halyard*'s class is mature** — the
  Lambert class has been operating successfully
  for decades. Players should expect the ship
  to be reliable.
- **The Kestrels are essential** for surface
  operations. Players should plan for landing
  operations as substantial undertakings.
- **Sister vessels** are useful narrative
  resources. Other Lambert-class ships exist;
  their crews and contracts are part of the
  broader deep-range science community the
  *Halyard* belongs to.
