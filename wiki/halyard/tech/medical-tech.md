# Medical technology in 2348

Medical technology in 2348 has matured along the
trajectory 2025 anticipated, with many of the late-
twenty-first-century *grand challenges* now
substantially solved. Cancer is largely curable;
most cardiovascular disease is preventable; most
infectious disease has effective treatments;
substantial regenerative medicine is routine.
Lifespan in advanced economies runs 100-110 years
typical; quality of life through age 90 has been
substantially extended.

What has *not* materialized: true rejuvenation,
genetic immortality, or the *quality-of-life-
forever* technology that 2025 sometimes hoped for.
Death remains *real*; medicine has substantially
delayed and managed but not abolished it.

## Major capabilities

### Diagnostics

- **AI-assisted diagnostic systems** — universal
  in clinical settings; substantially better than
  2025 clinical AI
- **Real-time blood-work** — handheld devices
  perform comprehensive analysis in seconds
- **Imaging technology** — substantially better
  resolution and lower-radiation than 2025;
  multi-spectral capability standard
- **Genetic analysis** — full-genome analysis is
  routine; substantial pharmacogenomic
  application
- **Continuous health monitoring** — wearable
  and implanted sensors; most adults have some
  form of continuous monitoring

### Surgery

- **Robotic-assisted surgery** — universal; most
  surgical procedures are robot-assisted
- **Minimally-invasive techniques** — substantial;
  most procedures use small incisions
- **Image-guided surgery** — universal; real-time
  imaging during procedures
- **Reconstructive surgery** — substantially
  capable; can address many injuries
  twenty-first-century medicine could not
- **Specialty surgery** — including substantial
  capability in nervous-system surgery and
  organ-replacement surgery

### Pharmaceuticals

- **Targeted therapy** — substantially capable;
  most drugs are designed for specific targets
- **Personalized medicine** — drugs are
  routinely customized for individual patients
- **Biological therapy** — substantial; including
  monoclonal antibodies, cell-based therapies,
  and gene therapy
- **Anti-infectives** — substantially capable;
  most infectious diseases have effective
  treatments

### Regenerative medicine

- **Tissue regeneration** — substantial
  capability for many tissue types
- **Organ replacement** — substantial; both
  biological (grown from patient cells) and
  synthetic
- **Limb replacement** — both biological and
  prosthetic options
- **The regrowth-gel system** — covered separately
  in `tech/regrowth-gel.md`

### Specialty medicine

- **Sleep medicine** — substantial; most sleep
  disorders have effective treatments
- **Mental health** — substantial; most psychiatric
  conditions have effective pharmacological and
  behavioral treatments
- **Reproductive medicine** — substantial; most
  fertility issues are addressable
- **Geriatric medicine** — substantial; the
  *quality-of-life-after-90* extension is the
  major achievement of contemporary geriatrics
- **Sports medicine** — substantially capable
- **Trauma medicine** — substantially capable

## Limitations

What *cannot* be done:

- **Reverse aging** — not in any general sense;
  longevity is extended, not reversed
- **Treat all neurological conditions** — major
  brain injuries, certain neurodegenerative
  diseases remain difficult
- **Treat all cancers** — most are treatable but
  not all
- **Replace consciousness** — the brain-cylinder
  technology of the Mi-go is *alien*; human
  medicine cannot perform consciousness transfer
- **Replicate the dead** — death is final;
  rejuvenation does not return the dead

## Aboard-spacecraft medicine

Spacecraft medicine has specific requirements:

- **Compact equipment** — surgical and diagnostic
  capability in small physical footprint
- **Vacuum-and-radiation-friendly** materials and
  procedures
- **Long-duration capability** — equipment
  operating without re-supply for months at a time
- **Low-gravity-tolerant** procedures and
  equipment
- **Substantial automation** — reducing crew
  burden for routine medical care

The standard deep-range medical bay can perform:

- Most diagnostics
- Most routine surgery (with appropriate skill)
- Substantial trauma management
- Most pharmaceutical care
- Limited regenerative medicine
- Substantial telemedicine consultation with
  inner-system specialists (with light-speed
  delay)

## The Halyard's medical bay

The *Halyard*'s medical bay specifications:

- **Surgical capability** — full surgical suite
  including robotic-assisted equipment
- **Diagnostic capability** — comprehensive;
  including imaging, genetic analysis, and
  comprehensive blood work
- **Pharmaceutical inventory** — substantial;
  approximately 280 pharmaceutical types
  including most contemporary therapies
- **Regrowth-gel capacity** — for emergency
  trauma; specific applications detailed in
  the regrowth-gel entry
- **Cryo capability** — for the planned cryo-
  block during the long transit
- **Telemedicine** — capability to consult with
  inner-system specialists (with substantial
  delay)
- **Emergency surgery** — capability for
  emergency procedures; the bay is designed for
  one-person surgeon operation in emergency
- **Pharmaceutical synthesis** — limited
  on-board synthesis capability

## The Medical Officer

The *Halyard*'s Medical Officer has substantial
training. The standard outer-system Medical Officer
is expected to handle:

- Routine clinical care
- Most surgical procedures within the bay's
  capability
- Trauma management
- Mental-health support
- Reproductive medicine
- Substantial diagnostic interpretation
- Pharmaceutical management

The current standard Medical Officer (whether the
PC or the NPC Vandermeer) holds:

- Medical degree from an accredited Earth or
  Mars institution
- Substantial residency training
- Specialty certification appropriate to the
  deep-range role
- Outer Authority deep-range certification

## Medical conditions for play

### Routine concerns

- **Microgravity effects** — bone density,
  muscle mass, vestibular adaptation; managed
  through routine exercise and supplementation
- **Radiation exposure** — managed through
  shielding and dose tracking
- **Sleep adaptation** — substantial adjustment
  for shipboard schedules; pharmacological
  support widely used
- **Nutritional management** — comprehensive
  shipboard nutrition

### Trauma

- **Decompression injury** — the most common
  serious shipboard trauma; managed through
  prevention plus rapid intervention
- **Vacuum exposure** — extreme; survival
  depends on rapid intervention
- **Burns and injuries** — standard trauma
  protocols
- **Specialty trauma** — radiation injury,
  toxic exposure, etc.

### Long-term

- **Mental health** — substantial concern in
  long-duration deep-range work; managed
  through routine pharmacological support and
  contemplative practices
- **Family-relationship maintenance** —
  asynchronous communication produces specific
  psychological pressures; counseling support
  available

## Notes for play

- **Medical capability is substantial** for
  most routine and emergency situations.
- **Death remains real**. PCs should not
  treat medical care as a guarantee of
  survival.
- **Regrowth-gel** is one of the campaign's
  most operationally useful medical
  technologies; it has limits, covered in
  the next entry.
- **The Medical Officer is a substantial
  asset** to the crew. PCs should engage
  with them as a meaningful resource rather
  than a background functionary.
