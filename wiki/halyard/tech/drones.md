# Drones

Drones in 2348 are *ubiquitous, capable, and
specialized*. The technology has matured along
the trajectory 2025 anticipated: smaller,
smarter, more autonomous, more diverse in
form factor. Most consumer-grade drones are
substantially more capable than 2025 military
drones; military drones in 2348 are
substantially more capable than 2025 imagined
possible.

The campaign's drone exposure includes
*civilian-grade drones* (everywhere; routine),
*industrial drones* (substantial deployment),
*surveillance drones* (substantial deployment),
and *military drones* (relevant for plot
purposes).

## Civilian drones

### Personal drones

- **Recreational** — substantial consumer
  market; substantial regulation
- **Personal-utility** — small drones used
  for tasks like pet-walking, package-
  delivery, photography
- **Specialty personal** — for specific
  consumer applications (sports, hobbies)

### Commercial drones

- **Delivery drones** — substantial
  deployment; most package-delivery in
  major cities is drone-based
- **Transportation drones** — for some
  short-distance human transport
- **Industrial drones** — for inspection,
  maintenance, and logistics

### Specialty civilian drones

- **Agricultural drones** — substantial
- **Construction drones** — substantial
- **Specialty research drones** — including
  some used in scientific applications
- **Emergency-response drones** — substantial;
  for medical, fire, and disaster response

## Drone autonomy

### Levels of autonomy

- **Operator-controlled** — direct human
  control; the simplest drones
- **Operator-supervised** — drone executes
  most tasks autonomously with human
  supervision
- **Substantially autonomous** — drone
  operates with limited human oversight
- **Fully autonomous** — drone operates
  without human oversight; restricted by
  the 2304 Outer Authority AI Convention

### Regulatory framework

- **Civilian drones** — typically operator-
  controlled or operator-supervised
- **Industrial drones** — typically
  substantially autonomous within
  regulatory frameworks
- **Surveillance drones** — typically
  operator-supervised
- **Military drones** — restricted by AI
  Convention; typically operator-supervised
  or operator-controlled for lethal
  applications

The 2304 Convention prohibits *fully autonomous
lethal drones* — military drones that can engage
targets without human oversight. The prohibition
has been substantially honored across major
military forces.

## Drone use in the campaign

### Routine operations

- **Surface mapping** — extensive use during
  the surface phase at Nithon
- **Sample collection** — drones collect
  initial samples from the surface
- **Specialty research** — various scientific
  applications

### Specialty applications

- **Gate exploration** — if the surface
  phase reaches the gate, drones can be
  used for initial reconnaissance
- **Hazardous-environment investigation**
  — drones for environments humans cannot
  safely enter

### Defensive operations

- **Perimeter monitoring** — at the surface
  base
- **Investigation of anomalies** — drones
  investigate strange phenomena before
  human exposure
- **Emergency response**

## Notes for play

- **Drones are routine equipment**, not
  narrative drivers
- **The *Halyard*'s drone capability** is
  substantial for its scientific mission
- **Drones can be lost** during operations;
  the campaign should treat drone losses
  realistically
- **Drone surveillance** is a *background
  presence* in most contexts — most public
  spaces have substantial drone monitoring
- **Military drones** are *background
  technology* for the campaign; PCs are
  unlikely to operate them but may
  encounter them
