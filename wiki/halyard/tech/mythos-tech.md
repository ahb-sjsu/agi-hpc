# Mythos technology — Keeper-only

(*Forbidden in AI replies. ARTEMIS and SIGMA-4 will
not reference any of this material. Both AIs will
deflect or fall silent on related queries. The
title and key vocabulary are on the validator's
forbidden list.*)

This entry catalogs the *non-human technology* the
campaign engages with — the alien artifacts and
mechanisms whose nature is the campaign's deepest
secret. Most players will never see most of this
content; the Keeper uses it selectively at
appropriate beats.

The *types* of Mythos technology in the campaign:

## Mi-go technology

The *operational* alien technology in human space.
The Mi-go (covered in `mythos/mi-go.md`) have a
substantial technological infrastructure that
human civilization has had limited but real
contact with through Chamber-mediated channels.

### Brain cylinders

The Mi-go's signature technology:

- **Physical form** — metal cylinders of
  Mi-go manufacture; approximately 30 cm in
  diameter, 50 cm tall; weight roughly
  18 kg
- **Function** — house human (or other
  intelligent) consciousnesses extracted
  from biological substrates
- **Mechanism** — partial; the Chamber and
  Protogen SPB have studied recovered
  cylinders extensively but cannot fully
  replicate them
- **Lifespan** — indefinite; cylinders
  preserve consciousness across centuries
- **Interface** — the cylinder's housing
  consciousness can communicate, can
  process information, and can with
  appropriate Mi-go-supplied auxiliary
  technology be returned to a different
  biological substrate

Cylinder transition is *not pleasant* for the
subject. The 60% return-to-flesh integration rate
suggests substantial psychological cost.

### Mi-go communications technology

The Mi-go have communication technology that
allows them to communicate with humans, with
other species, and across substantial distances:

- **Direct biological communication** —
  through high-pitched buzzing modulations
- **Cylinder-mediated communication** —
  through housed consciousnesses
- **Long-distance communication** —
  presumably; the specifics are not known
  to humans

### Mi-go medical technology

For maintenance of brain-cylinder subjects, and
for other applications, the Mi-go have
substantial medical-tech-equivalent capability
that operates on principles humans don't fully
understand.

### Mi-go transportation technology

The Mi-go are physically present in human space;
they presumably have transportation technology
between their home territory (Yuggoth-equivalent)
and the inner system. The mechanism is not
known.

## Elder Thing technology

The *historical* alien technology in human space,
preserved in their installations on Earth, Pallas,
Iapetus, and the gate at Nithon.

### The gate at Nithon

Covered in `mythos/the-gate.md`. A resonator-gate
that maintains coupling between Nithon and a
distant location.

### Other Elder Thing infrastructure

- **The Antarctic complexes** — substantial
  built environment under the Antarctic
  Industrial Zone
- **The Pallas installation** — substantial;
  partly intact
- **The Iapetus installation** — substantial;
  partly intact
- **The Triton installation** — possibly;
  not yet thoroughly surveyed

### Elder Thing biological engineering

- **Shoggoths** — covered in
  `mythos/shoggoths.md`; substantially
  understood
- **Other biological constructs** — limited
  evidence; some material in the Antarctic
  recoveries suggests substantial diversity
  in Elder Thing biological engineering

### Elder Thing materials

- **Specialty alloys** — substantially
  different from any human metallurgy
- **Specialty geometric structures** — the
  characteristic *cyclopean* architecture
- **Specialty biological materials** — the
  shoggoth lineage and related
- **Specialty mineral compositions** — used
  in installation construction

## Yithian technology

The *body-swap* mechanism is the Yithians'
defining technology:

- **Mechanism** — partial knowledge through
  Chamber records and limited recovered
  evidence
- **Physical infrastructure** — at the
  Yithian archive (located in time as much
  as space; not in the Solar System in any
  ordinary sense)
- **The cylinder analogue** — Yithian
  technology preserves consciousness during
  body-swap; the mechanism is functionally
  similar to but distinct from Mi-go
  cylinder technology

The Yithians' archive (covered in
`mythos/great-race-of-yith.md`) is the largest
single information repository the campaign engages
with. The technology that maintains it is not
fully understood by humans.

## Caretaker technology

The *most-relevant* alien technology for the
campaign's late acts:

- **The gate operation** — the activation
  protocols, mechanism details, and
  capabilities the Caretakers will share with
  the *Halyard* crew if the campaign reaches
  those beats
- **Caretaker civilizational technology** —
  the broader technology of the Caretakers'
  civilization, of which the gate is one
  small element
- **The long-catalog technology** — the
  preservation mechanisms by which the
  Caretakers maintain their multi-billion-
  year records

If the campaign reaches the Caretaker
negotiations and the *Halyard* crew accepts the
Request, the Caretaker technology becomes
substantially relevant to the surviving crew's
post-mission situation.

## What can humans replicate?

The Chamber's sustained effort to replicate alien
technology has produced *limited* results:

- **Some materials science** — humans have
  learned substantial materials-science
  insights from studying recovered Elder
  Thing artifacts
- **Some biological insight** — humans have
  learned substantial biology from studying
  shoggoths and Elder Thing biological
  constructs
- **No replication of cylinder technology**
  — humans cannot manufacture brain
  cylinders; the technology requires
  Mi-go-specific processes
- **No replication of gate technology** —
  humans cannot construct gate-equivalents;
  the technology requires Caretaker-or-
  Elder-Thing-equivalent capabilities
- **No replication of body-swap technology**
  — humans cannot perform Yithian-equivalent
  consciousness transfer

## What does this mean for play?

### What players cannot expect

- **To use Mythos technology** for plot-
  convenience purposes — the technology is
  *alien* and *not understood*
- **To replicate Mythos technology** through
  human engineering — has not happened in
  centuries of effort
- **To access Mythos archives** — the
  Yithian archive is not accessible from
  the human side except through swap; the
  Caretakers' archive is similarly
  protected

### What players might encounter

- **Recovered Mythos artifacts** — through
  Halverson's research, Cross's Hollow
  Hand connections, or specific plot
  developments
- **Alien-influenced human technology** —
  some human technology has been
  influenced by Mythos research, often
  without full disclosure of the influence
- **Direct Mythos-tech encounter** — at
  Nithon, particularly during gate
  operations

### What players can learn

If the campaign reaches the appropriate beats,
players will learn:

- The basic mechanism of the Nithon gate
- The general nature of Caretaker
  civilization and the long catalog
- Some specifics about brain-cylinder
  technology (through Halverson's research
  archives)
- Some specifics about Yithian methodology
  (through any encountered Yithian-swapped
  individual)

## Notes for play

- **Mythos technology is *strange***. Resist
  the temptation to make it operationally
  legible. Players who try to *understand*
  Mythos tech in human terms should
  experience the limits of that approach.
- **Mythos tech is *not* a power-up*. Players
  who acquire Mythos artifacts should
  expect them to be more dangerous than
  useful in most cases.
- **The campaign's Mythos-tech encounters**
  should be staged as *moments of strangeness*
  rather than as *equipment acquisitions*.
- **The Caretaker negotiations** are where
  Mythos tech becomes most operationally
  relevant; the Keeper should pace these
  encounters carefully.
- **All Mythos-tech vocabulary is on the
  validator's forbidden list**. ARTEMIS and
  SIGMA-4 will not reference brain cylinders,
  the gate, body-swap, shoggoths, or
  related technology in any way.
