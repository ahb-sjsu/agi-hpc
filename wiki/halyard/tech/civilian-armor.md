# Civilian armor in 2348

Civilian armor in 2348 falls into two principal
categories: *body armor* (worn for combat or hostile-
environment protection) and *EVA suits* (worn for
vacuum and hostile-atmosphere environments). The
two categories overlap substantially in practice;
many EVA suits incorporate ballistic-protection
features, and some specialty body-armor designs
have vacuum capability.

Civilian armor is widely available, with regulatory
frameworks varying by jurisdiction. The *Halyard*'s
crew has access to substantial civilian-grade armor
in the form of EVA suits; the security personnel
have additional ballistic-grade body armor.

## Body armor

### Standard civilian body armor

- **Concealable body armor** — soft body armor
  designed to be worn under clothing; protection
  against handgun rounds and limited rifle
  protection
- **Plate carriers** — visible body armor with
  rigid ballistic plates; protection against
  rifle rounds; substantial weight (4-8 kg
  typical)
- **Specialty civilian armor** — for specific
  applications (motorcycle protection, hunting
  protection, etc.)

Modern civilian body armor uses advanced composite
materials substantially better than 2025 equivalents:
similar protection at lower weight; substantial
multi-impact resistance; some self-repair
capabilities for soft armor.

Most civilian armor is *legal* in most jurisdictions
under licensing or registration requirements. Some
specific configurations are restricted (military-
grade plates with armor-piercing protection).

### Civilian armor regulatory framework

- **Earth jurisdictions** — most allow civilian
  ownership with licensing; some restrict the
  most-protective grades
- **Mars** — relatively permissive
- **Luna** — restrictive
- **Belt** — variable; mostly station-by-station

## EVA suits

The *fundamental* civilian armor of 2348. EVA suits
provide protection against:

- Vacuum (atmospheric loss)
- Cold (radiative cooling in space)
- Radiation (some)
- Micrometeorite impact (limited)
- Limited ballistic protection (most modern suits)
- Chemical and biological hazards (varies)

### Suit categories

#### Light EVA suits

For general vacuum work:

- **Mass**: 25-40 kg
- **Operational time**: 4-12 hours typical
- **Protection**: vacuum, cold, basic radiation,
  limited micrometeorite
- **Ballistic protection**: minimal
- **Cost**: $40K-200K (range of quality)

Most EVA workers carry their own light suits.

#### Heavy EVA suits

For hazardous-environment vacuum work:

- **Mass**: 40-80 kg
- **Operational time**: 12-24 hours typical
- **Protection**: substantial vacuum, cold, radiation,
  micrometeorite; limited ballistic
- **Specialty applications**: deep-space EVA,
  hostile-environment work, surface operations
  on hazardous bodies
- **Cost**: $200K-1M

The *Halyard*'s standard EVA inventory includes
heavy EVA suits for surface operations.

#### Combat-rated EVA suits

For tactical operations in vacuum:

- **Substantial ballistic protection** in addition
  to vacuum
- **Mobility-optimized** at the cost of some
  protection
- **Substantial sensor and weapon integration**
- **Specialty applications**: military boarding
  operations, station-defense, hazardous-
  environment combat

The MCRN and UNN issue combat-rated EVA suits to
their boarding-action specialists. The *Halyard*'s
arms locker contains 2 such suits issued to Cross
for emergency use.

### House Rules §4.2 (EVA suits)

The campaign's EVA suit mechanics are detailed in
the House Rules. Key features:

- **EVA Suit Integrity** — 4 points typical
- **Each mishap** — typically -1 Integrity
- **Integrity 0** — pressure loss; survival
  becomes immediate concern
- **Specific failure modes** — punctures,
  joint-seal failures, life-support failures,
  helmet-seal failures

The Integrity system is the campaign's mechanism for
making EVA suits *narratively meaningful* — they're
not invulnerable; they fail; they can be repaired
under appropriate conditions.

## EVA suit features

Modern EVA suits include:

### Life support

- **Oxygen recycling** — substantial closed-loop
  capability; most modern suits can operate for
  multiple hours on stored oxygen plus several
  more on recycling
- **CO₂ scrubbing** — universal
- **Temperature regulation** — substantial
  active cooling/heating
- **Hydration** — integrated water system

### Mobility

- **Joint design** — substantially improved over
  2025 EVA suits; reasonable mobility for most
  tasks
- **Power-assist (specialty)** — exoskeleton-class
  features for some heavy-EVA suits
- **Helmet design** — wide field-of-view; HUD
  integration; substantially improved over 2025

### Communications and sensors

- **Suit comms** — encrypted, paired with the
  wearer's standard handheld
- **Local sensor integration** — atmospheric,
  radiation, structural-integrity sensors
- **Tool-using** — manipulator-class tools that
  can be operated through the suit
- **HUD** — substantial information display

### Emergency systems

- **Oxygen reserves** — for reaching emergency
  refuge
- **Emergency-beacon** — automatic activation in
  catastrophic situations
- **Self-sealant** — limited ability to seal
  small punctures automatically
- **Decompression-injury management** — limited
  active support for decompression injuries

## Aboard the *Halyard*

The *Halyard*'s EVA inventory:

- **11 standard heavy EVA suits** — one per crew
  member; sized for individual fitting
- **2 specialty surface-EVA suits** — for the
  Surface Operations Specialist and the
  Xenoarchaeology Consultant; enhanced sensor
  and tool-using capabilities
- **2 combat-rated EVA suits** — issued to Cross;
  in the arms locker
- **1 emergency-EVA suit** — quick-don,
  short-duration; for emergency airlock
  evacuation
- **Substantial spare parts and repair
  capability**

Each crew member is responsible for maintaining
their assigned suit. The Surface Operations
Specialist (or whoever serves that role) handles
inventory and major maintenance.

## Notes for play

- **EVA suits matter.** The campaign will produce
  multiple scenarios where suit integrity is
  the difference between life and death.
- **The *Halyard*'s EVA inventory is good** for
  a science vessel of its class. Well-maintained,
  appropriate to mission profile.
- **PCs with EVA backgrounds** (Surface Ops,
  Engineer, etc.) should be assumed to have
  substantial suit-skill — they can perform
  in-the-field repairs, recognize warning signs,
  and adapt to suit malfunctions.
- **Civilian body armor is rare** aboard ship.
  Most PCs would not have body armor unless
  specifically issued for security purposes.
