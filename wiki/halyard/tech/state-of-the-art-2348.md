# State of the art, 2348 — overview

This entry establishes the technological baseline of
the campaign. It is the *what is and isn't possible
in 2348* reference. Specific tech categories
(computing, comms, weapons, armor, vehicles,
medicine) get their own entries; this one is the
broad map.

The general principle: **2348 is a hard-SF setting
with two centuries of incremental technology
development past 2025, no AGI, no FTL, no exotic
physics, no transhumanist breakthrough**. The technology
is *better* than 2025's, but in the way 2025 was
better than 1825 — refinement, application, scaling,
and the maturity of dozens of technical disciplines.

What 2025 thought of as *near-future* (fusion power,
working space habitats, automation, biotechnology,
neural interfaces) is now *mature*. What 2025 thought
of as *transformative* (artificial general
intelligence, faster-than-light travel, mind
uploading, molecular nanotechnology) has not
materialized.

## What's mature

These technologies have crossed from frontier into
*mature* status — widely deployed, commercially
operating, no longer surprising:

### Power and propulsion

- **Fusion power** — deuterium-tritium fusion has
  been the dominant electricity source on Earth,
  Luna, and Mars since the 2240s. Reactors range
  from large grid-feeding installations to vehicle-
  scale (the Epstein drive's reactor) to handheld
  emergency-power units.
- **The Epstein drive** — magneto-confined fusion
  drive (separate entry: `tech/epstein-drive.md`).
  Standard interplanetary propulsion for a century.
- **Mass drivers** — magnetic-launch surface-to-
  orbit cargo systems. Routine on Luna; expanding
  on Mars and the Belt.
- **Solar power** — substantial across all bodies
  with adequate insolation. Orbital and surface
  arrays.
- **Helium-3 fusion** — second-generation fusion;
  cleaner, more efficient than deuterium-tritium;
  still requires substantial helium-3 production
  (the Lunar Industrial Belt is the principal
  source).

### Computing

- **General-purpose computing** — many orders of
  magnitude beyond 2025; ubiquitous; effectively
  unlimited for ordinary applications
- **Quantum computing** — mature and routine for
  cryptographic and optimization applications
- **Expert AI systems** — extensive in industry,
  finance, science, medicine, and personal
  applications. Detailed entry:
  `tech/computing-and-ai.md`.
- **Holographic display** — standard. Most public
  displays use volumetric holography. Higher-end
  applications have *full-walk-around* immersion.

### Communications

- **Laser comms** — standard for ship-to-ship and
  ship-to-station; reliable, secure, high-bandwidth
- **Encrypted relay networks** — deep-space
  communications with managed light-speed delay
- **Personal-handheld communicators** — universal
- **Brain-computer interfaces (limited)** — for
  specific medical and operational applications;
  not consumer-grade

### Materials

- **Carbon-nanotube composites** — structural
  applications across all major engineering domains
- **Specialty alloys** — fusion-reactor materials,
  vacuum-and-radiation-tolerant materials,
  surface-and-vacuum dual-use materials
- **Programmable materials** — limited; specific
  applications (medical, manufacturing) where
  matter-state changes are operationally useful
- **Metamaterials** — for specific optical,
  acoustic, and electromagnetic applications

### Biotechnology

- **Gene therapy** — mature for many genetic
  conditions; routine clinical practice
- **Synthetic organs** — substantial; many
  conditions previously requiring transplant are
  now treated with synthetic replacement
- **Longevity treatments** — partially mature;
  typical lifespan in advanced economies is
  100-110 years; the *quality-of-life-after-90*
  has been substantially extended
- **Regenerative medicine** — including the
  *regrowth-gel* technology covered separately
- **Pharmaceutical biotech** — extensive; many
  conditions previously untreatable have
  effective treatments

### Manufacturing and materials

- **Distributed manufacturing** — small-scale,
  customer-specific production is widespread
- **Mass production** — at scales 2025 would
  recognize but with substantial improvement in
  efficiency and cost
- **Recycling and circular economy** — extensive;
  most consumer goods are designed for material
  recovery
- **Asteroid resource utilization** — mature; the
  Belt's industrial economy depends on it

### Medical care

- **Detailed entry**: `tech/medical-tech.md`
- The major killers of 2025 (cardiovascular
  disease, cancer, infectious disease, accidents)
  are substantially manageable. Cancer is largely
  curable; cardiovascular disease is preventable;
  most infectious diseases have effective
  treatments; accidents are still fatal but at
  much lower rates.

## What's frontier

These technologies exist but are not yet mature; they
are *cutting-edge* in 2348:

- **Implants and cybernetics** — see
  `tech/implants-and-cybernetics.md`. Substantial
  but not universal; substantial regulatory
  frameworks; rare at high integration levels.
- **Neural interfaces** — limited consumer
  applications; substantial military and
  medical applications.
- **Nanotechnology (industrial)** — mature for
  specific applications; the *molecular
  manufacturing* dream of 2025 has *not*
  materialized.
- **Brain-computer interface (high-bandwidth)** —
  research-level; not consumer.
- **Genetic enhancement (beyond therapy)** —
  legally restricted; technologically possible.
- **Long-duration cryogenic preservation** —
  used for spaceflight (cryo on long voyages);
  research-level for medical applications
- **Some biological-system-as-machine
  applications** — research-level; not yet
  consumer

## What's *not* possible

The following technologies *do not exist* in 2348,
despite occasional 2025 predictions:

- **Faster-than-light travel.** Not in any form.
  Communications, transit, all bound by c. The
  campaign explicitly assumes this.
- **Mind uploading.** Brain-state preservation in
  digital form has not been achieved. The Mi-go's
  *brain-cylinder* protocol is the closest thing
  in any direction; it is alien tech, not human
  tech, and is forbidden in any case.
- **Artificial general intelligence.** AGI as
  understood in 2025 — a synthetic general
  intelligence equivalent to human cognition —
  has not been achieved. The expert AI systems
  (including ARTEMIS and SIGMA-4) are
  domain-specialized; they don't generalize the
  way humans do.
- **True molecular nanotechnology.** *Drexlerian*
  nanotech (universal molecular assemblers) does
  not exist. Industrial nanotech is real but
  limited.
- **Genetic immortality.** Substantial life-
  extension exists; *immortality* does not.
- **Functional time travel.** Not in any form.
- **Antigravity / reactionless drives.** Not in
  any form. All propulsion is reaction-mass-
  based.
- **Cold fusion (room-temperature fusion).** Not
  achieved. Fusion is hot-fusion only.
- **Practical wormholes / portal physics.** Not
  human technology. The *gate at Nithon* is
  Elder Thing technology, alien, and not
  reproducible by human means.

## What's *forbidden* (not on technical grounds)

Some technologies are technologically possible but
*regulatorily forbidden* across most of human space:

- **Genetic enhancement of humans** — beyond
  therapy. Legally restricted under the 2298
  *Geneva Genetic Convention*.
- **Aggressive AI** — autonomous lethal AI
  systems. Restricted under the 2304 *Outer
  Authority AI Convention*.
- **Brain-computer integration** beyond therapy.
  Substantially restricted; clinical applications
  only.
- **Most weapons of mass destruction** — heavily
  controlled.
- **Cryogenic experimentation on humans** —
  beyond the limited spaceflight applications.
  Most jurisdictions prohibit research on
  consciousness preservation.

## Cost trends

Across two centuries, the cost-of-most-things has
fallen substantially relative to wages. Specific
patterns:

- **Energy** — substantially cheaper. Electricity
  is *nearly free* by 2025 standards.
- **Computing** — extraordinarily cheap. Effectively
  unlimited.
- **Communications** — nearly free.
- **Manufacturing** — substantially cheaper for
  ordinary goods; some specialty items remain
  expensive.
- **Medical care** — substantially cheaper for
  routine care; some advanced therapies remain
  expensive.
- **Housing** — *more* expensive in major cities;
  the verticalization era didn't reduce housing
  costs in proportion to other gains.
- **Spaceflight** — substantially cheaper but still
  expensive. The *Halyard*-class deep-range
  vessel costs about $400-600M; passenger
  spaceflight runs $4-10K Earth-Luna one-way.
- **Specialty labor** — increasingly expensive in
  real terms. Highly-trained professionals
  (engineers, scientists, doctors, specialty
  technicians) command premium wages.

## Notes for play

- The 2348 baseline is *generous*. Most things PCs
  want to do, technology can support.
- The *exceptions* are narratively significant: no
  AGI (so AI can't solve every problem), no FTL
  (so distance matters), no instant communication
  (so light-speed delay shapes plot), no true
  immortality (so death matters).
- A character's *technology comfort level* is a
  texture asset. Some characters are easy with
  contemporary tech; others find it overwhelming;
  others are obsolete-tech enthusiasts.
- Specific tech categories get their own entries.
  This entry is the *map*; the entries are the
  *territory*.
