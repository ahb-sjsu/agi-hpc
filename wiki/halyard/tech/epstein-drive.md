The **Epstein drive** is a magnetically-confined fusion torch —
a continuous-burn fusion reactor whose exhaust is directed
through a magnetic nozzle to produce thrust. Invented by
**Solomon Epstein** in 2285 at Tycho Station.

## Fuel + exhaust

Burns hydrogen and deuterium pellets (typically millimeter-
scale) at rates of several kilograms per second during cruise.
Exhaust velocity is high enough to enable brachistochrone
transits at non-trivial acceleration.

## Acceleration profiles

- **Cruise:** 0.3 g. Standard merchant / science.
- **Push:** 1.0 g. Urgent / military standard.
- **Hard burn:** 2.0–4.0 g. Combat or emergency. Juice required.
- **Maximum:** 6.0+ g. Experimental / desperate.

## Brachistochrone transit

Accelerate half-way, flip, decelerate half-way. Sample transits
at 0.3 g cruise:

| Route                      | Transit         |
|----------------------------|-----------------|
| Luna → Mars                | ~3 weeks        |
| Luna → Ceres               | ~4–6 weeks      |
| Ceres → Jovian             | ~6–8 weeks      |
| Inner → Saturn             | ~3–4 months     |
| Any → Neptune              | ~5–6 months     |
| Neptune → 80+ AU TNO       | ~6 months extra |

## Limits

- **Reaction mass** — fuel isn't the bottleneck; reaction mass
  is. Long voyages carry enormous tankage.
- **Heat management** — fusion drives run hot. Thermal radiators
  are a visible design element.
- **Drive-cone maintenance** — radiation-hot; only serviced in
  dry-dock.
- **Flip** — midpoint flip requires zero-g for minutes;
  disorients first-time passengers.

## Class certifications

- **Class I** — restricted to specific drive models; entry
  level.
- **Class II** — in-field maintenance on commercial Epstein
  drives; 8+ years logged drive time. Ship's Engineer slot on
  the *Halyard* is Class II.
- **Class III** — dry-dock-level, authorized for drive-cone
  work.
