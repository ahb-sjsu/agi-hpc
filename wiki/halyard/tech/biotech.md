# Biotechnology in 2348

Biotechnology in 2348 is *substantially mature*. The
technology has delivered most of what 2025 hoped for
in routine medical applications — gene therapy is
clinical, synthetic organs are routine, longevity
treatments substantially extend healthy lifespan,
agricultural biotech has stabilized food supplies
across Earth and increasingly off-world. What 2025
hoped for in *transformative* biotechnology — true
genetic immortality, the abolition of disease, full
human enhancement — has not arrived; the political
and biological constraints have been substantial.

This entry covers the major contemporary biotech
categories. Specific medical applications are
covered in `tech/medical-tech.md`; regenerative
medicine in `tech/regrowth-gel.md`.

## Gene therapy

The dominant therapeutic biotech of 2348:

### Routine clinical applications

- **Single-gene disorders** — most are now
  treatable. Cystic fibrosis, sickle-cell
  disease, hemophilia, Huntington's, many others
  — substantially manageable through gene therapy
- **Cancer therapy** — cancer-specific gene-
  therapy approaches are substantial; many
  cancers respond to targeted therapy
- **Immunological conditions** — gene therapy is
  used for substantial autoimmune conditions
- **Metabolic disorders** — many treatable
- **Some psychiatric conditions** — limited but
  developing

### Specialty applications

- **Pharmacogenomic-customization** — drugs are
  customized for individual patients based on
  their genetic profile; substantial application
- **Pre-pregnancy genetic counseling** — and the
  related selective-implantation procedures
  during in-vitro fertilization
- **Specific enhancement applications** —
  legally restricted; technologically possible

### Regulatory framework

- **Therapeutic gene therapy** — universally
  permitted
- **Genetic enhancement** — restricted under the
  2298 Geneva Genetic Convention
- **Germ-line modification** — heavily
  restricted; legal only for medical
  necessity

## Synthetic organs

Substantial capability:

- **Heart** — synthetic hearts are routine;
  substantially better than 2025 mechanical
  hearts; functional equivalence to natural
  in most measures
- **Kidney** — substantial; synthetic kidneys
  are routine for end-stage renal failure
- **Liver** — substantial; synthetic livers
  for advanced liver disease
- **Pancreas** — substantial
- **Lungs** — substantial
- **Bone marrow** — substantial; for various
  blood disorders
- **Specialty organs** — including substantial
  capability for specific endocrine and
  immunological applications

### Biological organs

In addition to synthetic options, *biologically-
grown* organs are increasingly available:

- Grown from patient cells — substantial
  immunological compatibility
- Custom-fitted to patient anatomy
- Full functional integration

The choice between synthetic and biological organs
typically depends on cost, availability, and patient
preference. Synthetic options are typically less
expensive and have shorter wait times; biological
options have better long-term outcomes in many
cases.

## Longevity treatments

The major contemporary biotech achievement: extending
healthy lifespan. Specifics:

### What's available

- **Cellular-aging interventions** — various
  approaches that slow cellular senescence;
  substantial effect on quality-of-life-after-90
- **Telomere maintenance** — limited; specific
  applications
- **Mitochondrial maintenance** — substantial
- **Stem-cell renewal** — substantial
- **Tissue rejuvenation** — substantial in
  selected tissues
- **Specific anti-aging drugs** — substantial
  pharmaceutical category

### Effects

- **Median lifespan** in advanced economies:
  100-110 years
- **Quality of life through 90** —
  substantially better than 2025 80-year
  baseline
- **Cognitive maintenance** — substantially
  improved; cognitive decline begins
  significantly later
- **Physical maintenance** — substantial
  exercise tolerance and general fitness can
  be maintained into 80s-90s

### Limitations

- **Maximum lifespan** — has not extended past
  approximately 130 years in the most successful
  cases
- **Late-life decline** — still occurs; the
  *quality-of-life-forever* dream has not
  materialized
- **Cost** — the most aggressive longevity
  treatments are expensive
- **Class disparity** — longevity outcomes are
  better for the wealthy; this is a
  political-cultural issue

## Agricultural biotechnology

Substantially mature:

- **Genetically-engineered crops** — universal in
  agricultural production
- **Pest-and-disease-resistant varieties** —
  universal
- **Climate-adapted varieties** — substantial
  development for climate change adaptation
- **Off-world-adapted varieties** — substantial
  for Mars, Belt-station, and Lunar agriculture
- **Synthetic meat and dairy** — substantial
  production; complementary to traditional
  agriculture rather than replacing it

### Specific developments

- **Cold-tolerant crops** for Mars
- **Low-light-tolerant crops** for Lunar and
  station agriculture
- **High-yield protein production** for
  dense-population feeding
- **Pharmaceutical-producing crops** for some
  applications

## Cellular agriculture

Synthetic-meat-equivalent and beyond:

- **Cultured meat** — substantial production;
  cost-competitive with traditional meat in most
  markets
- **Cultured dairy** — substantial
- **Specialty applications** — including
  off-world food production where traditional
  agriculture is impossible

## Pharmaceutical biotechnology

Substantial:

- **Monoclonal antibodies** — substantial
- **Cell-based therapies** — substantial
- **Gene-therapy delivery** — substantial
- **Personalized medicine** — substantial
- **AI-designed drugs** — substantial; AI tools
  designing custom drugs for specific applications

## Industrial biotechnology

Smaller but real:

- **Bio-based materials** — substantial; some
  industrial materials are now bio-produced
- **Bioremediation** — substantial; biological
  cleanup of contamination
- **Specialty industrial applications** —
  various

## Microbiome management

The *microbiome era* of medicine has matured:

- **Therapeutic microbiome management** — many
  conditions are treated through microbiome
  intervention
- **Pre-and-probiotic-supplementation** —
  substantial routine practice
- **Specific microbiome therapies** — for
  specialized conditions

## What's restricted

Some biotech is technologically possible but
regulatorily forbidden:

- **Genetic enhancement of humans** — beyond
  therapy
- **Germline modification** — except for
  medical necessity
- **Aggressive engineering of organisms**
  with potential ecological impact
- **Bioweapons** — heavily controlled; offensive
  bioweapons development is internationally
  prohibited

## What's unavailable

Despite occasional 2025 hopes:

- **Genetic immortality** — has not been
  achieved
- **Major organ regeneration outside specific
  applications** — substantial limits remain
- **Whole-brain regeneration** — not possible;
  brain tissue cannot be regrown
- **Re-personalization (consciousness
  transfer)** — not human technology

## Aboard the *Halyard*

The *Halyard* has substantial biotech capability
through the medical bay. Specific capabilities:

- **Pharmaceutical synthesis** — limited;
  for emergency applications
- **Cell-culture facility** — limited; for
  certain regenerative applications
- **Gene-therapy capability** — limited; the
  bay can perform some gene-therapy
  procedures, but most require inner-system
  consultation and substantial preparation
- **Microbiome-management capability** —
  substantial; routine microbiome care is
  standard
- **Diagnostic biotech** — substantial;
  comprehensive

The Medical Officer (whether PC or NPC) is
expected to handle most biotech-related medical
care. Specialist consultations are available
through telemedicine but with substantial
light-speed delay.

## Notes for play

- **Biotech-related capabilities are
  substantial**. PCs with biotech backgrounds
  bring useful knowledge.
- **Longevity treatments are common** but not
  universal. Most senior NPCs in the campaign
  have had some longevity treatment; the
  effects shape their behavior and aesthetics.
- **The longevity-class disparity** is a
  background social fact. Wealthy NPCs may
  appear distinctively younger than their
  ages suggest; working-class NPCs may not.
