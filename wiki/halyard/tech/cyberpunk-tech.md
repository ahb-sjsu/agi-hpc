# Underground / cyberpunk technology

(*ARTEMIS and SIGMA-4 visibility set to ``unknown``
— the underground tech category is not appropriate
for either AI to discuss in detail. Both AIs know
the category exists; specific knowledge is
classified or simply outside their training
allocations.*)

The campaign's *underground / cyberpunk* technology
category is the *off-record* tech that operates
outside the legitimate frameworks discussed in
other entries. This includes black-market
modifications, illegal implants, off-record
networks, and the various unsanctioned
technological practices that exist in every
sufficiently complex civilization.

The campaign's tonal-baseline is *hard-SF + cosmic
horror*; the cyberpunk register is a *quarantined*
side-stream rather than the primary tone. This
entry exists to map what's available without
making the underground tech the campaign's
defining aesthetic.

## What's in the underground

### Restricted implants

- **Performance-enhancement implants** above
  legal thresholds — substantial black-market
  for athletic, military, or specialty
  applications
- **Memory-enhancement implants** — restricted
  for civilian use; substantial black-market
- **Reflex-enhancement implants** — restricted;
  substantial black-market
- **Specialty illegal implants** — including
  weapons-integration implants, surveillance-
  capability implants, etc.

### Restricted weapons

- **Modified firearms** — beyond legal
  configurations
- **Custom weapons** — fabricated outside
  the legitimate firearms-manufacturing
  framework
- **Restricted ammunition** — armor-piercing,
  specialty rounds
- **EMP weapons** at scales beyond legal
  consumer access
- **Specialty illegal weapons** — including
  certain biological-and-chemical agents

### Off-record networks

- **Black-market networks** — for trade in
  restricted goods and services
- **Illicit communication networks** —
  encrypted networks operating outside
  legitimate frameworks
- **Specialty information networks** —
  for sensitive information trade
- **Cryptographic black-market** — including
  specialty cryptographic services for
  off-record activities

### Unsanctioned biotechnology

- **Underground gene-therapy** — therapy
  conducted outside legitimate medical
  frameworks
- **Restricted enhancement biotech** —
  performance enhancement above legal limits
- **Specialty illegal biotech** — including
  some experimental and unauthorized
  applications

### Identity and authentication forgery

- **False identity infrastructure** — for
  evading legitimate identification
  frameworks
- **Authentication-forgery tools** — for
  creating false credentials
- **Specialty identity-laundering services**

### Specialty cyberpunk infrastructure

- **Off-grid living** — substantially
  difficult given the comprehensive
  infrastructure of 2348, but possible for
  sufficiently determined individuals
- **Information-warfare tools** — for
  unauthorized cyber operations
- **Specialty surveillance-evasion** — for
  individuals seeking to operate outside
  legitimate frameworks

## Where it operates

### Earth

The underground operates everywhere on Earth but
is concentrated in:

- **Specific city districts** — where local
  authorities are weak
- **Off-record infrastructure** — including
  some areas where formal infrastructure has
  collapsed
- **Specialty markets** — wherever black-
  markets have established themselves

### Mars

The underground is *smaller* on Mars than on
Earth — the Republic's republican civic culture
and the smaller population make black-markets
more visible and more easily disrupted. But
substantial underground activity exists,
particularly:

- **In Tharsis cities** — where industrial-
  workforce demand for performance-enhancement
  produces market pressure
- **In Pavonis** — where the Foreign Quarter
  and the cosmopolitan mix produce off-
  record opportunities
- **In Hellas** — substantially smaller; the
  political-class density produces stronger
  enforcement

### Belt

The underground is *substantial* in the Belt:

- **Spinner culture** — substantial off-record
  infrastructure
- **Outer Belt** — where regulatory enforcement
  is weakest
- **Specialty stations** — some Belt stations
  have substantial black-market presence

### Outer system

The underground is *limited* but real:

- **Saturnian system** — small but present
- **Trans-Neptunian** — substantially limited
  by the population's small scale and
  professional concentration

## Major actors

### Organized crime

The major Earth criminal organizations have
adapted to 2348's regulatory environment:

- **Confederation crime syndicates** — substantial
- **European-Russian organized crime** —
  substantial despite reduced demographic
  base
- **Asian organized crime** — substantial;
  diverse
- **Latin American crime organizations** —
  substantial
- **African organized crime** — relatively
  smaller but real

The Belt has its own organized-crime ecology,
substantially more dispersed than Earth's.

### Specialty criminal services

- **Smuggling networks** — for moving restricted
  goods and people across jurisdictions
- **Money-laundering networks** — for
  obscuring financial flows
- **Specialty services** — including
  cyber-crime services, identity-forgery
  services, etc.

### Hacktivist groups

Beyond ordinary crime:

- **Politically-motivated hacktivists** —
  substantial activity on most major issues
- **Specialty hacking groups** — for
  specific causes or grudges
- **Limited-scale specialty groups** — for
  niche interests

## The Hollow Hand context

The *Hollow Hand* (covered in `mythos/the-hollow-
hand.md`) is *not* a cyberpunk-tech organization
in the standard sense. The Hollow Hand operates
through legitimate-seeming infrastructure with
selectively-deployed underground capabilities for
specific operations. They use cyberpunk-tech
*tools* but are not a cyberpunk-tech *organization*.

## The *Halyard* context

The *Halyard*'s standing crew has *limited*
documented underground-tech connections:

- **Halverson** — likely has *substantial*
  off-record networks through her Chamber
  affiliations; the specifics are not in any
  public record
- **Cross** — has *substantial* off-record
  networks through his FSec and Hollow Hand
  careers
- **Most other crew** — limited or no
  documented underground connections

Aboard ship: there is *no significant* underground-
tech inventory. The *Halyard* operates as a
legitimate scientific vessel; the underground-tech
operations of specific crew members are conducted
through their personal resources rather than the
ship's facilities.

## Notes for play

- **The underground exists** but is *not*
  the campaign's primary aesthetic. Use it
  selectively.
- **Cross and Halverson have access** to
  underground resources their public profile
  doesn't suggest. This is plot-relevant.
- **PC backstories** with underground-tech
  connections are *plausible* but should be
  discussed with the Keeper at Session 0.
  The cyberpunk register is a *side-stream*,
  not the campaign's default register.
- **Don't introduce cyberpunk-tech as a
  primary plot driver**. The campaign's
  primary plot is cosmic-horror; cyberpunk-
  tech is *texture*.
- ARTEMIS and SIGMA-4 will deflect on
  underground-tech-related queries.
