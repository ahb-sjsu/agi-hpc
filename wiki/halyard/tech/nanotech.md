# Nanotechnology in 2348

Nanotechnology in 2348 has matured *substantially*
in industrial applications and *barely at all* in
the *Drexlerian* (universal molecular assembler)
sense that twenty-first-century futurists hoped for.
This pattern is widely understood: real nanotech
delivers real but constrained capability; the
transformative-nanotech dream of 2025 was an
overestimate.

The technology that exists is *industrially
significant* and *medically useful* but not
*world-changing* in the way 2025 imagined.

## What's mature

### Industrial nanotech

- **Nanoscale-precision manufacturing** —
  substantial. Many industrial processes use
  nanoscale-precision tools.
- **Nanostructured materials** — substantial.
  Carbon-nanotube composites, nanostructured
  metals, programmable surfaces.
- **Catalysts** — substantial nanoscale
  catalyst engineering for chemical industry
- **Specialty surfaces** — including:
  - Self-cleaning surfaces
  - Anti-icing surfaces
  - Specific optical and electromagnetic
    properties
  - Programmable-color surfaces (for
    aesthetic applications)
- **Nanoscale electronics** — substantial;
  modern electronics use nanoscale features
  routinely

### Medical nanotech

- **Targeted drug delivery** — substantial.
  Nanoscale carriers deliver pharmaceuticals
  to specific tissues or cells.
- **Imaging contrast agents** — substantial
- **Specific therapeutic nanostructures** —
  for selected conditions
- **Diagnostic nanotech** — substantial
  capability for detecting disease markers

### Environmental nanotech

- **Water purification** — substantial use of
  nanostructured filters
- **Air purification** — substantial
- **Bioremediation enhancement** —
  substantial

## What's limited

The *Drexlerian* dream — universal molecular
assemblers, the ability to build arbitrary
materials from atomic feedstock through nanoscale
manufacturing — has *not* materialized. Specific
limits:

- **No general-purpose molecular assembler**
  exists
- **No "nanobots"** in the science-fiction
  sense — autonomous nanoscale robots
  performing complex tasks
- **No "grey goo"** scenarios — uncontrolled
  self-replicating nanotech
- **No nanoscale assembly** of complex
  structures from arbitrary feedstock

The reasons are technical: thermodynamic
constraints, energy requirements, error correction
in assembly processes, the fundamental physics of
manipulating individual atoms. Real nanotech
operates in *constrained domains* where the
physical conditions allow specific capabilities;
universal capability is, on current understanding,
unattainable.

## What's politically restricted

In addition to technical limits, some nanotech is
*regulatorily restricted*:

- **Aggressive self-replication** — any
  nanotech with substantial self-replication
  capability is heavily controlled
- **Targeted biological-agent delivery** —
  potentially weaponizable; tightly
  controlled
- **Substantial autonomous nanotech** — any
  nanotech with substantial decision-making
  capability is restricted

## Aboard the *Halyard*

The *Halyard*'s nanotech capabilities are
*standard for a deep-range science vessel*:

- **Surface treatments** — the ship's hull and
  interior surfaces use various nanostructured
  coatings (anti-microbial, anti-corrosion,
  specific optical properties)
- **Materials** — the ship's structural
  materials use nanostructured composites
- **Medical nanotech** — the medical bay has
  nanotech-based diagnostic and therapeutic
  capabilities
- **Specialty applications** — various
  research-grade nanotech tools for
  scientific applications

The ship does *not* carry:

- Any military-grade nanotech weapons
- Substantial nanoscale manufacturing
  capability
- Any restricted nanotech categories

## Specialty research applications

Halverson's research equipment includes some
specialty nanoscale tools:

- **High-resolution sample analysis** —
  substantial; the ship's mass-spectrometer
  and related instruments use nanoscale
  capability
- **Specialty sensor arrays** — including
  some that incorporate nanoscale-precision
  detectors
- **Specialty preparation tools** — for
  preparing biological and material samples
  for analysis

These are *normal research-grade equipment* by
2348 standards; nothing exotic.

## Notes for play

- **Nanotech is not a plot-driver** in the
  campaign. The technology serves practical
  applications without being narratively
  central.
- **PC characters with nanotech backgrounds**
  bring substantial materials-science or
  manufacturing-science knowledge.
- **Mythos-tech** (covered separately) is
  outside the human nanotech category. Elder
  Thing artifacts use what *might* look like
  nanotech but operates on principles humans
  don't fully understand.
- **Science-fiction "nanobots"** — players
  who reach for the SF-cliché concept will
  find that 2348 reality is more constrained.
  The campaign respects the technical limits.
