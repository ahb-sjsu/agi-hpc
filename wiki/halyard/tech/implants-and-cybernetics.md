# Implants and cybernetics

Implants and cybernetic augmentation in 2348 are
*substantial* but *regulatorily controlled*. The
technology has matured well past 2025 capabilities,
but the *transhumanist* future of pervasive cybernetic
augmentation has *not* materialized — instead, society
has developed regulatory frameworks that allow
specific medical and operational applications while
restricting more aggressive enhancement.

The campaign's PCs are unlikely to have substantial
cybernetic augmentation by default, but specific
implants are routine and a Keeper-relevant subset of
PC backstories may include them.

## Implant categories

### Medical implants

The largest category by volume. Universally available
and largely uncontroversial:

- **Cochlear implants** — for hearing restoration
- **Retinal implants** — for vision restoration in
  some conditions
- **Cardiac implants** — pacemakers, defibrillators,
  cardiac assist devices
- **Neural implants for therapy** — for treating
  Parkinson's, epilepsy, certain depressions, some
  pain conditions
- **Replacement organs** — biological or synthetic
- **Joint replacements** — substantial; most
  arthritis is now managed with replacement
- **Spinal implants** — for certain back conditions
- **Continuous-monitoring implants** — sensors that
  track health markers continuously; substantial
  population usage

### Sensory enhancement

Beyond medical replacement:

- **Enhanced vision implants** — improved acuity,
  spectral range, low-light performance; legal
  in most jurisdictions for civilian use with
  licensing
- **Enhanced hearing implants** — improved
  acuity, frequency range; legal in most
  jurisdictions
- **Smell-and-taste enhancement** — limited
  consumer market
- **Pain-management implants** — for chronic
  pain; substantial
- **Fatigue-management implants** — for
  sustained operations; restricted to specific
  professional applications

### Communication implants

- **Subvocalization detectors** — implants that
  read subvocal speech for silent
  communication; substantial military and
  intelligence-service use; restricted in most
  civilian applications
- **Direct-link communication** — implants that
  enable handheld-equivalent communication
  through neural interface; substantial military
  and high-end professional use; expensive
- **Brain-computer-interface (limited)** — for
  specific applications; substantially restricted
  in most jurisdictions

### Performance enhancement

Restricted in most jurisdictions:

- **Reflex enhancement** — neural implants that
  improve response time; legally restricted;
  some military applications
- **Memory enhancement** — implants supporting
  improved memory; substantially restricted;
  some military and intelligence applications
- **Strength augmentation** — substantial
  prosthetic-class augmentation; legally
  restricted for civilians
- **Endurance enhancement** — substantial
  metabolic-modulation implants; legally
  restricted

## Regulatory framework

### The 2298 Geneva Genetic Convention

The international framework establishing minimum
standards for implant and cybernetic regulation:

- Therapeutic applications are universally
  permitted
- Enhancement applications above specified
  thresholds require formal approval
- Specific applications (neural-interface above
  certain bandwidths, performance-enhancement
  above certain levels) are restricted
- Certain applications (genetic alteration,
  brain-computer-interface beyond therapy) are
  prohibited for civilians

### Domain-specific regulation

- **Medical practice** — medical implants are
  regulated as medical devices; substantial
  regulatory oversight
- **Military regulation** — military forces have
  specific authorities for performance-
  enhancement implants for service members
- **Civilian-professional regulation** — specific
  professional categories (some research, some
  intelligence work) have specific authorities
  for relevant implants

## Common applications

### Universal or near-universal

- **Identity implants** — many adults have
  cryptographic identity implants for
  authentication and access control
- **Health-monitoring implants** — many adults
  have continuous-monitoring implants
- **Communications backup** — some adults have
  basic-level communication implants

### Specific professional categories

- **Medical professionals** often have
  specific diagnostic or surgical-aid implants
- **Engineering professionals** sometimes have
  specific sensor or computational implants
- **Military and intelligence professionals**
  may have substantial performance-enhancement
  implants under their service authorities
- **Research professionals** may have
  specialty implants under research
  authorities

### Specialty applications

- **Athletes** — limited; most professional
  athletic competitions prohibit substantial
  enhancement implants
- **Performers** — some professional
  performers (musicians, dancers) have
  specialty performance-enhancement implants
- **Specialty workers** — some specialty
  professional categories (deep-range
  workers, EVA specialists) have specific
  enhancement implants

## The *Halyard*'s crew

Most of the *Halyard*'s crew have *some* implants:

- **Identity implants** — universal
- **Health-monitoring implants** — universal
- **Communications implants** — common; most
  crew have basic-level implants
- **Specialty implants** — variable by role and
  background

Specific crew with substantial implants:

- **Captain Marsh** — UNN-service-grade neural
  interface implants for emergency-coordination
  capability; substantial but not extreme
- **Halverson** — research-grade implants
  including substantial direct-link capability
  with ARTEMIS; the implants are visible in
  her medical records but their specific
  capabilities are partially classified
- **Cross** — multiple specialty implants
  appropriate to his FSec/Hollow Hand
  background; performance-enhancement and
  communication implants
- **Specialty crew** (Engineer, Surface Ops)
  may have profession-specific implants

## Cybernetic limbs and prosthetics

For amputation cases:

- **Biological prosthetics** — grown from
  patient cells; full integration; functional
  equivalence to natural limb in many cases
- **Synthetic prosthetics** — substantial
  capability; some applications are
  *enhanced* relative to biological
- **Exoskeleton-class prosthetics** — for
  specific operational applications; legally
  controlled

## The cyberpunk-tech category

This entry covers the *legal* implant landscape.
The *cyberpunk-tech* entry (covered separately)
addresses the underground market for restricted
or illegal implants — a different, more contested
category.

## Notes for play

- **Most PCs have implants** of some kind. The
  routine implants (identity, health-monitoring,
  basic communication) are part of the
  contemporary technological baseline.
- **Substantial implants** are character textures.
  A PC with substantial enhancement implants
  has distinctive abilities and substantial
  visibility to interested parties.
- **Implant-related plot devices** are useful
  Keeper resources. The fact of a PC's implants
  may matter at specific narrative beats —
  authentication, communication, performance
  enhancement, medical complications.
- **Halverson's ARTEMIS-link implants** are a
  specifically Keeper-relevant detail. The
  campaign's late acts may engage with this.
