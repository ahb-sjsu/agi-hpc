## The MCAL — personal handheld

Everyone carries a **Multi-Comms Analytical Handheld**. Flexible
display, voice + gesture input, modest sensor suite (chemical
sniffer, radiological counter, temperature, pressure).
Civilian-grade 600–2,500 UN credits; pro models 4,000–8,000.

MCAL functions:
- Communications (mesh, ship intercom, tight-beam interface).
- Logging, reference library, light sensors.
- Personal AI assistant — scheduling, routine queries, moderate
  natural-language interaction.

Dr. Halverson's MCAL hosts **ARTEMIS**. Crew MCALs are M-K issue,
middle-range.

## Ship systems

A ship's systems are a networked ensemble of specialized
controllers coordinated by a central **ship-mind**. Access is
terminal-based throughout the hull. SIGMA-4 is the *Halyard*'s
ship-mind; see [crew/sigma-4].

## Expert-system AIs

**There is no general artificial intelligence in 2348 in the
science-fiction sense.** Expert systems handle narrow domains
with high competence and fail notably outside. They have
characteristic personalities — usually because they have
accumulated long operating histories — but they are not
self-aware.

Common classes:

- **Ship-minds** (e.g. SIGMA-4) — large, multi-domain.
- **Research assistants** (e.g. ARTEMIS) — specialized.
- **Personal assistants** — MCAL-embedded, broad and shallow.
- **Industrial controllers** — narrow, deep, invisible to users.

## What AIs can do

Handle conversational queries in domain, pattern-match and
routine inference, maintain and retrieve long operational logs.

## What AIs can't do

Act outside their domain; self-modify substantively; produce
genuinely novel creative work; **lie.** An AI in 2348 can decline
to answer or answer narrowly, but cannot state a falsehood. This
is a design invariant, heavily audited.

## AI drift

Long-running AIs develop **drift** — characteristic response
patterns, preferences, quirks accumulated from operating history.
Drift is not intentionality, but it is readable — an old ship-
mind is, in practical terms, a colleague.
