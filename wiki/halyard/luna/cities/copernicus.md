# Copernicus

Copernicus is Luna's cultural and educational capital,
the home of the University of Luna's principal campus
and the Lunar Symphony's secondary venue. The 16 million
Copernicus residents inhabit the largest near-face
Lunar crater (96 km diameter, 3.8 km deep) and its
surrounding hinterland, in a city whose character is
distinctly different from any other major Lunar
settlement.

Where Tycho is political and Aristarchus is industrial,
Copernicus is *educational-cultural*. The University of
Luna shapes much of the city's life; the Lunar Temple
Buddhism's principal monastery is here; the literary,
artistic, and contemplative traditions of the Lunar
Republic are anchored to a substantial degree in
Copernicus.

## Site

Copernicus sits at the Copernicus impact crater on Luna's
near-face. The crater is the most prominent single
near-face feature visible from Earth; observers on Earth
can see the bright ray system extending from the crater
across the mare.

The crater's geometry has been used for the city's
construction in a pattern that draws on both Tycho's
political-monumental design and Aristarchus's industrial
practicality. The central peak hosts the University of
Luna's principal academic complex; the crater walls
host major cultural and residential development; the
crater floor hosts working-class residential.

## Layout

### The Central Peak — University Complex

The University of Luna's principal academic buildings
are built into and around the crater's central peak. The
University's main library, the senior administrative
buildings, the major lecture halls, and the principal
faculty buildings are clustered here. Population ~1M
(students, faculty, support).

### The Crater Walls — Cultural Districts

The Lunar Symphony's secondary venue, the major museums
(the Lunar Museum of Modern Art, the Lunar Library, the
Lunar Republic's literary archive), the cultural centers,
the major bookshops and intellectual cafes. The most
prestigious residential addresses for academic and
cultural personnel. Population ~6M.

### The Crater Floor — Residential

Working-class residential, mid-level commercial, and the
extensive student-housing complexes. Population ~7M.

### The Hinterland — Including the Temple

The major monastic complex of the Lunar Temple Buddhist
tradition is in the hinterland to the east of the crater.
The complex includes the *Copernicus Monastery* (the
principal training institution for Lunar Temple monks)
and several associated retreat centers. Population ~2M.

## The University of Luna

The principal Lunar university and one of the major
research-and-teaching institutions in human space. Total
enrollment approximately 480,000 students; faculty
approximately 14,000. The University was founded 2256;
its graduates have shaped Lunar civilization
substantially.

The University's strengths:

- **Astronomy and planetary science** — the
  University's astronomical observatories use Lunar
  far-side facilities for observations not possible
  from Earth
- **Engineering** — particularly fusion engineering
  and spacecraft engineering
- **Philosophy and contemplative studies** — closely
  associated with the Lunar Temple tradition; one of
  the major centers of contemplative philosophy
- **Materials science** — substantial Lunar
  Industrial Belt cooperation
- **Political science and law** — second only to
  Tycho's University of Luna for these fields

## The Lunar Temple Monastery

The Copernicus Monastery is the principal training
institution for Lunar Temple Buddhism. The monastery's
three-decade-long training cycle produces the senior
Temple monks who lead the tradition's chapters across
human space.

The monastery's relationship with the University is
unusually close. Many University faculty are
practicing Buddhists; the contemplative-studies
program is integrated with monastic training; the
University's philosophy department draws substantially
on Buddhist thought.

The Monastery operates the *Copernicus Retreat Centers*
— several substantial facilities offering 3-day to
3-month meditative retreats. The retreats draw
substantial visitor traffic from across Luna and from
Earth and Mars.

## Politics

Copernicus's politics are *progressive-intellectual*.
The city's congressional delegation tends toward the
Republican Independence Bloc with substantial Polar
Caucus and small-party representation. Local politics
emphasize:

- University-and-research funding
- Cultural-institution support
- Environmental and ecological concerns (the city has
  one of the most active environmental movements on
  Luna)
- Skepticism of Tycho-establishment political
  arrangements

The current Mayor is Hara Vasquez-Kim, a former
University philosophy professor.

## Economy

Copernicus's economy is roughly $0.9T UN-equivalent.
Sectors:

- **Higher education** — the dominant sector; the
  University of Luna's annual budget alone is ~$84B
- **Cultural production** — the Lunar Symphony,
  publishing, art markets, the literary scene
- **Research** — substantial scientific research
  beyond the University's direct programs
- **Tourism** — the cultural and contemplative
  tourism is substantial
- **Specialty manufacturing** — high-precision
  scientific instruments

## Cultural texture

Copernicus's cultural register is *intellectual-
contemplative*:

- **The university register.** Most Copernicus
  residents have *some* relationship to the
  University. The intellectual culture, the
  vocabulary, the conversational register all
  reflect this.
- **The contemplative register.** The Lunar Temple
  presence shapes a substantial fraction of the
  city's cultural life. Meditation circles,
  contemplative discussion groups, the *seated
  minute* practice (mentioned in
  `earth/culture/religion-2348.md`) are
  widespread.
- **The progressive register.** Copernicus is the
  political progressive heartland of Luna.
- **The crater-and-far-side register.** Copernicus
  has unusual access to Luna's far-side
  astronomical infrastructure; the cultural
  production engages with deep-space astronomy as
  a major theme.

## Notes for play

- A Copernicus PC will have an *intellectual-
  contemplative-progressive* register that
  distinguishes them from Tycho or Aristarchus
  PCs.
- The University background is natural; Copernicus
  PCs default to academic, cultural, or
  contemplative backstories.
- The Lunar Temple Buddhist background is one of
  the more distinctive religious-tradition backstories
  available; Copernicus is the natural home for it.
