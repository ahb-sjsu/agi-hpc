# Aristarchus

Aristarchus is the largest of Luna's purely industrial
cities, the principal lithium-and-rare-earth processing
center for human space, and one of the major nodes in
the Lunar Industrial Belt. The 19 million Aristarchus
residents live and work around the Aristarchus Crater
on Luna's western near-face — a city whose entire
identity is shaped by its industrial mission.

The city's character is *industrial-working-class*. Where
Tycho's cultural register is political-cosmopolitan,
Aristarchus's is engineering-blue-collar. The city is
proud of being the *workshop* of the Republic.

## Site

Aristarchus sits at the Aristarchus impact crater on
Luna's western near-face hemisphere. The crater is 40 km
in diameter and 3.7 km deep; like Tycho, the crater
geometry has been used to advantage in the city's
construction.

The site's industrial significance is geological:
Aristarchus and its surrounding region contain unusual
concentrations of:

- **Lithium-bearing minerals** — the Aristarchus
  region is the largest single lithium source in
  human space
- **Rare-earth elements** — substantial deposits in
  the crater's deeper geology
- **Specialty silicates** — used in fusion-component
  manufacturing
- **Helium-3** — substantial near-surface deposits in
  the regional regolith

## Layout

### The Crater

The original 2227-2270s industrial development. Most of
the city's principal refineries, processing plants, and
heavy-industrial facilities. Population ~4M (industrial
workers and immediate families).

### The Inner Ring

The first major residential expansion (2270s-2310s).
Mid-rise vertical districts, working-class to middle-
class. The largest fraction of Aristarchus's
population. Population ~10M.

### The Outer Ring

Recent expansion (2310s-2340s). Lower-density residential
and commercial; the city's growth zone. Population ~5M.

## The industrial complex

Aristarchus's industrial complex is one of the most
sophisticated in human space:

- **The Aristarchus Lithium Authority** — operates the
  city's largest single industrial facility; processes
  approximately 60% of human space's lithium
- **The Western Belt Refinery Consortium** — the
  rare-earth processing operation
- **The Aristarchus Helium-3 Extraction Network** —
  surface-and-near-surface helium-3 mining
- **Specialized manufacturing** — fusion components,
  spacecraft batteries, precision instruments

The work is industrial. Aristarchus has the highest
fraction of *manual-and-technical* workers of any major
Lunar city; the population skews toward engineers,
technicians, machine operators, and skilled-trade
workers.

## Politics

Aristarchus's politics are *labor-progressive*. The
city's congressional delegation tends toward the
Republican Independence Bloc (the Assembly's progressive
opposition); some delegations have included
representation from smaller labor-affiliated parties.

The current Mayor is Marisol Patel-Brennt, a former
lithium-extraction engineer. The city's political
register is *labor-oriented, technically literate,
suspicious of cosmopolitan-establishment Tycho*.

## Economy

Aristarchus's economy is roughly $1.6T UN-equivalent —
larger than any other Lunar city except Tycho.
Sectors:

- **Industrial processing and manufacturing** —
  dominant; the Lithium Authority alone employs
  approximately 280,000 people
- **Mining** — substantial; both Aristarchus-region
  and imported asteroid-belt material
- **Heavy industry** — fusion components, spacecraft
  manufacturing, large-volume specialty chemistry
- **Logistics** — Aristarchus is a major Lunar
  Industrial Belt freight node

## Cultural texture

Aristarchus's cultural register is *industrial-Lunar*:

- **The labor-pride register.** Aristarchus residents
  take pride in the city's industrial role. The
  cultural production celebrates work, makers,
  the *making* of things.
- **The technical register.** Aristarchus's literary,
  artistic, and musical production has unusually high
  technical literacy. Engineering vocabulary appears
  in everyday speech; the cultural reference base
  includes substantial industrial-process material.
- **The Tycho-skeptical register.** Aristarchus
  residents view the political-cosmopolitan culture
  of Tycho with mild skepticism. The cultural
  identity is *we make things; they argue about
  things*.
- **The crater-aesthetic.** Like Tycho, Aristarchus's
  crater geometry shapes the city's visual culture.

## Notes for play

- An Aristarchus PC has access to *industrial-labor-
  Lunar* register that no other Luna-PC option
  provides.
- The technical-engineering background is natural;
  Aristarchus PCs default to engineering, technical,
  or trade backgrounds.
- The labor-political register makes Aristarchus
  PCs natural fits for Republican Independence Bloc-
  aligned political positions if the campaign
  engages with Lunar internal politics.
