# Kepler-Reiner

Kepler-Reiner is Luna's principal port city, the
operational center of the Lunar Republic's merchant
marine, and the moon's busiest passenger-and-cargo
transit hub. The 14 million Kepler-Reiner residents
live and work around the rim and floor of the
Kepler-Reiner crater complex, in a city whose entire
economic existence depends on the long-distance trade
flows that pass through Lunar space.

The city's character is *commercial-merchant*. Where
Tycho is political and Copernicus is intellectual,
Kepler-Reiner is *trading*. The Lunar merchant marine
unions, the major shipping companies, the Earth-Luna-
Mars passenger services, and the substantial freight
operations are concentrated here.

## Site

Kepler-Reiner is built around the Kepler crater complex
on Luna's near-face equatorial region. The site was
chosen in the 2240s for the equatorial position
(advantageous for orbital launch and rendezvous), the
crater geometry (useful for terminal infrastructure),
and the proximity to existing Earth-Luna passenger
services that had been operating from the region since
the 2210s.

The city's port infrastructure includes:

- **Surface launch facilities** — for vehicles
  carrying passengers and cargo to and from Lunar
  orbit
- **Sub-surface terminal complexes** — passenger
  processing, cargo handling, customs operations
- **Orbital docking infrastructure** — Kepler-Reiner
  has its own orbital station network handling the
  larger ships that don't land surface-direct
- **The Kepler-Reiner Orbital** — the largest single
  Lunar orbital station, with about 80,000
  permanent residents

## Layout

### The Port District

The principal port-and-transit district. Surface
launch facilities, terminal buildings, customs
operations, the major shipping companies' offices.
Population ~3M.

### The Port-Adjacent District

Hospitality, services, the *Foreign Quarter* of
visiting Earth, Mars, and Belt traders. Hotels,
restaurants, cultural venues catering to transit
populations. Population ~5M.

### The Residential Districts

Working-class to middle-class residential. The largest
fraction of Kepler-Reiner's population. Population ~5M.

### The Hinterland

Industrial-and-supply infrastructure for the port
operations. Population ~1M.

## Operations

Kepler-Reiner's port handles approximately:

- **80% of Earth-Luna passenger traffic** — most
  Earth-Luna shuttles arrive and depart from
  Kepler-Reiner; the secondary terminals (Tycho's,
  Aristarchus's) handle smaller fractions
- **60% of Lunar bulk-cargo trade** — most
  intra-system bulk freight flows through
  Kepler-Reiner
- **40% of Mars-Luna passenger traffic** — the
  Mars-Luna direct service uses Kepler-Reiner
  as its principal Luna terminal
- **70% of Belt-Luna trade** — Belt freighters
  prefer Kepler-Reiner for its specialized
  handling capacity

The merchant-marine union infrastructure is concentrated
here. Most Lunar-flagged commercial vessels list
Kepler-Reiner as their port of registry; the merchant-
marine training schools, hiring halls, and union
offices are substantial.

## Politics

Kepler-Reiner's politics are *commercial-pragmatic*. The
city's congressional delegation tends toward the Lunar
Industrial Party with strong representation from
merchant-marine-affiliated political subgroups. Local
politics emphasize:

- Continued port-infrastructure investment
- Trade-and-commerce-friendly federal policy
- Workforce-and-training programs for the merchant
  marine
- International trade-relationship management

The current Mayor is Mateo Singh-Volkov, a former
shipping-company executive.

## Economy

Kepler-Reiner's economy is roughly $1.4T UN-equivalent.
Sectors:

- **Trade and logistics** — dominant; the port's
  operations and the supporting industries
- **Merchant marine** — the dominant employer for
  many Kepler-Reiner working-class residents
- **Hospitality** — the substantial visitor flows
  generate substantial hospitality-sector
  employment
- **Light manufacturing** — port-supplying
  manufacturing
- **Government services** — customs, immigration,
  trade regulation

## Cultural texture

Kepler-Reiner's cultural register is *commercial-
cosmopolitan*:

- **The merchant register.** Most Kepler-Reiner
  residents have *some* connection to the merchant
  marine or trade industries. The cultural
  references reflect this — shipping vocabulary,
  trade-route awareness, the rhythms of commerce.
- **The cosmopolitan register.** The transit
  population gives Kepler-Reiner a steady flow of
  Earth, Mars, Belt visitors. The city's
  restaurants, cultural venues, and social life
  reflect this.
- **The pragmatic register.** Kepler-Reiner is
  *less* politically engaged than Aristarchus or
  Copernicus; the cultural register is *get the
  work done, manage the trade flows, keep the
  port running*.
- **The merchant-marine traditions.** The Lunar
  merchant marine has its own subculture — songs,
  stories, traditions, characteristic ship-names,
  the rituals of the merchant trade. This is
  Kepler-Reiner's specific cultural contribution to
  the broader Lunar identity.

## Kepler-Reiner and the *Halyard*

Kepler-Reiner is one of the *Halyard*'s likely
Earth-Luna-Mars routing options. The ship's outbound
routing did not pass through Kepler-Reiner, but a
return-leg refueling or repair stop is possible if
the campaign needs to bring the crew through a Lunar
context.

## Notes for play

- A Kepler-Reiner PC will have a *merchant-cosmopolitan-
  pragmatic* register that distinguishes them from
  other Lunar PC options.
- The merchant-marine background is natural; many
  Kepler-Reiner PCs have shipping, trade, or
  port-operations expertise.
- The cosmopolitan-trading register makes
  Kepler-Reiner PCs natural fits for outer-system
  contract work.
