# Tycho

Tycho is the political capital of the Lunar Republic, the
largest single Lunar city, and one of the more
distinctive urban environments in human space — a city
built into and around the iconic Tycho impact crater on
the moon's southern face. The 28 million Tycho residents
inhabit the crater rim, the crater floor, and the
surrounding hinterland, with the Lunar Assembly's
chambers built directly into the crater's central
peak.

The city's character is *political-cosmopolitan*. Tycho
hosts the Republic's federal government, most major
foreign embassies and trade missions, and the largest
single concentration of administrative, diplomatic, and
political-class population on the moon.

## Site

Tycho sits in the crater of the same name on Luna's
southern hemisphere. The crater itself is approximately
85 km in diameter and 4.8 km deep; the crater floor and
walls have been substantially developed over the past
two centuries.

The site has practical advantages:

- The crater walls provide natural radiation shielding
  for surface installations
- The crater's geometry is well-suited to spin-habitat
  construction
- The site is far enough from the equatorial Lunar
  Industrial Belt to be culturally and politically
  distinct

The settlement's elevation profile uses the crater's
topology: the central peak hosts the Assembly chambers
and major government buildings; the crater walls host
substantial residential and commercial development; the
crater floor hosts the working-class residential
districts.

## Layout

### The Central Peak District

The Lunar Assembly's chamber is built into the
mountain at the crater's geometric center. The Lunar
Court, the First Citizen's Residence, the major
federal ministries are clustered here. The district's
architecture is *deliberately monumental* — the Lunar
Republic's founders chose the central-peak siting to
emphasize political seriousness. Population ~1M
(government, support).

### The Crater Walls

Substantial residential and commercial development on
the inner slopes of the crater. The most prestigious
residential addresses; the major hotels; the foreign
embassies and trade missions; the high-end shopping
and cultural districts. Population ~10M.

### The Crater Floor

Residential and working-class commercial. The largest
fraction of Tycho's population. Population ~14M.

### The Hinterland

The surrounding crater-adjacent settlements;
agricultural domes, secondary commercial districts, the
Lunar Defense Service's headquarters complex. Population
~3M.

## The Lunar Assembly

The Republic's principal legislative chamber sits at
Tycho's central peak. The Assembly's design — a
unicameral body of 280 members elected by population
apportionment — was deliberate; the founders rejected
bicameralism as adding political friction the Republic
didn't need.

The Assembly meets in continuous session (300 working
days per year) in a chamber designed for political
debate rather than ceremonial display. The chamber is
relatively small for the body's size; members debate
in close quarters.

The Assembly building also houses:
- The Lunar Court (top floor; the symbolic
  architectural statement is that the Court sits
  *above* the legislature in the building's
  hierarchy)
- The Premier's offices
- The major committee rooms
- The Lunar Republic's archive

## Politics

Tycho's local politics are *establishment-centrist*.
The city's congressional delegation in the Assembly
tends toward the Lunar Industrial Party (currently in
government); the Republican Independence Bloc has
representation but is the opposition.

The current Mayor is Adisa Singh-Kovac. Tycho's local
politics are subordinate to the national-political
flows that shape the city; many political careers
that begin elsewhere on Luna culminate in Tycho.

## Economy

Tycho's economy is roughly $2.4T UN-equivalent. Sectors:

- **Government and federal services** — dominant;
  the Republic's federal government employs about
  340,000 people in Tycho directly
- **Diplomacy and international relations** — the
  city hosts most major foreign missions and the
  associated services
- **Higher education** — the University of Luna's
  Tycho campus is the principal Lunar university
  for political science, law, and international
  relations
- **Cultural production** — the Lunar Symphony
  (founded 2308) is one of human space's
  prestigious orchestras; the Tycho literary scene
  is the moon's most active
- **Tourism and hospitality** — Tycho receives
  substantial Earth-side tourism

## Cultural texture

Tycho's cultural register is *political-cosmopolitan-
established*:

- **The political-class register.** Most Tycho
  residents have *some* connection to the federal
  political establishment. The cultural references
  reflect this — political vocabulary, institutional
  awareness, the conventions of public-political
  speech.
- **The cosmopolitan-international register.** The
  diplomatic presence makes Tycho the most
  internationally varied city on Luna. Earth, Mars,
  Belt visitors are constant.
- **The crater-aesthetic.** The crater's physical
  presence shapes the city's visual culture. Tycho
  art, photography, and design engage with the
  geometry of the crater constantly.
- **The young-Republic register.** The Lunar Republic
  is younger than Mars's Republic and substantially
  younger than Earth's federal institutions. Tycho's
  political culture has a recognizably *new*
  quality — confident but not yet inheritor-
  confident.

## Tycho and the *Halyard*

No member of the *Halyard*'s standing crew is Tycho-
born. The city is a likely passing-through point for
any Lunar-born PC; many Earth-Mars-Belt travelers
stop in Tycho during their journeys.

## Notes for play

- A Tycho PC will have a *political-class* register
  that distinguishes them from other Lunar PC
  options. Civil service, political journalism,
  federal-government background are all natural.
- The cosmopolitan-international background is
  useful for PCs who need to plausibly have
  contacts across human space.
