# Martian Congressional Republic Navy — overview

![Disclosed naval tonnage, 2347 — UNN, MCRN, OPA, Lunar comparison](https://halyard.atlas-sjsu.duckdns.org/art/fleet-tonnage.svg)

The MCRN is the smallest of the major navies by tonnage and,
since approximately 2335, the most operationally effective in
the human inner system. The institutional self-image is
*outnumbered, out-financed, and out-fighting* — the smaller
republic's professionalized service against a metropole's
larger but slacker fleet. The self-image is mostly accurate.

## Origin

The MCRN was constituted on 11 January 2275 from the assets,
personnel, and command structure of the Mars Defense Force
(2244–2275). The MDF had been a UN-authorized auxiliary; in
practice it was the Council's military, and the transition to
MCRN required mostly a change of flags and oaths.

The early MCRN had perhaps 18,000 personnel and 31 hulls —
mostly ex-UNN frigates that had been transferred to MDF
authority during the 2261 Concordat. Its first major test was
the Mars-Earth War of 2279, which it survived more by Earth's
unwillingness to escalate than by its own combat capability.

The slow buildout from 2280 through 2330 emphasized:

- A professionalized officer corps with deep technical
  training
- Doctrine of force-on-force engagement (Earth-as-enemy)
- Smaller but more capable hulls
- An aggressive cultivation of the Phobos and Deimos
  industrial bases

By 2330, the MCRN was a significantly more capable per-ton
force than the UNN. The 2331 buildout was a deliberate scaling
of an organization that already worked.

## Structure

The MCRN is organized into Fleets:

### First Fleet — Inner System

The largest. Based at Phobos. Operates the deep-space carriers
(*Donnager* class), the Mars-orbit defense (*Pavonis* class
cruisers), and the cislunar deterrent. The Mars-Earth
strategic balance is, primarily, First Fleet's responsibility.

### Second Fleet — Asteroid Approaches

Based at Deimos. Operates in the Mars-Belt approach lanes.
Smaller hulls, more numerous. Most contact between the MCRN
and the Belt happens through Second Fleet, which has a
complicated and often-friendly relationship with several
OPA-aligned organizations.

### Third Fleet — Outer System

Based at Phobos with forward presence at Vesta and Ceres.
The smallest fleet. Operates frigates, corvettes, and a
single deep-range cruiser (*MCRN Lidia Volkonsky*). Third
Fleet's operations are dispersed; a Mars-flagged deep-range
contractor will encounter Third Fleet vessels twice on an
average outbound leg.

### Reserve Fleet

A standing reserve of ~120 hulls in mothballed status at
Phobos, available for full activation in 60 days. The
Reserve includes several capital-class ships dating to the
2310s that are still combat-effective.

## Tonnage and order of battle (public figures)

Per the 2347 MCR Defense White Paper:

- **Carriers / Capital ships** (*Donnager* class): 16
- **Cruisers** (*Pavonis*, *Tempest*): 87
- **Frigates and destroyers**: 312
- **Corvettes and patrol craft**: 290
- **Auxiliaries**: 410+
- **Reserve fleet**: 120 hulls

Disclosed tonnage is 9.8 million tons; effective tonnage
(including reserve) is closer to 11.4 million tons. This is
roughly 70% of the UNN's disclosed tonnage. The combat-
effectiveness multiplier (per the MCR's own assessment) is
roughly 1.4×, giving the MCRN a slight edge in a force-on-
force engagement.

Earth's UNN officer corps, internally, accepts this analysis.
The political classes on both sides do not.

## Officer culture

The MCRN officer corps is, by most measures, the most
professionalized military service in human history. Officers
are selected through:

- The **Mars Naval Academy** at Olympus (the prestige path)
- The **Phobos Engineering College** (the technical path)
- The **Republican Reserve Officer's Training Corps**
  (universities; the broadest path)

Selection is competitive: the Naval Academy admits roughly
1,400 cadets per year from a pool of 32,000 applicants. Cadet
washout rates are 28% in the first year, 12% in the second,
8% in the third, and 5% in the fourth. The graduates who
make it are, by inclination and selection, intensely capable
and exhaustingly serious.

The officer culture has three notable features:

1. **Technical depth.** Every MCRN officer holds an
   engineering specialty in addition to a command rating.
   This is unusual; UNN officers can be commanders without
   technical specialties. The MCRN considers this dangerous
   sloppiness.
2. **Republicanism.** Officers swear an oath to the
   Constitution, not to the President, the Congress, or the
   Republic-as-state. The institutional culture takes this
   seriously. Coups have been considered impossible since
   the 2280s.
3. **Civilian retirement.** A typical MCRN career runs to
   captain (about 20 years); a minority continues to flag
   rank. Most officers retire to civilian life — academic,
   industrial, political — and remain politically engaged.
   The current President is a typical example.

The career paths through MCRN service to civilian leadership
have given Mars an unusually large fraction of its political
class with military backgrounds. This is healthy in some ways
(deep institutional knowledge, civic-republican ethos) and
unhealthy in others (group-think on questions of force
posture, blind spots about Belt politics, an enduring
suspicion of Earth that is sometimes more reflexive than
analytical).

## Doctrine

MCRN doctrine is summarized as **the Three Imperatives**:

1. **Defensive parity with Earth.** The MCRN must be capable
   of fighting the UNN to a standstill in any plausible
   engagement scenario. The doctrine assumes Earth is the
   primary potential adversary and treats other threats as
   secondary.
2. **The frontier defense.** The Belt and the outer system
   must be kept stable, or at least non-hostile, so that the
   MCRN's Inner-System focus is not compromised. Second
   Fleet and Third Fleet are the instruments of this
   imperative.
3. **The civilian primacy.** The MCRN must be subordinate to
   civilian government in fact as well as form. The
   institutional culture polices this aggressively. Officers
   who appear to be drifting toward partisan politics are
   shuffled out of operational roles.

The doctrine deprioritizes coercive diplomacy. Where the UNN
sends a carrier group as a political signal, the MCRN sends
ambassadors. Where the UNN escalates by deployment, the MCRN
escalates by exercise. The asymmetry is by design.

## The Mars-Earth balance

The MCRN's analysis of the Mars-Earth balance, as of 2348,
is roughly:

- **Tonnage parity** since 2342.
- **Combat-effectiveness advantage** of roughly 1.4×.
- **Doctrine advantage** in force-on-force; doctrine
  disadvantage in long-duration coercive presence.
- **Political-strategic disadvantage**: the UN can absorb
  attrition that the MCR cannot.
- **Operational-tempo advantage**: the MCRN can sustain
  higher-intensity operations for longer.

The MCRN's senior leadership therefore believes that, in a
high-intensity short war, the MCRN wins. In a long war, Earth
wins by attrition. The political consequence: the MCRN
favors decisive resolution if conflict is forced; the UNN
favors prolonged stalemate if conflict cannot be avoided.

Both navies prefer not to fight. The political class on
both sides knows this. There has been no serious advocacy
for war, on either side, since the 2336 Belt crisis.

## The MCRN and the *Halyard*

Two of the *Halyard*'s standing crew have direct MCRN
connections:

- **Lt. Rae Kosugi**, lander pilot, is a current MCRN
  Reservist (Captain, junior grade). She has reported the
  *Halyard* contract to her reserve liaison; the report is
  in MCRN Third Fleet's files. The MCRN does not direct
  her actions on the *Halyard* but expects a debrief on
  return.
- **Maren Korvaly**, second engineer, was MCRN-trained
  (Phobos Engineering College, 2333) but resigned her
  commission in 2342 over personal disputes whose details
  are not in the public record. She remains on Mars-Belt
  shortlists for naval-engineering contract work.

The MCRN's Third Fleet maintains a sensor presence along the
*Halyard*'s outbound transit. Two Third Fleet vessels (the
**MCRN Tula** and the **MCRN Bellatrix**) will hail the
*Halyard* on the outbound leg as a courtesy. Both are
patrol corvettes with experienced commanders; both will
exchange routine pleasantries and depart.

## Notes for play

- A character with MCRN service in their backstory is
  unremarkable for a Martian PC and noteworthy for a
  non-Martian.
- The MCRN does not direct the *Halyard*. It watches.
- An MCRN officer encountered in the field (fleet
  liaison, port officer) is professional, slightly humorless,
  and exhaustingly competent. They are not enemies. They are
  also not friends.
- Mars is *not* the campaign's antagonist. The MCRN, like
  the UNN, is mostly background presence. If Mars-related
  intrigue surfaces, it surfaces in Act 4 or 5, after
  Nithon.
