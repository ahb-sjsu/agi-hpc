# The Northern Lowlands

The Northern Lowlands — the broad plains north of the
Martian dichotomy boundary — are the contemporary frontier
of Mars: the most rapidly growing demographic region, the
agricultural breadbasket of the planet, and the
cultural-political home of what most Martians recognize as
*the new Mars*. Its 160 million residents are the youngest
population on Mars by median age (32, vs. the planetary
38), the most recently arrived from inner-system
emigration, and the most actively involved in pushing the
terraforming project's frontier.

The Lowlands are *low* — most of the region sits at or
below the Martian datum. Low elevation means thicker
atmosphere; thicker atmosphere means easier outdoor life,
larger-scale agriculture, and the slow possibility of
*open habitation* that the Mars Atmospheric Authority
projects as achievable in the Northern Lowlands within
another century.

## Geography

The Northern Lowlands cover roughly 20 million square
kilometers of plain, divided into three major basins:

- **Acidalia Planitia** — the western basin, the largest.
  Approximately 9 million sq km; population 64M.
- **Utopia Planitia** — the central basin, second largest.
  Approximately 7 million sq km; population 58M.
- **Arcadia Planitia** — the eastern basin, smallest.
  Approximately 4 million sq km; population 38M.

The plains are smoother and less geologically distinct
than the highland regions, with substantial dust cover and
relatively few major surface features. The terraforming
program has, over a century, brought the Lowlands' average
atmospheric pressure to 0.45 bar and surface temperatures
that fluctuate seasonally between -18°C in winter and
+12°C in summer. The atmosphere is filter-mask-required
for outdoor activity but does not require respirators
for normal periods.

Several major *seas* are emerging in the Lowlands as the
terraforming program continues:

- **Lake Acidalia** — a managed water body in the
  western Acidalia basin, currently 280 km across at
  its widest point. Continues to expand.
- **Lake Utopia** — central Utopia basin, currently 220
  km across. Continues to expand.
- **The Arcadian Wetlands** — a complex of smaller water
  bodies and managed wetlands rather than a single
  large lake.

## Population distribution

The Lowlands' 160 million residents are concentrated:

- **Acidalia cities** — 64M total. The largest is
  **New Cydonia** (18M), the regional capital and the
  home of the Mars Atmospheric Authority's lowland
  research center. Others: Acidalia City (12M), Mareotis
  (8M), the smaller settlements.
- **Utopia cities** — 58M total. Largest: **Utopia
  Capital** (16M), a deliberately-named city that serves
  as the basin's political and cultural center.
  Others: Cebrenia (10M), Hyblaeus (8M), the smaller
  settlements.
- **Arcadia cities** — 38M total. Largest: Arcadia City
  (10M). Others: smaller, more dispersed.

Plus a substantial agricultural rural population (roughly
20% of the region's total) on greenhouse-and-open-field
farms scattered across the plains.

## The agricultural transformation

The Northern Lowlands are Mars's agricultural breadbasket.
The transformation from barren plain to net-food-exporter
has been one of the great achievements of the past
century:

- **2270s**: First experimental open-air agriculture
  attempted in southern Acidalia, with limited success
  (atmospheric pressure was still too low for most
  Earth-origin crops)
- **2300s**: Atmospheric pressure crossed thresholds
  that allowed cold-hardy Earth crops to grow with
  greenhouse cover; major expansion of greenhouse
  agriculture across the lowlands
- **2330s**: First *open-field* successful crops
  (cold-hardy varieties, mostly grains) at lowland
  elevations
- **2348**: Roughly 60% of Mars's grain production, 40%
  of vegetable production, and a substantial fraction
  of fruit production happens in the Northern Lowlands;
  the region is a *net food exporter* to other Mars
  regions

The agricultural workforce is large — perhaps 30 million
Martians are involved in agricultural production in some
form. The work is mostly highly mechanized but requires
substantial human supervision and seasonal labor.

The agricultural transformation has also created Mars's
first *rural class* — Martians who live in genuinely
small towns and farms, not in cities. This is novel for
Mars; the planet's pre-Lowland population had been
overwhelmingly urban (the rest of Mars's geography did
not support rural settlement).

## The frontier register

The Northern Lowlands have a distinctive *frontier*
cultural register. Markers:

- **Recent arrival.** Most Lowlands residents arrived in
  the past 30-50 years, many directly from Earth or
  the Belt. The cultural register is *new-arrival
  ambitious*.
- **Class mobility.** Acquiring agricultural land in the
  Lowlands has been the most accessible route to wealth
  on Mars in the past half-century. The Lowlands have
  produced, by Martian standards, a *new wealth* class
  that has substantial political weight and limited
  inherited cultural weight.
- **Pioneer mythology.** The Lowlands' cultural
  production heavily emphasizes pioneer themes —
  homesteading, frontier-building, the slow
  transformation of land. The Lowland literary
  tradition (centered on New Cydonia and Utopia Capital)
  has produced some of the Republic's most popular
  contemporary fiction.
- **Land-and-place attachment.** Lowlands residents,
  unlike most Martians, have a strong attachment to
  *specific places*. The Acidalia identity, the Utopian
  identity, the Arcadian identity are politically and
  culturally meaningful to the populations.
- **Practical religiosity.** The Lowlands have higher
  rates of religious participation than the rest of
  Mars. The Catholic Church and the Lunar Temple
  Buddhism have built substantial congregations here;
  the smaller traditions have followings as well. The
  rural-and-frontier register has historically aligned
  with religious participation.

## Politics

The Lowlands caucus in the Mars Congress has been
growing in weight since the 2310s as the population grew.
By the 2346 election, the Lowlands caucus was the
third-largest (after Tharsis and Valles) and the most
*new-power* in its political register — willing to
challenge older Hellas-Tharsis political dispositions.

The Lowlands' political profile is *populist-progressive*
on most issues. The caucus has been a strong voice for:
- Continued atmospheric-engineering investment (the
  Lowlands benefit most from continued atmospheric
  thickening)
- Agricultural-policy support and subsidy
- Frontier-development funding
- Skepticism of the older Hellas-Tharsis political
  establishment

The caucus chair is Senator Hira Khan of New Cydonia. Khan
is widely identified as the rising star of the
*post-Tharsis* generation of Mars politicians.

## Major cities

### New Cydonia (18M)

The Lowlands' regional capital, located in southern
Acidalia. Founded 2287; grew rapidly with the
agricultural expansion. The city is the home of the Mars
Atmospheric Authority's lowland research division and
the largest agricultural-research university on Mars
(the University of New Cydonia).

The city's architecture is distinctively *frontier-modern*
— mid-rise vertical districts (lower than Hellas or
Tharsis cities; the easier atmospheric conditions reduce
the need for the most aggressive atmospheric engineering),
with substantial open-air public spaces, parks, and
greenhouse-park hybrid environments.

### Utopia Capital (16M)

The political center of the central Lowlands basin.
Founded 2294 explicitly as a regional administrative
hub. The city's name was chosen by deliberate vote of
the founding settlers — *Utopia* meaning *no-place* in
its original Greek, but in 2294 ironically naming
something *new and intentional*. The naming has
endured.

The Utopia Congress (the regional administrative body for
the Utopia basin) is here. The University of Utopia is
the basin's principal research and educational
institution.

### Acidalia City (12M)

A major industrial-agricultural hub in the central
Acidalia basin. Less politically prominent than New
Cydonia but economically substantial.

### Mareotis (8M)

A river-port city on Lake Acidalia's northern shore.
The Lowlands' analogue to a Valles city — water-
adjacent, atmospherically eased, culturally varied.

### Cebrenia (10M)

A major Utopia basin city, agricultural-industrial.

### Hyblaeus (8M)

A Utopia basin city, primarily agricultural. The Hyblaeus
Agricultural Cooperative is one of the largest farming
collectives on Mars.

## Cultural texture

- **The pioneer-progressive register.** The Lowlands feel
  they are *making* the new Mars, much as the Tharsis
  generation felt they had made the old Mars. The
  cultural production reflects this — confident,
  forward-looking, slightly prone to romanticizing
  frontier life.
- **The agricultural literacy.** Most Lowlands residents
  have direct or near-direct experience with
  agricultural production. The cultural references — to
  weather, seasons, crops, livestock — are familiar.
- **The new-money sensibility.** Lowlands wealth is
  recent. The cultural register has the slight
  awkwardness of new wealth — confident but not yet
  inheritor-confident.
- **The atmospheric ease.** Lowlands residents have
  noticeably less mask-discipline than highland
  Martians. Outdoor life is more available; the
  cultural production includes more outdoor scenes,
  more weather, more land.

## Notes for play

- A Lowlands PC will be more *populist*, more *frontier*,
  more *new-arrival* in their register than other
  Martians. The cultural texture is distinctive.
- An agricultural backstory is more accessible for a
  Lowlands PC than for any other Mars region. This is
  useful texture; it gives the PC a non-urban
  background that most Martians lack.
- The Lowlands politics are useful for a PC who needs
  to be politically engaged but not in the
  Tharsis-republican mode. Lowlands progressivism is a
  distinct flavor.
