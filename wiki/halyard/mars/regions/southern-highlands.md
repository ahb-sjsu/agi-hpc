# The Southern Highlands

The Southern Highlands are Mars's *old country* — the
region of the earliest pre-secession settlements, the
most conservative cultural register on the planet, the
historical home of the Martian martial tradition, and the
demographic minority that has held disproportionate
cultural weight throughout the Republic's history. The
region's 100 million residents live across the high,
dry, ancient terrain south of the dichotomy boundary,
in a landscape that — in 2348, with the terraforming a
century into its second century — remains the part of
Mars least transformed by atmospheric change.

The Southern Highlanders take pride in being the *least
changed* part of Mars. Where the Lowlands have water
and the Valles has rivers and Hellas has its lake, the
Highlands remain mostly cold, mostly thin-aired, mostly
red. Highland residents prefer it this way.

## Geography

The Southern Highlands cover roughly 35 million square
kilometers — about a third of Mars's surface — at
elevations generally 2-6 km above the Martian datum. The
terrain is heavily cratered (the southern hemisphere has
been less geologically active across Mars's history; old
craters persist), with major regional features
including:

- **Hellas's Eastern Highland Approach** — the basin's
  eastern transition. Mixed terrain.
- **The Argyre Basin** — a smaller impact crater
  (1,800 km diameter) west of Hellas. Population ~14M;
  the basin's atmosphere is somewhat denser than the
  surrounding highlands but not as dense as Hellas.
- **The Noachis-Tyrrhena Plateau** — the central
  highlands plateau. The most ancient terrain on Mars
  (Noachian period); population ~38M across many
  smaller communities.
- **The Sirenum-Aonia Region** — the southwestern
  highlands. Population ~28M.
- **The Promethei-Eridania Region** — the southeastern
  highlands. Population ~20M.

The region's atmospheric pressure varies from 0.34 bar
at the lower elevations to 0.18 bar at the highest. Even
at lower elevations, the Highlands require filter-mask
discipline for outdoor activity; respirators are
recommended for any extended outdoor work above 4 km.

Surface temperatures are extreme: summer highs around
+8°C in the lower elevations, winter lows reaching -52°C
in the upper plateau. The southern winters are notably
harder than anywhere else on Mars.

## Population

The Highlands' 100 million residents are concentrated
in:

- **The Argyre Basin** — 14M, with the city of Galle
  (8M) as the regional center
- **The Noachis-Tyrrhena Plateau cities** — 38M.
  Largest are Tyrrhena (12M) and Noachis (10M); plus
  many smaller settlements averaging ~500K each
- **The Sirenum-Aonia Region** — 28M. Largest:
  Sirenum (8M); plus many smaller settlements
- **The Promethei-Eridania Region** — 20M. Largest:
  Promethei (6M); plus smaller settlements

## The pre-secession heritage

The Southern Highlands hold the oldest continuous
Martian settlements. The original 2074 Hellas-North
station was replaced; the original 2086 Tharsis-Pavonis
expanded beyond recognition. But several Highland
settlements — Tyrrhena (founded 2089), Noachis (2094),
Galle (2102) — retain their original cores intact, with
preservation cultures that have kept the founding-era
architecture, street patterns, and (in some cases)
original buildings.

The Highland heritage cities are Mars's *historical
landscape*. The University of Mars's Department of
Republican History runs the largest archive of pre-
secession material in the system; many of the
Republic's founding documents are preserved here.

The Highlands' cultural identity has been substantially
shaped by this heritage. *We were here first* is a
recognized regional sentiment.

## The martial tradition

The Highlands are Mars's military-cultural heartland. The
region's per-capita rate of MCRN service is approximately
2.4× the planetary average. Many of the most prestigious
MCRN officer-family lineages trace back to the Highlands.
The MCRN's most-decorated combat units have
disproportionate Highland representation.

The reasons are partly historical (the Highlands were
the political center of the early Mars Defense Force),
partly cultural (the region's traditions emphasize
service, discipline, sacrifice), and partly economic
(the Highlands' economy has fewer alternatives to
military careers than other regions).

The Highland martial tradition has produced:

- The largest concentration of MCRN officer-family
  lineages
- The Highland Military Academy at Tyrrhena (a
  secondary commissioning institution after the Mars
  Naval Academy at Hellas City)
- The Highland Reservist culture — the highest rate
  of MCRN reserve service per capita on Mars
- The cultural memorialization of military service
  through annual ceremonies, monuments, and a robust
  tradition of military fiction and history-writing

## Politics

The Highland caucus in the Mars Congress has been the
most internally cohesive of the regional caucuses. The
Highland political register is *conservative-republican*:

- Strong pro-Republic
- Strong pro-MCRN
- Suspicious of Earth, of the OPA, of any institution
  that could be perceived as compromising Republican
  sovereignty
- Skeptical of populist progressivism (sees the Lowlands
  caucus as politically immature)
- Quietly skeptical of the Hellas establishment (sees
  it as too cosmopolitan)

The Highland caucus's chair is Senator Marcus Goncalves
of Galle. Goncalves is widely identified as the senior
voice of the Highland conservative tradition.

## Economy

The Highlands' economy is the smallest of Mars's
regional economies, roughly $1.6T UN-equivalent. Sectors:

- **Mining and extraction** — the Highlands' geology
  produces specific resources (rare-earths,
  specialty metals) that are economically important
  despite the region's small overall economy
- **Defense industry** — substantial; many MCRN
  procurement contracts route through Highland
  manufacturers
- **Agriculture** — limited; mostly higher-elevation
  greenhouse agriculture
- **Heritage and education** — the heritage tourism
  and the academic-historical institutions
- **Services** — substantially smaller than other
  regions

The Highlands are *not* a wealthy region by Mars
standards. The region's cultural prestige substantially
exceeds its economic weight.

## Cultural texture

The Southern Highland register has features distinct
from other Mars regions:

- **The conservative-republican register.** Highland
  cultural production is *traditional*, *attached to
  the Republic's founding moments*, *suspicious of
  innovation*. The Highland literary tradition runs
  to historical fiction, military memoir, and
  philosophical conservatism.
- **The martial register.** The MCRN's cultural
  weight is heavy here. Most Highland families have
  multiple service members across generations;
  service traditions, military commemorations,
  patriotic fiction are part of the cultural
  background.
- **The cold register.** Highland residents live in
  the harshest weather on Mars. The cultural
  production reflects this — winter-themed
  literature, music, painting; an aesthetic
  appreciation for *the harsh and the spare*.
- **The frontier-historical register.** Highland
  residents are aware of being *what came before*.
  The cultural identity has a specific historical
  consciousness that other regions do not share.
- **The religious-traditional register.** The
  Highlands have higher rates of religious
  participation than the planetary average, with
  particular strength of Catholic and Orthodox
  Christian traditions and Islamic communities.
  The terraforming-civic-religion is treated more
  reverently here than elsewhere.

## Notes for play

- A Highland PC will have a *traditional, martial,
  conservative-republican* register that distinguishes
  them from any other Mars-PC option.
- The MCRN background is more probable for a Highland
  PC than for any other Mars region — most MCRN
  officer-class PCs are Highland-born.
- The Highland cultural attachment to the Republic
  creates specific tensions for a Highland PC who
  would normally be on a Belt-flagged contract like
  the *Halyard*'s. The contract is acceptable but
  carries some quiet political cost.
- The cold-and-harsh sensibility makes Highland PCs
  natural fits for outer-system contract work; they
  are pre-adapted to harsh environments.
