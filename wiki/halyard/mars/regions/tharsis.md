# The Tharsis Plateau

The Tharsis Plateau is the industrial heart of Mars, the
original colonial heartland, and the home of the largest
single MCRN naval-base complex on the planet. Its 220
million residents live across a chain of cities running
along the volcanic arc — Olympus, Pavonis, Ascraeus, Arsia,
plus a long tail of smaller industrial settlements — at
elevations that reach 21 km above the Martian datum at the
peak of Olympus Mons.

The plateau's defining features are its *altitude*, its
*industrial density*, and its *political weight*. Tharsis is
where Mars *built itself*, in the eyes of the post-secession
political class; the plateau's industrial output is what
made the Republic possible.

## Geography

Tharsis is a volcanic uplift covering roughly 30 million
square kilometers (about a quarter of the Martian surface).
The plateau rises in a broad dome from the surrounding
plains to its peak elevations:

- **Olympus Mons** — 21 km above datum. The largest
  volcano in the solar system. The slopes have been
  developed industrially; the summit is a high-altitude
  scientific reserve.
- **Ascraeus, Pavonis, Arsia** — the three smaller
  shield volcanoes (each ~14-18 km above datum) along
  the central spine of the plateau.
- **The Tharsis Tholi** — smaller volcanic features
  scattered across the plateau.
- **Valles Marineris (eastern flank)** — the great
  canyon system that drops off the plateau's eastern
  side; covered in its own entry.

The plateau's elevation means thinner atmosphere even at
the city sites, which mostly sit at 6-9 km above datum.
Tharsis cities use atmospheric pressurization extensively;
*outdoor* life is filter-mask-required at all elevations
above 10 km, and respirator-required above 14 km.

## Population distribution

The 220 million Tharsis residents are concentrated in:

- **Olympus** (32M) — the largest city on the plateau,
  built into the lower slopes of Olympus Mons. The
  Mars Naval Academy's secondary campus is here. The
  Olympus Industrial District is the largest single
  manufacturing complex on Mars.
- **Pavonis** (28M) — the second-largest city. Pavonis
  is the equatorial space-elevator terminus — the
  *Mars Lift* anchor point, completed 2278 — making it
  Mars's principal Earth-orbit interface.
- **Ascraeus** (22M) — heavy manufacturing and
  metallurgy. The Tharsis Steel Authority is
  headquartered here.
- **Arsia** (18M) — petrochemicals and atmospheric
  processing. The Tharsis Atmospheric Plants
  contribute substantially to the planetary
  terraforming effort.
- **Tharsis Mid** (44M) — the central industrial belt
  between the volcanoes. Multiple cities, mostly
  industrial-residential mix.
- **Tharsis Outer** (76M) — the lower-elevation outer
  ring of the plateau, where most residential
  development concentrates and where most agricultural
  infrastructure (greenhouse complexes) is located.

## The Mars Lift

Pavonis's space elevator — the *Mars Lift* — is the
plateau's signature engineering achievement and one of
Mars's most important strategic assets. The Lift runs from
a Pavonis ground anchor at 6.4 km elevation to a
geostationary counterweight some 20,000 km above Mars's
surface, with cargo-and-passenger capacity that handles
roughly 80% of all Mars-Earth and Mars-Belt traffic.

The Lift's economic importance to the Tharsis region
cannot be overstated. Pavonis's industrial role is
substantially driven by Lift-adjacent logistics. The
plateau's manufactured goods can reach Mars orbit cheaply;
this is why Tharsis's manufacturing has remained
competitive with Belt-based alternatives.

The Lift is a target. The MCRN's largest single naval
base complex (the Pavonis Reserve) exists in part to
defend the Lift. The Lift's destruction has been a
contingent threat in every Mars-Earth crisis of the past
century; the political class on both sides understands
that destroying the Lift would be a casus belli that
Mars could not let pass.

## Industry

Tharsis is Mars's industrial engine. Specific sectors:

- **Heavy manufacturing.** Tharsis's heavy-industry output
  is the largest concentration on Mars and one of the
  three largest in human space (with Asia's Yangtze and
  Earth's AIZ). Spacecraft components, Epstein-drive
  parts, fusion-reactor structural components, the
  bulk of Mars's machine-tool industry.
- **Metallurgy.** The Tharsis Steel Authority operates
  the largest steel and specialty-alloy production
  complex on Mars. The Tharsis copper, aluminum, and
  rare-earth refineries are major contributors.
- **Atmospheric and water-system industry.** The
  Tharsis Atmospheric Plants produce a substantial
  fraction of Mars's terraforming atmospheric input.
  The Plants' work is both terraforming-direct (gas
  release for atmospheric thickening) and supporting
  (oxygen and nitrogen for residential systems).
- **Energy.** Mars's largest fusion-power complex
  (Tharsis Fusion Authority) operates from the plateau.
- **Defense industry.** The MCRN's primary procurement
  base is Tharsis. Most MCRN ships are constructed in
  the Pavonis or Olympus shipyards (which are *orbital*
  shipyards, fed by the Lift).

## Politics

Tharsis is the political heartland of the Republic. The
plateau's electoral weight in the Mars Congress is
substantial; the Tharsis caucus has historically been the
most internally cohesive of the Mars regional caucuses.

The plateau's political character is *industrial-
republican* — strongly pro-Republic, strongly pro-
terraforming, strongly anti-Earth (the Tharsis caucus
has been the most consistent voice for hard MCRN posture
toward Earth), and strongly working-class in its
self-image (despite the substantial wealth concentrated
in the plateau's industrial elite).

The Tharsis Caucus's current chair is Senator Yuri
Volkonsky, the senior Senator from Pavonis. Volkonsky is
considered, by most Mars political observers, the most
likely successor to President Voss-Tanaka in the 2350
election.

## The MCRN presence

Tharsis hosts the largest single concentration of MCRN
naval infrastructure on Mars:

- **The Pavonis Reserve** — the principal MCRN First
  Fleet base, with orbital shipyards, training centers,
  and personnel facilities for approximately 40,000
  active-duty personnel
- **The Olympus Officer Academy** — the secondary Mars
  Naval Academy campus, specializing in engineering
  officers
- **The Tharsis Training Range** — the largest live-fire
  exercise area on Mars, used for ground-force and
  vehicle-system training

The MCRN's economic and political weight in Tharsis is
enormous. Many Tharsis families have multi-generational
service traditions. The MCRN's procurement contracts
flow through the Tharsis industrial complex and back
into the Tharsis economy.

## Cultural texture

Tharsis cultural life has a distinctive register:

- **Industrial pride.** The Tharsis identity is
  *builder-engineer-republican*. The plateau's residents
  consider themselves the *making* of Mars. The
  cultural production reflects this — songs, novels,
  films about workers, engineers, the physical labor of
  building a planet.
- **The Olympus literary tradition.** The Olympus
  cultural scene has produced most of Mars's
  contemporary literary fiction. Olympus writers tend
  toward *industrial-realist* or *engineer-philosophical*
  modes; the Olympus Literary Review is the second-
  most-prestigious Martian literary publication after
  Hellas's.
- **The Pavonis cosmopolitan layer.** Pavonis's role
  as the Mars-Earth interface gives it an unusually
  cosmopolitan layer. Earth and Belt visitors pass
  through; Pavonis's restaurants and cultural venues
  are the most internationally varied on Mars.
- **The harsh-altitude register.** Tharsis residents
  live at altitudes that would be uncomfortable on
  Earth. The cultural register reflects this — a
  practical relationship with respiration equipment, a
  comfort with the sky's color (which is darker at
  altitude than the lowland reddish-brown), an
  understanding of the practical physics of low-pressure
  environments.
- **The volcanic awareness.** Most Tharsis residents
  live on the slopes or near the slopes of major
  volcanoes. The volcanoes are not seismically active
  in any time frame relevant to human habitation, but
  the awareness of living *on a volcano* is part of the
  cultural identity. Olympus appears in roughly the way
  Mt. Fuji appears in Japanese culture — as an object of
  ongoing aesthetic and emotional attention.

## Notes for play

- A Tharsis PC will be more *industrial*, more
  *republican*, and more *nationally engaged* than a
  Hellas PC. The two regions produce different Martian
  PCs.
- The MCRN background is more probable for a Tharsis PC
  than for any other Mars-region PC.
- The altitude-fluency is a useful asset; Tharsis PCs
  default to comfort with low-pressure environments,
  which transfers to comfort with EVA work.
- Pavonis's role as the Mars-Earth interface makes
  Pavonis backstories particularly useful for PCs who
  need to plausibly have spent time on Earth.
