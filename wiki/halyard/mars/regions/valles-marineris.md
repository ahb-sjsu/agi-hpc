# Valles Marineris

Valles Marineris — the great canyon system that runs
4,000 km along Mars's equatorial belt — is the planet's
trade-and-transit corridor and one of its most
distinctive cultural regions. The canyon's 180 million
residents live in cities along the canyon floor, on the
canyon walls, and (since the river began flowing in 2317)
along its banks.

The Valles is, in 2348, the *most Earth-like* part of Mars.
The canyon's floor sits 5-7 km below the Martian datum,
giving it the thickest atmosphere on the planet outside
the Hellas Basin. Liquid water flows. Vegetation covers
substantial areas of the canyon floor. The cities are
*atmospherically open* in a way that no Tharsis or
highland Mars city is.

## Geography

Valles Marineris extends from the Tharsis Plateau in the
west to the Chryse Basin in the east — about 4,000 km of
canyon system, with depths reaching 7 km below the
surrounding plateau. The canyon's width varies from 100
to 600 km; the floor is a complex of subsidiary canyons,
plateaus, and (now) river valleys.

Major sub-regions:

- **Western Valles** (Coprates, Capri, Eos) — the
  highest-elevation eastern reaches of the canyon's
  western section. Industrial and commercial; the
  largest cities are here.
- **Central Valles** (Melas, Candor, Ophir) — the
  deepest and most atmospherically thick zone. The
  river-valley agriculture is most developed here.
- **Eastern Valles** (Eos transition, Chryse approach)
  — the lower-elevation eastern reaches where the
  canyon descends toward the Chryse Basin and the
  Northern Lowlands.

## The river

The Valles river — the *Marineris* — is one of Mars's
defining contemporary features. Constructed water flow
began in 2317 with the controlled release of polar-cap
melt through the Tharsis-fed Valles inflow. By 2330 the
river was flowing year-round through the canyon's central
section; by 2348 the river extends along most of the
canyon's length, with seasonal flow patterns that
approximate Earth's pre-modern Nile.

The river is *artificial in origin* but *natural in
behavior*. The Mars Atmospheric Authority manages the
flow rate; the riverbed itself has developed natural
features (bends, sediment deposits, seasonal floods,
ecosystem development) that the MAA does not control.

The river-valley ecosystem includes:
- Riparian vegetation, mostly Earth-introduced cold-
  hardy species (cottonwood-relatives, willow-relatives,
  reeds)
- Aquatic plants, several species of which are the first
  Earth-introduced plants to *naturalize* on Mars —
  reproducing without ongoing human intervention
- Fish populations, deliberately introduced (carp,
  trout, several species of native-Earth-river fish
  selected for cold-tolerance)
- Insect populations, deliberately introduced and now
  part of an emerging Mars river ecosystem
- Bird populations, recently introduced (the 2342 bird
  release at Melas Chasma was a major Mars cultural
  event); ongoing experimental establishment

## The Riverbarge culture

The Valles's most distinctive cultural feature is the
*riverbarge tradition*. The river supports navigable
shipping along most of its length; the cities of the
Valles use the river as a primary transit and trade
artery. The barges — slow, cargo-and-passenger
vessels designed for the river's specific flow patterns —
are the dominant transit form *between* cities along the
river, and a substantial residential category in their
own right.

Roughly 12 million Martians live full-time on barges, in
floating-residential communities anchored at major river
hubs and migrating seasonally with the river's flow
patterns. The barge population has its own subculture:
*riverbarge Martians*, recognized as a distinct cultural
group, with their own dialects, music, food traditions,
and political associations.

The riverbarge culture has been one of Mars's most
unexpected cultural developments. The Republic's founders
in 2272 did not anticipate the river; the river itself
was a 2317 engineering achievement; the riverbarge
culture is what people *did* with the river. Most Mars
political observers regard it as one of the Republic's
most *Mars-specific* cultural achievements — something
that could not have happened on Earth, that has its own
character not derived from any Earth-side source.

## Major cities

The largest Valles cities, going west to east:

### Coprates City (28M)

The largest Valles city. At the western end of the central
canyon. The Coprates Industrial District is a major
manufacturing and refining center; the Coprates River
Authority manages river flow regulation for the upper-
canyon section. Coprates is the western approach to the
Valles river system.

### Melas (24M)

The cultural capital of the Valles. Sits at the deepest
point of the canyon (-7.2 km below datum), with the
thickest atmosphere on Mars (0.62 bar at city level —
slightly thicker than Hellas Lake). Melas's atmospheric
conditions allow nearly Earth-normal outdoor activity;
the city's parks, restaurants, and cultural venues
extensively use outdoor space in a way Tharsis or
highland cities cannot.

The Melas Symphony, the Melas University, the major
Valles museums, the Mars Riverbarge Council's
headquarters — all here.

### Candor (16M)

A river-valley industrial city, primarily manufacturing
and biotech. Candor's biotech sector specializes in
Mars-adapted Earth-organism breeding — the work that
produced the river ecosystem's current species mix.

### Ophir (14M)

The Mars commercial center. Smaller than Melas but more
financially important. The Ophir Bourse handles
commodity trade for the Valles region. Most Mars-Belt
trade contracts are negotiated in Ophir.

### Eos (12M)

The eastern Valles transitional city, where the canyon
opens toward the Chryse Basin. Eos is the gateway to
the Northern Lowlands; trade flows between the Valles
and the lowlands move through Eos.

### Smaller Riverbarge Hubs

Numerous smaller cities anchored at river hubs handle
the long tail of Valles population (~86M across many
locations).

## Politics

The Valles caucus in the Mars Congress is the second-
largest after the Tharsis caucus. The Valles politicians
have, historically, been *commercial-pragmatic* in their
register — less republican-fervor than Tharsis, more
trade-and-business than Hellas, more cosmopolitan than
the Northern Lowlands.

The Valles Caucus's current chair is Senator Aurora
Mendiola of Melas. Mendiola is a centrist-progressive
politician with a strong following among the riverbarge
communities. She has been a credible candidate for
the 2350 presidential nomination; she has not yet
declared.

## Cultural texture

The Valles cultural register has features the rest of
Mars does not:

- **The water register.** Valles residents have a
  relationship with water — the river, the
  evaporation, the seasonal floods — that no other
  Mars region has. This shapes the cultural production:
  Valles literature is heavy on water imagery; the
  music incorporates river sounds; the cuisine uses
  river fish and river-grown produce.
- **The atmospheric ease.** The thick atmosphere makes
  outdoor life genuinely available. Melas residents in
  particular have an *outdoor culture* — café terraces,
  riverbank walks, outdoor concerts — that other Mars
  cities cannot quite replicate.
- **The cosmopolitan trade culture.** The Valles is
  Mars's commercial corridor. Most major foreign
  visitors to Mars (Earth, Belt, Luna) spend
  significant time in the Valles. The cultural register
  is correspondingly outward-facing.
- **The riverbarge subculture.** Riverbarge Martians
  have their own everything — dialect, music, food,
  political organizations, even a small but distinct
  literary tradition. A *Halyard* PC who is
  riverbarge-born brings a recognizable texture to the
  table.

## Notes for play

- A Valles PC has a *commercial-cosmopolitan* register
  distinct from Tharsis (industrial-republican) or
  Hellas (formal-bibliophilic). Different Mars PC
  flavors.
- The river-valley life makes Valles PCs more
  Earth-comfortable than most Martians. A Valles PC
  visiting Earth has less culture shock than a Tharsis
  or Northern Lowlands PC.
- A riverbarge background is one of the most
  distinctive Mars-PC backgrounds available. Use it
  carefully — riverbarge Martians have particular
  sensitivities about their culture being treated
  superficially.
