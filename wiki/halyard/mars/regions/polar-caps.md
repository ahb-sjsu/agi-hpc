# The Mars Polar Caps

The Mars polar regions — *North Polar* and *South
Polar* — are the planet's smallest and most specialized
population centers. The combined 40 million residents
work in atmospheric monitoring, water-ice extraction,
fusion-power generation, and the substantial scientific-
research operations that the polar geographies make
possible.

The two polar regions have distinct characters: the
North Polar is *industrial*, the South Polar is
*scientific*. Both are cold, both are remote, both are
recognizable to other Martians as *the polar regions*
in the way that Antarctic Industrial Zone workers are
recognizable on Earth.

## The North Polar Region

### Geography

The North Polar Cap is a deposit of water ice (with
seasonal carbon-dioxide ice cover) approximately 1,000
km in diameter, surrounded by the Northern Lowlands
plain. The cap's central elevation reaches about 3 km
above the surrounding plain; the cap itself is up to
3 km thick at its center. The region's atmosphere is
substantially Lowland-equivalent (0.40-0.45 bar) at
the cap's perimeter, thinner at the cap's center.

The North Polar climate is harsh in the Mars sense:
summer highs at the cap perimeter reach -8°C; winter
lows descend to -82°C. The polar night runs about 80
Earth days at the geographic pole.

### Population and economy

The North Polar's 18 million residents are concentrated
in:

- **Boreum** (8M) — the regional capital and the
  largest single polar city. On the cap perimeter at
  about 80°N. Industrial and administrative.
- **Cap Industrial Zone** (6M) — distributed across
  the cap perimeter, in ice-extraction and water-
  processing communities
- **Northern Outposts** (4M) — smaller communities
  on the cap and at high latitudes

The economic structure is dominated by:

- **Water-ice extraction.** Mars's largest single
  source of bulk water is the North Polar Cap. The
  extraction operations supply roughly 60% of Mars's
  industrial and residential water (the rest comes
  from atmospheric capture, sub-surface aquifers in
  other regions, and ongoing river-system water
  recovery).
- **Atmospheric monitoring.** The Polar Atmospheric
  Stations are Mars's primary atmospheric-data
  collection points. The Mars Atmospheric Authority's
  largest single research operation is at Boreum.
- **Fusion-fuel processing.** The North Polar
  deuterium-extraction facilities (taking deuterium
  from the cap's water ice) produce a substantial
  fraction of Mars's fusion fuel.
- **Scientific cold-temperature industry.** Specialized
  manufacturing that benefits from the polar
  environment — cryogenics, vacuum-and-cold-state
  materials processing, precision instruments.

### Politics

The North Polar caucus in the Mars Congress is small but
politically distinct. The caucus tends to align with
Lowlands populist progressivism on most issues but has
its own specific concerns around water-rights
management and atmospheric-monitoring funding.

The caucus chair is Senator Frederick Boriakov-Singh
of Boreum, a former water-extraction engineer. He has
been a quiet but consistent voice for *northern
infrastructure* in Mars federal politics.

### Cultural texture

The North Polar register is *industrial-cold*. The
cultural production emphasizes:

- The relationship with ice — work, weather, beauty
- The polar night as cultural fact (the long darkness
  is structuring; northern art and music are heavily
  marked by it)
- The pragmatic-technical register — engineers,
  technicians, scientists
- A specific kind of quiet stoicism that the rest of
  Mars recognizes as *northern*

## The South Polar Region

### Geography

The South Polar Cap is the smaller of the two, about
400 km in diameter, primarily water ice with a thicker
seasonal carbon-dioxide cap. The cap sits in the
Southern Highlands at high elevation (3-4 km above
datum); the surrounding terrain is the harshest on
Mars.

The South Polar climate is the harshest on the planet:
summer highs reach -28°C; winter lows reach -105°C
(the lowest temperatures recorded on Mars). The polar
night is similar to North Polar but with markedly
colder temperatures. Outdoor work in winter requires
the most aggressive protective equipment used anywhere
on Mars.

### Population and economy

The South Polar's 22 million residents are
concentrated:

- **Australis** (10M) — the regional capital, on the
  cap's edge at about 78°S. The Mars Exploration
  Reserve is here.
- **Cap Research Zone** (8M) — the scientific-
  research stations spread across the cap perimeter
  and high latitudes
- **Southern Outposts** (4M) — smaller communities,
  largely scientific

The economic structure is dominated by:

- **Scientific research.** The Mars Exploration
  Reserve is the largest single scientific-research
  organization on Mars; its work spans atmospheric
  science, planetary geology, biology (the
  experimental introduction of cold-tolerant
  organisms is centered here), and astronomy (the
  South Polar Observatory complex is one of human
  space's premier astronomical facilities).
- **The Mars Astronomical Observatory.** The South
  Polar's clear, cold, low-light-pollution
  environment makes it Mars's premier astronomical
  site. The Observatory's instruments include some
  of the largest ground-based telescopes in the
  solar system.
- **Specialized cold-temperature industry.**
  Smaller-scale than the North Polar's but
  high-margin; precision instruments, specialty
  cryogenics.
- **Government services.** The Reserve and the
  Observatory generate substantial administrative
  employment.

### The Mars Exploration Reserve

The MER is the institutional embodiment of Mars's
scientific establishment. The Reserve operates:

- The largest single research budget in the
  Republic outside the MAA's terraforming program
- Approximately 84,000 employees across the South
  Polar and selected Mars-wide field offices
- The South Polar Observatory complex
- The Mars Biology Experimental Station (the largest
  cold-tolerant-organism research facility in human
  space)
- The Mars Atmospheric History Project (the
  long-cycle paleoclimatic study of Mars's
  atmospheric record from cap-ice cores)

The MER's reputation is *substantial scientific weight,
slight institutional eccentricity*. The Reserve has its
own internal political culture; its relationship with
the Mars federal government is *generally cooperative,
occasionally testy*.

The MER's most-known recent project is the **Mars-side
Pre-Spaceflight Survey** — a long-running effort to
document any Mars surface anomalies that might indicate
pre-human technological activity. (The project's results
have been: nothing definitive; some interesting
geological features that have plausible non-human
explanations; a small set of features that *do not*
have plausible non-human explanations and are subject
to ongoing investigation.)

The MER does not know about the Antarctic Elder Thing
recovery operations on Earth. The Reserve's 2342
identification of an *anomalous geological feature* on
Mars's far southern Lambert glacier is, however,
relevant. The feature's nature is being investigated
quietly; the formal report has not been published; the
classified preliminary assessment indicates structural
characteristics consistent with engineered construction.

This is, currently, a Mars-internal investigation with
no off-world disclosure. The campaign's late-act options
include the possibility that this Mars-side discovery
becomes politically significant if combined with the
*Halyard*'s findings.

### Politics

The South Polar caucus is the smallest in the Mars
Congress. The caucus's political register is *technocratic-
scientific*; the caucus's votes typically follow
expert-opinion lines rather than ideological lines.

The caucus chair is Senator Mira Sokolova-Patel of
Australis, a former MER researcher. Sokolova-Patel is
politically influential beyond her caucus's size
because of her credibility on scientific-and-technical
questions.

### Cultural texture

The South Polar register is *scientific-isolated*. The
cultural production emphasizes:

- Long-form intellectual inquiry — the Australis
  literary tradition produces some of the Republic's
  most demanding fiction and philosophy
- The astronomical sensibility — much of the region's
  cultural production engages directly with cosmology,
  cosmic time, the universe at large scale
- Cold-and-darkness aesthetics — similar to North
  Polar but more philosophically inflected
- Institutional research culture — the patient,
  collaborative, multi-decade work-style that
  characterizes major scientific institutions

## Notes for play

- A Polar PC has the smallest baseline population
  representation of any Mars-region option, making
  Polar PCs distinctive.
- North Polar PCs default to industrial-technical
  backgrounds; South Polar PCs default to scientific-
  research backgrounds.
- The MER scientific background is one of the
  more useful Mars-PC backgrounds for the campaign;
  a scientist familiar with Mars-side anomalies is
  positioned to engage with the *Halyard*'s
  discoveries productively.
- The Mars-side anomaly investigation is a Keeper
  resource: a Mars-side parallel to the *Halyard*'s
  findings that can surface in late acts to amplify
  the political stakes.
