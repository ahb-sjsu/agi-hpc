# The Hellas Basin (region)

The Hellas Basin region encompasses the political,
intellectual, and atmospheric heart of Mars. The basin's
280 million residents — the largest single regional
population on the planet — live around the Hellas Lake
and across the surrounding mid-elevation rim. This is
the region of Hellas City (the capital), Lyot, Crocco, and
the dense cluster of secondary cities along the basin's
shoreline and approaches.

The basin's defining advantage is *atmospheric depth*. The
2,300-km-wide impact crater drops 7-8 km below the
surrounding plateau; the resulting atmospheric column at
the basin floor is the thickest on Mars, supporting the
permanent lake, the most Earth-like climate, and the
greatest concentration of *outdoor-comfortable* urban
life.

## Geography

The Hellas Basin is the largest impact crater on Mars
and one of the largest in the solar system. The basin
floor sits 7-8 km below the surrounding highland plateau;
the rim rises to about 9 km above the basin's deepest
point. The diameter is approximately 2,300 km.

Major sub-regions:

- **The Lake Shore Zone** — the immediate Hellas Lake
  perimeter (the lake itself is ~600 km across at its
  widest). Hellas City, Crocco, and the smaller
  lakeshore communities are here.
- **The Western Approaches** — the western basin rise.
  Lyot is the major city here; substantial agricultural
  development on the lower slopes.
- **The Northern Approaches** — the basin's most
  industrialized perimeter. Major manufacturing along
  the basin's northern rim.
- **The Eastern Highland Transition** — the basin's
  eastern boundary, blending into the Southern
  Highlands.

The basin's atmospheric pressure varies from 0.58 bar at
the lake (the densest atmosphere on Mars) to 0.32 bar at
the rim's higher elevations. Surface temperatures are
the most moderate on Mars: lake-shore highs reach +18°C
in summer, lows fall to -8°C in winter. Snow falls in
the basin's higher elevations seasonally; freezing rain
and *real rain* (since the river-system extension to the
basin in 2334) occur in the lake-shore zone.

## Population distribution

The Hellas Basin's 280 million are distributed:

- **Hellas City** — 7.4M. The capital. (See `mars/cities/
  hellas-city.md`.)
- **Crocco** — 14M. The second-largest basin city,
  on the eastern lake shore. Heavy manufacturing and
  shipbuilding (the lake itself is large enough for
  substantial barge construction).
- **Lyot** — 22M. The western basin's largest city.
  Agricultural-industrial. The Mars Naval Academy's
  third campus (focused on logistics and engineering)
  is here.
- **The Lake Shore Communities** — 38M across many
  smaller cities and towns lining the lake's shore.
  Mostly residential and recreational; the cultural
  heart of the basin's lake-life tradition.
- **The Western Approaches** — 56M. Agricultural and
  industrial.
- **The Northern Approaches** — 64M. Industrial.
- **The Eastern Highland Transition** — 78M. Mixed.

## The Hellas Lake

The lake is the basin's defining feature and the most
significant single body of water on Mars. The lake's
characteristics:

- **Size**: roughly 600 km × 280 km (asymmetric;
  largest at the basin's deepest point)
- **Depth**: variable; up to 250 m at the basin's
  center, much shallower along most shores
- **Salinity**: brackish (about a third of Earth ocean
  salinity); the result of slow accumulation of
  atmospheric and ground-water dissolved minerals
- **Ecosystem**: deliberately introduced; carp, perch,
  several algal species, and an experimental Mars-
  native chemo-autotroph population that has begun
  natural colonization of the bottom sediment
- **Navigation**: extensive. Sailboats, motorboats,
  ferries, barges; the lake's working economy supports
  fishing fleets, transport services, and a
  substantial recreational-sailing community

The lake has its own cultural significance. The
*Lake-Shore Walk* (the publicly-accessible 1,800-km
shoreline path) is one of Mars's most-traveled
recreational routes. The annual *Lake Festival* in
midsummer is the largest single cultural event on Mars.
Lake imagery dominates Mars-region popular culture.

## Politics

The Hellas Basin is the political heart of the Republic.
The basin's caucus in the Mars Congress is, in raw
numbers, the largest of the regional caucuses, but its
political weight is more *establishment* than dominant.
The Hellas-area political tradition is *centrist-
formal*; the basin tends to produce political
moderates and consensus-builders rather than the
Tharsis caucus's republicans or the Lowlands' populists.

The basin's congressional delegation has historically
held a disproportionate share of cabinet-level positions.
The basin's voters are widely regarded as the *deciders*
in close elections.

The Hellas Provincial Council (a regional body separate
from the city council of Hellas City proper) handles
basin-wide coordination on water rights, atmospheric
management, and inter-municipal issues.

## Economy

The basin's economy is the largest single regional
economy on Mars, roughly $4.2T UN-equivalent. Sectors:

- **Government and federal services** — Hellas City as
  the capital generates substantial government-sector
  employment
- **Education and research** — the University of Hellas
  and the basin's other institutions employ ~1.4M
  directly
- **Manufacturing** — the Northern Approaches and Crocco
  shipyards
- **Agriculture** — extensive in the basin's lower-
  elevation perimeter
- **Cultural production** — Mars's largest publishing
  industry, Symphony, and arts establishment
- **Lake-economy services** — fishing, transport,
  recreational, lakeside hospitality

## Cultural texture

The Hellas Basin's cultural register is *formal,
established, lakeside*:

- **Lake-life as common culture.** Most basin residents
  have a relationship with the lake — sailing,
  swimming, walking the shoreline, lake-based
  recreation. This is unusual for Mars.
- **The Hellas literary register.** The basin's
  literary culture is highly developed and somewhat
  conservative — the Hellas Literary Review is the
  Republic's flagship literary publication; the
  University of Hellas's writing programs train most
  of the major Mars literary figures.
- **The atmospheric ease.** Basin residents take for
  granted what other Martians cannot — outdoor cafes,
  open-air concerts, the seasonal migrations of
  *real birds* (yes, birds; introduced 2336, established
  in the basin by 2342).
- **The pre-Republican traces.** Some of the older basin
  families have a quiet pre-Republican identity. They
  are not separatists; they are conservative in the
  cultural sense — preferring the Mars that existed
  before secession.
- **The political-class density.** Most basin
  residents have *some* connection to the federal
  political establishment. Senate staffers, civil
  servants, federal-court personnel, lobbyists, the
  Mars Atmospheric Authority's administrative staff —
  all live in the basin. This shapes the basin's social
  life in distinctive ways.

## Notes for play

- A Hellas Basin PC has the most *establishment* register
  of any Mars-PC option. Quiet, formal, well-educated.
- The basin's cultural register is closer to Earth-
  European than other Mars regions — slightly cultivated,
  preservation-oriented.
- Lake-life is a useful texture asset. A Hellas PC may
  have learned to sail; may have spent summers at a
  family lake-shore cabin; may have a relationship with
  the lake as place that other Mars PCs lack.
