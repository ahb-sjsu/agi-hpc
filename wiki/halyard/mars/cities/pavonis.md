# Pavonis

Pavonis is Mars's space-elevator city, the planet's
principal Earth-orbit and Belt-orbit interface, and the
single most strategically significant location on the
Republic's territory. The 28 million Pavonis residents
live around the *Mars Lift* — the cable elevator that
connects Pavonis's ground anchor to a geostationary
counterweight 20,000 km above the planet — and depend,
directly or indirectly, on the Lift's operations.

The city's character is *cosmopolitan-strategic*. Pavonis
is the most internationally varied city on Mars, the
most economically dynamic, and the most politically
sensitive — a target whose loss would cripple the
Republic's interplanetary economy.

## Site

Pavonis sits at the equator (approximately 0°N latitude)
on the equatorial saddle of the Pavonis Mons volcano. The
elevation at the Lift's ground anchor is 6.4 km above
the Martian datum; the city extends across the lower
slopes of the volcano and into the surrounding plateau.

The equatorial location is the reason for the Lift —
the centrifugal physics of the space-elevator system
require an equatorial ground anchor. Pavonis Mons's
size and geological stability made it the only viable
site on Mars for the Lift's construction.

The city's atmospheric environment is similar to other
Tharsis cities — filter-mask required, respirators
recommended for higher elevations. The Lift's ground
infrastructure is heavily pressurized; the city's
arcology cluster around the anchor is one of the most
sophisticated atmospheric-engineering systems on Mars.

## The Mars Lift

The Lift is Pavonis's defining feature. The structure:

- **Ground anchor**: at 0°N, 113°W on Pavonis Mons.
  An enormous reinforced concrete-and-steel structure
  with the cable's lower attachment.
- **The cable**: 20,000 km of carbon-nanotube-and-
  composite cable, the largest single structural
  artifact on Mars and one of the most ambitious
  engineering achievements in human space.
- **Geostationary counterweight**: a constructed
  asteroid-mass counterweight at the cable's upper
  end. The counterweight serves both as the Lift's
  physical balance and as a major orbital
  infrastructure platform.
- **Climbers**: cargo-and-passenger vehicles that
  traverse the cable. Capacity: 200,000 tons of cargo
  per day, 8,000 passengers per day. The Lift runs
  continuously; climbers leave the ground every few
  minutes during peak operations.
- **Counterweight infrastructure**: the orbital end
  hosts the *Pavonis Orbital* — Mars's largest
  orbital city, with a population of approximately
  1.4 million across the counterweight platforms and
  associated stations. The MCRN's First Fleet's
  primary orbital base is here.

The Lift's economic significance is enormous. The Lift
handles approximately 80% of all Mars-Earth and Mars-
Belt cargo traffic. Without the Lift, Mars's
interplanetary economy would be substantially smaller;
the Lift's destruction has been the implicit threat in
every Mars-Earth crisis of the past century.

## Layout

### The Lift Anchor District

The immediate surroundings of the Lift's ground anchor.
Mostly Lift-related: cargo-handling, passenger
processing, customs, the Lift Authority's offices.
Population ~3M (workers and families).

### Pavonis Central

The city's commercial center. The Pavonis Bourse
(commodity trading), the major banks, the cosmopolitan
hotels, the international corporate offices. Population
~8M.

### The Foreign Quarter

The most internationally varied district on Mars.
Earth, Belt, and Lunar enterprises operate here in
substantial numbers. The Foreign Quarter has its own
cultural ecosystem — restaurants, cultural centers,
embassies-in-fact (the major Earth and Belt powers
maintain trade missions and diplomatic offices).
Population ~5M.

### The Industrial District

Heavy manufacturing focused on Lift-and-orbital
production. Many MCRN naval components are
manufactured here for orbital final assembly. Population
~6M.

### The Outer Districts

Residential and mid-level commercial. Population ~6M.

## Politics

Pavonis's politics are *cosmopolitan-pragmatic*. The
city's congressional delegation tends to be more
moderate than the broader Tharsis caucus's. The
international-trade and Lift-operations economy gives
the city's political class strong institutional
investment in stable Mars-Earth-Belt relationships.

The current Mayor is Senator Asha Mendoza-Singh, a
former Lift Authority director. The city's political
character is *trade-oriented, internationally engaged,
slightly less ideologically republican than the rest
of Tharsis*.

## Economy

Pavonis's economy is roughly $2.4T UN-equivalent — the
second-largest single-city economy on Mars. The Lift
is the dominant economic factor; almost everything in
Pavonis exists in some relationship to the Lift.

Sectors:

- **Lift logistics and cargo** — the largest sector;
  encompasses the Lift Authority itself, the cargo-
  handling industry, the customs operations, the
  freight-forwarding services
- **International trade** — the Pavonis Bourse and
  the city's trade-finance industry; substantially
  larger than equivalent operations elsewhere on Mars
- **Manufacturing** — Lift-supplying and orbital-
  production manufacturing
- **Higher education** — the University of Pavonis
  and the Lift Engineering Institute
- **Defense** — substantial MCRN procurement and
  logistics
- **Tourism and hospitality** — Earth and Belt
  visitors arrive by Lift; the Foreign Quarter's
  hospitality industry is one of the largest on Mars

## Cultural texture

Pavonis's cultural register has features no other Mars
city has:

- **Cosmopolitan internationalism.** The Foreign
  Quarter's cultural ecosystem makes Pavonis the
  most internationally varied city on Mars. Earth
  visitors, Belt visitors, Lunar visitors are
  routine; their cultural traces are visible in
  food, language, and art.
- **The Lift as horizon.** The Lift cable is visible
  from much of the city — a thin line rising from
  the volcano into the sky, disappearing into the
  upper atmosphere. The cable's visual presence
  shapes the city's aesthetics.
- **The trade register.** Commercial and trade
  cultures dominate. Pavonis residents take
  business and trade seriously in ways other
  Martians don't quite match.
- **The orbital-adjacent register.** Many Pavonis
  residents have been to orbit, have family on
  Pavonis Orbital, or work in Lift-adjacent
  industries that involve regular orbital travel.
  The orbital-comfortable register is widespread.
- **The slight cosmopolitan separation.** Pavonis's
  cultural production is somewhat separated from
  the rest of Mars. The city's literary scene, its
  music, its visual arts have been called *less
  Martian* by other Martian cultural producers,
  meant pejoratively.

## Pavonis Orbital

The orbital city at the Lift's counterweight is
substantial. Pavonis Orbital is:

- The largest orbital population center in human
  space (1.4M residents)
- The MCRN's First Fleet's principal naval base
- Mars's primary deep-space port; most outbound
  Mars-flagged vessels depart from here
- The home of the Mars Orbital Shipyards, where
  most MCRN warships are constructed

A *Halyard* PC who has spent time on Pavonis Orbital
is rare but plausible — the orbital city is a major
contractor pipeline for outer-system work.

## Notes for play

- A Pavonis PC has the most *cosmopolitan* register
  of any Mars-PC option. International contacts,
  trade-and-business background, Lift-related
  expertise are all natural textures.
- A Pavonis Orbital backstory is useful for PCs who
  need to plausibly have spent time off-Mars in
  contractor capacities.
- The strategic-significance frame makes Pavonis a
  useful narrative anchor for Mars-related plot
  developments. If the Mars dimension of the
  *Halyard* contract becomes politically significant
  in late acts, Pavonis is where the political
  temperature rises first.
