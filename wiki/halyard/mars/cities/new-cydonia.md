# New Cydonia

New Cydonia is the regional capital of the Northern
Lowlands and the largest city of Mars's contemporary
frontier. Founded 2287 in the southern Acidalia basin
as a deliberately-planned regional administrative
center, the city has grown to 18 million residents in
sixty-one years — a growth rate unmatched anywhere
else on Mars.

The city's character is *frontier-modern, agricultural-
adjacent, scientifically-oriented*. New Cydonia
represents the *new Mars* in its most articulate
form: a city built consciously, around the
terraforming project's most ambitious lowland-
agricultural component, with a population that
arrived overwhelmingly within the past two
generations.

## Site

New Cydonia sits in the southern Acidalia basin at
approximately 35°N latitude, 25°W longitude, at
elevations between -3.6 km and -2.8 km below the
Martian datum (the basin floor). The site's
selection in 2284 was based on:

- Atmospheric pressure (among the densest on Mars)
- Proximity to Lake Acidalia (then expanding;
  the lake reached the city's northern outskirts
  by 2310)
- Access to agricultural development zones
- Strategic position for coordinating across the
  three Lowlands basins

The city's atmospheric environment at lake-shore
level is approximately 0.45 bar — substantially better
than highland Mars, comparable to the Hellas
peripheral zones, requiring filter masks for outdoor
activity but tolerable for extended periods.

## Layout

### The Capital District

The original 2287 city core. Government and
administrative buildings, the main university, the
cultural center. The architecture is *frontier-modern*
— mid-rise vertical districts (8 to 12 stories
typical, with a few taller arcology buildings),
generous public spaces, the deliberate inclusion of
greenhouse-park hybrid environments. Population ~3M.

### The Agricultural Research Belt

A district of greenhouse complexes, experimental
farms, and the University of New Cydonia's agricultural
research facilities. Most of Mars's agricultural
research happens here. Population ~2M.

### The Residential Districts

Multiple districts of mid-rise residential
construction, mostly built 2310s-2340s as the city
grew. The architecture is consistent (the city has
strict design codes); the population is younger
than the planetary average. Population ~9M.

### Lake-Shore District

The northern district along Lake Acidalia's shore.
Recreational, residential, and commercial. The
*New Cydonia Lake Walk* is the city's most-used
public space. Population ~4M.

## The Mars Atmospheric Authority's lowland research center

New Cydonia hosts the MAA's principal lowland
research operation. The Authority's lowland research
center is one of the largest single research
operations on Mars; it employs approximately 84,000
people directly and shapes the broader scientific-and-
academic ecosystem of the city.

The center's work focuses on:

- Atmospheric pressure expansion (lowering the
  pressure threshold for various human activities)
- Ecosystem development (the introduction of Earth-
  origin species adapted to Mars conditions)
- Long-term climate modeling (the projections that
  guide MAA strategic planning)
- Pre-conversion preparation (planning for the
  transition from filter-mask life to mask-free
  life, which the MAA projects as achievable in
  the lowlands within a century)

The center's results have been substantial. The
*lowland atmospheric breakthrough* of 2317 — when
basin-floor pressure crossed thresholds that allowed
multiple Earth-origin species to be established —
was substantially New Cydonia's work.

## Politics

New Cydonia is the political center of the Northern
Lowlands. The city's congressional delegation has been
the most influential Lowlands representation in the
Mars Congress; New Cydonia's senators hold senior
committee positions on the Lowlands Caucus's behalf.

The current Mayor is Hira Khan (also the Lowlands
Caucus chair in the Senate; the dual role is
characteristic of the Lowlands' political culture,
which integrates regional and federal politics
unusually closely). Khan is the rising star of the
post-Tharsis political generation.

The city's political character is *populist-
progressive*. New Cydonia has been a strong voice
for:

- Continued atmospheric-engineering investment
- Agricultural-policy support
- Frontier-development funding
- Skepticism of older Hellas-Tharsis political
  establishments

## Economy

New Cydonia's economy is roughly $0.8T UN-equivalent.
Sectors:

- **Atmospheric and ecosystem research** — the
  largest single sector; mostly MAA-funded
- **Agriculture and agricultural research** — both
  the agricultural production base and the research
  sector that supports it
- **Higher education** — the University of New
  Cydonia's enrollment is approximately 380,000
- **Government** — Lowlands regional administration
  and federal-government regional offices
- **Light manufacturing** — agricultural equipment,
  greenhouse infrastructure, processed-food
  production

## Cultural texture

New Cydonia's cultural register is *new-frontier-
scientific*:

- **The pioneer-progressive register.** The cultural
  production engages with the *making* of the new
  Mars. Literature, music, and visual art treat
  agricultural transformation, ecosystem development,
  and atmospheric change as primary subjects.
- **The agricultural literacy.** Most New Cydonia
  residents have direct or near-direct experience
  with agriculture. The cultural references are
  agricultural; the calendar follows growing
  seasons.
- **The scientific-research register.** The MAA's
  research presence shapes the city's intellectual
  life. Academic lectures, scientific publications,
  and research-popularization are major cultural
  activities.
- **The young-and-new register.** The population's
  median age is 36 (vs. 38 planetary). The cultural
  production has the energy of new arrival, the
  willingness to try new forms, the slightly
  unfinished quality that comes with rapid growth.
- **The lake aesthetic.** Lake Acidalia, expanding
  steadily, dominates much of the city's
  landscape and aesthetic. The cultural production
  engages with the lake constantly — its growth,
  its ecosystem, its presence.

## New Cydonia and the *Halyard*

No member of the *Halyard*'s standing crew is New
Cydonia-born. The city's agricultural-research and
atmospheric-engineering specializations are not
directly relevant to the campaign's main plot lines.

## Notes for play

- A New Cydonia PC will be more *progressive,
  pioneering, scientifically engaged* than other
  Mars-PC options.
- The agricultural-or-research backstory is natural
  for New Cydonia PCs and useful for the campaign's
  scientific-investigation register.
- The young-frontier register is a good fit for PCs
  who want to be specifically *new Mars* in their
  identity, distinct from the older Tharsis-Hellas
  political-cultural traditions.
