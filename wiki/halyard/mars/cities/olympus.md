# Olympus

Olympus is the largest city on the Tharsis Plateau, one of
the four largest cities on Mars (with Hellas, Pavonis, and
Coprates), and the cultural capital of the Tharsis
republican-industrial tradition. Its 32 million residents
live in a city built into the lower slopes of Olympus Mons
— the largest volcano in the solar system, 21 km tall — at
elevations from 6 to 11 km above the Martian datum.

The city's name is, of course, the volcano's. Olympus is
unembarrassed about being *built on a god*. The city's
self-image, the cultural production, the political register
all draw on the proximity to Olympus Mons in ways the
city's residents take seriously.

## Site

Olympus sits on the southeastern flank of Olympus Mons, in
the *aureole* — the broad apron of slumped volcanic
material that surrounds the volcano's base. The city's
elevation profile rises from 6 km above datum at the
lower city to 11 km at the upper terraces.

The atmospheric profile makes the city's vertical extent
significant: outdoor activity at the lower city requires
filter masks but is tolerable; activity at the upper
terraces requires respirators and is uncomfortable for
extended periods. The city has substantial atmospheric
engineering — pressurized public spaces, residential
arcologies with internal atmospheric control, a network
of pressurized-corridor connections between major
buildings — that allows residents to live across the
elevation range without continuous suit use.

## Layout

### The Lower City

The lower-elevation district. Most residential
construction; the working-class districts; the city's
substantial industrial-housing complex serving the
nearby Olympus Industrial District. Population ~14M.

### The Mid-City

The commercial and cultural center. The University of
Olympus, the major museums, the cultural venues, the
shopping districts. Population ~10M.

### The Academy District

The Mars Naval Academy's secondary campus and its
surrounding service infrastructure. The campus sits at
about 8 km elevation; the academy's grounds include the
*Mars Naval Memorial* (the largest single MCRN
commemorative site on the planet). Population ~3M
(Academy and adjacent).

### The Upper Terraces

The most-prestigious residential district at high
elevation. The view from the upper terraces — looking
across the Tharsis plateau, with the smaller Tharsis
volcanoes visible on a clear day — is one of Mars's
recognized scenic vistas. Population ~3M.

### The Industrial Belt

To the east of the city proper, the Olympus Industrial
District — the largest manufacturing complex on Mars.
The IBD includes:

- The Tharsis Heavy Industries complex (largest single
  manufacturing facility on Mars; primarily heavy
  machinery and structural components)
- The Olympus Materials Authority (the second-largest
  metallurgical complex on Mars after Ascraeus)
- The Olympus-Pavonis Logistics Network (the rail and
  cargo systems connecting the IBD to Pavonis's space
  elevator)

Population ~2M (industrial-housing).

## The volcano

Olympus Mons is the city's defining feature. The
21-km-tall volcano rises west of the city; the upper
slopes are scientific reserve (the Mars Atmospheric
Authority's Olympus Summit Station is the highest
permanently-staffed installation on Mars at 19 km
elevation).

The volcano is geologically inactive on time scales
relevant to human habitation. The last documented
eruption was approximately 25 million years ago; the
volcano's rate of activity, calculated from regional
geology, suggests another eruption is possible within
the next several million years but not within human
historical time.

The volcano's cultural significance is enormous. Olympus
appears in:

- The Republic's political iconography (Mars's federal
  seal includes a stylized Olympus profile)
- The city's commercial branding (Olympus-made products
  are marked with a stylized volcano outline)
- Cultural production at every level (Olympus
  literature, music, painting, film)
- The everyday vocabulary (*from the volcano*,
  meaning *from Olympus the city*; *climbing the
  mountain*, meaning *making one's career in
  Olympus*; *the volcano sees you*, a Tharsis
  encouragement)

## Politics

Olympus is the political center of the Tharsis caucus.
The city's political register is *industrial-republican-
hard-line*: pro-Republic, pro-MCRN, pro-terraforming,
suspicious of Earth, suspicious of cosmopolitan
moderation.

The current Mayor is Elena Tanaka-Voss, a former MCRN
officer (and a relative of President Voss-Tanaka; the
political class on Mars is small). Tanaka-Voss has been
a credible voice for the next generation of Tharsis
political leadership.

The city's congressional delegation has historically held
senior positions in the federal government; many of
Mars's most prominent political figures across the
Republic's history have been from Olympus.

## Economy

Olympus's economy is roughly $1.4T UN-equivalent — second
on Mars only to the greater Hellas region. Sectors:

- **Heavy manufacturing** — dominant. Olympus
  Industrial District accounts for ~22% of Mars's
  total manufacturing output.
- **Defense industry** — substantial. Olympus is the
  primary procurement source for MCRN heavy components.
- **Higher education** — the University of Olympus and
  the Mars Naval Academy's secondary campus.
- **Cultural production** — the Olympus literary
  scene, the city's symphony, the major museums.
- **Logistics** — Olympus-Pavonis interface for the
  space-elevator's cargo throughput.

## Cultural texture

Olympus's cultural register is distinctive:

- **The volcano-aesthetic.** The city's cultural
  production engages with Olympus Mons constantly. The
  literature, music, and visual art treat the volcano
  as a primary subject. The Olympus Symphony's
  signature piece is *Olympus* (composed 2284, still
  performed regularly).
- **The industrial-builder register.** The Tharsis
  cultural identity is most concentrated here. The
  city celebrates engineers, builders, makers; the
  major civic monuments are to industrial achievement
  rather than to political-historical events.
- **The republican formality.** Public-political
  speech in Olympus is more formal than elsewhere on
  Mars. Civic ceremonies, federal-government
  observances, public commemorations all run with a
  particular gravity.
- **The Academy substrate.** The Mars Naval Academy's
  presence shapes a substantial fraction of the
  city's social life. Academy cadets, graduates,
  faculty, and their families are visible everywhere;
  the city's social calendar is heavily MCRN-marked.
- **The altitude register.** Olympus residents take
  the elevation seriously. The cultural production
  engages with thin air, with the body's adaptation,
  with the physical *up-ness* of the city.

## Olympus and the *Halyard*

Captain Iona Marsh has institutional connections to the
Mars Naval Academy's Olympus campus — she taught a
visiting-officer course there during a 2336 sabbatical.
Her contacts at the Olympus Academy are part of why she
has credibility with MCRN officers; the Olympus
network is the senior network in MCRN officer culture.

Two of the *Halyard*'s standing crew (Korvaly and the
captain) have institutional Olympus connections in their
backgrounds.

## Notes for play

- An Olympus PC will be more *industrial-republican*
  than other Mars-PC options. The volcano-aesthetic
  and the MCRN background are useful textures.
- The upper-terrace prestige is a useful character
  beat: an Olympus PC from the upper terraces is
  *establishment* in a way that a lower-city Olympus
  PC is not.
- The Olympus Industrial District background is
  natural for engineering-focused PCs.
