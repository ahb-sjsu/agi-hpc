# Coprates City

Coprates City is the largest city in the Valles
Marineris region, the principal river-port at the
canyon's western end, and the Republic's third-largest
city after Hellas and Olympus. The 28 million Coprates
residents inhabit the canyon-mouth metropolitan area
where the Valles river system effectively begins —
where the Tharsis-fed inflow becomes the navigable
*Marineris* and where the Valles trade corridor
begins.

The city is *industrial-commercial-river*. Its character
combines Tharsis-influence (industrial production), Valles-
character (river-life, water-aware), and its own
distinctive role as the *gateway* between the upland
plateau and the canyon-floor world.

## Site

Coprates sits at the western mouth of Valles Marineris,
at the intersection between the Tharsis Plateau's eastern
escarpment and the Valles canyon system. The city's
elevation profile is dramatic: the upper city sits at
about 1.5 km above the Martian datum (level with the
plateau); the lower city descends to -3 km below the
datum (in the canyon). The Coprates Falls — a
constructed waterfall where the upper Marineris flows
over the plateau's edge into the canyon — is the city's
most-photographed feature.

The atmospheric profile reflects the elevation: the
lower-city level has substantially thicker atmosphere
than the upper-city level. The city's atmospheric
engineering manages the gradient through pressurized
elevators and corridor systems connecting the levels.

## Layout

### Upper Coprates

The plateau-level district. Industrial and commercial.
The Coprates Industrial District (one of the major
manufacturing complexes feeding the Lift via Pavonis)
is here. Population ~10M.

### The Falls Approach

The transitional district where the upper-and-lower
cities meet. Includes the Coprates Falls themselves
(the constructed waterfall and its surrounding
park-and-promenade) and the elevator-and-corridor
infrastructure connecting the two levels. Population
~4M.

### Lower Coprates

The canyon-floor district. River-and-port focused;
the city's residential center; the working-class
districts. Population ~12M. The lower city's
atmospheric ease is similar to Melas — outdoor cafes,
parks, riverside promenades are extensively used.

### The River District

The commercial waterfront where the Marineris river
begins its eastern flow. Major riverbarge construction
and maintenance facilities, the Coprates River
Authority, the Mars Rivership Federation's headquarters.
Population ~2M.

## The river infrastructure

Coprates's role as the *origin point* of the navigable
Marineris makes it the river system's principal
infrastructure hub. The river management, flow control,
sediment management, and ecology coordination operations
all run from Coprates.

The Coprates River Authority is one of Mars's most
operationally important agencies. It manages:

- The water-flow rate from the Tharsis-fed inflow
- The seasonal flow patterns (deliberately calibrated
  to mimic Earth's pre-modern Nile)
- The riverbarge traffic-control systems
- The riparian-ecosystem monitoring and management
- The flood-control infrastructure
- The water-quality monitoring across the river's
  length

A failure at Coprates would cascade across the entire
Valles river system. The Authority's facilities are
correspondingly hardened; the political-staffing of the
Authority is treated with seriousness.

## Politics

Coprates's politics are *industrial-Valles*. The city's
congressional delegation tends to align with the Valles
caucus on most issues but with stronger industrial-
manufacturing emphasis than the rest of the Valles.

The current Mayor is Camila Voss-Rossi, a former
metallurgical engineer with deep ties to the Coprates
Industrial District. The city's political register is
*pragmatic-trade-industrial*.

## Economy

Coprates's economy is roughly $1.1T UN-equivalent — the
fourth-largest single-city economy on Mars. Sectors:

- **Heavy manufacturing** — substantial; the Coprates
  Industrial District is one of Mars's major
  manufacturing zones
- **River infrastructure and services** — the
  Coprates River Authority and associated services
- **Trade and logistics** — the gateway between the
  Tharsis plateau and the Valles canyon system
- **Riverbarge construction and maintenance** —
  Coprates's barge yards build a substantial fraction
  of the Valles riverbarge fleet
- **Higher education** — the Coprates Polytechnic is
  Mars's principal river-engineering institution

## Cultural texture

Coprates's cultural register combines Tharsis and Valles
elements:

- **The river-industrial register.** Coprates's
  cultural production engages with both *making* and
  *flowing*. The literature, music, and visual arts
  combine industrial themes (workers, factories,
  metallurgy) with river themes (flow, navigation,
  ecology).
- **The Falls aesthetic.** The Coprates Falls are
  one of Mars's most iconic visual landmarks. The
  imagery appears in cultural production constantly.
- **The riverbarge connection.** Coprates is one of
  the major centers of riverbarge culture; many
  riverbarge Martians have homeport associations
  here. The city's cultural life is partially shaped
  by the riverbarge subculture's presence.
- **The vertical city.** The dramatic elevation
  difference between upper and lower Coprates
  produces a specific cultural awareness: the city
  *visibly* operates at different altitudes; the
  cultural production engages with the gradient.

## Coprates and the *Halyard*

No member of the *Halyard*'s standing crew is Coprates-
born. The Coprates Industrial District is, however, the
manufacturing source for several of the *Halyard*'s
mid-life refit components; the ship's last major refit
in 2344 sourced equipment from Coprates.

## Notes for play

- A Coprates PC has access to both Tharsis-industrial
  and Valles-river cultural elements. The combination
  is distinctive.
- The river-engineering background is a useful PC
  texture for technical roles; Coprates produces
  particularly strong civil and hydraulic engineers.
- The Falls imagery is a useful tonal reference — a
  PC's flashback to the Falls is an evocative scene
  that communicates Coprates origin without
  exposition.
