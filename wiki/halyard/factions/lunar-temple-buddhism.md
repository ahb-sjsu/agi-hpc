# Lunar Temple Buddhism

The Lunar Temple is the dominant Buddhist tradition in
human space, the major innovation in twenty-third-
century religious life, and one of the most
operationally significant religious organizations in
deep-range work. The tradition has approximately 400
million adherents worldwide; its monastic discipline
and lay practice have shaped the contemplative culture
of Earth as well as Luna.

## Origin

The Lunar Temple emerged in the early Lunar
industrialization era as a synthesis of:

- **Theravada Buddhism** — particularly the strict
  monastic discipline (vinaya)
- **Zen** — particularly the contemplative
  techniques and the integration of practice with
  daily work
- **Lunar station-monastic adaptations** — practical
  modifications for life in low-gravity, sealed-
  environment communities
- **Twentieth-century scientific-philosophical
  Buddhism** — particularly the contemplative
  practice traditions emphasized by various
  Western Buddhist teachers

The tradition's founding lineage traces to **Master
Yoshiko Hara-Brennt**, a Theravada-trained Buddhist
who emigrated to Luna in 2226 and established the
first Lunar Temple monastery at Copernicus in 2241.
The tradition expanded substantially through the
2240s-2280s, with major monasteries founded across
Luna and (later) Earth, Mars, and selected Belt
locations.

## Practice

The Lunar Temple's practice has distinctive features:

### Monastic discipline

- Three-decade-long monastic training cycle
  (substantially longer than most other Buddhist
  traditions)
- Strict vinaya discipline with adaptations for
  station and ship life
- Monks are *not* expected to remain monastic for
  life; many monks complete training and return to
  lay practice with the discipline preserved
- Approximately 28,000 active monks across all
  monasteries (a small fraction of the
  tradition's total membership)

### Lay practice

The Lunar Temple's lay practice is markedly different
from monastic practice:

- **The seated minute** — a one-minute meditation
  intended to be done at intervals throughout the
  day, often at chimes integrated into station and
  city public-address systems. The seated minute is
  the tradition's most-spread practice and has been
  adopted by many non-Buddhists.
- **Daily meditation** — typically 15-30 minutes,
  morning or evening. Most regular Lunar Temple
  practitioners do this.
- **Weekly practice meetings** — at the local
  temple. Includes group meditation, dharma
  talks, communal practice.
- **Annual retreats** — typically 7-14 days at a
  monastery or retreat center. Most committed
  practitioners do at least one annual retreat.
- **The work-as-practice teaching** — particularly
  important for industrial Lunar contexts. The
  tradition emphasizes that ordinary work, done
  with attention, is a form of practice.

### Doctrinal emphases

- *Less metaphysical commitment* than most older
  Buddhist traditions. Lunar Temple teaching is
  often described as "Buddhism oriented toward
  inner stability rather than salvation."
- *Strong emphasis on contemporary application* —
  the tradition engages directly with the
  philosophical questions of contemporary life
  (cosmological scale, technological change,
  off-world existence).
- *Compatibility with scientific knowledge* —
  the tradition has been deliberate about not
  contradicting established science; the
  metaphysical claims that older Buddhist
  traditions sometimes make are typically softened
  or reinterpreted.

## Distribution

The tradition's adherents are distributed:

- **Luna** — about 60 million adherents, the
  tradition's heartland. Most major Lunar cities
  have substantial temples.
- **Earth** — about 280 million adherents, drawn
  primarily from the late twenty-second and
  twenty-third centuries. Heavy in East Asia,
  spreading globally.
- **Mars** — about 28 million adherents.
  Substantial in Hellas City and the Lowlands.
- **Belt** — about 24 million adherents.
  Substantial in spinner culture (the meditative
  practice fits ship-life patterns).
- **Outer system** — about 8 million adherents.
  Distributed across Saturnian system and selected
  outer-Belt populations.

## Major monasteries

- **Copernicus Monastery** (Luna) — the principal
  training monastery; founded 2241 by Master Hara-
  Brennt. Approximately 1,400 monks in residence.
- **Aristarchus Monastery** (Luna) — secondary
  Lunar monastery; specialty in industrial-
  contemplative integration. About 800 monks.
- **Tycho Monastery** (Luna) — third Lunar
  monastery; specialty in contemplative-political
  integration (the *engaged Buddhist* lineage).
  About 400 monks.
- **Honshu Monastery** (Earth, Japan) — the
  largest Earth-side Lunar Temple monastery.
  About 800 monks.
- **Lagos Monastery** (Earth, Nigeria) — the
  African Lunar Temple monastery. About 300 monks.
- **Hellas Monastery** (Mars) — the Mars-side
  monastery. About 400 monks.
- **Several smaller monasteries** worldwide.

## Deep-range work

The Lunar Temple has a substantial deep-range
operational presence:

- **Ship-side chaplaincy** — Lunar Temple monks
  occasionally serve as ship-side religious
  resources, particularly on long-duration
  vessels with substantial Buddhist crews
- **The contemplative-disciplines training** for
  deep-range workers — multi-day or multi-week
  preparation programs offered by Lunar Temple
  monasteries to deep-range contractors. Hundreds
  of *Halyard*-class crew complete such programs
  each year.
- **Long-retreat infrastructure** — the
  monasteries' retreat centers offer
  multi-month retreats specifically for
  contemplative preparation for deep-range work.

## The Lunar Temple and the *Halyard*

The *Halyard* contract does not include a Lunar
Temple monastic chaplain (unlike the Catholic
Society of the Long Watch chaplain). However:

- Several of the *Halyard*'s crew members likely
  have *some* Lunar Temple practice background.
  The seated-minute practice, in particular, is
  widespread among deep-range workers regardless
  of formal religious affiliation.
- The contemplative-preparation program at
  Copernicus Monastery is a likely backstory
  element for any of the *Halyard*'s
  long-experienced crew. Captain Marsh, in
  particular, has a documented Copernicus
  retreat in her career history (2331,
  three-month *long retreat*).

## Notable figures

- **Master Tenzin Aboubakar** — current senior
  abbot at Copernicus Monastery; the tradition's
  most senior living teacher.
- **Master Sara Kovac-Volkov** — Aristarchus
  Monastery; specialty in industrial-
  contemplative integration.
- **Master Adisa Owolabi** — Lagos Monastery;
  the tradition's most prominent Earth-side
  teacher.

## Notes for play

- A Lunar Temple PC has a *contemplative-
  disciplined* background that distinguishes them
  from most other PC religious-tradition options.
- The seated-minute practice is a useful
  *cross-tradition* element. Even non-Buddhist PCs
  may have adopted the seated-minute practice; it's
  widespread in deep-range communities.
- The contemplative-preparation training is a
  useful narrative resource. A PC who has done
  the training has *specific tools* for managing
  isolation, time, and psychological pressure.
