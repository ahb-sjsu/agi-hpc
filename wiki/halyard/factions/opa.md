# Outer Planets Alliance (OPA)

The OPA is the political voice — and only the voice — of the
Belt. It is not a state, not a government, and not, despite
the name, an alliance in the formal-treaty sense. It is a
federation of organizations: labor unions, trade guilds,
provincial associations, advocacy groups, and a small
residue of historical militias that have not yet been
formally retired.

The OPA was the *idea* that Belters had political identity
worth coordinating, before it was an institution, and that
sequence still shapes how it works.

## What it is, structurally

The OPA's central body is the **Plenum** — a quarterly
meeting of delegates from member organizations. The Plenum
sets the OPA's official positions on inter-civilization
issues, coordinates joint campaigns, and provides the
diplomatic interface between the Belt and Earth/Mars/Luna.

Member organizations include:

- **The Asteroid Miners' Federation** — about 1.4 million
  active miners across the inner Belt. The largest single
  member.
- **The Spinner Crews' Confederation** — the merchant marine
  unions. About 800,000 spinners.
- **The Station Workers' Association** — the largest of
  the station-workers' organizations, federating local
  unions on Ceres, Vesta, Pallas, Hygiea, and dozens of
  smaller stations.
- **The Engineers' Syndicate** — the technical professions.
  Smaller than the labor unions but disproportionately
  influential in OPA policy.
- **The Provincial Associations** — Ceres, Vesta, Pallas,
  Hygiea, Ganymede, Titan each send delegates.
- **The Cultural Federations** — the Lang Belta Society,
  the Belt Arts Council, the Belt Historical Association.
- **The Outer Coordination Office** (OCO) — the Trans-
  Neptunian and Kuiper-edge coordinating body, which has
  representation in the Plenum but operates with
  significant autonomy.

The OPA's permanent staff — about 1,200 employees — works
out of offices on Ceres, Tycho, and a smaller liaison office
on Earth (in Geneva). The staff supports the Plenum,
maintains diplomatic relationships, and runs the OPA's
information services.

## What it is not

The OPA is not:

- **A government.** It cannot tax, conscript, or legislate.
  Member organizations remain sovereign in their own
  spheres.
- **A military.** It does not field naval forces. Its
  member organizations include some armed groups (mostly
  legacy; current activity is minimal).
- **Unanimous.** Its member organizations frequently
  disagree publicly. The Plenum's resolutions are advisory.
- **The whole Belt.** Many Belt residents are not
  affiliated with any OPA member organization. The OPA
  represents *organized* Belter politics.

## Origin

The OPA emerged from the labor disputes of the 2270s and
2280s. The proximate causes were:

- The **Tycho-Ceres Strike** of 2278, which united miners
  on multiple bodies for the first time around shared
  grievances.
- The **2281 Compact-of-the-Asteroids**, in which 14 Belt
  labor organizations formally agreed to coordinated
  bargaining with Earth and Mars employers.
- The **Belter Cultural Renaissance** of the late 2280s,
  which gave the political movement an identity that
  transcended labor specifics.

The Plenum itself first convened in 2289. The OPA's
official founding date is 14 March 2289, deliberately
chosen to echo Mars's 2272 Declaration date.

## Internal politics

The OPA's internal politics fragments along several axes:

### The Inner-Outer axis

Inner-Belt organizations (Ceres, Vesta, Pallas) have
priorities that don't fully align with Outer-Belt
organizations (Ganymede, Titan, the Kuiper edge). Inner-Belt
issues are more about Earth-relations and trade; Outer-Belt
issues are more about local autonomy and frontier
governance.

### The Old Guard vs. New Caucus

The Old Guard — surviving members of the founding
generation, conservative on OPA structure, deferential to
the labor unions — versus the New Caucus, which favors more
formal institutional development and has stronger
representation from cultural and engineering federations.

### The Mars Question

Should the OPA-MCR relationship be warmer (the *Solidarity
Caucus*) or cooler (the *Independence Bloc*)? The
Solidarity argument is that Mars and the Belt share an
off-world interest against Earth dominance; the Independence
argument is that Mars is just another inner-system power
and the Belt should treat it as such.

### The Earth Question

How antagonistic should the OPA's posture toward Earth be?
The full spectrum runs from *commercial accommodation* (the
Cunha line, dominant on Ceres) through *negotiated
opposition* (the Frontier League line, dominant in the
spinner unions) to *active resistance* (the Beratnas line,
mostly historical now).

## Key figures

The current senior figures of the OPA:

- **Naomi Daniyal** — Spokeswoman of the OPA, since 2342.
  Ceres-based, comes from the Cunha Caucus background.
  Widely respected; the OPA's external face. Personal
  reputation: warm, direct, competent.
- **Chrisjen Avasarala-Onaolapo** — Plenum Chair. Manages
  the Plenum's procedural work. Quiet but enormously
  influential.
- **Mateo Volkov** — Director of the Outer Coordination
  Office. Trans-Neptunian operations. Has, by Plenum-
  internal reputation, more autonomy than any other OPA
  director.
- **Yuki Singh-Brennt** — Chair of the Spinner Crews'
  Confederation. The most influential labor leader in the
  Belt.
- **Adisa Onoa** — Provincial Governor of Ceres (also
  named in the Ceres entry). The current most-prominent
  provincial politician.

The OPA's day-to-day work is mostly done at the secretariat
level by career staff, not by the high-profile politicians.

## Diplomatic relationships

- **With Earth (UN)** — Functional. The UN recognizes the
  OPA as the *de facto* spokesperson for Belt interests
  and negotiates with it on most Belt-related issues.
  The UN does not formally recognize the OPA as a state
  but treats it as a quasi-state for practical purposes.
- **With Mars (MCR)** — Cooperative-correct. The MCR
  recognizes the OPA. Bilateral relations have warmed
  since the 2336 crisis. There are no major active
  disputes.
- **With Luna** — Pragmatic. The Lunar Republic and the
  OPA cooperate on shared outer-system issues. Trade
  relationships are well-established.
- **With other Belt-internal authorities** — The OPA's
  relationship with the various provincial governments
  (Ceres Provincial Council, Titan Council, etc.) is
  *complementary* — the OPA handles inter-civilization
  issues, the provinces handle local governance.

## The Mao-Kwikowski relationship

Mao-Kwikowski Mercantile, the *Halyard*'s charter authority,
is not formally part of the OPA. Mao-Kwikowski is a
private Belt-flagged consortium with substantial
independence from the OPA structure.

The relationship between the OPA and Mao-Kwikowski has
historically been: cool but functional. Mao-Kwikowski's
labor practices are *acceptable* by OPA standards but not
exemplary. Mao-Kwikowski has resisted full Engineers'
Syndicate certification of its in-house engineering
divisions; the OPA has tolerated this because Mao-Kwikowski
provides employment to many Belters who would otherwise
struggle.

The *Halyard* contract is, from the OPA's perspective, a
private commercial venture by a Belt-flagged consortium.
The OCO has a file. The file is not large. The OPA's
position is *watch and wait*; whatever the *Halyard* finds,
the OPA will be among the first to know.

## What the OPA cares about

The OPA's standing strategic interests, ranked roughly:

1. The continued recognition of Belter cultural and political
   distinctiveness by Earth, Mars, and Luna.
2. The preservation of the Belt's economic role (resource
   extraction, manufacturing, transit).
3. The continued autonomy of the major Belt provinces
   from Earth or Mars administrative penetration.
4. The Enceladus Treaty (which most OPA members consider
   civilizationally important even if it doesn't directly
   benefit Belt interests).
5. The control of any first-contact scenario by Belt
   institutions, if first-contact occurs in the outer
   system.

That last point is relevant. The OPA's standing position on
non-human life — adopted at the Plenum of 2331 — is that
*any non-human contact in the outer system should be managed
through OPA-coordinated protocols, not unilaterally by
inner-system powers.* The position has never been tested.
The *Halyard* contract has the potential to test it.

## Notes for play

- An OPA-affiliated PC has political identity in a way
  that an Earth or Mars PC doesn't usually have. They
  *belong* to organizations and those organizations have
  positions.
- The OPA is *not* the campaign's antagonist. It is also
  not, exactly, the campaign's ally. It is a watching
  presence — a third interest with views about what the
  *Halyard* finds.
- A surprise OPA appearance in late acts (an Outer
  Coordination Office liaison, a Spinner-Confederation
  observer who happens to be on Ceres when the *Halyard*
  returns) is a useful narrative resource.
