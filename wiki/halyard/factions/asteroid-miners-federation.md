# The Asteroid Miners' Federation

The Asteroid Miners' Federation is the largest single
member organization of the OPA, the Belt's principal
labor union for mining and resource-extraction
workers, and the political-cultural anchor of the
*Frontier League* faction within the broader OPA
politics. The AMF's 1.4 million active members work
the asteroid Belt's mining operations across hundreds
of bodies; the Federation's institutional weight
within the OPA Plenum reflects this concentration.

## Structure

The AMF is organized federally. Component
organizations:

- **Local mining unions** — approximately 240 local
  unions across the Belt's mining bodies. Each
  local handles its own collective bargaining,
  workplace safety, and local political
  organization.
- **Provincial federations** — coordinating bodies
  at the provincial level (Vesta, Pallas, Hygiea,
  Ganymede-Callisto, etc.)
- **The AMF Council** — the Federation's central
  governance body, composed of provincial
  representatives. Meets quarterly.
- **The AMF Executive** — the Federation's senior
  leadership; elected by the Council.

The AMF's permanent staff is small (approximately
800 employees); the Federation's strength is in its
membership and its democratic-internal organization
rather than in centralized administrative
infrastructure.

## Membership

The Federation's 1.4 million members include:

- **Asteroid surface miners** — workers who do
  direct extraction work on Belt bodies
- **Spinner-mine crew** — the mobile mining
  operations that follow specific extraction
  programs
- **Refinery workers** — the metallurgical staff at
  the major Belt refining centers (heavily
  concentrated at Vesta, Callisto, Aristarchus on
  Luna)
- **Specialty mining workers** — workers in the
  more specialized extraction operations (Io's
  volcanic-cycle mining, Hyperion's irregular-
  geometry work, the polar-volatile extraction
  operations)

Membership is *not* compulsory; the Federation has
been deliberately voluntary across its history. Most
mining workers join (the institutional benefits are
substantial), but non-membership is a recognized
choice.

## Political role

The AMF's political role within the OPA is
substantial. The Federation:

- **Holds the largest single OPA Plenum
  representation** of any member organization
- **Anchors the Frontier League faction** — the
  more confrontational pro-labor faction within
  the OPA's internal politics
- **Has been the institutional driver** of
  several major Belt political moments,
  including the 2278 *Tycho-Ceres Strike*, the
  2336 Belt crisis, and various smaller labor
  actions across the past century

The Federation's political register is *unflinchingly
pro-labor, suspicious of Inner-System cooperation,
committed to OPA institutional independence*. The
Federation's leaders tend to be more confrontational
than the broader OPA Plenum's average.

## Leadership

The AMF Executive's senior leadership:

- **Yuki Singh-Brennt** — actually the Spinner Crews'
  Confederation chair (separate organization), but
  her career trajectory illustrates how AMF and SCC
  leadership often overlap. AMF members frequently
  rotate through SCC roles and vice versa.
- **Hara Volkov** — the AMF Executive's current
  chair. Vesta-based; former Vestan local union
  organizer; elected to the Federation chair in
  2342. Volkov is one of the OPA Plenum's most
  important figures; the Frontier League's most
  visible voice.
- **Marcus Akande** — the AMF's Vice-Chair.
  Callisto-based.
- **Adisa Mendoza-Brennt** — the AMF's senior
  political coordinator; handles the Federation's
  diplomatic-and-political-relationship
  responsibilities with other OPA member
  organizations and external bodies.

## Historical significance

The AMF traces its institutional origin to the
**1989 Tycho Compact** (the smaller miners' union
that consolidated Belt mining-union representation in
the late twenty-first century) and the **2168
Asteroid Federation Charter** (the formal
constitution of the federation as known today).

The Federation has been *load-bearing* in Belt
political-historical events:

- **The 2278 Tycho-Ceres Strike** — the AMF was the
  primary organizing institution. The strike was a
  founding moment for the broader OPA.
- **The 2336 Belt crisis** — the AMF's political
  positioning shaped the crisis's trajectory; the
  Federation's confrontational register pushed
  the OPA's negotiating posture toward more
  aggressive demands.
- **The 2339 Ceres Riots** — the AMF was *not* the
  primary organizer (Cunha-Caucus organizations
  were more central) but the Federation's
  political weight shaped the aftermath.

## Standing strategic interests

The AMF's contemporary strategic priorities:

- **Worker safety** — particularly important given
  the dangerous conditions of asteroid mining
  work. The Federation has been a consistent
  voice for stricter safety standards.
- **Wage and conditions** — collective bargaining
  with the consortium-level employers (most
  asteroid mining is done under multi-year
  consortium contracts)
- **Health-care access** — for the long-term
  health consequences of low-gravity work
- **Pension and retirement** — for the substantial
  population of retired-or-disabled mining
  workers
- **Workers' compensation** — for the (frequent)
  industrial accidents in mining work
- **Institutional independence** — preserving the
  Federation's autonomy from Inner-System
  regulatory pressure and from broader OPA
  centralization

## Notable historical figures

- **Master Belter Adisa Brennt** (1942-2018) — the
  Federation's mythological founder; her speeches
  during the 2168 Charter convention are still
  read at AMF meetings.
- **Margaret Volkonsky-Kwabena** (2087-2174) — the
  Federation's most-decorated organizer; led the
  2150s mining safety reforms.
- **Hara Akande** (2178-2271) — the Federation's
  representative during the OPA's emergence in the
  late 2280s; a foundational figure of modern OPA
  politics.

## The AMF and the *Halyard*

The AMF has no direct interest in the *Halyard*
contract; the expedition is not a mining operation
and does not involve Federation-represented workers
in a primary way.

However, the *Halyard*'s eventual findings will
involve OPA-level political conversations in which
the AMF's voice will be heard. The Federation's
position is, generally, *pro-Belt-control* of any
Belt-relevant scientific findings and *pro-strict-
governance* of anything that could be exploited by
Inner-System interests.

## Notes for play

- An AMF-affiliated PC will have a distinctive
  *labor-OPA-Frontier-League* register that
  distinguishes them from other Belt-PC options.
- The Federation's political register is useful
  for PCs who want to engage with Belt internal
  politics during play.
- The mining-work background is natural for AMF
  PCs; the Federation's culture is *unflinchingly
  working-class*.
