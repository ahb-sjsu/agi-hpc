# Protogen Applied Sciences

Protogen Applied Sciences is a multinational research
corporation headquartered in Geneva, with substantial
research and operational facilities on Earth, Luna, Mars,
and the Belt. Its public profile is that of a high-end
scientific research firm — pharmaceuticals, advanced
materials, planetary science, exotic-environment biology.
Its private profile is somewhat more complicated.

## Public face

Protogen's public-facing operations are legitimately large
and legitimately scientific:

- **Pharmaceutical research** — about $14 billion in
  annual R&D spending. Protogen-developed drugs include
  several of the top-tier mood regulators currently
  prescribed on Earth.
- **Advanced materials** — composites, structural alloys,
  fusion-reactor components. Protogen materials are in
  most of the high-end spacecraft built since 2310.
- **Planetary science research** — Protogen funds 40+
  outer-system survey missions, university partnerships,
  and data-collection contracts. The *Halyard* expedition
  is, in this sense, one of many Protogen-adjacent
  research projects.
- **Exotic-environment biology** — laboratory work on
  extremophiles, space-adapted microbiology, and (the
  most public-facing of this work) the development of
  Mars-atmosphere-tolerant agricultural varieties.

Annual revenue is roughly $96 billion UN-equivalent. The
corporation is publicly traded on the Earth and Lunar
exchanges, with major shareholders including several
sovereign-wealth funds, three pension consortia, and a
nontrivial fraction held by founding-family entities.

The current CEO is **Director Mariana Faustino**, a former
Lunar Industrial Authority executive.

## Private face

Protogen's private operations include several activities
that are legal but politically sensitive, and a handful of
activities that are not:

### Privileged-information collection

Protogen operates a sophisticated data-collection apparatus
that is technically distinct from intelligence work but
functionally similar. The corporation maintains:

- **Outer-system survey access** through commercial and
  academic partnerships (the *Halyard* contract is one
  data point in this network).
- **Scientific-publication monitoring** — early access to
  pre-prints, conference proceedings, and grant applications
  across most of the major scientific disciplines.
- **Government-research contractor relationships** — many
  Protogen scientists hold dual appointments with UN
  agencies (FSec, Outer Authority, Mars liaison
  programs), giving Protogen privileged information about
  government research priorities.
- **Personal-network cultivation** — the corporation
  maintains close relationships with about 1,200 senior
  scientists in human space. Halverson is one of these.

This information network is mostly legal. The corporation's
privacy and IP-handling practices have, however, been the
subject of multiple investigations and one substantial 2331
fine (the *Cassiopeia Settlement*, a $4 billion penalty for
research-data appropriation from a UN-funded program).

### Recovery operations

Protogen runs a small but significant **Recovery Division**
specializing in the retrieval of materials, samples, and
data from research operations that have, for one reason or
another, become *complicated*. Recovery Division operates
quietly, in cooperation with private contractors, and has
been involved in roughly twenty significant outer-system
recovery missions in the past 30 years.

The Recovery Division's activities are legal in most
jurisdictions but operate in legal gray areas. The
Division's direct relationship with the *Halyard*
contract is not in the public record, but FSec
analysis (Halverson file, item 3) suggests that Recovery
Division has operational interest in any Nithon-origin
material the *Halyard* might bring back.

### The Special Programs Branch

The corporation's **Special Programs Branch** (SPB) is the
most opaque of Protogen's structures. SPB's activities are
not publicly described. Internal Protogen materials suggest
SPB conducts research on:

- Anomalous biological samples (provenance unspecified)
- Materials with unusual property profiles
- Information artifacts of unconventional origin

SPB facilities are located on Earth (in the Antarctic
Industrial Zone), on Luna (a small dedicated annex at
Aristarchus), and at one trans-Neptunian station whose
location Protogen does not disclose. SPB's annual budget
is roughly $1.2 billion, an unusually large fraction for an
opaque program at a publicly traded corporation.

## Halverson's relationship

Dr. Iris Halverson, the *Halyard*'s xenoarchaeology
consultant, has a complicated employment history with
Protogen:

- **2329–2335**: Senior Research Fellow, Protogen Earth.
  Specialty: pre-spaceflight contact-protocol research
  (the academic-political work on how human institutions
  should respond to non-human life if encountered).
- **2335–2342**: Resigned from Protogen, taking academic
  appointments (University of Lagos, University of
  Nairobi).
- **2342–2347**: External consultant to Protogen
  Recovery Division (semi-public; the consulting
  relationship is on her CV).
- **2347–present**: Apparently no formal Protogen
  relationship. The *Halyard* contract is structured as
  Mao-Kwikowski to Halverson, with no Protogen visible
  in the chain.

The FSec analysis (Halverson file, item 3) assesses with
high confidence that Halverson's Protogen relationship has
not, in fact, ended; rather, the apparent termination is
operational cover. The *Halyard* expedition's findings —
whatever they are — are, FSec believes, intended to be
delivered to Protogen Recovery Division on return, with
a fraction redirected to the consortium's nominal interests
as a deniability layer.

## How Protogen relates to other factions

- **With the UN/UNN/FSec**: A complicated, partially
  cooperative relationship. Protogen contracts are a major
  source of UN scientific work; FSec has Protogen on its
  surveillance lists; the corporation's lawyers and FSec
  lawyers are familiar with each other.
- **With Mars/MCR**: Protogen's Mars operations are
  smaller and more carefully separated. Mars's posture
  toward Protogen is *suspicious-cooperative*. The MCR's
  intelligence service watches Protogen Mars operations
  closely.
- **With the Belt/OPA**: Protogen operates extensively in
  the Belt. The relationship with the OPA has been
  steadily friction-laden — Protogen's labor practices,
  data-handling, and Recovery Division operations are
  all sources of complaint.
- **With Mao-Kwikowski**: Officially independent. Practically
  intertwined. The two corporations have shared shareholders
  in several jurisdictions, joint-venture relationships in
  three sectors, and a long history of personnel rotating
  between them.

## Notes for play

- An ARTEMIS query about Protogen returns surface detail
  (the corporation's public profile) but nothing about the
  Recovery Division or SPB. ARTEMIS is, in fact, an
  artifact of Halverson's Protogen relationship — the
  handheld is Protogen-issued, and ARTEMIS's training
  data was curated by Protogen researchers. This is a
  substantive plot point and should not surface to
  players in casual conversation.
- A SIGMA-4 query about Protogen returns the public face
  with no awareness of the Recovery Division relationship
  to the *Halyard* contract.
- The Protogen connection is one of the campaign's
  load-bearing reveals. It is not the deepest secret;
  the Chamber and the actual non-human contact are
  deeper. But the Protogen reveal is the *gate* — once
  the players understand the corporate context, the
  later reveals land harder.
- Recovery Division operatives are, if introduced,
  professional and lethal. They are not the campaign's
  primary antagonists but they will protect their
  interests.
