# MCR Intelligence Branch

(Both ARTEMIS and SIGMA-4 visibility set to ``unknown``
— the MIB's specific operations are classified above
the AIs' allocations. Both AIs know the agency exists
and have its public-record information; neither has
access to operational details.)

The MCR Intelligence Branch is the Republic's principal
intelligence service, the institutional counterpart to
Earth's FSec, and one of the major intelligence
services in human space. The MIB's operations cover
counterintelligence on Mars, intelligence collection
abroad, and substantial signals-intelligence capacity
across the inner system.

The MIB is professionally regarded as *more capable
per unit* than FSec — smaller, more focused, more
operationally selective. Earth's intelligence
establishment treats MIB operations with substantial
respect.

## Origin

The MIB was constituted 2275 from the intelligence-
collection arm of the post-secession Mars Defense
Force. The original MIB was small (perhaps 800
personnel) and focused on counter-Earth-intelligence
operations.

The agency expanded substantially through the
2290s-2310s as the broader Mars-Earth strategic
balance evolved. By the 2320s the MIB had achieved
roughly its current scale and operational profile.

The 2330s naval buildout era produced parallel
expansion in MIB capabilities; the agency's current
capacity is substantially larger than its 2310-era
predecessor.

## Structure

The MIB has four operational divisions:

### Division of Operations (DO)

The collection arm. Approximately 6,400 personnel.
Geographic and functional bureaus:

- **Earth Bureau** — operations against UN, FSec,
  UNN, and Earth-private targets
- **Belt Bureau** — operations across the Belt;
  smaller than Earth Bureau but more politically
  sensitive
- **Lunar Bureau** — operations against the Lunar
  Republic; smaller; mostly liaison rather than
  collection
- **Outer Systems Bureau** — operations beyond the
  inner system; small but specialized
- **Counterintelligence Division** — operations on
  Mars itself; substantial

### Division of Analysis (DA)

The all-source analytic shop. Approximately 2,800
personnel. Produces the daily *President's
Intelligence Brief* and the weekly *Cabinet
Synopsis*.

### Division of Technical Intelligence (DTI)

Cryptographic services, signals intelligence, and
specialized technical operations. Approximately
1,800 personnel.

### Division of Special Operations (DSO)

The classified arm. Approximately 600 personnel.
Operations are not publicly described.

## Doctrine

MIB doctrine is summarized internally as **the
Republican Imperatives**:

1. **Constitutional restraint.** MIB operates under
   substantial constitutional and statutory
   constraints, including warrant requirements for
   most domestic operations, judicial review of
   prosecutions, and a ban on assassination of
   MCR citizens.
2. **The republican ethic.** MIB officers are
   trained to view their role as *protecting the
   Republic from threats* rather than as agents of
   any particular political faction. The agency's
   institutional culture has been deliberate about
   distinguishing the Republic's interests from any
   particular government's preferences.
3. **The strategic competence imperative.** MIB
   leadership has emphasized building *operational
   competence* — the agency's reputation depends on
   being good at its work.

The Republican Imperatives have been honored more
consistently than equivalent doctrine at FSec. MIB
has had remarkably few public scandals across its
history; the agency's institutional discipline is
substantial.

## Jurisdiction and operations

MIB's operations face the standard intelligence-
service jurisdictional constraints:

- **Domestic operations** — limited to MCR
  citizens and entities; require warrant or
  emergency authorization
- **External operations** — broader latitude;
  most operations against UN-jurisdiction or
  OPA-jurisdiction targets are conducted with
  *plausible deniability* protections
- **Cooperative operations** — MIB has cooperative
  arrangements with the OPA's relevant security
  services, with the Lunar Republic's intelligence
  service, and with several private intelligence
  organizations (Lloyd's intelligence, in particular)

## The MIB's posture toward FSec

MIB and FSec have a *correct, professional, and
mutually wary* relationship. The two agencies:

- Maintain liaison contacts at multiple levels
- Share intelligence on issues of mutual interest
  (mostly counter-OPA and counter-private-
  consortium concerns)
- Conduct *regular* counterintelligence operations
  against each other; both agencies expect the
  other to be running operations against them
- Avoid direct operational conflict; the
  professional norm is *don't expose each other's
  operations unless absolutely necessary*

Both agencies have long-running operations against
each other that have, at this point, become the
*background condition* of the Mars-Earth intelligence
relationship.

## The MIB and Mythos-adjacent operations

(*This section is Keeper-only.*)

The MIB has *limited but real* awareness of Mythos-
adjacent activity. Specifically:

- **The Pallas reserve** — the MIB has more
  detailed information about the Pallas reserve
  than the Belt-internal political establishment
  would assume. Mars-OPA cooperation on the
  reserve has produced cross-information flows.
- **The Mars-side anomaly** (the Lambert glacier
  feature mentioned in the polar-caps entry) — the
  MIB is the principal investigator of this
  feature in collaboration with the Mars
  Exploration Reserve. The investigation is
  substantially classified.
- **The Halverson file** — the MIB maintains a
  file on Halverson independently of FSec's. The
  MIB's file emphasizes Halverson's potential
  significance for Mars's strategic position
  rather than for Earth's federal interests.
- **The Halyard expedition** — tracked through
  Third Fleet's sensor coverage and through
  general intelligence collection. The MIB has
  not made the *Halyard* a priority target but
  is monitoring it with appropriate attention.

The MIB's standing position is *Republican
neutrality* — the agency's interest is in protecting
the Republic's interests, not in advancing any
particular outcome. If the *Halyard*'s findings
become politically consequential, the MIB will
analyze them carefully before recommending Mars
posture.

## The MIB and the *Halyard*

The MIB's interest in the *Halyard* expedition is
*professional but not active*. No MIB officers are
currently aboard the *Halyard*; no MIB operations
are currently directed at the contract.

If the *Halyard*'s findings produce significant
political consequences, the MIB's analytic and
operational responses will follow. A MIB officer
named **Senior Officer Kira Rossi** has been
assigned (since 2347) as the *Halyard* file's
primary analyst.

## Notes for play

- A current-serving MIB officer PC would be a
  major plot development. The Keeper should
  discuss this carefully at Session 0.
- Former MIB service is more plausible. The
  agency's career paths produce many ex-officers
  in private security, intelligence-adjacent
  consulting, and (for some) the broader Mars
  political establishment.
- The MIB is *not* the campaign's antagonist. The
  agency's professional discipline means MIB
  responses to the *Halyard*'s findings will be
  patient, careful, and deliberately not
  destabilizing.
- ARTEMIS will deflect on MIB-related queries.
  SIGMA-4 will give public-record information
  about the agency without operational details.
