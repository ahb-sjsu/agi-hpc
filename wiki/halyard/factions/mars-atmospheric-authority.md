# The Mars Atmospheric Authority

The Mars Atmospheric Authority is the institutional
embodiment of the Martian terraforming project, the
single largest employer on Mars after the federal
government, and the institutional anchor of the
Republic's civic religion (covered in
`mars/culture/terraforming-as-religion.md`). The MAA's
work has been the load-bearing project of the
Republic across its entire post-secession history;
the Authority's directors are politically influential
in ways that other agency directors are not.

## Mandate

The MAA was constituted 2284 from the institutional
remnant of the UN Atmospheric Initiative (the original
2098 program). The Authority's mandate:

- Continued atmospheric thickening across Mars
- Atmospheric composition management (oxygen,
  nitrogen, argon, trace gases)
- Climate management at planetary scale
- Ecosystem development (the introduction of Earth-
  origin species, ongoing ecosystem stewardship)
- Atmospheric monitoring and modeling
- Long-term planning for the eventual transition to
  *open habitation* — the projected end-state
  where surface environments do not require
  atmospheric augmentation

## Operations

The MAA operates across a range of scales:

### The Atmospheric Plants

The MAA operates 18 major *atmospheric plants*
distributed across Mars. These are large-scale
industrial facilities that:

- Release engineered gas mixtures into the atmosphere
- Manage planetary-scale atmospheric composition
- Operate continuously across decadal time scales

The largest plants are at Tharsis (the Tharsis
Atmospheric Plants, operating from Arsia and
Pavonis) and the Northern Lowlands (the Acidalia
Plant Complex). Each major plant employs 10,000-
40,000 people directly.

### The Polar Stations

The North and South Polar atmospheric monitoring and
research stations (mentioned in
`mars/regions/polar-caps.md`). The Polar stations are
the MAA's primary atmospheric-data collection points
and the institutional center of long-term modeling.

### The Lowland Research Center

The MAA's principal lowland research operation, based
at New Cydonia. The center coordinates the
agricultural-and-ecosystem development work that has
made the Northern Lowlands Mars's contemporary
agricultural breadbasket.

### The Strategic Planning Office

The MAA's headquarters operation in Hellas City. The
Office handles long-term modeling, policy
coordination with the federal government, and
international coordination with relevant Earth and
Belt institutions.

## Personnel

The MAA employs approximately 280,000 people directly.
The personnel structure:

- **Senior leadership** — the Director, the Deputy
  Directors, the heads of major programs
- **Scientific staff** — atmospheric scientists,
  climate modelers, biologists, ecologists
- **Engineering staff** — the engineers who design,
  operate, and maintain the atmospheric plants
- **Operational staff** — the workers who run the
  plants, monitoring stations, and field operations
- **Administrative staff** — the bureaucratic
  infrastructure

The MAA's hiring is competitive; positions are
prestigious; the workforce is highly trained. About
40% of MAA scientific staff hold advanced degrees.

## The MAA director

The MAA's director is, by long custom, a non-political
career civil servant. The director is appointed by
the President with Senate confirmation but is
*expected* to be apolitical and to serve across
multiple presidential administrations.

The current director is **Eduardo Mendiola-Akande**, a
career atmospheric scientist appointed in 2336 (during
the Vasquez administration). Mendiola-Akande has
served across two presidential administrations
without apparent political conflict.

## The 2326 spending crisis

The MAA's most famous institutional moment was the
2326 Spending Crisis. The Volkov-Tanaka administration
proposed substantial budget cuts that the MAA's then-
director (Maria Onaolapo-Volkov) refused to comply
with on the grounds that the cuts would damage long-
term terraforming goals.

The crisis produced a constitutional standoff: the
director was a presidential appointee but argued she
was institutionally obligated to her predecessor-
defined operational priorities; the President argued
his budgetary authority was clear. The standoff was
resolved when the President personally negotiated with
Onaolapo-Volkov and a modified budget was passed.

The crisis established a precedent: the MAA director
has unusual institutional independence within the
federal executive. Subsequent Presidents have been
careful about MAA budget proposals.

## The Authority's broader institutional role

Beyond its formal operations, the MAA functions as the
institutional embodiment of the Republic's civic
religion. The MAA:

- Conducts the seasonal terraforming ceremonies
  (mentioned in the civic-religion entry)
- Publishes the annual *Mars Atmospheric Report*,
  one of the most-read documents in Republican
  public discourse
- Coordinates the *Tree-Counting* and similar
  collective ceremonies
- Serves as the institutional reference for
  schools, universities, and cultural institutions
  on terraforming-related matters

This *quasi-religious* role gives the MAA influence
beyond its formal authority. When the MAA director
takes a public position, the Republican political
class pays attention.

## The MAA and the *Halyard*

The MAA has *no specific interest* in the *Halyard*
contract. The expedition's findings, whatever they
are, would not directly engage with the terraforming
project.

However:

- The MAA's *long-term-thinking* register is shared
  by Halverson's institutional culture; some of the
  MAA's senior staff have professional connections
  with Halverson's academic institutions
- The MAA's *patient-careful* register is, in many
  ways, the closest contemporary parallel to the
  Caretakers' register. Mars's culture of patient
  multi-generational work is the kind of approach
  the Caretakers find legible.

## Notes for play

- An MAA-employed PC is *uncommon* but useful. The
  scientific-civic-religion register provides
  distinctive character texture.
- The MAA's institutional culture is a useful
  reference for the campaign's late-act political
  conversations. Mars's experience of *successful
  long-term collective work* offers a frame the
  Earth political class doesn't have.
