# The Council of the Confluences

The Council of the Confluences is the most significant
contemporary institutional development in
post-Compact-era Islamic religious life — an informal
network of senior Sunni, Shi'a, and Ibadi scholars
that meets quarterly to coordinate scholarly responses
to questions affecting all Muslims. The Council has no
formal authority but its consensus statements are
widely respected; its 2304 *Statement of the
Confluences* on non-human life is one of the most
significant religious documents of the past century.

## Structure

The Council is *deliberately* informal. Key features:

- **Membership** — approximately 60 senior scholars
  (jurists, theologians, philosophers) drawn from
  across the major Sunni, Shi'a, and Ibadi
  traditions
- **Geographic distribution** — members from Cairo,
  Tehran, Istanbul, Jakarta, Lagos, Mumbai,
  Damascus, Riyadh, the Council includes scholars
  from each of the major Islamic intellectual
  centers
- **Convening** — quarterly meetings at rotating
  sites; the meetings are *consultative* rather
  than legislative; the Council reaches *consensus
  positions* rather than binding rulings
- **Permanent staff** — minimal; the Council
  operates through scholar-secretaries and
  associated institutions rather than a centralized
  bureaucracy

The Council's lack of formal authority is
deliberate. The historical Islamic scholarly
tradition has been suspicious of centralized religious
authority; the Council was designed to *coordinate
scholarship* without becoming a Vatican-equivalent.
The design has worked.

## Origin

The Council emerged in the 2240s-2270s as an
informal scholarly network. The 2271 *Compact-of-
Cairo* (a meeting of senior scholars convened to
address a specific contemporary question about
Muslim life under federal-tax authority) produced
the first *consensus statement* and became the
model for what the Council would become. The
quarterly-meeting pattern was established by 2284;
the Council has met continuously since.

## Major statements

The Council has issued several major consensus
statements across its history. The most
campaign-relevant:

### The 2304 Statement on Non-Human Life

Issued in response to the Europa Findings of 1298 and
the broader scientific-philosophical implications of
confirmed non-human (microbial) life. The statement's
key teaching:

> "The Creator's attention is not exhausted by what
> we have found, and what is found should be regarded
> with the courtesy proper to a creature of God. The
> discovery of intelligent non-human persons would
> raise questions that have not yet been settled in
> the scholarly tradition; we counsel that such
> questions, if they arise, be approached with
> humility, attention to the *adab* of encounter,
> and willingness to develop new understanding under
> the guidance of those qualified to lead it."

The statement parallels the Catholic Church's 2298
*Humani Dignitas Stellaris* in providing a
*placeholder* — a theological position that
recognizes non-human persons' moral standing without
prejudging the deeper questions.

### The 2331 Statement on Off-World Religious Practice

Addresses the practical questions of Islamic religious
practice in off-world conditions:

- *Salah* (prayer) timing under variable solar-day
  conditions
- *Qibla* (direction of Mecca) in off-world locations
- *Sawm* (fasting during Ramadan) in conditions of
  variable day-length
- Sacramental questions in spaceflight

The statement provided practical pastoral guidance
that has been widely accepted across Muslim
communities in space.

### The 2347 Statement on the Outer System

A recent statement addressing the specific religious-
philosophical situation of Muslim communities in the
Saturnian system and beyond. The statement
encourages *patient practice* and engagement with the
*long silences* of deep-range work.

## The Council and the Halyard

The Council has *no specific knowledge* of the
*Halyard* contract beyond what is publicly available.
However:

- The 2304 statement has prepared the major Islamic
  scholarly traditions for the discovery of
  intelligent non-human persons. The theological
  framework is in place.
- If the campaign reaches the Caretaker negotiations,
  Muslim PCs (and any Council-affiliated NPCs) will
  bring this scholarly framework to the conversation.
- The Council itself would, *after* the campaign's
  events become public, convene to address whatever
  has happened.

## Major figures

- **Sheikh Mustafa Aboubakar al-Lagosi** — the
  Council's most prominent contemporary voice;
  Lagos-based; the principal author of the 2347
  statement.
- **Ayatollah Hossein Mendiola-Reza** — the senior
  Shi'a scholar in the Council; Tehran-based.
- **Sheikh Adisa Volkov-Singh** — the Council's
  most prominent contemporary voice on
  contemporary-philosophical questions; Mumbai-
  based.
- **Sheikh Eduardo Rossi-Onaolapo** — the senior
  Council member from the Indonesian-Malaysian
  scholarly tradition; Jakarta-based.

## Notes for play

- A Muslim PC's relationship to the Council
  matters as backstory texture. *Council-aware*
  Muslim PCs (those whose religious life
  includes engagement with Council statements)
  have access to a substantial intellectual
  framework.
- The 2304 statement is, like the Catholic
  *Humani Dignitas Stellaris*, a useful
  narrative resource for the campaign's late-act
  philosophical conversations.
