# The Catholic Church in 2348

The Catholic Church is the largest single religious
institution in human space, with approximately 1.2
billion adherents, an institutional presence on every
inhabited body, and an operational continuity that
extends from the first century into the twenty-fourth.
The Church in 2348 is *smaller* than its
twenty-first-century peak, *more demographically
distributed* than it has ever been, and — in ways
that matter for the campaign — *theologically
prepared* for the discovery of non-human persons in
ways most other Earth institutions are not.

## Structure

The Church's institutional structure has been
preserved with minimal alteration across the
post-Compact era. Key features:

- **The Pope** — the bishop of Rome and the
  Church's senior figure. The current Pope is
  **Innocent XV** (elected 2336), originally
  Cardinal Aderemi Mendoza-Patel of Lagos.
  Innocent's tenure has emphasized engagement with
  the off-world communities and the integration
  of African-and-Asian theological traditions.
- **The College of Cardinals** — currently 218
  members. The College's geographic distribution
  has shifted substantially since 2025; African
  and Asian cardinals now constitute approximately
  60% of the College, with Latin American
  cardinals at 20% and European-North American at
  20%.
- **The Roman Curia** — the Church's central
  administrative apparatus. Headquartered in the
  rebuilt Vatican (Rome was extensively storm-
  damaged in the 2197 floods and rebuilt under the
  2204-2231 Renaissance Restoration program).
- **The diocesan structure** — bishops in roughly
  4,200 dioceses across human space, including:
  - Earth (3,800 dioceses)
  - Luna (28 dioceses)
  - Mars (380 dioceses)
  - Belt (160 dioceses; smaller because of the
    body's scattered population)
  - Outer system (12 dioceses; mostly attached to
    Saturnian system communities)

## Demographic distribution

The Church's adherents are distributed:

- **Africa** — 380 million. The continent's
  Catholic population is the largest single
  regional concentration; Catholic
  cultural-religious life in Africa is substantial.
- **Latin America** — 280 million. Historically
  the Church's center; demographically smaller now
  but culturally and theologically influential.
- **Asia** — 220 million. Heavy in the Philippines,
  South Korea, parts of Vietnam, Indonesia, and
  China.
- **North America** — 110 million. The Confederation
  Catholic population.
- **Europe** — 80 million. The historical heart;
  demographically depopulated.
- **Off-world** — about 130 million. Distributed
  across Luna, Mars, Belt, and outer system.

## Theological development

The Church's theological work since the late
twentieth century has been substantial. Three areas
of particular relevance for the campaign:

### The 2298 *Humani Dignitas Stellaris*

The encyclical that addresses the Church's position on
the discovery of non-human intelligent life. The
encyclical was issued in response to the Europa
Findings of that year and the broader scientific
implications of confirmed non-human (microbial) life.

Key teachings:

- Non-human intelligent beings, if discovered, are
  *creatures of God*
- The Church's obligation to such beings is "the
  courtesy proper to creatures of God"
- The Church does not "presume to specify in
  advance" the precise theological status of
  non-human persons
- The encyclical established a theological
  *placeholder* — a position that affirms
  non-human persons' moral standing without
  prejudging the deeper questions of their
  spiritual status

The encyclical was not unanimous. A small but
articulate theological school (sometimes called the
*restrictivist* school) argued for tighter
theological limits. The restrictivist position has
not been doctrinally embraced; *Humani Dignitas
Stellaris* remains the Church's official
position.

### The 2331 *Caritas in Cosmica*

A follow-on encyclical addressing the practical
implications of the 2298 teaching. The encyclical
provides:

- Pastoral guidance for clergy operating in the
  outer system
- Guidance on sacramental questions in spaceflight
  conditions
- A framework for theological engagement with
  non-human persons should they be encountered

The encyclical explicitly contemplates *first
contact* scenarios. It is, in retrospect, more
prophetic than its drafters intended.

### The 2346 *Lumen Stellarum*

A recent apostolic letter from Innocent XV
specifically addressing the contemplative life in
deep-range work. The letter encourages clergy and
laity in deep-range conditions to develop
*contemplative practices appropriate to long
silences*. The letter has been widely read in
deep-range communities, including by non-Catholics.

## Church operations in deep-range

The Church maintains a substantial deep-range pastoral
operation:

- **Outer-system chaplaincy** — clergy assigned to
  major deep-range vessels and stations
- **The Society of the Long Watch** — a relatively
  new religious community (founded 2287) of clergy
  specifically trained for deep-range work
- **Sacramental adaptations** — the Church has,
  across centuries, developed pastoral practice
  for distance, isolation, and the practical
  conditions of off-world life

The *Halyard* expedition includes a Society of the
Long Watch chaplain among its passenger manifest. The
chaplain — **Father Patel** — joined the expedition
at Ceres and will travel with the crew.

## The Church and the *Halyard*

Father Patel is a relevant minor NPC. He is:

- Approximately 60 years old
- Born in Mumbai; ordained in Bangalore in 2317
- Society of the Long Watch since 2336
- Three previous deep-range assignments before the
  *Halyard*
- Quiet, intellectually substantial, pastorally
  experienced

Patel's role on the *Halyard* is *chaplaincy* —
available to crew of any tradition (Catholic and
otherwise) for spiritual and pastoral support, but
without proselytization. The Society's training
emphasizes *being available* over *spreading the
faith*.

If the campaign reaches the Caretaker negotiations,
Patel is one of the crew members who will *think
through the theology* with substantial sophistication.
The 2298 and 2331 encyclicals give Patel an
articulate framework for engaging with the discovery
of intelligent non-human persons.

## Notes for play

- A Catholic PC's relationship with the Church
  matters as backstory texture. The off-world
  Catholic experience is distinctive (Father
  Patel's career path is one example of the
  Society of the Long Watch trajectory).
- The Church is a *resource* for the campaign's
  late-act philosophical conversations. Catholic
  PCs (and the chaplain Father Patel) bring
  theological vocabulary that the secular PCs
  lack.
- The 2298 encyclical's *placeholder* position is
  particularly useful narratively. The Church has
  prepared for the discovery of non-human persons
  *without committing to specific theological
  conclusions*. This is a feature, not a bug; the
  framework is robust precisely because it doesn't
  predict the specifics.
