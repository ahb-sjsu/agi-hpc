# Africa in 2348

Africa is the demographic, cultural, and increasingly political
center of Earth. With 5.2 billion residents — roughly a quarter
of Earth's population — and the most consistent post-twenty-
first-century growth of any continent, the African Secretariat
has spent the past 150 years rising from the periphery of the
old industrial-North-Atlantic order into the center of Earth's
self-image.

Most contemporary Earthers, asked where the cultural capital of
their planet is, would name a city in Africa.

## Demographic outline

Africa's 5.2 billion residents are unevenly distributed:

- **West Africa** — 1.4 billion. Lagos is the dominant city;
  Accra, Abidjan, Dakar are major regional centers. The
  Gulf of Guinea coastal corridor is the most densely
  urbanized stretch in the entire continent.
- **East Africa** — 1.1 billion. Nairobi, Addis Ababa, Dar es
  Salaam are the key cities. The East African Rift valley
  corridor is heavily urbanized; the highlands are denser
  than the coast.
- **North Africa** — 0.8 billion. Cairo remains the largest
  city; Casablanca, Tunis, Algiers are major. The Saharan
  margin has been substantially restored since the 2240s
  through the Sahel Greening Program.
- **Central Africa** — 0.9 billion. Kinshasa is the largest
  city. The Congo Basin remains the planet's most extensive
  intact tropical biome, partly by deliberate UN
  conservation policy.
- **Southern Africa** — 0.6 billion. Johannesburg-Pretoria
  is the regional megacity; Cape Town, Maputo, Luanda are
  major.
- **The Madagascar/Indian Ocean fringe** — 0.4 billion,
  including the rebuilt-coastal communities of Madagascar
  and the floating-platform habitats off the East African
  coast.

The continent's median age is 32 (vs. Earth's overall 46).
Africa's demographic curve, unlike most other continents,
remained strongly positive through the twenty-first century
and only began plateauing in the 2280s. The pediatric
population is large; the educational systems are strained but
generally functional; the labor market is younger and more
fluid than elsewhere on Earth.

## The cultural ascendancy

The African cultural ascendancy of the past century is one of
the most consequential shifts in human affairs since
secession. Specific markers:

- **Cinema and holo-serials.** The current generation of
  Earth's most-watched serial productions is shot in Lagos,
  Nairobi, Accra, and Lagos again. The hybrid English-Yoruba-
  Igbo creole that has become the de facto language of
  African popular culture is now intelligible to most
  Earthers under 50.
- **Music.** West African musical traditions have been the
  dominant influence on Earth's popular music since the
  2280s. The Lagos Conservatory of Music's archive is the
  largest single collection of pre-electronic African music
  in human space.
- **Literature.** The African Renaissance — a literary
  movement whose best-known figures (Onaolapo, Mbeki,
  Wuyt, Adichie-Akande) are taught in Earth schools
  worldwide — has shaped the contemporary Earth novel.
- **Cuisine.** West African and Ethiopian-influenced cuisines
  are now standard offerings in Earth's restaurant culture.
  *Suya*, *jollof*, *injera* are familiar to most
  Earthers regardless of background.
- **Philosophy.** The University of Lagos's Faculty of
  Speculative Studies and the University of Nairobi's
  Department of Cosmological Ethics produce most of the
  Earth-side public intellectuals on the *what does it
  mean to be a person in 2348* question. The African
  philosophical register — communalist, attentive to
  obligation, skeptical of pure individualism — has become
  the dominant Earth-public-discourse register.

## The Secretariat

The African Secretariat administers UN programs across the
continent under the staggered-rotation appointment system.
The current Secretariat (2342–2354) is led by Folami
Onaolapo, a former development economist whose program
emphasizes:

- Continued integration into UN federal structures
- Investment in education and the work-lottery's African
  tier
- The *Sahel Greening Program*, the continent-spanning
  ecological restoration effort that has been the
  Secretariat's signature project for two decades

Onaolapo's politics are pragmatic-centrist. She has been
described as *the most powerful woman on Earth* by the few
publications that pay attention to who actually exercises
political power in 2348. The description is roughly correct;
the African Secretariat has more institutional weight than
most parties to the question realize.

## Major cities

See `earth/cities/lagos.md` for Lagos detail. Other major
African cities, briefly:

### Nairobi (24M)

The intellectual capital of East Africa. The University of
Nairobi is the second-largest African university; the city's
publishing industry is the continent's most prolific. Nairobi's
international airport (Jomo Kenyatta International) is the
continent's largest air-traffic hub. The city has a distinctive
high-altitude climate (1,800 m above sea level) that gives it
an unusually mild local atmosphere by twenty-third-century
standards.

### Cairo (28M)

Older than any other major city in human space. The Cairo of
2348 is a layered city — ancient Egypt, Islamic Cairo,
nineteenth-century European-influenced Cairo, twentieth-
century Cairo, and the contemporary vertical-Cairo coexist in
overlapping districts. The city is the cultural capital of
North Africa and the seat of the Council of the Confluences
(Islamic scholarly coordination body). The Egyptian
Antiquities Museum's collection is the largest in human space
and has been the subject of multiple successful
repatriation campaigns; most pre-Roman Egyptian artifacts that
were in European or American museums in 2025 are now in
Cairo.

### Kinshasa (22M)

The largest city in Central Africa and the major industrial
center for tropical-resources processing. Kinshasa's
relationship with the Congo Basin biome is a major political
issue: the city's economy depends on the basin, and the basin's
preservation is the continent's largest conservation
commitment. The balance has held since the 2270s, sometimes
narrowly.

### Johannesburg-Pretoria (21M)

The Southern African megacity, formed by the merger of two
separate cities in 2236. Continues to be the financial center
of Southern Africa. The Pretoria district is the seat of the
South African national government; the Johannesburg district
is the commercial center.

### Addis Ababa (18M)

Ethiopia's capital and the cultural center of the Horn of
Africa. The Ethiopian Orthodox Christian tradition remains
visible in the city's institutional life; the Addis Ababa
University's Department of African History is one of the
continent's most prestigious. The city's elevation (~2,400 m)
gives it a cool climate that has been a post-climate-shift
asset.

### Lagos (64M)

Covered separately. The largest city on Earth.

## The Sahel Greening Program

Africa's signature continental-scale environmental
intervention. Begun 2247 as a UN-funded reforestation and
soil-restoration program for the Sahel band south of the
Sahara, the SGP has, over a century, gradually pushed back
the desert margin by an average of 180 km along its full
length.

The SGP is the continent's longest-running collective project
and the closest thing African political culture has to a
*civic religion* equivalent of the Mars terraforming
program. Most Africans grew up with the SGP as a fact of
collective life. The annual *Tree-Counting* ceremony, when
each Sahelian community reports its annual contribution to
the program, is a significant cultural event.

## The continent's posture

Africa's collective posture toward the rest of human space:

- **Toward Earth-other** — confident. African political
  culture in 2348 does not feel itself in subordinate
  position to Europe, the Americas, or Asia. The continent
  has been the demographic and cultural center of Earth
  long enough that the post-colonial cultural anxieties of
  the early twenty-first century are mostly historical
  curiosities.
- **Toward Mars** — friendly distance. Most Africans regard
  Mars with mild curiosity rather than the weight of
  Earth-Mars-relations the European and North American
  political classes give it.
- **Toward the Belt** — sympathetic. Africa's
  post-colonial political memory aligns with Belter
  experience in ways the African political class has not
  failed to notice. The OPA has substantial diplomatic
  presence in Lagos and Nairobi.
- **Toward Luna** — pragmatic. Major trade relationships,
  little political tension.

## Notes for play

- An African PC is one of the *easier* Earth backgrounds for
  the campaign tonally. The cultural confidence is real;
  the post-Compact identity is settled; the character has
  a clear contemporary frame.
- Lagos and Nairobi are the most likely Earth-side
  origins for a sophisticated, polyglot Earth PC. The
  African Secretariat's outer-system work-lottery is one
  of the largest sources of contractor talent on Earth.
- The cultural prestige of African letters means an
  African-born PC is *expected* to have read more widely
  than a comparable PC from another continent. This is a
  texture asset.
