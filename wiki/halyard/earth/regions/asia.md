# Asia in 2348

Asia carries 8.8 billion people, more than half of Earth's
population, distributed across the densest urban environments
in human history. The continent is, by any reasonable measure,
the *industrial* center of Earth — its manufacturing, its
energy infrastructure, its supply chains, its electronics and
biotech and pharmaceutical sectors all operate at scales the
rest of Earth merely participates in.

It is also Earth's most internally varied region. Asia in
2348 contains the most ancient continuous urban traditions
(Beijing, Xi'an, Varanasi, Damascus); the most aggressively
modern megacities (Shanghai, Mumbai, Jakarta, Manila); and
the largest range of religious, linguistic, and political
cultures of any continent.

## Demographic outline

The continent's 8.8 billion are distributed approximately:

- **South Asia** — 3.9 billion. India is by far the largest
  country in human space at 2.1 billion residents; Bangladesh,
  Pakistan, Sri Lanka, Nepal, the smaller mountain states.
  Mumbai, Delhi, Dhaka, Karachi, Kolkata are the major
  megacities.
- **East Asia** — 2.1 billion. China at 1.4 billion remains
  Earth's second-largest country; Japan, the Korean
  Peninsula, Taiwan, Mongolia round out the region. Shanghai,
  Beijing, Tokyo, Seoul, Guangzhou-Shenzhen are the major
  cities.
- **Southeast Asia** — 1.4 billion. Indonesia is the
  region's demographic anchor at 480 million; the
  Philippines, Vietnam, Thailand, Malaysia, the smaller
  states. Jakarta, Manila, Ho Chi Minh, Bangkok, Kuala
  Lumpur are the major cities.
- **Central Asia** — 0.8 billion. The post-Soviet states
  plus Iran and Afghanistan. The region's population grew
  significantly in the late twenty-first century and has
  been politically and culturally distinct from the larger
  zones above.
- **West Asia** — 0.6 billion. The Arabian Peninsula, the
  Levant, Anatolia (politically grouped with Europe).
  Tehran, Riyadh, Damascus, Baghdad are the major cities.

The Asian Secretariat treats *Anatolia* as part of
Europe-Anatolia for governance purposes, despite the
geographic placement; this is a 2243 administrative decision
that has held.

## The dense urban environments

Asia's contribution to the contemporary urban form is the
*hyper-dense vertical megacity*. Whereas African and
European cities have been *vertical*, Asian cities have been
*compounded vertical* — floor density per square kilometer
that Earthers from other continents find disorienting on
first visit.

Specific markers:

- **Shanghai** has districts where the population density
  exceeds 200,000 per square kilometer at street level
  (compared to a Lagos arcology district's roughly
  120,000). The city's vertical structure is so deeply
  layered that some inner districts have not had direct
  sunlight at street level in 80 years; they have engineered
  daylight via spectrum-corrected lighting that is
  functionally indistinguishable.
- **Mumbai** is, by floor area per square kilometer, the
  densest single city on Earth. Its arcology cluster
  (the *Sky-Cluster*, completed 2237 and continuously
  expanded) is the original twenty-third-century vertical
  city; Lagos, Shanghai, and São Paulo all built theirs
  with Mumbai as the explicit reference.
- **Tokyo** retains its twenty-first-century pattern of
  *low-rise dense* urban form in much of its area, despite
  the broader Asian shift to vertical. The city's
  preservation of older patterns is partly cultural choice
  and partly a consequence of the Pacific seismic risk
  profile, which discourages the tallest constructions.
- **Jakarta** is the planet's most architecturally
  innovative megacity. The Pacific-platform habitats off
  its coast are the largest floating urban installations
  on Earth.

## The industrial role

Asia is Earth's manufacturing base in 2348. The numbers:

- Approximately 64% of Earth's heavy industrial output
  originates in East and Southeast Asia
- Approximately 58% of Earth's pharmaceutical
  manufacturing is in South Asia (Mumbai-Pune, Hyderabad,
  Dhaka)
- Approximately 71% of Earth's electronics-and-computing
  manufacturing is in East Asia
- Approximately 48% of Earth's textile and apparel
  manufacturing is in Southeast Asia and South Asia
- Approximately 80% of Earth's bulk-cargo merchant marine
  is registered in Asian ports

This manufacturing dominance has political consequences. The
Asian Secretariat has, since the 2280s, been the most
fiscally significant of the continental Secretariats; its
budgetary contributions to UN federal programs are the
largest of any continent. The Secretariat's political weight
in UN federal institutions is correspondingly large.

## The cultural plurality

Asia is the most internally varied of Earth's continents.
The major religious traditions — Hinduism, Islam,
Buddhism, Christianity, Sikhism, Jainism, Shinto, Taoism,
Confucianism — all retain substantial active membership.
The major language families — Indo-European,
Sino-Tibetan, Austronesian, Dravidian, Altaic, Semitic —
are all alive and politically meaningful. Most Asians are
multilingual to a degree that Earthers from other continents
find impressive.

The cultural production is correspondingly varied. Mumbai's
Bollywood-inheritor cinema produces more films per year
than Lagos and Hollywood combined; Shanghai's animation
industry dominates East Asian children's entertainment;
Tokyo's literary fiction is the most-translated of any
city on Earth; Jakarta's music scene exports across
Southeast Asia and the Belt.

The cultural production is *not* unified. There is no
single *Asian* aesthetic in 2348; there are dozens of
distinct cultural threads, in conversation but not
synthesizing.

## Politics

Asian politics in 2348 are stable, fragmented, and dense.

The Asian Secretariat operates above a layered structure of
national governments — China, India, Japan, Indonesia,
South Korea, the Philippines, Thailand, Vietnam, Iran,
the Central Asian republics, the Arabian Peninsula states,
and many smaller polities. Each national government retains
constitutional authority on local matters; the Secretariat
handles continental coordination and UN federal
implementation.

The current Asian Secretary is Anjali Banerjee, an Indian
former physicist whose tenure (2342–2354) has emphasized
infrastructure investment and the *Asian-African Trade
Compact*, a 2344 free-trade agreement with the African
Secretariat that has substantially reduced cross-continental
tariffs.

Internal Asian politics have several persistent tensions:

- **China-India** — the two most populous countries on
  Earth maintain a complicated bilateral relationship.
  Trade is enormous; political cooperation is selective;
  border disputes have been settled but not forgotten.
- **The Sino-Russian relationship** — Russia, technically
  geographically European but politically heavily
  Asian-aligned, maintains a close relationship with
  China that Russian European partners find irritating.
- **The Arabian Peninsula compact** — the post-2240s
  consolidation of the Arabian Peninsula states into a
  loose confederation that has, on most issues, been the
  most coherent regional bloc within Asia.
- **The Indonesia-Philippines axis** — the rising power of
  the Southeast Asian region, which has been challenging
  the older Asian power centers (China, India, Japan)
  for decades.

## Daily life

The average Asian Earther in 2348 lives in a vertical
district in a megacity, takes mid-level transit to
contracted work, and spends evenings in some combination
of street life, holographic entertainment, and the
contemplative practices that have become widely adopted
across the continent (Buddhist meditation, yoga, the
various secular contemplative movements).

The food culture is extraordinarily varied. The signature
Earth-cultural achievement of Asia is, arguably, that any
metropolitan resident has access to dozens of different
regional cuisines within a 30-minute commute. *I had
Vietnamese for breakfast and Korean for lunch* is
unremarkable in any major Asian city.

Religious practice is widespread but quiet. Most Asian
Earthers consider their religious commitments private; the
public-square religious display that occasionally features
on Earth in some other regions is rare in Asia outside of
festivals.

## The continent's posture

- **Toward Earth-other** — collegial. Asian political
  culture has been the most consistently UN-federal-loyal
  of the continents.
- **Toward Mars** — practical. Asia has the largest
  trade relationship with Mars of any continent. Mars-
  Asian engineering exchanges are common.
- **Toward the Belt** — practical-cooperative. Asian
  shipping interests dominate Belt-Earth trade.
- **Toward Luna** — close. Most Earth-Luna passenger
  traffic flies through Asian ports. Many older Asian
  Earthers retire to Luna.

## Notes for play

- An Asian PC's specific national/regional/cultural
  background matters more than for an Earth PC from other
  continents. *Mumbai-Indian, Shanghai-Chinese, Tokyo-
  Japanese, Jakarta-Indonesian* are different starting
  points with different cultural textures.
- The multilingualism is real. An Asian PC speaks at least
  three languages fluently by default, and many speak four
  or five.
- The post-Compact Asian political consciousness is
  *quiet patriotism* — the assumption that Asia is the
  most important continent on Earth, expressed without
  particular emphasis. Other Earthers find this slightly
  strange.
