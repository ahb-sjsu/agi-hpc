# The Antarctic Industrial Zone

(Note: ARTEMIS is set to ``unknown`` for this region. The
zone has classified components — specifically the Elder
Thing recovery sites — that ARTEMIS is not authorized to
discuss in detail. SIGMA-4, as a ship-mind, has the
public-record knowledge of the zone's industrial
operations.)

The Antarctic Industrial Zone is the only Earth region that
is, formally, a UN federal territory rather than a
member-state territory. Established 2228 under the Antarctic
Mining Treaty, the AIZ has operated for 120 years as the
continent's largest single industrial complex — a sprawl of
mining, refining, fusion power, and logistics infrastructure
across roughly 12 million square kilometers of polar
landscape.

It is also home to the largest concentration of classified
research operations on Earth, of which the most
significant — though one most Earthers will never hear of —
is the joint UN-Protogen Elder Thing research program at
the deep-interior sites.

## Population and structure

The AIZ has approximately 0.4 billion residents, almost all
on three-year contract rotations. Permanent residents are
few; most are senior administrators, technical specialists,
or families of long-term contract workers.

The settlement pattern follows the operational structure:

- **The Coastal Strip.** The continent's perimeter, where
  most population concentrates. McMurdo (3M), Davis (2M),
  Esperanza (1.5M), Mawson (1.5M), Sanae (1M), and a
  long tail of smaller stations. The coastal cities are
  the AIZ's primary residential and administrative
  centers; passenger traffic, shipping, and most surface
  transport originate here.
- **The Mining Corridors.** Three major industrial
  corridors run from the coast into the continental
  interior: the Ross-Beardmore corridor (carrying the
  largest of the East Antarctic mining operations), the
  Filchner-Ronne corridor (West Antarctic operations), and
  the Amery-Lambert corridor (the rare-earths corridor).
  Each corridor has its own operational center and
  population (typically 30-80M per corridor).
- **The Polar Plateau Stations.** The continental interior,
  where the largest fusion power complexes operate and
  where the deepest mining occurs. The Polar Plateau is
  also where the classified research sites are located.
- **The orbital interface.** The AIZ has dedicated
  orbital infrastructure — the *Antarctic Lift* (a
  cable-elevator system completed 2287 between McMurdo
  and a Lagrangian counterweight) — that handles bulk-
  cargo lift to Earth orbit. Most Earth-side bulk space
  freight originates from the AIZ.

## Industrial role

The AIZ is the single largest concentrated industrial
zone on Earth. Specifically:

- **Rare earths.** The AIZ produces about 38% of Earth's
  rare-earth metals, most of which feed Asian and
  Confederation electronics manufacturing.
- **Fusion fuels.** The AIZ's deep-ice deuterium and
  helium-3 separation plants are the largest on Earth.
- **Bulk minerals.** Iron, copper, aluminum, titanium —
  Antarctic operations supply roughly 22% of Earth's
  metallic raw materials.
- **Fusion power.** The Polar Plateau power stations
  generate enough electricity to power the entire
  continent and substantial export to South America and
  Australasia. The AIZ is Earth's largest single
  fusion-power generation zone.
- **Bulk-cargo space lift.** Roughly 60% of Earth's
  bulk-cargo space freight originates from the AIZ
  (the rest of Earth's space freight is split between
  Lagos, Wenchang, Sriharikota, and the smaller ports;
  the Lift's economics dominate the bulk market).

## Governance

The AIZ is administered as a UN federal territory by the
**Antarctic Authority**, an independent UN agency reporting
to the Secretary-General. The Authority's structure:

- **The Director** — currently Yousef Talib-Wesson, a
  former engineer with a long career in Antarctic
  operations
- **The Operational Council** — managers of the three
  corridors and the coastal-strip cities
- **The Industrial Compact** — the contractual structure
  that governs the major industrial operators (mostly
  large multinationals: Sinopec-Antarctic, Schneider-
  Polar, Mitsubishi-Polar, the Lloyd's underwriting
  consortium that handles risk for most operations)
- **The Workers' Federation** — the labor organization
  that handles the rotation-contract negotiations and
  worker welfare

Most ordinary AIZ residents are aware of three things: the
Authority issues the rules, the Industrial Compact pays the
wages, and the Workers' Federation handles complaints.
This works.

## The classified component

The AIZ's classified research operations are physically
located at three Polar Plateau sites:

- **Site Endurance** — the principal Elder Thing recovery
  site, at the South Pole-Aitken approach. The 2284
  recovery operation that produced most of Earth's
  Elder Thing biological samples occurred here. Continued
  operations are joint UN-Protogen.
- **Site Resolution** — a secondary research facility,
  300 km from Endurance, focusing on materials analysis
  of recovered Elder Thing artifacts.
- **Site Patience** — the most-classified, the smallest,
  the one whose existence is *not* in the public record.
  Patience is where the SPB's three Antarctic shoggoths
  are studied in long-term observational containment.

These operations are *not* visible to ordinary AIZ
residents. The sites operate under cover of standard
mining-research operations; the personnel are vetted
through FSec and Protogen joint clearance procedures;
the supply lines are routed through a specific subset of
the Endurance station's nominal-civilian supply chain.

The classified nature of these operations is not a
campaign secret; the Chamber knows about all three sites,
the Mi-go know about Endurance and Resolution (and
about Patience indirectly), the Caretakers are aware of
all three. What is *campaign-secret* is the Halverson-
Protogen relationship to these sites and the role
that Patience-site research has played in preparing
Halverson for the *Halyard* contract.

## The labor force

Most AIZ workers are on three-year contracts. The
contracts pay roughly 4× the equivalent Earth-side wage
for similar work, plus a hardship allowance, plus
health-and-safety provisions that exceed normal
workplace standards. The contracts attract a specific
demographic: workers who want substantial income for
defined periods, willing to live in a polar climate, and
typically without strong family commitments at home.

The post-contract trajectory is well-mapped:
- About 40% of contract workers complete a single
  three-year rotation and return to Earth-mainland life
  with substantial savings
- About 35% take two or more rotations across a career,
  separated by Earth-side periods
- About 15% become long-term AIZ residents (5+
  rotations or permanent positions in the
  administrative tier)
- About 10% transition to off-world contracts (the
  *Halyard* expedition's outer-system tier draws
  substantially from this population — Captain Marsh's
  career began with a single AIZ rotation she didn't
  finish, departing for UNN service partway through)

The work is hard. The pay is good. The culture is
specifically AIZ — slightly insular, heavily oriented
around shift schedules and corridor identities,
distinct from any other Earth-side region.

## Notable cities

### McMurdo (3M)

The AIZ's administrative center. The Authority's
headquarters, the largest passenger-rocket terminal,
the principal Earth-side end of the Antarctic Lift.
Most foreign visitors to the AIZ pass through McMurdo;
the city has, by AIZ standards, an extensive cultural
and recreational infrastructure (universities,
performing-arts venues, restaurant districts).

### Davis (2M)

The principal East Antarctic coastal city. The Ross-
Beardmore corridor's coastal terminus. Davis has been
the AIZ's secondary administrative center for a
century; the city's cultural life is more
working-class than McMurdo's.

### Esperanza (1.5M)

The Antarctic Peninsula city. The AIZ's only
Spanish-speaking-majority city; the city retains
strong cultural ties to Argentina and Chile.

### Mawson (1.5M)

The Indian Ocean coastal city. Heavily involved in
Amery-Lambert corridor operations; the city's
population is unusually internationally diverse.

### Sanae (1M)

The Atlantic coastal city. The Filchner-Ronne corridor's
terminus. The city's political character is
*conservative-corporate* by AIZ standards; many of
the senior industrial-operator administrative offices
are here.

## Culture

The AIZ has its own cultural character despite being
constituted from a constantly rotating population:

- **The shift-cycle life.** The dominant social
  institution is the rotation contract. Everyone is
  somewhere in the cycle — three years in, year one,
  year two, year three, returning to Earth, returning
  to the AIZ for another contract. Conversations
  acknowledge this constantly.
- **The corridor identity.** Workers in the same
  corridor — Ross-Beardmore, Filchner-Ronne, Amery-
  Lambert — develop strong shared identities that
  cross national-origin lines.
- **The polar register.** AIZ life is *cold*, *quiet*,
  *organized around interior climate-controlled
  spaces*. The cultural production reflects this — a
  literature of confinement and patience, an
  architecture of internal environments, a music that
  is largely indoor and acoustic.
- **The pragmatic register.** AIZ workers have, almost
  uniformly, a strong pragmatic orientation. The
  philosophical and contemplative cultures of other
  Earth regions are present but subordinate to a
  *get the work done* register.

## Notes for play

- An AIZ-rotation backstory is one of the most useful
  for a *Halyard* PC. The transition from AIZ contract
  work to outer-system contract work is well-trodden.
  Many of the *Halyard*'s Earth-born crew (Captain
  Marsh, Halden Cross, possibly others) have AIZ
  experience in their backgrounds.
- AIZ-rotation PCs have a recognizable *register* — a
  pragmatic competence, a comfort with confined
  environments, an indifference to weather complaints.
- The classified Antarctic operations are *Keeper-only*
  knowledge. Most PCs do not know about them. PCs with
  Protogen connections (Halverson-adjacent backstories)
  may have *suspicions*. PCs who served at Site
  Endurance specifically (rare; would be a major
  backstory beat) would know more.
- ARTEMIS will *deflect* on questions about the AIZ's
  Polar Plateau classified operations. SIGMA-4 will
  give the public-record information without
  hesitation.
