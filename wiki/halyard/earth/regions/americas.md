# The Americas in 2348

The Americas in 2348 are a continent of two distinct cultural
zones — the *northern* Anglophone-Francophone block (the
US-Canadian Confederation, with the smaller Inuit Nation
attached) and the *southern* Latinate block (Mexico through
Argentina) — held together administratively by the Americas
Secretariat and culturally by a long history of mutual
influence and frequent friction.

The continent's 3.6 billion residents are half the population
of Africa, twice that of Europe, and considerably less than
Asia. The region's role in human civilization is, by widespread
off-world assessment, *quietly important*. The most commonly
identified North American export is *administrative
competence*; the most commonly identified Latin American
export is *cultural confidence*. Both characterizations are
slightly unfair and broadly correct.

## Demographic outline

- **The US-Canadian Confederation** — 720 million. The
  political consolidation of the US, Canada, and (since 2271)
  the Inuit Nation. The largest political entity in North
  America by population and area.
- **Mexico** — 360 million. The largest Latin American
  country and the cultural anchor of the Americas
  Secretariat's southern half.
- **Brazil** — 480 million. The most populous Latin American
  country, the continent's economic powerhouse, and the
  home of São Paulo (Earth's third-largest city).
- **The rest of South America** — 980 million. Argentina,
  Colombia, Venezuela, Peru, Chile, Bolivia, Ecuador,
  Paraguay, Uruguay, the Guianas; each with a distinct
  political and cultural identity.
- **Central America and the Caribbean** — 320 million. The
  isthmus states; Cuba, Hispaniola, Jamaica, Puerto Rico,
  the smaller islands.
- **The Polar North** — about 40 million in the Inuit
  Nation, Alaska, the Canadian Arctic, and Greenland's
  southwestern coast. Has grown substantially since the
  climate shift opened arctic agriculture and trans-polar
  shipping.

The continent's median age is 44 (slightly above Earth's
overall). Demographic patterns vary enormously between zones —
the US-Canadian Confederation has a stable population near
replacement; Mexico has been declining slowly since the
2280s; Brazil's growth plateaued in the 2310s; Argentina
peaked decades ago and has been depopulating.

## The US-Canadian Confederation

Constituted 2189 from the merger of the United States, Canada,
and Mexico (Mexico departed by mutual agreement in 2241 and
returned to independence). The Confederation is a federal
republic with a complex constitutional structure that
preserves both US and Canadian institutional traditions —
the federal capital is Washington, the second-tier capital is
Ottawa, and most federal departments operate from one or the
other.

The Confederation's politics have been *quiet* by historical
US/Canadian standards since the 2310s. The bitter
political polarization of the early-twenty-first-century
United States gradually subsided through the consolidation
period; the Confederation's federal politics in 2348 are
centrist-technocratic, similar to the European national
governments in tone if not in scale.

The Confederation's economy is large (about $14T
UN-equivalent) and diversified. The Confederation hosts:

- The largest single financial sector in human space (New
  York; Chicago; Toronto; smaller centers)
- Earth's largest information-technology and software-
  development industries
- A substantial manufacturing sector, especially in
  aerospace, biotech, and pharmaceuticals
- The continent's largest universities (the surviving
  Ivy League, the University of California system, the
  University of Toronto, McGill)
- Significant cultural production (Hollywood, the New
  York-and-Toronto literary scene, the Nashville-Memphis
  music tradition)

The Inuit Nation, formally part of the Confederation since
2271, retains substantial cultural autonomy and operates
much of the continent's polar trans-shipping infrastructure.
The Nation's territories include most of Greenland's
arctic interior, the Canadian Arctic Archipelago, and a
substantial slice of Alaska.

## Mexico

Mexico's 2241 departure from the Confederation has not
produced enduring tension. Mexico-US relations are
correct and frequently warm. Mexico's economy is largely
integrated with both the Confederation and Brazil; the
Mexican Peninsula is a major manufacturing hub for
electronics, automotive, and pharmaceuticals.

Mexico City (32M) is the continent's third-largest city,
the largest Spanish-speaking city in human space, and the
cultural capital of Mexican-Caribbean Latin culture.

## Brazil

Brazil is the demographic and economic heavyweight of
South America. Politically a federal republic, culturally
the most globally influential Latin American country (its
music, film, literature, and sport have long since
become Earth-cultural staples), economically diversified
across agriculture, manufacturing, services, and the
Amazonian biome's controlled-extraction economy.

São Paulo (74M) is the continent's largest city and one of
the three largest on Earth. The Amazon basin is, like the
Congo Basin in Africa, a UN-protected major biome with
managed extraction.

## The rest of South America

Argentina, Colombia, Venezuela, Peru, Chile, Bolivia,
Ecuador, Paraguay, Uruguay, the Guianas — each retains its
own political life and cultural identity. The continent's
political fragmentation has been higher than its African or
Asian counterparts; there is no equivalent of the African
Union or the ASEAN-style cooperation in South America. The
Mercosur trade bloc remains the major institutional
framework.

The continent's cultural and political center of gravity is
in Brazil and Argentina, with substantial weight in
Colombia and Mexico. The Andean states (Peru, Bolivia,
Ecuador, Chile in part) have a distinct cultural register
that has been increasing in international visibility since
the 2310s.

## Central America and the Caribbean

The isthmus states (Guatemala, El Salvador, Honduras,
Nicaragua, Costa Rica, Panama) form a region with strong
internal cultural ties and a complex relationship with both
Mexico and the rest of Latin America. Costa Rica is the
continent's most stable democracy by long historical
record; Panama's role as the trans-isthmus commercial
center has continued.

The Caribbean — Cuba, Hispaniola (Haiti and the
Dominican Republic), Jamaica, Puerto Rico, the Lesser
Antilles — has a cultural significance disproportionate
to its 90-million population. Caribbean music, literature,
and political thought have been continuously influential
across the Americas.

## Politics

The Americas Secretariat administers UN federal programs
across the continent. The current Secretary is Eduardo
Mendiola-Soares, a Brazilian-Mexican former economist.
The Secretariat's politics are pragmatic; tensions
between the northern and southern halves are managed
rather than resolved.

National-level politics vary widely. The US-Canadian
Confederation has stable centrist-technocratic governance.
Mexico runs vigorous multi-party democracy. Brazil
oscillates between centrist and populist coalitions.
Argentina has had two constitutional crises in the past
fifty years, both resolved peacefully. The smaller states
range from stable democracies to functioning if
sometimes-troubled republics.

The Americas-wide political conversation has, since the
2310s, been heavily influenced by:

- The relationship with Mars (Latin America has
  particularly strong ties; many Mars-born have Latin
  American families)
- The relationship with the Belt (the Caribbean has
  unusually strong cultural ties to Belters of African
  descent)
- Climate adaptation (the Americas have spent more on
  coastal-defense and water-management infrastructure
  than any other continent)

## Major cities

### São Paulo (74M)

Earth's third-largest city. Brazil's economic and cultural
capital. Covered separately in `earth/cities/sao-paulo.md`.

### Mexico City (32M)

The continent's third-largest city after São Paulo and
New York. Largest Spanish-speaking city in human space.
The Federal District has been substantially restored
since the 2230s flood-and-subsidence crisis; the city's
groundwater management is a continental case study.

### New York Arcology (28M)

The largest city in the Confederation. Covered
separately in `earth/cities/nyc-arcology.md`.

### Buenos Aires (15M)

The cultural capital of Argentina and the southern
Latin literary establishment. Tango remains a
recognized continental dance form; the city's
publishing industry, while smaller than São Paulo's,
is more prestigious.

### Bogotá (12M)

Colombia's capital. The high-altitude (2,640 m)
location has been a post-climate-shift advantage; the
city's growth has been steady through the past century.

### Toronto (8M)

The Confederation's secondary capital. The largest
English-speaking Canadian city. The University of
Toronto's research output is the continent's most
prolific.

### Havana (3M)

Cuba's capital and the cultural anchor of the
Caribbean. The city's pre-Compact architecture is
extensively preserved; the cultural scene is one of
the continent's most vital.

## The continent's posture

- **Toward Earth-other** — diplomatically engaged.
  The Americas Secretariat is the second-largest
  contributor (after Asia) to UN federal programs.
- **Toward Mars** — close. Many Mars-born have
  family in the Americas; Mars-Confederation engineering
  exchanges are common; Mars-Brazil cultural exchanges
  are extensive.
- **Toward the Belt** — engaged. The Confederation has
  the second-largest Belt-trade volume on Earth (after
  Asia); the Caribbean has unusual cultural ties to
  Belters.
- **Toward Luna** — close. New York, São Paulo, Mexico
  City all run direct passenger services to Luna.

## Notes for play

- An Americas PC's specific country/cultural background
  matters. *Confederation-American, Mexican, Brazilian,
  Argentine, Colombian, Cuban, Puerto Rican* are
  distinct starting points.
- The Confederation-Latin cultural distinction is
  *real* but not antagonistic. Most Americans (broadly)
  navigate both registers comfortably.
- The continent's *quiet competence* register makes
  Americas PCs particularly suitable for engineering,
  medical, and security roles.
