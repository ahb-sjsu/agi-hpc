# Earth — timeline, 2200 to 2348

The 148 years between the consolidation crisis of 2200 and the
launch of the *Halyard* contract are taught in Earth schools as
*The Long Stabilization* — the period when human civilization,
having spent the twenty-first century overshooting nearly every
biophysical and political constraint it ran into, slowly
contracted into a sustainable equilibrium. The story they tell
is mostly true. The portion they leave out is the cost.

This entry is the one-page version of the full chronology;
nested entries (`earth/history/<event>.md`) drill into the
significant inflection points.

## 2200–2230 — The Late Crisis

By the dawn of the twenty-third century, the population spike of
the late twenty-first had peaked at 11.4 billion and begun a
decade-long decline that surprised every demographer. The cause
was not famine — the worst famines were behind, narrowly — but
exhaustion. Children-per-woman dropped below 1.0 across most of
the developed world. Migration northward continued but slowed.
The atmosphere had stabilized at +2.6°C above the pre-industrial
baseline; no one had any confidence that it would not move
again.

The political institutions that mattered were the ones that had
proven they could *deliver*. The UN's Climate Adaptation
Authority, set up in 2147, had become the world's largest
infrastructure agency. Three of the surviving multinationals
(Mitsubishi, Sinopec, Schneider Industries) had been brought
into a consortium structure with the UN to manage power
generation. The old superpowers — the United States, China,
Russia, the EU — had reduced themselves, exhausted, into more
modest constitutional regimes. **Africa was rising.**

This period saw:

- The 2207 dissolution of the World Trade Organization, replaced
  by UN Trade Council with binding authority.
- The 2214 cession of the Spratly Islands to a multilateral UN
  protectorate. (Mostly symbolic; the Spratlys were partly
  underwater.)
- The 2218–2222 Atlantic Hurricane Compact, in which the US,
  the Caribbean states, and the EU joined a shared evacuation
  and rebuild fund. The compact still operates.
- The 2223 collapse of the Indian state's third-tier
  administration, requiring UN emergency takeover of public
  health and water in five states for nine years.
- The 2228 Antarctic Mining Treaty, which authorized industrial
  extraction in the AIZ under joint UN-corporate operation.

By 2230 the world had crossed the demographic peak, the worst
of the climate shocks, and the most painful adjustments to the
food supply. It had also, irreversibly, accepted the UN as the
referee of last resort.

## 2230–2270 — The Stabilization

This is the era in which the world that exists in 2348 took its
visible shape. Three things happened in parallel:

**The verticalization of cities.** New construction shifted
upward, aggressively. The first generation of true arcologies —
Mumbai's Sky-Cluster (begun 2237), Shanghai's Pudong-North
(begun 2241), Lagos's Marina Stack (begun 2249) — set the
template that the rest of the megacity world would follow:
kilometer-tall residential blocks, mid-level transit, atmospheric
engineering above. By 2265 most of Earth's population was within
50 kilometers of an arcology cluster.

**The development of Luna.** The 2244 Lunar Industrialization
Compact opened the Moon to large-scale infrastructure
construction. By 2270 the Lunar Industrial Belt — the visible
arc of refineries, mass drivers, and habs along the equator —
was spelling itself out across the night sky as a continuous
glittering line. Luna provided the metals, the helium-3, and
eventually the manufacturing base that allowed Earth's vertical
construction wave to continue.

**The first of the long programs.** UN-funded multi-decadal
programs began that would shape the rest of the century:

- The Atmospheric Stabilization Program (2238) — slow-cycle
  carbon drawdown, ocean alkalinity adjustment, stratospheric
  aerosol management. Considered a success, with reservations.
- The Coastal Defense Program (2242) — sea wall systems, planned
  retreat, salt marsh restoration. A patchwork by design.
- The Universal Healthcare Convergence (2249) — bringing the
  global standard of care to within a band that the WHO could
  certify. Took 22 years.
- The General Provision Pilot (2251) — first run in Tanzania,
  Vietnam, and Bangladesh. Considered a success; expanded
  through 2280s.

By 2270, Earth had something like a stable equilibrium. Not
prosperity, not peace, not happiness, but *function*. People
expected the lights to be on tomorrow.

## 2270–2310 — The Compact Era

The political event of the 2270s was the slow-motion
consolidation of UN authority through the Compact-of-Geneva
process. A series of legal and institutional reforms,
concluding in 2291, gave the UN:

- Direct taxation authority on income above a regional median
- Federal law enforcement (the FSec, established 2284)
- A standing military force (the UNN, in something like its
  modern form, by 2289)
- Universal Provision authority (2291)
- Outer-system jurisdiction (the Outer Authority, 2295)

The Compact was not a single moment. It was a series of
treaties and amendments that the old national governments
ratified as the price of continued legitimacy. Some
ratifications were enthusiastic (most of Europe, most of
Africa). Some were reluctant (the US-Canadian Confederation
ratified the federal-tax authority in 2289 only after a
constitutional crisis). Some were forced (the 2293 Kashmir
Settlement that ended the last of the partition wars).

In parallel:

- **Mars secession** (2272). The Martian Congressional Republic
  declared independence. Earth recognized it within fifteen
  months under heavy economic pressure from the Belt and an
  internal Earther anti-war movement that surprised the
  political class. The Mars-Earth War of 2279 was small,
  brief, and inconclusive; the constitutional settlement that
  followed defined the modern relationship.
- **OPA emergence** (mid-2280s). The Outer Planets Alliance
  coalesced from a dozen Belter labor and political
  organizations. Not a state. Not, until 2331, a formal
  diplomatic counterparty for Earth.
- **The Lunar Republic recognized** (2304). Luna had been
  drifting toward independence for forty years; the
  Settlement of 2304 made it formal, with a UN-Lunar Joint
  Authority over the orbital approaches and a complex
  passport regime.

By 2310 the broad shape of human political space was fixed:
**UN-Earth, MCR-Mars, OPA-Belt, Lunar Republic** and a few
contested zones (Ceres, the Jovian moons, Titan).

## 2310–2348 — The Long Cruise

The last four decades have been quieter. The major theme is
*deepening rather than expansion* — Earth getting denser, Mars
getting more habitable, the Belt getting more integrated, the
outer system getting more populated.

Notable events:

- **The Atmospheric Lock** (2317). The Stabilization Program
  reached its target band; CO₂ stopped rising. Considered the
  technical victory of the century.
- **The MCR Naval Buildout** (2331–2339). The MCRN matched UNN
  tonnage by 2335 and exceeded it by 2339. Earth-Mars relations
  cooled. There was no war, but there were two close calls.
- **The Ceres Riots** (2339). Belter labor protests on Ceres
  killed 11 people; the OPA emerged as a coherent political
  voice in the aftermath.
- **The Outer-System Boom** (2340–2348). Industrial expansion
  into the Jovian and Saturnian systems accelerated. Titan now
  has a permanent population over 2 million. Trans-Neptunian
  surveys, like the *Halyard* expedition, become common enough
  that the General Survey Service publishes a quarterly
  bulletin.
- **The Drone Survey** (2347). A long-range Survey Service
  drone passes 2301-DR-44 ("Nithon") at 84 AU and records four
  seconds of anomalous radio carrier on a frequency astronomers
  associate with neutral hydrogen. The recording reaches Earth
  in March 2348. Mao-Kwikowski Mercantile is on the survey
  publication list.

## What the timeline obscures

The timeline above is the official Earth-school version. It is
*broadly* accurate but it has several silences worth naming —
not because they are secret, but because they are uncomfortable
to teach:

- **The Late-Crisis casualty count is a range, not a number.**
  Estimates of excess deaths in the 2200–2230 period run from
  400 million to 1.1 billion. The UN never settled on a
  figure; the Asian Secretariat's 2284 commission was disbanded
  before publishing.
- **The Compact-of-Geneva consolidation was not bloodless.** The
  Indian administrative collapse of 2223–2232 cost an unknown
  number of lives. The Kashmir Settlement was preceded by a
  five-year intermittent war. The 2289 Confederation
  constitutional crisis came within a presidential pardon of
  becoming a coup.
- **The General Provision is not free.** The taxation regime
  that funds it falls heavily on the Comfortable stratum, which
  has never quite forgiven the Strata for not being more
  visibly inconvenienced. The political tension is permanent.
- **Mars and Earth came close to war twice in the 2330s.** The
  details are classified Tier-3 and would not appear in any
  ARTEMIS or SIGMA-4 retrieval.
