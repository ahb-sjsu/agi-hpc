# Earth in 2348 — overview

Earth in 2348 carries twenty billion people on a planet that was
already politically, atmospherically, and ecologically stressed
when the population crossed eight. The intervening century and a
half were spent stabilizing — climate, food, water, and the
political institutions large enough to coordinate the work — and
the result is a planet that is dense, comfortable for most, and
quietly exhausted.

## Governance

The United Nations governs, in a sense, having grown out of the
old diplomatic forum into a federal layer that handles taxation,
law enforcement, naval projection, and (since the 2291
Compact-of-Geneva) universal basic assistance. The UN's authority
sits above two older layers that did not vanish:

1. **The Continental Secretariats.** Africa, Asia-Pacific,
   Europe-Anatolia, the Americas, and Australasia-Oceania each
   have a Secretariat that administers UN-level programs at
   regional scale: infrastructure, refugee resettlement (mostly
   internal), the General Provision, public health, and the
   continental courts. The Secretariats are appointed bodies, not
   elected, with a 12-year staggered rotation that has, with one
   exception in 2334, prevented continental capture by any
   single political bloc.

2. **The old national governments.** Nation-states still exist —
   shrunken, constitutional, and largely cultural. A citizen of
   Lagos has a Nigerian passport AND a UN identity chip, pays
   Nigerian taxes for local services and UN taxes for everything
   else, and votes in Nigerian municipal elections AND in the
   Pan-Continental Assembly's sectoral seats. Most Earthers
   identify as *Earthan* first and national second; their
   great-grandparents would have considered this a kind of
   surrender, and in some sense it was.

The combined effect is that no Earther in 2348 is governed by
fewer than three layers (city / nation / continent / UN), and
most are governed by four or five. The administrative tonnage is
enormous. The system mostly works because the UN funds the
General Provision and runs the supply chains, and most political
disputes are about who gets a slightly larger slice rather than
whether the bread will arrive.

## The General Provision

Every UN citizen is entitled to the General Provision: a monthly
income of 1,400 UN credits, indexed to regional cost-of-living,
plus a housing voucher and universal health coverage. The
Provision is real. It is *enough*. It is not enough to live well
in a top-tier megacity, but it is enough to live, and most of
Earth lives on Provision plus contract work.

The Provision has been the load-bearing political settlement of
the post-Compact era. It absorbs unemployment shocks, smooths
intergenerational labor transitions, and deflates the political
energy of mass movements that would otherwise have organized
against the consolidation of UN authority. A subsection of the
political left calls this co-optation; a subsection of the
political right calls it dependency; the middle 75% of the
electorate calls it normal.

See `earth/institutions/general-provision.md` for the program's
mechanics, eligibility rules, and the work-lottery system that
governs upward mobility.

## Demographic distribution

Earth's twenty billion people are unevenly distributed:

- **Africa** — 5.2 billion. The continent that had the best
  twenty-first century: stable institutions, demographic growth,
  and a cultural confidence the rest of Earth has learned to
  take seriously. Lagos, Nairobi, Kinshasa, Addis Ababa,
  Johannesburg are the real capitals of Earth, in the sense
  that everyone watches their elections and reads their writers.
- **Asia** — 8.8 billion. Still most of humanity. Shanghai,
  Mumbai, Dhaka, Jakarta, Manila — megacities so dense their
  atmospheres are engineered.
- **Europe and Anatolia** — 1.1 billion. Depopulated, aging,
  pleasant. The cultural museum of humanity, kept up by
  remittances and tourism.
- **The Americas** — 3.6 billion. Mixed. The US-Canadian
  Confederation is stable and comfortable; much of Central and
  South America runs on a layered local + UN administration.
  São Paulo is the third-largest city in human space.
- **Australasia and Oceania** — 0.7 billion. Surprisingly
  unchanged. Includes several floating-platform habitats in the
  Pacific.
- **The Antarctic Industrial Zone** — 0.4 billion, almost
  entirely contract workers on three-year rotations.

See `earth/regions/` for per-continent depth.

## Class

Earth has no formal nobility, no formal class system, and three
hundred functional gradations of status that everyone navigates
without naming. The broad strokes:

- **The Strata.** The top two or three percent. Old inheritors,
  corporate seniors, top-tier specialists. Coastal arcologies
  with views and private medicine. Most have at least one
  Luna-resident relative.
- **The Comfortable.** Maybe twenty percent. Senior
  professionals, skilled trades, successful contractors. Apartments
  owned outright, lunar holidays, the regular complaint about
  Provincial weather.
- **The Working Provisioned.** Most of Earth. Provision plus
  contract work, vertical-district apartments, holo-serial
  evenings, an annual flight for a family visit.
- **The Off-Roll.** The bottom few percent. Provision plus the
  informal economy; undocumented internal migrants, the
  chronically ill, the self-exiled. Not destitute in the
  historical sense — nobody starves in 2348 — but stuck.

## The shape of cities

Most new construction since 2260 has been *up*. Megacities are
tiered:

- **Ground level** is walkable, human-scale, retail, social.
- **Mid-levels** carry freight, transit, services.
- **Residential stacks** rise to roughly a kilometer; above that,
  arcology penthouses and observatories.
- **Atmospheric engineering** keeps the inhabited stacks cool,
  clean, and rainless. The first major arcology rain-routing
  project (Mumbai, 2272) is now a tourist landmark.

Transit is rail, bike, and pedestrian. Private cars are a luxury
signal. Air travel is common but expensive. Passenger rockets
run Earth-to-Luna twice daily from six ports (Lagos, Kourou,
Baikonur, Wenchang, Sriharikota, and the rebuilt Kennedy).

## The skies at night

In clear weather over a low-pollution coastline, the unmistakable
arcs of Luna's industrial belt catch sunlight; a child of 2348
learns to pick out the belt by age four and Mars by age five.
Jupiter is bright enough that "as bright as Jupiter" replaced
"as bright as Venus" in colloquial speech around 2310.

Mars itself, when it is in the right part of the sky, has the
faint amber tint that is the first visible evidence of
terraforming. Earthers do not love this. *Especially* the older
ones.

## Earth's posture toward the rest of human space

Earth's official position in 2348 is that it is the cradle of
humanity, the seat of the longest-running political institution,
and the senior partner in the Mars-Earth-Belt triangle. Each of
those claims is contested by parties Earth would prefer not to
contest with.

- **Toward Mars** — Earth treats Mars as a petulant former
  colony. Mars treats Earth as a richly furnished retirement
  home. Both treat each other better in private than in public.
- **Toward the Belt** — Earth treats the Belt as a labor pool,
  a supply chain, and a political nuisance. The Belt treats
  Earth as a market and an enemy.
- **Toward the outer system** — Earth maintains the Outer
  System Auxiliary (UNN) and the General Survey Service. Most
  outer-system traffic is contract work, much of it managed
  through the work-lottery system.

This campaign begins on Ceres and spends most of its time
beyond Neptune. Earth, for our purposes, is what the crew left.
Many of them will not see it again.

## Notes for play

- An Earth PC backstory is shaped by which stratum the character
  came from. Most outer-system contractors are Working Provisioned
  or Comfortable — Strata don't take these jobs and Off-Roll
  rarely qualify.
- "Earther" as an identity carries a particular flavor of
  melancholy that Belters mock and Martians find tedious. Lean
  into it for tone; don't make it a joke.
- The General Provision is not socialism in the twentieth-century
  sense. It is the political settlement that allowed the
  consolidation. PCs from Earth take the Provision as a fact of
  life, not a virtue or a complaint.
