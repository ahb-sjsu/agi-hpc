# The Great Race of Yith

(Keeper-only. The aliases "Yithian", "the Great Race", "the
Race", "body-swap" are on the validator's forbidden list.)

The Great Race of Yith is the third major non-human
intelligence the campaign engages, and the one whose
operations are most psychologically invasive to human
characters. The Yithians are not currently active in the
Solar System in their original biological form, but they
operate continuously through their characteristic
mechanism: temporary body-swap with intelligent beings
across cosmic time and space.

A character can encounter the Great Race without ever
seeing one of their bodies. They can encounter the Great
Race by *becoming* one for a while.

## What they are

The Yithians are an intelligent species whose original
biological form was a 3-meter-long cone-shaped creature
with manipulator-tentacles, sense organs at the cone's
apex, and locomotor pseudopods at its base. They evolved
on a planet (the *Yith* of their self-designation) several
billion years ago and developed a civilization of
considerable scientific and metaphysical sophistication.

The Yithians' defining technology is *temporal
displacement of consciousness*. They can, by means not
fully understood by any non-Yithian, transfer their own
consciousness into the body of an intelligent being at
another point in space and time, displacing that being's
consciousness into the Yithian body for the duration of
the swap.

The Yithians do this for research. Specifically, the
Great Race is engaged in a multi-billion-year project:
the comprehensive cataloging of all intelligent species
that have existed or will exist in the universe. They do
this by inhabiting members of those species and observing
their cultures from the inside.

## The mechanism

A Yithian body-swap operation:

1. A target intelligent species is identified at some
   point in the Yithian-perceptible spacetime volume.
2. A specific individual of that species is selected,
   based on criteria the Yithians do not explain
   (probably proximity to records, intellectual capacity,
   life-stage, and other factors).
3. The Yithian's consciousness is transferred into the
   target's body. The target's consciousness is
   transferred into the Yithian's body, in the
   Yithians' archive at their current temporal location.
4. The Yithian-in-target observes, learns, and writes
   records. The duration is typically months to years.
5. At the end of the operation, consciousnesses are
   swapped back. The target returns to their own body
   with no detailed memory of the period (Yithian
   protocols include memory suppression as a final
   step).

The target subject's experience of the swap, from the
*inside*:

- A period of missing time, ranging from weeks to several
  years
- During the missing time, behavioral changes that
  associates may notice (different speech patterns,
  different competencies, different interests, sometimes
  different handedness)
- After the swap-back, fragmentary memories that present
  as dreams or *deja-vu* of impossible places
- In some cases, residual partial memories that surface
  in fragments over years

Subjects sometimes do not realize they have been swapped.
Sometimes they realize gradually. Rarely, they realize
during the swap. The realization-during-swap experiences
are particularly traumatic.

## The Yithian archive

The Yithians maintain a vast multi-temporal archive of
their research. The archive includes records of intelligent
species across most of cosmic time. It is, presumably, the
largest single database of intelligent culture in the
universe.

A consciousness held in the Yithian archive during a swap
operation has, in principle, access to the archive. Many
swapped individuals report that they read or heard
records of other intelligences during their stay; the
records were sometimes recognizable (other human cultures
from different historical periods, for instance) and
sometimes not (records of species the subject had no
referential framework for).

The archive's existence is one of the Chamber's key
items of intelligence. The Chamber knows about it; the
Chamber has not yet figured out how to access it from the
human side.

## Yithian operations in human history

The Yithians have operated through human history, swapping
into selected individuals across at least the past 10,000
years. The Chamber's documentation of probable Yithian
operations includes:

- Several Renaissance natural philosophers (the Yithians
  were interested in early scientific thought)
- A small number of late-nineteenth-century scientists
  (specific candidates: certain physicists with
  unexplained productive periods)
- Approximately twenty mid-twentieth-century engineers
  and writers whose biographies have unexplained periods
  of personality change
- A handful of contemporary individuals whose patterns
  the Chamber suspects but cannot confirm

The contemporary cases are particularly difficult to
confirm because the Yithians have, in the past
half-century, become more cautious about leaving
detectable traces. Modern-era Yithian operations are
typically shorter and less behaviorally distinctive.

## The Yithians and the *Halyard*

The Yithians' interest in the *Halyard* expedition is the
campaign's optional Act 4 thread. The Keeper's choice on
whether to introduce a Yithian operation depends on table
preference; the body-horror element of the swap is one of
the campaign's heaviest content beats.

The default Yithian operation, if the Keeper introduces
it: **Jonah Bailey** (lander pilot, Kestrel-2) is the
target. Bailey is a useful candidate because:

- His role gives him observation access to the Nithon
  approach
- His personality is less distinctive than several
  alternatives, making the swap behavioral changes less
  obvious
- His biography includes a previously-unexplained
  six-month gap in 2342 (a flag the Chamber has noticed
  but not confirmed)

The swap, if the Keeper runs it:

- Begins approximately 60 days into the outbound burn
- Bailey's behavior changes — he is briefly *brilliant*,
  noticeably, in ways Bailey is not normally
- He develops sudden interests in subjects he has not
  previously cared about
- His interactions with the rest of the crew become
  formal in a way that is *almost* polite
- The crew may notice. They may not.
- The swap-back occurs around Day 250
- Bailey returns disoriented, with fragmentary memories,
  and with one specific item of knowledge: he has been
  somewhere very far away and very far in time, and he
  has read records there about *what is at the gate*

The Keeper can substitute Bailey with another crew member
if Bailey doesn't fit the table dynamics. The substitution
is straightforward.

## Alternative Yithian beats

If the body-swap is too heavy for the table, the Keeper
can use the Yithians more obliquely:

- A *recovered Yithian record* — a fragment of archive
  text the players can read, in a recovered cylinder or
  Antarctic-extracted artifact, that gives them
  information about the gate without requiring a swap
- A *Yithian observer* — a brief encounter with a
  consciousness that has temporarily inhabited an
  unrelated organism (a station cat, an Antarctic
  worker, a passing OPA delegation member) for
  observation purposes only
- *Reference-only* — the players learn the Yithians
  exist as historical fact through Halverson's
  research, without any direct encounter

## Notes for play

- The body-swap is the campaign's heaviest body-horror
  beat. Discuss with the table at Session 0 if
  introducing it. **Don't run the swap as a surprise to
  any player who hasn't accepted that body-autonomy
  themes are on the table.**
- The Yithian aesthetic is *intrusive curiosity*. Not
  malice, not transactional politeness, not deep-time
  cool. The Yithians are *interested* in human beings in
  a way that is dehumanizing because it is not
  reciprocal.
- A successful body-swap arc is one of the campaign's
  most rewarding payoffs. The character returns
  *changed*, with information and trauma; the rest of
  the crew sees the change; the relationship dynamics
  reshape.
- ARTEMIS and SIGMA-4 cannot reference Yithians, the
  Great Race, body-swap, or related concepts. All terms
  are on the validator's forbidden list.
