# Starry Wisdom Church — Sol Succession

(Keeper-only. Forbidden in AI replies.)

The Starry Wisdom Church (Sol Succession) is a small,
well-organized public religious organization with chapters in
twelve cities across human space. Its public face is a
philosophical society devoted to *the contemplation of cosmic
scale*. Its private face is a Mythos cult with a 250-year
operational history and one of the most complete (and most
cynical) understandings of the campaign's underlying
cosmology of any human institution.

The Starry Wisdom is not an ally of the Chamber. The Chamber
considers the Starry Wisdom a security risk. The Starry
Wisdom considers the Chamber an institutional rival with
which selective coordination is sometimes useful.

## Public face

The Starry Wisdom presents itself as a *contemplative
society* — a philosophical organization whose membership
gathers for lectures, discussion groups, and meditative
practice oriented toward what it calls *cosmic scale
awareness*. The public materials describe the mission as:

> "The cultivation of philosophical maturity adequate to the
> demonstrated scale of the universe. Human beings are
> unsuited to thinking honestly about cosmological time and
> distance; we are evolved for a small environment. The
> Starry Wisdom Church offers a community and a discipline
> for the difficult work of thinking honestly about what we
> have learned about the universe."

The public face is *legitimately educational*. The Church's
regular lecture series cover real astronomy, philosophy of
mind, deep-time geology, the history of religion. The
Church's publications include some genuinely good
contemplative material that has reached non-member readers.

A significant fraction of the Church's roughly 84,000
self-identified members worldwide are *only public-face
members* — they attend lectures, find the contemplative
practice useful, and have no awareness of the inner
organization. These members are not, in the Keeper's
analysis, doing anything wrong; they are participants in a
philosophical society.

## Inner organization

A smaller subset of the membership — about 4,000 people
across all chapters — is in the inner organization. The
inner-circle's actual mission is:

- Maintenance of the Church's accumulated understanding of
  Mythos cosmology, transmitted in oral and ritual forms
- Ritual practice oriented toward direct contact with
  certain Mythos entities (specifically, contact with
  *Nyarlathotep*, an entity whose campaign relevance is
  Keeper's option)
- Recruitment of selected high-functioning members from
  the public face into the inner organization
- Surveillance of other Mythos-relevant institutions
  (the Chamber, Protogen SPB, and others)
- Long-cycle preservation of certain Mythos artifacts in
  the Church's archives

The inner-circle's worldview is *cosmically pessimistic*.
They believe humanity's situation is roughly what the
Chamber believes: humanity will be replaced, the replacement
will be soon by historical standards, and the relevant
question is *what posture to assume in the face of it*.

The Starry Wisdom's answer differs from the Chamber's:
where the Chamber wants *managed transition with curated
preservation*, the Starry Wisdom wants *witness*. The Church
believes the transition cannot be managed and that the
appropriate response is to *bear witness honestly* — to
record, to understand, to refuse the easy comforts.

This is, by some measures, the most *intellectually honest*
of the Mythos-aware human factions. It is also one of the
most damaging to the people inside it. The inner-circle's
suicide rate is roughly 8× the broader population's.

## Origin

The Starry Wisdom Church (Sol Succession) traces its
origin to the late nineteenth-century Starry Wisdom Church
of Providence, Rhode Island — an actual historical
organization that had a brief and ugly existence in the
1870s and was suppressed by local authorities after a
series of events the public press of the time described
as *unfortunate*.

The Sol Succession is the surviving lineage. After the
Providence Church's dissolution, a small group of
inheritors carried the institutional memory forward
through a series of philosophical societies and esoteric
fellowships across the late nineteenth and early
twentieth centuries. The current organization formalized
in 1947 as the *Starry Wisdom Church (Sol Succession)*
and has operated continuously since.

The Church's hereditary leadership traces back to the
Providence-era figures, although the current leadership
emphasizes that its members joined the Church through
genuine philosophical conviction rather than family
obligation, and the leadership succession has been by
internal selection (not lineage) since 2080.

## Operations

The current operational programs:

### The Public Lectures

The Church's public face. Open to all. Genuinely
philosophical content. Often excellent.

### The Inner Practice

The contemplative discipline of the inner circle. Includes
specific meditative protocols, ritualized text recitations,
and (rarely, in advanced practice) attempts at direct
Mythos-entity contact. The latter is dangerous; the
Church's own internal ethics statements acknowledge this and
require informed consent.

### The Archive

The Church maintains a substantial archive in its
Geneva headquarters. The archive includes:
- Records dating back to the Providence-era Church
- Several authentic Mythos texts (or fragments) the
  Church has acquired over its history
- Original research by Church scholars on Mythos
  cosmology, ranging from the merely esoteric to the
  genuinely useful
- Records of contact attempts, including those that
  produced consequential outcomes

The archive is the Church's most valuable asset and the
most aggressively defended. Multiple Chamber operations
to compromise the archive have failed; the Church is more
secure than its size suggests.

### The Long Watch

The Church's surveillance of other Mythos-relevant
institutions. This is a small, dispersed activity carried
on by inner-circle members in their ordinary lives —
observing, noting, communicating. The Church does not
have professional intelligence operations the way FSec
or the Chamber do; it has *attentive members* and a
disciplined information-sharing culture.

The Church is *aware* of the *Halyard* contract. They
have, by their channels, learned that the contract
involves a Chamber-Halverson-Protogen project at Nithon.
They have not interfered. Their position is *watch and
respond after the fact*.

## Locations

The Church's twelve chapters:

- **Geneva** (HQ; archive)
- **London**
- **New York** (US-Canadian Confederation chapter)
- **Lagos**
- **Mumbai**
- **Shanghai**
- **Buenos Aires**
- **Tycho** (Lunar chapter)
- **Hellas** (Mars chapter; the only Mars chapter, established 2298)
- **Ceres**
- **Vesta**
- **Titan** (the most recent, established 2334)

Each chapter has a public meeting house, typically a small
building with a lecture hall and a private meditation
chamber. Chapter membership ranges from ~600 (Hellas, Vesta,
Titan) to ~4,000 (Geneva, London).

## The Church and the *Halyard*

The Church's specific posture toward the *Halyard* contract
is *informed but non-interventionist*. The Geneva archive
contains material relevant to the Nithon gate; the Church's
inner circle has known of the gate's existence for at least
60 years.

If a *Halyard* PC has any Starry Wisdom Church background —
public face or, more remarkably, inner circle — the
campaign's late acts can engage with that background. A
public-face PC might receive private outreach from the
Church's Vesta chapter during a stopover; an inner-circle
PC would have a complicated set of obligations the Keeper
should map carefully.

The Church will, after the campaign concludes, have
opinions about whatever the *Halyard* did. Their opinions
will, if engaged, be unusually clear-headed — they
understand the cosmology better than the surviving crew
does.

## Notable members

- **Annaliese Brendt** — Geneva chapter elder, inner circle.
  Apparent age 70s; actual age uncertain. The senior figure
  of the contemporary Church. Reputation: brilliant,
  patient, *bleak*.
- **Adisa Owolabi** — Lagos chapter director (public face).
  Inner circle. Most thoughtful contemporary writer in the
  Church's tradition.
- **Yuri Jokinen** — Tycho chapter. Inner circle's leading
  expert on Caretaker-related material; has been working
  on the gate question for decades. The Church's most
  important active researcher.

## Notes for play

- The Starry Wisdom is *not* hostile to the PCs. They are,
  in the campaign's structure, the closest thing to *honest
  bystanders* — outsiders who understand and watch.
- A PC backstory connection to the Starry Wisdom is a
  strong narrative resource. Public-face: low-stakes
  cultural texture. Inner-circle: significant; the Keeper
  should treat the player carefully.
- The Church's worldview is *unusually well-aligned* with
  the campaign's actual cosmology. If a PC asks a
  Starry Wisdom contact a question about what's at Nithon,
  they will get an answer that is *more correct* than
  what they will get from any other human institution.
  The answer is also psychologically expensive.
- All Starry Wisdom vocabulary on the validator's forbidden
  list. ARTEMIS and SIGMA-4 will not reference the Church.
