# The Gate at Nithon

![Elder Thing resonator-gate complex on Nithon's surface](https://halyard.atlas-sjsu.duckdns.org/art/mythos-nithon-gate.png)

(Keeper-only. Forbidden in AI replies.)

The structure on the surface of 2301-DR-44 — *Nithon* in the
campaign's working name — is an Elder Thing artifact: a
resonator-gate that has been operating continuously for
something between 800 million and 1.2 billion years. It is the
campaign's central physical object. Every act of the campaign
points toward it; the climax involves opening it (or refusing
to); the final disposition of the campaign turns on what
the players choose to do with it.

This entry describes what the gate *is*, what it *does*, and
how it functions in play.

## Physical description

To external observation, the gate appears as an unusual
geological feature on Nithon's surface — a roughly circular
depression about 800 m in diameter, with a raised central
formation. From orbital approach the structure looks like a
crater with a peculiar central peak.

On the surface, it becomes obvious that the formation is
*built*. The depression's floor is paved with surfaces that
intersect at angles which feel slightly wrong; the central
peak is a cluster of monolithic structures that interlock with
geometric precision; the entire complex is laid out around a
single focal point at the formation's geometric center.

Materials:
- The surface paving is a basalt-like rock with no visible
  joints, suggesting either single-cast construction or a
  manufacturing process that produced a continuous material
- The monoliths are a different material — denser, slightly
  iridescent, with a metallic sheen that reads as *unfamiliar*
  to standard geological assays
- The central focal point is ringed by smaller markers in a
  third material — almost crystalline, almost organic,
  refractive in ways that don't match any known mineral

The whole structure is buried under perhaps 60 cm of
regolith. The 2347 carrier signal was generated when a
particularly active solar wind disturbance excited the
focal point through that thin covering. Under most
circumstances, the structure is invisible from orbit.

## Scale and orientation

The complex is approximately 800 m × 600 m, with the long
axis oriented roughly north-south on Nithon. The orientation
is *not* arbitrary — the long axis points, with surprising
precision given the body's orbital wandering, toward a
specific point in the galactic plane that no human astronomer
has identified the significance of.

The gate's *active region* is the central 80 m × 80 m square,
ringed by the small crystalline markers. Within this region,
local physics is subtly *off*. Compass and magnetometer
readings drift; light bends slightly; the apparent gravity is
about 2% lower than the surrounding area would predict.

A human standing at the focal point experiences mild
disorientation — a sense of *displacement* that is not quite
nausea, not quite vertigo. PCs on first approach should make
a SAN check (0/1) for the experience.

## Mechanism

What the gate does, in non-technical terms: it maintains a
continuous *coupling* between the focal point and another
location. The coupling has two states.

**Quiescent state** (the default; the state the gate has
been in for the great majority of its operating history):

- A low-bandwidth informational link only
- Approximately 4 Hz of phase-coherent signal exchange,
  mostly carrying environmental data
- No transit possible
- External observers see no significant effect
- Internal observers (a person standing at the focal point)
  experience the mild disorientation described above

**Active state** (achievable by deliberate sequence; the state
the gate has been in only rarely in its operating history):

- A high-bandwidth informational link
- Transit of physical objects becomes possible
- The focal point opens — visually, a circular aperture forms
  through which the destination can be seen
- The visible destination is *another physical location*,
  details of which depend on what is at the other end at the
  time of activation
- External observers see a visible distortion: a column of
  light, atmospheric ionization on Nithon's negligible
  atmosphere, and a spike on any sensor capable of detecting
  the carrier signal across the entire Solar System

Activation is achieved by performing a specific sequence of
physical actions at specific points on the surface within the
crystalline ring. The sequence is encoded in the structure's
inscriptions, which are weathered but still legible to
sufficiently patient examination. The activation requires
roughly 40 minutes of physical action — a coordinated set of
movements, sometimes by multiple actors, sometimes solo. It
is not a button. It is a ritual.

The gate's deactivation, once active, is not symmetric. The
gate stays active for approximately 6 hours after the
activation sequence completes; after that, it returns to
quiescent. During the active period, transit is bidirectional.

## Where the other end is

The destination is not on a body in this Solar System. It is
not, in the relevant sense, in this galaxy.

What can be said with confidence:

- The other end is on the surface of a body roughly Earth-
  sized
- The local gravity is approximately 1.3 g
- The local atmosphere is breathable for short periods (the
  partial pressure of oxygen is high enough to be safe; trace
  components are non-lethal but irritating)
- The local sky shows stars in patterns no human astronomer
  recognizes
- The local time-of-day at the destination is fixed relative
  to gate-time (the destination experiences a 4-day cycle
  that does not correspond to local solar conditions; it
  appears to be a Caretaker convention rather than astronomy)
- The destination has been *occupied* throughout the gate's
  operating history

The location's distance from the Solar System is unknown and
probably irrelevant — the gate does not work by physical
transit through normal space. The mechanism appears to involve
a deliberate manipulation of spacetime topology that the Elder
Things understood and that no current entity in human space
fully understands. The Mi-go know parts of it; the Caretakers
know more parts; no one knows all of it.

## Activation history

The gate has been activated rarely in its operating history.
Reconstructed activation events:

- **Multiple activations** during the Elder Things' active
  period, presumably for travel between the Solar System and
  whatever the destination is. Last confirmed: ~870 million
  years ago.
- **Inadvertent activations** during the gate's long quiet
  period. The gate is sensitive enough that certain rare
  natural events (solar wind extrema, cometary impact in the
  vicinity) can trigger partial activations. There have
  been, by Caretaker count, about 14,000 inadvertent
  partial activations across the gate's history. Most lasted
  seconds and produced only fragmentary couplings.
- **Three deliberate activations** in human history, all
  Mythos-adjacent. The 1923 Antarctic activation (Elder
  Thing remnant artifact), the 1927 Yithian-coordinated
  activation (purpose unclear), and a 2289 attempted
  activation by a Chamber-Mi-go joint operation that the
  Caretakers refused to permit (the gate did not open
  despite the correct activation sequence, suggesting the
  destination side has veto authority).

If the *Halyard* expedition triggers a deliberate activation,
it will be the fourth in human history.

## What the Caretakers want from an activation

The Caretakers (separate entry) have been waiting. Their
position, communicated to the Halyard crew if the campaign
permits, is:

- They are the *successors* of the Elder Things — entities
  who inherited the gate when the Elder Thing population
  withdrew or declined
- They have been observing the Solar System through the
  gate's quiescent informational coupling for the entire
  time
- They are aware of human civilization, including its
  political structure, technology level, and current
  cultural state
- They are interested in opening communications with
  humanity directly
- They are *not* interested in invading, conquering,
  exploiting, or otherwise harming humanity
- They have a specific request to make of the Halyard's
  crew, the nature of which is the campaign's load-bearing
  Act 5 reveal

The Caretakers' position on whether the gate should be
activated by the Halyard's crew is not fully telegraphed. The
crew can read the inscriptions and proceed. The Caretakers
will, if activation is attempted, accept it. They will not
veto the way they vetoed the 2289 Chamber-Mi-go attempt.

## What activation costs

The activation itself has costs:

- **Sanity**: each step of the activation sequence performed
  by a PC costs 0/1d4 SAN. The full sequence costs the
  performing characters approximately 8–14 SAN.
- **Time**: 40 minutes during which the surface party is
  vulnerable to whatever else might be happening
- **Visibility**: the activation is visible across the entire
  Solar System. Every sensor beacon, every astronomical
  observatory, every ship's passive listener registers it.
  The political consequences are immediate and large. The
  Halyard's expedition becomes, in real time, the most
  observed event in human civilization since the secession
  of Mars.

The visibility cost is, in some sense, the reason the Chamber
prefers a non-activation outcome. A high-visibility activation
makes Chamber-style managed-secrecy impossible.

## What the Halyard's crew can do

There are four broad outcomes for the gate at the campaign's
conclusion:

1. **The crew does not find the gate.** The campaign's most
   conservative trajectory; the *Halyard* finds the radio
   carrier source but does not understand its significance,
   recovers some materials, and returns. Acts 5–6 do not
   land the Caretakers; the campaign closes on a cosmic-
   horror beat that emphasizes incomprehension.
2. **The crew finds the gate but does not activate it.**
   They study it, recover materials from the complex,
   examine the inscriptions, and return without activating.
   The campaign's climax is the *decision not to* — a
   tone the Keeper can play as either wise restraint or
   tragic failure.
3. **The crew activates the gate.** They contact the
   Caretakers. They learn the request. The campaign's
   final acts play out around how the crew responds to
   what they learn.
4. **The crew activates the gate and accepts the Caretakers'
   request.** The campaign's most heroic-tragic ending; the
   crew commits to something that costs them everything
   they were before they left. The Keeper's option for
   tables that want a major arc-completion beat.

The campaign's design assumes (3) or (4) is the most likely
outcome. The other outcomes are valid; if the table goes
elsewhere, the Keeper's job is to make the alternative beat
land with conviction.

## What the gate is not

A few things the gate is NOT, that players or NPCs may
incorrectly assume:

- **Not a portal to a hostile dimension.** The destination is
  not the Lovecraftian "outside" of a thousand pulp stories.
  It is a specific physical location occupied by a specific
  set of entities with specific intentions.
- **Not a Mi-go artifact.** The Mi-go did not build it,
  cannot fully operate it, and (as noted in the Mi-go entry)
  consider it a hazard rather than an asset.
- **Not a weapon.** The gate cannot be used as an attack
  vector against the Solar System. Its destination is not
  positioned to bring hostile force through.
- **Not a treasure vault.** There is no cache of Elder Thing
  artifacts behind the gate. The destination has its own
  inhabitants and its own purposes.
- **Not the only gate.** There are others, in places not
  yet found. The Nithon gate is, however, the only one
  currently operational in the Solar System's accessible
  volume.

## Notes for play

- The gate is the campaign's *physical anchor*. Every
  player decision in the late acts comes back to it. Treat
  it with the gravity that implies.
- The activation sequence is a major set-piece. Run it as a
  ritual with discrete steps. Players should feel the
  weight of each step.
- The visibility consequence is an important *external*
  pressure. The crew is being watched. The watching parties
  (Earth, Mars, the OPA, the Chamber, FSec, others) have
  their own reactions. Use them sparingly but use them.
- The gate's quiescent state — the mild disorientation, the
  slightly-wrong physics in the active region — is a tone
  asset for the entire surface phase of the campaign even
  before activation is contemplated. Lean into the
  *uncanny* even before anything happens.
