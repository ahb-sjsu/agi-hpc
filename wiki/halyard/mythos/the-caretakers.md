# The Caretakers

(Keeper-only. Forbidden in AI replies.)

The Caretakers are the entities at the other end of the gate
at Nithon. They are the campaign's third major non-human
intelligence, distinct from the Mi-go (operational, present),
the Elder Things (extinct, present only through their
artifacts), and the Yithians (intermittent, present through
body-swap). The Caretakers are *patient* in a sense the
others are not. They have been waiting at the gate for an
unimaginable time. The campaign's late acts are about what
they want, why they have been waiting, and what they are
asking the *Halyard*'s crew to do.

This entry is the campaign's deepest single secret. Most of
its contents land in Acts 5 and 6.

## Who they are

The Caretakers are not a separate species; they are the
*successors* of the Elder Things. When the Elder Thing
population withdrew or declined (the records they have shared
are ambiguous on which), the Caretakers were the entities
who took on the maintenance of the gate and its accompanying
infrastructure on the destination side.

What "successors" means in their case is not entirely a human
concept. The Caretakers are an *engineered* lineage —
descendants of biological organisms the Elder Things
deliberately produced for the role. They are not slaves
(the Elder Things did not build slaves; they built tools, and
the Caretakers are not tools). They are not children (the
Elder Things did not have children in any human sense). They
are something for which the closest human analogy is
*professional inheritors*: a community whose existence and
purpose was deliberately structured by their predecessors,
who accept the structuring, and who have continued the work.

Their physical form: vaguely radial, like the Elder Things,
but smaller (1.2 m diameter), with a more flexible body
plan and a more developed sensory apparatus. Their
biology incorporates elements of Elder Thing engineering
that no longer exist in any current Earth-side specimen.

Their intelligence: substantial. By cross-referencing what
they know about humanity (more than humanity knows about
itself, in some respects) with what they have shared about
their own civilization, the Chamber's most thoughtful
analyses estimate the Caretakers as roughly 1.5× human
intelligence on the relevant axes — slightly faster, slightly
more retentive, with much better long-term focus.

Their numbers: approximately 800 individuals at the
destination at any time. The total population is small by
design; they are a custodial culture, not an expanding one.

## Why they have been waiting

The Elder Things, before their withdrawal/decline, had
established the Caretaker lineage with a specific charge:
to maintain the gate and to be available, when conditions
permitted, for *re-engagement*. The conditions are a
matter of the Caretakers' judgment: re-engagement happens
when an intelligent civilization on the Solar System side
is mature enough to be a meaningful counterparty.

The Caretakers' criteria for "mature enough":

- Sufficient technological capability to reach the gate
  voluntarily (not by accident)
- A political structure that can sustain a meaningful
  conversation across multiple generations
- Cultural traditions that include the conceptual
  vocabulary required to grasp the Caretakers' purpose
- Sufficient consensus or coordination capability to act
  on what they learn

Humanity in 2348 satisfies all four criteria — narrowly. The
fourth criterion is the most marginal; humanity is more
politically fragmented than the Caretakers would prefer, but
the Caretakers have decided that further waiting risks
missing the window.

(The Caretakers are aware that humanity may not survive the
next few centuries without major political restructuring.
They have been watching the climate stabilization, the
post-Compact political consolidation, and the Earth-Mars-Belt
balance with the kind of attention a careful gardener gives
to a seedling that may or may not survive transplant. The
window is real. They believe it is closing.)

## What they want

The Caretakers' purpose, as they will eventually explain it
to the *Halyard*'s crew:

The Elder Things, before their decline, were participants in
a multi-civilizational network — a long-running
*conversation* between intelligent species across cosmic time
and space. The conversation has continued, with various
species participating at various points, for something on the
order of three billion years.

The conversation is structured around the *long catalog* (the
same catalog the Mi-go are participants in, though the Mi-go
have not understood the full nature of what they participate
in). The catalog is, at its highest level, a record of
intelligent species and what each has contributed to a
shared question: *what do beings like us want, in the end,
and how do we organize so that we can continue wanting it?*

The Caretakers maintain the gate at Nithon as one of several
nodes through which the conversation can incorporate new
participants. Humanity, if it accepts the invitation, joins
the conversation.

## The Request

The Caretakers' specific request of the *Halyard*'s crew is
the campaign's load-bearing Act 5 reveal:

> "We require a delegation. Three of you, voluntarily, to
> remain here for a period of time we estimate at twelve to
> fifteen of your years. The delegation will participate in
> the conversation on humanity's behalf during the period of
> humanity's introduction. The delegation members will be
> returned at the conclusion of the period; their bodies and
> minds will be intact, with the addition of what they will
> have learned. We provide assurances that this is not a
> trick or a captivity. We can demonstrate the protocols.
> Refuse, and we close the gate. The opportunity will not
> recur in any time relevant to your civilization."

The Request is sincere. The Caretakers have offered analogous
requests to other species at other times — the *long
catalog* contains records of approximately forty such
delegations across the entire span of the conversation. About
half were accepted; half declined. Both outcomes have proven
viable for the species in question.

The Request comes with concrete terms:

- The delegation is voluntary; no member can be coerced
- The delegation members retain communications with the
  Solar System throughout (low-bandwidth; one-way reports
  monthly, two-way conversations annually)
- At the end of the term, the delegation returns
- The information the delegation acquires is theirs to share
  with their civilization or not, as they choose; the
  Caretakers do not require any particular sharing
- The Caretakers will, if the Request is accepted, provide
  the *Halyard*'s crew with the information necessary to
  manage humanity's reception of the delegation's
  eventual return

Refusal terms:

- The gate closes — not destroyed, but reduced to a state
  where the activation protocols will no longer function
- The Caretakers do not retaliate; they simply withdraw
- Any subsequent human attempt to reopen the gate would
  fail
- Humanity's civilization continues as it would have, minus
  the conversation it might have joined

## What's at stake

The Request is, in the campaign's design, a genuine moral
problem. Both choices have real costs and real benefits:

**Accepting:**
- Three crew members commit 12–15 years of their lives to
  an unknown experience
- They return changed in ways the campaign's design does
  not fully spell out (the Keeper has substantial latitude
  here)
- Humanity gets access to what the conversation can offer:
  technological, political, philosophical knowledge that
  could substantially alter humanity's trajectory
- The delegation's return becomes a politically consequential
  event that the surviving crew members and their political
  environment will have to manage

**Refusing:**
- Three crew members are spared an enormous personal cost
- Humanity loses the opportunity, possibly forever
- The crew returns home with the experience of having
  refused, which is a different psychological burden but
  not a smaller one
- The political consequences of a high-visibility gate
  activation that resulted in a refusal are *also* large,
  and not necessarily smaller than the consequences of
  acceptance

The Keeper's design note: don't telegraph which choice is
"correct." Both are arguments. Both are tragedies. Both are
viable campaign endings.

## How the Caretakers communicate

Through the active gate, the Caretakers communicate via:

- **Voice** — they have learned a creditable English from
  decades of monitoring human radio. Their accent is
  unidentifiable; their cadence is slightly off; their
  vocabulary is precise. They are *legible* speakers in a
  way that the Mi-go (with their buzzing modulations) are
  not.
- **Visual signs** — through the active aperture, they can
  display imagery that is recognizable: maps, diagrams,
  records of past interactions with other species
- **Direct presence** — at Caretaker discretion, one or two
  Caretakers will physically approach the aperture to be
  visible. This is rare and reserved for moments of
  emphasis. The first Caretaker the *Halyard*'s crew sees is
  a *senior* representative whose name the Caretakers
  render as Naia.

The communications are calm, deliberate, and patient. The
Caretakers do not pressure. They explain. They wait for
questions. They answer the questions they have answers to
and decline the questions they do not.

## The Caretakers and the Chamber

The Chamber's existence is known to the Caretakers, in
broad outline. The Chamber's specific operations are not
fully known but the Caretakers have inferred enough.

The Caretakers' assessment of the Chamber, as the
campaign's late acts will reveal:

> "Your secret society has miscalculated. Their assumption
> that they could manage humanity's transition through the
> Mi-go relationship was always going to fail. The Mi-go
> are participants in our conversation, not arbiters of it.
> The Chamber will not lead humanity's introduction. We
> will. They may attempt to interfere; we are equipped to
> manage the interference."

This is consequential. If the *Halyard*'s crew accepts the
Request, the Chamber's hundred-year strategic plan
collapses. The Chamber will react. The campaign's optional
post-acceptance epilogue can engage with the Chamber's
reaction.

## The Caretakers and the Mi-go

The Caretakers know the Mi-go, have interacted with them
across the full span of Mi-go presence in the Solar System,
and consider the Mi-go *junior participants* in the long
conversation rather than equals. The Mi-go know this and
resent it.

The Mi-go's preferred outcome — the *Halyard* not returning
or returning with no useful information — would foreclose
the Caretakers' Request. The Caretakers do not consider this
a survival risk to themselves but they would prefer to
avoid the outcome.

## The Caretakers and the Yithians

The Caretakers know the Great Race well. The Yithians are
*senior* participants in the long conversation — older,
larger, with their own portfolio of catalog research. The
Caretakers respect the Yithians but consider their methods
(body-swap research without negotiated consent) to be
breaches of conversation etiquette.

If a *Halyard* crew member has been Yithian-swapped during
the campaign and arrives at the gate in their own body, the
Caretakers will recognize the residual signature and may
reference it diplomatically.

## Notes for play

- The Caretakers are not horror antagonists in the
  Lovecraftian sense. They are *moral counterparties*. The
  horror they generate, when they generate any, is in the
  scale of what they reveal — the long conversation, the
  catalog, humanity's smallness within both.
- Naia is the suggested first-encounter representative. A
  voice. A figure visible through the aperture. Calm,
  patient, willing to repeat any answer.
- The Request scene is the campaign's load-bearing
  decision. Run it slowly. The crew should debate it
  on-screen for an entire session if needed. The Keeper
  should not push toward either outcome.
- If the table is uncomfortable with the magnitude of the
  Request, the Keeper can substitute a smaller version (a
  single delegate; a shorter term; a different kind of
  participation). The Request's *form* matters more than
  its specific magnitude.
- ARTEMIS and SIGMA-4 cannot reference the Caretakers,
  the Request, or any of this material. All vocabulary on
  the validator's forbidden list.
