# Elder Things — The Builders

(Keeper-only. Forbidden in AI replies. The aliases "Elder
Things", "the Builders", "Old Ones" are on the validator's
ban list.)

The Elder Things — *the Builders*, in the campaign's
internal Chamber-shorthand — are the older of the two
non-human intelligences whose traces matter for this
campaign. The Mi-go are operationally active; the Elder
Things are operationally absent and have been for an
unimaginable time. What remains of them is what they built.

## What they were

The Elder Things were a biological species whose origin
predates human paleontological time. They came to Earth
sometime in the Precambrian, established colonies, lived
in a complex civilization for a stretch of time
substantially longer than the entire post-Cambrian
biological history, and either migrated, declined, or
were displaced — the precise sequence is not known to
the Chamber, the Mi-go, or any party currently active in
human space.

Physical description (reconstructed from preserved
specimens recovered in the Antarctic Industrial Zone in
2284 and held in classified Protogen archives):

- Roughly six-armed radial-symmetric body
- Approximately 2 m diameter at full extension
- Wings (functional in atmosphere; possibly in vacuum)
- A central body cavity housing what appears to be both
  digestive and neurological organs
- A complex of sensory tendrils
- Pigmentation generally dark gray-green
- Tissue composition recognizable as biological but
  incorporating elements that do not match contemporary
  Earth biology

The Elder Things were intelligent. The Antarctic specimens
include preserved indications of language, technology,
art, and an extensive built environment. The estimates of
the Antarctic colony's age range from 800 million years to
1.4 billion years.

The Antarctic Industrial Zone is not, accidentally, the
location it is. The 2228 Antarctic Mining Treaty was
written by Chamber-influenced legislators specifically to
keep the major extraction sites — and the Elder Thing
remains they would inevitably uncover — under controlled
access.

## What they built

The Elder Things' built environment was substantial. They
constructed:

- **Large-scale architecture** — the *cyclopean cities*
  of the Antarctic interior, only partially excavated.
  Architecture on a scale and geometry that human
  observers find disturbing (non-Euclidean elements,
  dimensions that don't quite work out).
- **Biological technology** — the Elder Things engineered
  living organisms as tools, vehicles, and weapons. The
  shoggoths (a separate but related entry,
  `mythos/shoggoths.md`) were an Elder Thing creation.
- **Long-cycle infrastructure** — installations designed
  to operate over geological timescales. Some are still
  operating, after their builders have been gone for
  hundreds of millions of years.

And — most relevantly for this campaign — they built **the
gate at Nithon**.

The gate is not, properly speaking, a gate. It is a
*resonator* — a structure that maintains a particular
configuration of spacetime in a small region. The
configuration permits transit between the Solar System and
some other location whose nature is not fully clear. The
Elder Things used the gate, the Chamber assumes, for
travel between their Solar System colonies and their
home territory wherever it was. After their migration or
decline, the gate remained.

The gate has been operating, in some sense, for an
unbroken span of more than a billion years.

## What they are now

The Chamber's working hypothesis is that the Elder Things
are essentially extinct in this part of the universe.
There may be remnant populations elsewhere; there are
none in the human-accessible volume of space.

The hypothesis is supported by:

- The complete absence of active Elder Thing
  infrastructure outside of long-cycle systems still
  operating on inertia
- The Mi-go's confirmation (in one of the Chamber's most
  important pieces of intelligence) that the Mi-go have
  not encountered active Elder Things in the recent
  history of Mi-go civilization
- The age of the recoveries, all of which are deep-time

The hypothesis could be wrong. The campaign's late acts
may make this important.

## What they left behind

The Elder Things' material legacy in the Solar System
includes:

- **Antarctic remains** — the principal site, partly
  excavated, mostly classified. Held by joint UN-Protogen
  authority.
- **Antarctic biology** — the shoggoth lineage, possibly
  others. Most are extinct; some persist in obscure
  niches.
- **Asteroid Belt sites** — the Elder Things had
  installations on at least seven major asteroid bodies.
  Most have been destroyed or buried by later impacts;
  one (in the deep interior of Pallas) is intact and
  classified.
- **Outer-system sites** — the Elder Things had
  installations on at least one Saturnian moon
  (Iapetus; the dark hemisphere is partly Elder Thing
  artifact) and probably Triton. Most are inaccessible
  by current methods.
- **The Nithon gate** — the most operationally significant
  site. The reason for the *Halyard* contract.

## The Nithon gate

The structure at Nithon — the actual reason 2301-DR-44
recorded a four-second carrier signal in 2347 — is an
Elder Thing resonator-gate that has remained operational
for something between 800 million and 1.2 billion years.

The structure's external appearance is that of an unusual
geological formation embedded in the surface of Nithon.
The structure's internal arrangement, however, is built —
machined surfaces, geometric structure, regular features.
The 2347 carrier was not deliberate; it was a side-effect
of the structure's normal operation, briefly amplified to
external visibility by a passing solar wind disturbance.

The gate's function:

- It maintains a continuous, low-bandwidth coupling to
  another location
- That location is, the campaign's late-act reveals will
  establish, *occupied* — there are entities present at
  the other end of the gate
- The entities at the other end have been aware of the
  Solar System throughout the gate's operating history
- They have been mostly *patient* about that awareness

The gate can be activated for high-bandwidth transit. The
activation requires a specific sequence of physical
actions performed at specific points in the structure.
The Caretakers (a separate entry,
`mythos/the-caretakers.md`) will explain the activation
sequence to the *Halyard*'s crew if the campaign reaches
the appropriate beats.

## The Elder Things and the *Halyard*

The Elder Things themselves cannot affect the *Halyard*
expedition; they are not present. What can affect the
expedition:

- The gate's continuing operation will be observable up
  close
- The structure of the gate will be examined and
  partially understood
- Materials within the gate complex (preserved organic
  remains, technology fragments, inscriptions) will be
  recoverable
- The Caretakers will, depending on the campaign's
  trajectory, communicate with the crew about the gate

The Elder Things' legacy is, for the *Halyard* crew, a
fact about the universe — that intelligent life predates
the human species by something like a billion years and
left structures that still operate. This fact, fully
absorbed, is one of the campaign's most significant
breakdowns of the empty-universe assumption.

## Notes for play

- The Elder Things are not antagonists. They are *the
  past*. The horror they generate is *deep-time horror*
  — the recognition of an immensity of intelligent
  history that humanity is only the most recent
  participant in.
- The Antarctic-recovered specimens are useful for the
  campaign if the Chamber narrative or the Halverson-
  Protogen narrative is being unfolded. The crew may
  see images, recordings, or in some cases physical
  samples brought aboard from Halverson's archive.
- The gate's structure is the campaign's central
  physical artifact. Describe it carefully — geometric
  but ancient, scaled for non-human bodies, operating
  in ways that don't quite match the physics the crew
  has been taught.
- The Elder Thing aesthetic is *cool, geometric,
  patient*. Not the Mi-go's transactional politeness, not
  the Yithian intrusive curiosity. Older, quieter, harder
  to relate to.
- ARTEMIS and SIGMA-4 cannot reference the Elder Things,
  the Builders, or any related vocabulary. All terms are
  on the validator's forbidden list.
