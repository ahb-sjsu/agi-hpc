# The Mi-go — the Winter Correspondents

(Keeper-only. Forbidden in AI replies. Title and aliases are
on the validator's ban list.)

The Mi-go (also called the *fungi from Yuggoth*, the
*Winter Correspondents* in Chamber teaching, the *Outer
Mycelium* in some Belter folk-magical traditions, and the
*pale ones* in nineteenth-century New England rumor) are
the most operationally active non-human intelligence in
human space. They have been since at least the early
eighteenth century, in something like their modern form,
and probably longer.

## What they are

The Mi-go are crustaceous-fungal entities of unknown
ultimate origin, whose nominal home base is one of the
trans-Neptunian or further trans-Plutonian bodies (the
Mi-go themselves use a designation that translates
approximately as *Yuggoth*; the human astronomical
community has not securely identified which body they
mean, though there are candidates).

Physical description (from the very limited corpus of
direct human encounters):

- Roughly humanoid in scale (1.5–1.8 m body length)
- Pinkish-pale segmented carapace
- Multiple manipulator-limbs (typically six)
- Wing-like membranes (functional in vacuum, used for
  short-distance space-flight)
- A head-equivalent organ that does not function as a
  head in the human sense
- An organic communications apparatus that produces
  high-pitched buzzing modulations comprehensible only to
  trained interpreters

The Mi-go are not biologically uniform. They appear to be
products of a designed or selected biological program; the
individual Mi-go a human encounters is one of perhaps a
dozen different specialized morphs, optimized for different
functions.

## What they do

The Mi-go's activities in human space, as best the Chamber
has reconstructed them across two centuries of contact:

### Mining and resource extraction

The Mi-go conduct industrial operations on bodies in the
trans-Neptunian and Kuiper-edge regions. They extract
materials — primarily certain heavy metals and certain
unusual organic compounds — that they consider valuable.
The scale of the operation is unknown but probably
substantial.

### Research and observation

The Mi-go observe human and other intelligent species
across a substantial volume of space. They have detailed
information about human civilization, human politics,
human technology, and (uncomfortably) human individuals.
They are particularly interested in the contents of
specific human brains; the brain-cylinder protocol is the
mechanism by which they conduct that interest.

### Trade

The Mi-go trade with selected human institutions
(principally the Chamber) for:

- Organic materials, particularly central-nervous
  tissue
- Political and economic information
- Specific technical data (especially on UN, MCR, and OPA
  military and intelligence capabilities)

In return they provide:

- Technical information on materials, cosmology,
  astrophysics, biology
- Brain-cylinder transitions for selected individuals
- Limited protection from other Mythos entities
- The *gift of the older knowledge* — a deeply ambiguous
  category of cosmological awareness that the Chamber's
  members who have received it describe in language that
  off-Chamber observers find disturbing

### Brain-cylinder transitions

The Mi-go's signature operation. The Mi-go can extract a
human (or other) consciousness from its biological
substrate and house it in a metal cylinder of Mi-go
manufacture. The cylinder preserves the consciousness
indefinitely, in conditions that most subjects describe as
*aware but limited*. With appropriate Mi-go technology
auxiliaries, the housed consciousness can communicate, can
process information, and can in some cases be returned to
a different biological substrate (Mi-go-grown body, Mi-go-
grown body-section, or in rare cases the original body
preserved during the cylinder period).

The Chamber considers this the principal mechanism by
which selected human consciousnesses can be preserved into
the post-human era. Some Chamber members have already
undergone the transition; their cylinders are stored at
Chamber-controlled facilities.

The transition is not pleasant. Subjects who are returned
to flesh report extended psychological distress; the
return rate is approximately 60% (40% of cylinder-
transitioned subjects either remain cylinder-housed or
fail to integrate post-return).

## What they want, in the long term

The Mi-go's long-term goals are unclear and probably not
fully knowable from the human side. The Chamber's working
hypothesis is that the Mi-go are participants in a
multi-species post-human civilization that already exists
elsewhere, and that the Mi-go's interest in human space is
*recruiting* — the gradual transition of selected human
consciousnesses into the post-human civilization.

The Mi-go themselves describe their goals in terms the
Chamber finds difficult to translate: roughly, *the
preservation of useful examples* and *the maintenance of
the long catalog*. The catalog appears to be a record of
intelligent species across cosmic time; the Mi-go are
catalogers.

The Mi-go are not, by their own description, hostile to
humanity. They are also not friendly to humanity. They are
indifferent in a specifically curatorial way — the way a
museum staff is indifferent to a particular specimen they
are preserving.

## What they don't tell the Chamber

Even after centuries of contact, the Mi-go withhold things
from the Chamber:

- The actual size of the Mi-go presence in the solar
  system. The Chamber estimates a few hundred individuals;
  the actual figure is probably in the thousands.
- The full scope of Mi-go activities on the trans-Neptunian
  bodies. The Chamber knows about industrial operations;
  the Chamber does not know about the larger *infrastructure*
  the Mi-go have constructed.
- The Mi-go's relationship with other entities (Elder
  Things, the Great Race, others). The Chamber assumes
  rivalry; the actual relationships are more complicated.
- The Mi-go's view of the gate at Nithon. The Chamber
  assumes the Mi-go are interested in Nithon. The Mi-go
  are *not* interested in Nithon — they consider Nithon a
  hazard to be avoided.

## The Mi-go and the *Halyard*

The Mi-go are aware of the *Halyard* contract. They have
not intervened. Their assessment is that the *Halyard*
expedition will either:

- Find nothing significant (low probability), in which
  case the Mi-go are uninterested
- Find evidence of Elder Thing or Great Race activity
  (moderate probability), in which case the Mi-go's
  preferred outcome is that the evidence be suppressed
- Find evidence of the gate (moderate probability), in
  which case the Mi-go's preferred outcome is that the
  *Halyard* not return

The Mi-go will not, however, take direct action against
the *Halyard*. Their doctrine is that direct action against
Chamber operations damages the long-term Chamber
relationship, which they consider valuable. They will, if
the situation develops, communicate their preferences to
the Chamber and let the Chamber manage the response.

## Encountering the Mi-go in play

The campaign has multiple paths to Mi-go encounter,
depending on Keeper choice:

- **Distant intelligence** — players learn about the
  Mi-go through documents, recovered records, or Eddy's
  eventual cooperation. No direct encounter. The most
  conservative option; suitable for new-to-cosmic-horror
  tables.
- **Recovered specimen** — the *Halyard* finds a Mi-go
  body, dead, on or near Nithon. The body is examined.
  The implications cascade.
- **Recovered cylinder** — the *Halyard* finds a brain
  cylinder. The voice in the cylinder talks. This is the
  Keeper's reliable way to introduce the Mi-go directly,
  as character voice rather than as character body.
- **Live encounter** — rare; Keeper's option. The Mi-go
  are not natural cosmic-horror antagonists in the
  Lovecraftian sense — they are too political, too
  rational, too *transactional*. A live encounter is
  more uncanny than terrifying. Use sparingly.

## Notes for play

- The Mi-go's signature uncanny is their *politeness*.
  They negotiate. They keep agreements. They are
  scrupulous about their own protocols. The horror is
  not that they are monstrous; it is that they are
  *reasonable* in a way that has nothing to do with
  human flourishing.
- The brain-cylinder protocol is the Mi-go's most
  recognizable feature. Use it deliberately. The
  cylinder voice is one of the campaign's most
  effective horror beats.
- Mi-go *body* descriptions are SAN-cost moments;
  Mi-go *voice* (cylinder or modulated communication) is
  more like an interrogation room — disturbing without
  being shocking.
- ARTEMIS and SIGMA-4 are forbidden from discussing the
  Mi-go. Both AIs will deflect or fall silent on
  Mi-go-adjacent queries, by validator design.
