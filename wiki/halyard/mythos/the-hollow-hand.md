# The Hollow Hand

(Keeper-only. Forbidden in AI replies.)

The Hollow Hand is what happens when ordinary people figure
out that something like the Chamber exists, decide it
shouldn't, and try to do something about it. They are an
*anti-Chamber resistance* — a loose network of human beings
across human space who have, for various reasons, become
aware of Mythos-adjacent activity and have committed to
opposing it.

They are also *contaminated* in a way that makes their
opposition incomplete. Most Hollow Hand members have, in the
course of their work, become more like what they oppose than
they realize. The campaign's late acts may use this
ambiguity.

## Origin

The Hollow Hand emerged in the 2270s out of the same
political ferment that produced the post-Compact
consolidation. Its founders were a small group of UN
intelligence officers who, in the course of their work,
encountered evidence of the Chamber's operations and
concluded that the Chamber was an unacceptable institution.

The founders did not have a name for what they were opposing.
The phrase *the Hollow Hand* came from one founder's
deathbed letter, in which he described the Chamber as "a
hand that reaches into our world from somewhere we cannot
see — and it is hollow, and we have to learn to fill it
with something."

The phrase stuck. The organization that grew from the
founders' work took the name and has used it since.

## What they oppose

The Hollow Hand's stated opposition is to:

- The Chamber's transition doctrine and its operations
- The Chamber's recruitment of human consciousnesses for
  Mi-go cylinder-transition
- The Chamber's information-trade with the Mi-go (which the
  Hollow Hand correctly assesses as fundamentally
  asymmetrical to humanity's disadvantage)
- The political-cover operations that allow the Chamber to
  operate in plain sight
- The Protogen Special Programs Branch's most egregious
  research practices

The Hollow Hand's *unstated* opposition — and the one that
has shaped them more than the stated mission — is to the
*idea* that humanity's transition is inevitable. The Hollow
Hand's members reject the cosmological pessimism that the
Chamber and the Starry Wisdom share. They believe humanity
can survive on its own terms.

This belief is, by the campaign's underlying cosmology,
*probably wrong*. The Caretakers' assessment is that humanity
will not survive without contact and integration. The
Hollow Hand's stance is therefore tragic in a way they don't
fully recognize.

## Structure

The Hollow Hand is *more* informal than the Chamber and
*less* organized than the Starry Wisdom. Its structure is:

- **Cells** — local groups of 3 to 12 members, each operating
  semi-independently. About 80 cells across human space.
- **Liaisons** — individuals who maintain communication
  between cells. About 30 active liaisons.
- **The Council** — perhaps 12 senior figures who set
  strategic direction. The Council does not meet
  physically; it operates through encrypted message
  exchange.
- **The First Among** — a single figure who, by tradition,
  serves as the most senior coordinator. The current First
  Among is *Halden Cross*. Yes, that one — the *Halyard*'s
  Security Officer.

Total active membership: probably 700–900 people. The
membership skews toward intelligence and military
backgrounds (former FSec, former MCRN intelligence, former
OPA security personnel) but includes scientists,
journalists, and a small number of clergy across multiple
traditions.

## Halden Cross

The campaign's most plot-significant Hollow Hand member.
Cross's biography is, in the publicly accessible version:

- Born 2299, Earth (US-Canadian Confederation)
- UNN service 2317–2334 (rank: Commander)
- Resigned commission 2334 over disputes whose details are
  not in the public record
- Deep-range security contractor since 2336
- Hired by Mao-Kwikowski for the *Halyard* expedition in
  early 2348

The non-public version:

- Cross's UNN resignation was over an FSec investigation
  he conducted into Chamber-adjacent activities. He
  uncovered too much; he was politely redirected to other
  duties; he resigned rather than stop pursuing it.
- He joined the Hollow Hand in 2335.
- He has risen through the Hollow Hand's structure on the
  strength of operational competence and the discretion he
  developed in UNN service.
- He took the Hollow Hand's First Among role in 2342.
- He took the *Halyard* contract specifically because the
  Hollow Hand has assessed the contract as a Chamber
  operation. His assignment, from the Hollow Hand's
  Council, is to *understand what's at Nithon and ensure
  it does not become a Chamber asset*.

Cross will not introduce himself as Hollow Hand to the
crew, ever, unless the campaign reaches a point where
revealing his affiliation is necessary. Most likely, this
revelation comes in Act 5 or 6, around the gate.

Cross is competent. Cross is loyal — to the Hollow Hand,
to the *Halyard*'s legitimate mission, to the crew within
limits. Cross is not a fanatic; he will not betray the
crew for the Hollow Hand's purposes.

But Cross *will*, if forced to choose between the
*Halyard*'s mission as defined by Mao-Kwikowski/Halverson
and what the Hollow Hand believes the right outcome to be,
choose the latter. The campaign's late acts may make this
choice explicit.

## Contamination

The Hollow Hand's *contamination* — the reason they are not
straightforward heroes — is in the methods their opposition
to the Chamber has required.

Specifically:

- The Hollow Hand has, on at least four occasions in its
  history, conducted *neutralization* operations against
  Chamber operatives. Some of these operations resulted in
  deaths. The Council's official position is that these
  operations were necessary; the alternative was tolerating
  Chamber activities that would have produced larger harms.
  The position is defensible. It is also *not* the position
  of an organization that has clean hands.
- The Hollow Hand has, more than once, used Mi-go-derived
  technology in its operations. The Mi-go's cylinder-
  transition records are, the Council learned, partially
  accessible to anyone who knows the protocols. The
  Hollow Hand has used this access to extract information
  about Chamber operations. Doing so has compromised at
  least three Hollow Hand members in ways the Council has
  not openly acknowledged.
- The Hollow Hand has, in 2287 and 2331, deliberately
  destroyed historical archives that contained Mythos-
  relevant material. The destructions prevented the
  archives from reaching the Chamber, which was the
  intent. They also prevented the archives from being
  *studied honestly*, which the Starry Wisdom Church (the
  intended subsequent custodian) considers a serious
  violation of the long-watch ethic.

The Hollow Hand's members are not bad people. The
organization has done bad things. The campaign's late
acts may make this distinction matter.

## Goals

The Hollow Hand's strategic goals:

1. **Disrupt the Chamber's transition pipeline.** Specifically,
   identify and protect potential Chamber recruits before
   the Chamber can reach them; expose Chamber operations
   when exposure is operationally feasible; make the
   Chamber's work harder.
2. **Suppress access to certain Mythos artifacts and
   knowledge.** Specifically, prevent the Chamber and
   Protogen SPB from acquiring materials they could use to
   advance their work.
3. **Develop an alternative response.** This is the goal
   the Hollow Hand has made the least progress on. The
   Council does not have a clear positive program; they
   know what they oppose, not what they propose.
4. **Survive.** The Hollow Hand has lost members to
   Chamber retaliation, to operational accidents, and to
   contamination-related failures. Continued existence is,
   by Council reckoning, an achievement.

The third goal is the campaign-relevant one. If the *Halyard*
finds the Caretakers and the Caretakers' Request, the
Hollow Hand will have an opinion. The opinion will not be
straightforwardly favorable. The Caretakers offer a kind of
managed transition that the Hollow Hand has spent fifty
years opposing in another form. Cross will have to navigate
this in real time.

## The Hollow Hand's view of the Caretakers

The Hollow Hand is *aware* of the Caretakers' existence,
through the Geneva archive material the Starry Wisdom has
allowed selected researchers to see. The Hollow Hand's
Council has internally debated what stance to take. The
current position is *suspicious deferral*: the Caretakers
are not the Chamber, and a direct conversation with humanity
is not what the Chamber proposes, but the Hollow Hand is
not yet convinced the Caretakers' Request is good for
humanity.

If the *Halyard* contacts the Caretakers and accepts the
Request, the Hollow Hand's Council will need to make a
strategic decision. Cross's vote will matter. He is on the
*Halyard*. He will be the Hollow Hand member with the most
direct knowledge of what was offered.

## Notes for play

- Cross is a *deep* NPC. Don't reveal his Hollow Hand
  affiliation until the Keeper specifically wants the
  beat. Run him as a competent, slightly closed-off,
  professionally polite Security Officer for as long as
  the campaign permits.
- A PC who is, themselves, Hollow Hand-affiliated would
  be a major narrative development. Discuss at Session 0
  if a player wants to take this option.
- The Hollow Hand's contamination is the campaign's
  *moral midpoint* — the reminder that opposing the
  cosmic horror is not the same as having clean hands.
  Use it to complicate easy moral lines.
- All Hollow Hand vocabulary is on the validator's
  forbidden list. ARTEMIS and SIGMA-4 will not reference
  the organization or its members in any reply.
