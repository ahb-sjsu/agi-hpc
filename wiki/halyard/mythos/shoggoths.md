# Shoggoths

(Keeper-only. Forbidden in AI replies.)

The shoggoths are the most successful surviving Elder Thing
biological creation in the Solar System. They are not
intelligent in the contemporary sense, not common, and not
present in the campaign except as plot-adjacent presence.
But they exist. They matter. The PCs are unlikely to
encounter one in the course of the *Halyard* expedition; if
they do, the encounter is the campaign's most viscerally
horrifying single beat.

## What they are

Shoggoths are an engineered organism — protoplasmic, polymorphic,
roughly 5–8 m in volume at adult size, capable of forming and
dissolving structural elements (limbs, sensory organs, internal
tools) on demand. The Elder Things designed them as
general-purpose servitors: muscle, fine manipulation, and
limited problem-solving capacity in a single deployable
biological unit.

The Elder Things' design tradeoff was *flexibility for
intelligence*. A shoggoth can do almost any physical task; it
cannot understand abstract concepts, plan for the long term,
or develop a culture. Its cognition is roughly equivalent to a
clever non-human animal — about an octopus's reasoning capacity
but with none of the curiosity.

This was the design intent. The Elder Things wanted a tool,
not a partner. They got one.

## Where they come from

The Elder Things produced shoggoths at scale during their
active period. The shoggoth lineage dates back at least 800
million years and possibly longer. They were used in:

- Construction (the cyclopean cities of the Antarctic
  interior were partly shoggoth-built)
- Maintenance (industrial equipment, biological infrastructure)
- Defense (shoggoths could be deployed against external
  threats and were, by all evidence, effective)
- Biological research (some shoggoths were specialized for
  laboratory work)

When the Elder Things declined or withdrew, the shoggoths did
not. They had been engineered for biological persistence —
they reproduce by fission, can survive on a wide range of
nutrients, and don't require the Elder Things' active
maintenance to live.

The post-Elder-Thing shoggoth population gradually declined,
through accident, environmental change, and intra-shoggoth
competition, but did not disappear. By 2348, perhaps a few
dozen shoggoths persist in the Solar System.

## Where they are

The known persistent shoggoth populations are:

### Antarctica

The principal current population. Approximately 8–12 shoggoths
inhabit the deepest sections of the un-excavated Elder Thing
city complexes under the Antarctic Industrial Zone. The 2284
recovery operation that produced the Antarctic Elder Thing
specimens also produced the first confirmed contemporary
shoggoth observations.

The Antarctic shoggoths are managed by Protogen's Special
Programs Branch under tight containment protocols. Three
shoggoths have been the subject of long-term study; the
others are observed at distance and not interfered with.

The shoggoths are aware of human presence and *non-aggressive
unless approached in their territory*. They have, as best the
SPB has determined, no interest in venturing outside the deep
sections of the Antarctic complexes.

### Pallas

The deep-interior Elder Thing site on Pallas (mentioned in the
elder-things entry) contains 2–4 shoggoths in a state SPB
researchers describe as *dormant*. The dormancy appears to be
a metabolic adaptation to the asteroid's low resources; the
shoggoths cycle into and out of activity depending on the
local conditions.

The Pallas site is classified by joint UN-OPA agreement and
is not accessible to the *Halyard*'s expedition.

### Possibly elsewhere

The Iapetus and Triton sites may also contain shoggoths.
These sites have not been thoroughly surveyed. The Caretakers
have, in the conversations the campaign's late acts may
include, mentioned that the gate-maintenance protocols
include some shoggoth presence in the trans-Solar
infrastructure, but the geographic distribution is not
fully clear.

## Behavior

A shoggoth in its passive state is, by Earth-creature
standards, almost inert. It moves slowly, processes nutrients
from the surrounding environment (it can metabolize a wide
range of organic and some inorganic compounds), maintains
internal homeostasis, and does not initiate activity unless
prompted.

A prompted shoggoth — one given a task by an Elder Thing
controller, or one in territorial-defense mode — is
extraordinary:

- Speed: surprising. A shoggoth can move at sustained
  10–15 km/h and burst-sprint at 30 km/h. The fluidity of
  its movement is faster than its bulk suggests.
- Strength: enormous. A shoggoth can deform structural
  elements that human-grade machinery cannot. Steel
  flooring, vehicle hulls, and most construction materials
  are not protective against direct shoggoth contact.
- Adaptability: it can form whatever appendages it needs.
  Climbing surfaces, cutting through walls, manipulating
  fine objects — all are within its physical repertoire.
- Resilience: distributed body plan; no central organs that
  can be targeted. Shoggoths can be killed by sufficient
  thermal damage (they cannot survive sustained temperatures
  above ~120°C) or by deep cellular disruption (the SPB has
  developed a chemical agent that works), but conventional
  weapons mostly inflict damage in proportion to mass
  removed, and the mass is ~5–8 m³.
- Sound: shoggoths produce a low atonal *piping* — a
  characteristic sound that the recovered Elder Thing
  records describe as *the call of the works*. The piping
  is a basic communication signal, used between shoggoths
  and (originally) between shoggoths and Elder Thing
  controllers.

The piping is the shoggoth's signature horror cue. PCs who
have read the recovered records (Halverson has copies; she
might lend them) will recognize the sound when they hear it.

## What the SPB has learned

Protogen's Special Programs Branch has had three Antarctic
shoggoths in observational study since the 2310s. The
findings:

- Shoggoths can be conditioned to follow simple instructions,
  with patience. The conditioning resembles animal training
  more than command. It takes years.
- Shoggoths recognize Elder Thing biological markers. SPB
  researchers wearing artificial Elder Thing pheromone
  preparations are tolerated more readily than ordinary
  humans.
- Shoggoths have a primitive *consensus mechanism* — when
  multiple shoggoths are in proximity, their behavior
  coordinates in ways that suggest low-bandwidth chemical
  signaling. The mechanism is not strong enough to count
  as group intelligence but is more sophisticated than
  pure pheromonal aggregation.
- Shoggoths under sustained Elder Thing-style command can
  perform construction tasks of remarkable complexity. SPB
  has demonstrated this in controlled trials. The tasks
  were modest (building specific structures from supplied
  materials) but the consistency of execution suggested
  the original Elder Thing operational use was viable.

The SPB's research is, even by Chamber standards,
problematic. It depends on a cooperative relationship between
SPB researchers and entities that are biologically alien and
whose welfare is impossible to assess by human standards.
SPB has internally debated whether the research should
continue. It continues.

## The shoggoths and the *Halyard*

The Halyard's expedition does not visit Antarctica. It does
not visit Pallas. The expedition is unlikely to encounter a
live shoggoth.

However:

- Halverson has *recordings* of shoggoth behavior. She may
  share them with the crew, in clinical context, during the
  long transit. The recordings include the piping. PCs who
  hear the piping will not forget it.
- The Caretakers, if contacted, may reveal that the gate
  infrastructure on the destination side includes some
  shoggoth-derivative organisms, modified for the
  destination's environment. The crew, if they accept the
  Request, will encounter these. They are not exactly
  shoggoths, but they are the same biological lineage.
- Eddy, the Chamber operative, is informed about the SPB
  research and could discuss it if a player asks the right
  questions in the right context. Eddy is also explicitly
  not authorized to share specifics; if pressed, they
  will deflect.

The shoggoths' role in the *Halyard* campaign is therefore
*background reality*. They exist. They are part of what's
out there. They underline how far from human-scale the
Elder Thing legacy goes.

## Cinematic potential

If the Keeper wants a single high-stakes shoggoth sequence,
the campaign's most natural place is in an Act 6 epilogue
arc:

- The crew returns from Nithon. The political fallout
  begins. Halverson defects from the Chamber and offers
  the crew her archive in exchange for protection.
- The Chamber sends a recovery team to seize the archive
  before it can be read.
- The crew flees with Halverson to a Protogen Antarctic
  site she had researcher access to.
- In the deep tunnels, the recovery team catches up. So
  does a shoggoth.

This is not a campaign-default scene. It's a Keeper option
for tables that want a high-action coda. Most tables don't
need it; the Caretaker negotiation is enough.

## Notes for play

- Don't introduce the piping casually. When the players
  hear it, even on a recording, it should land.
- The shoggoth is the campaign's *escape velocity* for
  body-horror cosmic horror. Use it deliberately and
  rarely.
- A shoggoth encounter is not a winnable combat. PCs cannot
  shoot or stab their way out of it. The encounter has to
  be solved through escape, distraction, or specific
  knowledge (the SPB chemical agent, the Elder Thing
  pheromone trick).
- All shoggoth-adjacent vocabulary is on the validator's
  forbidden list. ARTEMIS and SIGMA-4 will not reference
  shoggoths, the piping, or related material.
