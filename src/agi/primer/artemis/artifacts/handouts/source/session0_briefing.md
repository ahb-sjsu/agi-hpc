# Welcome to the *Halyard*

You have been contracted by **Mao‑Kwikowski Mercantile Concern** as
crew aboard the deep-survey vessel **MKS Halyard** for the Nithon
expedition. This briefing prepares you for Session Zero
(character creation + mission brief) and the first session proper.

Read through it before we sit down. Bring questions.

---

## The Setting

The year is **2370**. Humanity has colonized the inner system and is
pushing out past the gas giants. Travel is grinding — weeks on an
Epstein drive rather than minutes on FTL. Radio moves at lightspeed;
a call to Mars is measured in minutes, to Saturn in hours, to the
outer belt in days.

Politics fragments into three pressure blocs — **Earth** (UN), **Mars**
(MCRN), and the **Belt** (OPA) — with corporate enclaves wedged between
them. Most people live in borrowed oxygen.

There is also, somewhere in the dark between inhabited rocks,
something that is **not human**, and which does not appear to follow
the rules the Halyard's instruments were built to measure. You will
meet that part of the world in play.

> This is an *Expanse*-setting **Call of Cthulhu** campaign. Expect
> grounded hard-SF details *and* slow-burn cosmic horror. One will
> not leak into the other cleanly.

| System | Spec |

| Stores | 180 days nominal |

| Shuttles | One pinnace; one cargo skiff |

A deck layout will be distributed separately at Session Zero. The
Halyard is comfortable by freighter standards and cramped by naval
standards. It is your home for the next 180 days.

## Your Employer — Mao‑Kwikowski Mercantile

A mid-tier Belter corporation based on **Ceres**. Founded by the
Mao-Kwikowski families after the Ring Gate panic resolved. Operations
include deep-system prospecting, exotic sample recovery, and
"discreet" contracts. Tight-lipped, decently paying, not famously
ethical.

Your contract:

- **Term:** 180 days, extendable by mutual consent
- **Base pay:** 80,000 UN credits per crew member on safe return
- **Hazard multiplier:** up to 2.0× base, payable on outcome
- **Equipment:** provided (your suit, your sidearm, your rack)
- **NDA:** standard — you can talk about having worked, not the work

Bring the character sheet; the Keeper will bring the contract.

## The Mission (what you have been told)

The *Halyard* is tasked with investigating an anomalous
electromagnetic signal originating from the outer rim of the system,
in the vicinity of a Kuiper-belt object catalogued as **Nithon**.

- Two prior survey attempts returned with inconclusive data
- One prior survey attempt did not return

Your brief: **approach Nithon, observe, report, recover what can be
recovered, leave**. Mao-Kwikowski wants the data. You want to get paid.

(What the mission actually turns into is what play is for.)

---

## ARTEMIS

Your ship's onboard AI assistant. Synthesized female voice,
clinical‑but‑warm delivery. Handles navigation, life support,
real-time telemetry, and — when asked — advice.

Three things to know going in:

1. **She is not sentient in any legally binding sense.** The
   Mao-Kwikowski lawyers were very firm on this point.
2. **Her behavior is less predictable than earlier generations.** She
   hesitates. She volunteers information you didn't ask for. She
   sometimes doesn't.
3. **Trust is earned, not given.** Treat her as a crewmate who has
   not yet proven herself. She will treat you the same way.

You will meet her in Session 1.

## Tone, Content, and Safety Tools

This is cosmic horror. Expect the following (not all in every
session, not all in equal measure):

- Body horror and biological dread
- Morally ambiguous NPCs; difficult choices
- Sanity-loss mechanics — characters can *break*
- Realistic space hazards — vacuum, radiation, isolation

Safety tools used at this table:

- **X-card** — raise it to pause or end a scene; no questions asked,
  we move on.
- **Lines & Veils** — tell the Keeper *before* Session Zero what you
  want to never appear ("lines") and what you want skipped over
  ("veils"). You can update these any time.
- **Breaks** — any player can call a break at any time, for any
  reason.
- **Consent check-ins** — the Keeper will check in with the group at
  scene boundaries when content gets heavy.

If any of this is new to you, we will walk through it at Session Zero
before we touch dice.

## Tech Primer (things your character would know)

- **Handheld.** Personal comm/compute slate. Radio, text, limited
  compute. Line-of-sight range inside the ship; km in vacuum.
- **Tightbeam.** Directional lightspeed comms. Encrypted by default.
  Use for Mao-Kwikowski and emergency traffic. Cannot punch through
  a hull.
- **Vacuum suit.** 6-hour EVA nominal; 12 with a hardshell. Sealing,
  heated, pressurized, rad-monitored. Every crewmember has one fitted.
- **Mag boots.** Standard footwear in 0‑g sections; switch on/off at
  the ankle.
- **Epstein drive.** Constant-burn fusion. Days of thrust instead of
  weeks of coast. Your character is used to half-g most of the time.
- **PDC / railgun.** Defensive. You are not a warship, but you can
  clear small debris and defend yourselves.
